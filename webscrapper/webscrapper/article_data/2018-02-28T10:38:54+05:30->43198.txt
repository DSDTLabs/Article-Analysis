CHANDIGARH: Referring to Pakistan as an artificial state emerged from contradictions, former deputy national security adviser Arvind Gupta said here on Tuesday that the marginalisation of Pakistani political parties had created a space for radicalisation and militancy. 

 Gupta, who is also director of  , was delivering the inaugural address of the two-day national seminar on 'Emerging Geo-Strategic Manifestations in Pakistan: Implications for India'. The seminar is organised by the Panjab University (PU) department of defence and national security studies.
 
 Lieutenant General M M Naravane, general officer commanding-in-chief (GOC-in-C),  , highlighted in his key-note address how China was replacing the US as Pakistan's biggest arms supplier. He said peace on the borders couldn't be achieved by brinkmanship but by effectual statesmanship. Lt Gen Bhopinder Singh (Retd), former lieutenant governor of Andaman and Nicobar Islands and Puducherry talked about Pakistan's flawed geography, sense of territorial vulnerability, heightened belief in its geopolitical significance, and its eternal focus on Ummah.
 
 PU vice-chancellor   spoke about the potential role that the academia of both India and Pakistan could play in the attempt to deescalate tensions, resolve all disputes, and build friendly relations between the two countries. At the end of the inaugural session, professor   highlighted the increase in ceasefire violations across the Line of Control (LoC).
 
  Air Marshal   (retd) assessed Pakistan's air power, Maj Gen Amarjit Singh talked about its strategic culture, Vijay Sakhuja spoke on the neighbour's maritime strategy, and   weighed Pakistan's military capability.
