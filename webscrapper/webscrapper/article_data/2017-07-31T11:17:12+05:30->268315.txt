THIRUVANANTHAPURAM: A peace meeting called by chief minister   in the background of the tense political situation in the state, following the murder of an RSS worker, has begun here on Monday.
 
 Apart from senior leaders of both the CPM and BJP, RSS state chief Gopalankutty Master is also participating in the closeted meeting chaired by chief minister Pinarayi Vijayan at Mascot Hotel in the city. CPM state secretary Kodiyeri Balakrishnan, BJP state secretary Kummanam Rajasekharan, senior BJP leader O Rajagopal also attended the meeting.
  
 Meanwhile, chief minister Pinarayi Vijayan vent his anger against news camera crew that entered the discussion hall before the CM. "Get out'', a visibly angry chief minister told the media thrice, before entering the hall.
  
 Generally, media is not allowed to cover the entire proceedings of such high voltage meetings of serious political ramifications. However, media on most occasions is allowed to take pictures of the leaders sharing the dais and thereafter they are asked to leave the hall. Or else, it would be informed to the media in advance that the venue will not be open for media.
  
 The chief minister had on Sunday assured both the Union home minister   and state governor justice P Sathasivam that the government would take all possible steps to ensure peace in the state. 
 
 The BJP had called for a state wide hartal on Sunday in protest against the murder of an RSS worker, allegedly by CPM rivals in Thiruvananthapuram city on Saturday night.
