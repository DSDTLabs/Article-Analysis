BATHINDA: Ten days after the death of farmer   during the International   protest,   of BKU (Ekta Ugrahan) held a protest and blocked traffic on Wednesday on the   to seek a compensation of Rs 10 lakh and job for one of the members of his family. The body has been lying at the Bathinda civil hospital mortuary.

 BKU (Ekta Ugrahan) activists on Wednesday afternoon assembled at the Bathinda district administrative complex and after protesting for two hours blocked the Mansa road leading to the flyover. “We are protesting for the last 10 days and the body is lying in the mortuary but the state government is unmoved. We will keep on protesting till our demand is met,” said BKU (Ekta Ugrahan) vice-president Jhanda Singh Jethuke.
 
 Mukhtiar (65) of Lehra Dhurkot village had died of cardiac arrest on May 1 when the farmers and labourers held a protest march from the Teachers Home to Bhagat Singh’s statue near Arya Samaj Chowk. Bathinda deputy commissioner Diprava Lakra said, “The state government is looking into the matter and trying to diffuse the situation.”
