 A police officer informed that the accused Ashish Savkare is the president of  , who along with four-five teachers visited Sanjeev Jaiswal who is the returning officer for the civic body polls.
 
 During the meeting, Savkare and other teachers requested   to drop their names from the election duty and accordingly considering the genuine case of two female teachers he gave them relaxation from poll duty, informed a police officer.
 
 However, the commissioner turned down the request of Savkare who got furious and got up from his seat and began to hurl abuses at Jaiswal and also rushed towards him to assault him but was stopped by the security guards, added the officer quoting a complaint of Sandeep Malvi, the deputy municipal commissioner. 
 
 Subsequently, a case was registered with   and subsequently Savkare was arrested and booked under IPC sections 353 (assault or criminal force to deter public servant from discharge of his duty), 332 (voluntarily causing hurt to deter public servant from his duty), 504 (intentional insult with intent to provoke breach of the peace), 506 (criminal intimidation) and relevant sections of Representation of Peoples Act-1951. The accused was later released on bail.
