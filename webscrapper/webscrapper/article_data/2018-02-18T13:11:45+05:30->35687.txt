* Prime Minister   today inaugurated the new   headquarters, a multi-storeyed modern construction fitted with latest communication technologies, in the presence of top party leaders, including its president  . 

 
 * The new party office is located at   and will make the BJP the first major national party to shift its office outside Lutyens' Bungalow Zone, following a   directive that party offices should be relocated from there. 
 
  
  
 
 * With the inauguration of the new party headquarters, the dream of crores of BJP workers has come true, said BJP President Amit Shah during the programme.
 
 * "Forming a political party is not tough in India. There are several parties with their own views, ideologies and working. The fact that there are several parties in the nation adds to the beauty of democracy in India," said PM Modi.
 
 * Looking back on the legacy of the BJP, the Prime Minister said: "Ours was a journey that began with greats such as Dr Shyama Prasad Mookerjee and Pandit Deendayal Upadhyaya. Generations of Karyakartas have given their life for the party. Ours is a party committed to Rashtra Bhakti."
 
 * Modi and Shah had laid the foundation stone of the new headquarters in August last year and a Mumbai-based architecture company had designed it. 
 
  
  
 
 * The premises has three buildings fitted with modern communication tools, enabling party office-bearers to co-ordinate official works with their counterparts in states and local levels in real time. 
 
 * Since taking over as its president in 2014, Shah has worked to have party-owned offices in all states and districts, a task in progress. 
 
 * Former party presidents L K Advani and Murli Manohar Joshi were present during the inauguration ceremony. Also in attendance were a host of Union ministers, including Home Minister Rajnath Sing, Minister for External Affairs Sushma Swaraj and Transport Minister Nitin Gadkari.
 
  
  
 
  
  
  
 * The BJP's shift may put pressure on other parties to follow suit as almost all of them have been functioning for decades out of bungalows. 
 
 * The saffron party has been working from Ashoka Road while the main opposition   office is at Akbar Road and the two premises are part of the pool of government residences. 
 
 * The BJP plans to move to the new office from the current one as early as next week, official sources said.
