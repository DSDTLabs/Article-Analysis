MUMBAI: A police constable suffered a knee injury when four youths on two bikes hurled floor   at him in   (E) early on Sunday. 

 Sumit Kadam (30) signalled to them to stop during a   at Hussain Tekdi on the Western Express Highway (WEH) at 3am. A police beat marshal, on witnessing the incident, chased one of the bikers and caught the pillion rider, Numan Sarang (19), a Mira Road resident, a few metres away. Police suspect they were caught in a previous nakabandi and feared being fined. 
 
 Sarang and his friends were heading towards Mira Road from Mahim Dargah. The nakabandi was carried out for north-bound traffic heading towards Goregaon. “The four, including Sarang, a school dropout, were carrying floor tile pieces and stones,” said Kherwadi police senior inspector Rajendra Patil. Kadam is attached to the same police station.
 
 One of the bikers and Sarang tried to escape after entering a narrow lane adjacent to the WEH. Sarang was caught when the bike skidded and he was thrown off.
 
 Police have booked the four under sections 279 (rash driving), 332 (causing hurt to deter public servant from his duty), 336 (act endangering life or personal safety of others), 353 (assault or criminal force to deter public servant from discharge of his duty) and 34 (common intention) of the Indian Penal Code. Sarang was produced before the Bandra court on Sunday and remanded to police custody till March 21. Sarang, who is jobless, had tried his luck in Dubai but returned.
