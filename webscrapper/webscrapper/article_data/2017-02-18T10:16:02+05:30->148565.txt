 INSV Tarini is a sloop built by Divar-based Aquarius Shipyard and is slated to be the platform for the first Indian all-women circumnavigation of the globe expedition of the  .
 
 In order to promote ocean sailing activities and encourage women empowerment, Indian Navy conceptualized the idea of the first Indian all-women circumnavigation of the globe. A team of six women officers have been selected for this endeavor.
 
 The officers have undergone basic sail training courses at INWTC Mumbai, followed by navigation, seamanship, communication and meterology courses at various naval training schools at Kochi.
 
 The boat is skippered by lieutenant commander Vartika Joshi and the crew members include lieutenant commander Pratibha Jamwal, lieutenants Aishwarya Boddapati, Patarapalli Swathi, Vijaya Devi and Payal Gupta.
 
 INSV Tarini has been built to a stock design called Tonga 56, by Van de Stadt, from Netherlands. The hull is made of wooden core fiberglass sandwich construction which ensures better performance than aluminum and steel. INSV Tarini carries a suite of six sails including a main sail, head sails, downwind sails and storm sail. Her mast is 25m tall and has been custom built by Southern Spars, Cape Town, for sailing in extreme conditions.
 
 The boat boasts a Raymarine navigation suite and an array of latest satellite communication systems through which she can be contacted anywhere in the world.
 
 Trials of the newly-built INSV Tarini were successfully completed on January 30 and all learning experience from   has also been incorporated in this vessel. The construction of the yacht was diligently overseen by the Warship Overseeing Team, Goa, and the vessel has been delivered before the scheduled date of delivery.
