CHANDIGARH: The Punjab State Council of Science and Technology ( ) will carry out an energy audit of all government buildings and educational institutions in the state to help in energy conservation. 

 Expressing concern over the "reckless energy consumption" in government buildings and institutions, chief minister Amarinder Singh gave directions for the audit at a meeting of   (PGSC) Society.
 
 Jatinder Kaur Arora, executive director, PSCST said the energy audit carried out by the council of the Pushpa Gujral Science City (PGSC), followed by implementation of various energy conservation measures at a meagre investment of Rs 1.7 lakh led to energy saving to the tune of Rs 12 lakh per annum.
 
 The council has also carried out energy audit of several other building complexes such as Viraasat-e-Khalsa, Mandi Board and Inder Kumar Gujral Punjab Technical University, besides various industries.
 
 During the meeting, chief minister Amarinder Singh also gave his approval for the upgradation of Pushpa Gujral Science City Science City theatre from it's present 3D version to 5D.
