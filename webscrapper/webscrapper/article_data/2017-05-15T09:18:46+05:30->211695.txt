AMRITSAR: Sikhs from around the world recited Jap Ji Sahib at different locations for reclaiming the site of Gurdwara   at Har ki Pauri in Haridwar on Sunday.

 In Pakistan, Sikhs gathered at the birth place of first master Guru Nanak Dev at Gurdwara   to recite Jap Ji Sahib. They also displayed posters and placards inscribed with messages of their support to Sikhs of India for the cause of construction of Gurdwara Gyan Godhri.
 
 Pak Sikh Ramesh Singh from Nankana Sahib told TOI on Sunday that a large number of Sikhs from Lahore had also converged there to express solidarity with gurdwara movement initiated under the aegis of Akal Takht. “I haven’t been to Haridwar but since Guru Nanak Dev had visited the town, it is a holy place for us,” he said.
 
 In Amritsar, Shiromani Gurdwara Parbandhak Committee (SGPC) held a function to recite Jap Ji Sahib at Gurdwara Manji Sahib Diwan Hall.
 
 Later, Akal Takht jathedar   said that the then Uttar Pradesh government had acquired the gurdwara land in the name of beautification but never returned it to Sikhs. A meeting of various Sikh organizations had been convened at Teja Singh Samundari Hall on May 24, to contemplate on future course of action, he added.
 
 SGPC president Kirpal Singh Badungar and other SGPC office-bearers recited Jap Ji Sahib along with other Sikhs.
 
 In Delhi, main function was organized by Delhi Sikh Gurdwara Management Committee at Gurdwara Bangal Sahib, which was attended by representatives of different religions.
 
 Talking to TOI, DSGMC president Manjit Singh GK said that religious leaders including Dr Lokesh Muni, Dr Umar Ahmed Ilyasi, Goswami Sushil, and   offered the prayers.
 
 He said Panth was hopeful of completion of construction of Gurdwara Gyan Godhir before 550th birth anniversary of Guru Nanak Dev in 2019. Reports of recitation of Jap Ji Sahib were also received from various other cities and villages across the country.
