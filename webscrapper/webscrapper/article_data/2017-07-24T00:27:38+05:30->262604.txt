 The Safdarjung observatory, which is considered as the base for Delhi’s weather, received 4.7mm of rainfall in a 24-hour period till 8.30 am, with another 14mm of rainfall recorded in the next 9 hours, Met officials said. The maximum temperature was recorded at 34.6 degrees Celsius, while the minimum was 26.5 degrees Celsius, one degree below normal for the season.
 
 Humidity also continued to remain high over the last 24 hours, oscillating between 65% and 97% according to Met officials and is expected to stay over 60% in the next two days. Other weather stations around the capital also received strong rainfall, with Palam, Lodhi road and Ridge all recording 12.4mm, 13.2mm and 11mm of rainfall respectively in a 9-hour period from 8.30 am to 5.30 pm on Sunday. All three places received 8mm, 4mm and 4.4mm of rainfall respectively, prior to that in a 24-hour period till 8.30 am on Sunday, Met officials said.
 
 Officials from the Regional Weather Forecasting Centre (RWFC) in Delhi say more rains are expected on Monday and Tuesday with overnight showers also forecast in the next 24 hours.
 
 “The next 48 hours are likely to see more thunder/rain activity which will see the maximum temperature drop even further. Humidity, however, will still stay in the high range for the next few days,” said an official.
