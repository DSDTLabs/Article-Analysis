 Rajeshwar has also named former engineer-in-chief S P Negi, Youth Congress leader Jasbir Jassi and others in the complaint and claimed that on May 9, they all forcibly captured a part of the property that belonged to him.
 
 He had alleged that his belongings were thrown out after breaking the locks. Though property caretaker had lodged a complaint following the incident, he had withdrawn it later. Afterwards,   himself lodged a complaint with the police.
 
 Police have registered a case under Section 448 (punishment for house-trespass), 427 (mischief causing damage to the amount of fifty rupees) and 34 (acts done by several persons in furtherance of common intention) of the IPC.
 
 A senior police official, requesting anonymity, said a complaint was received from Rajeshwar Singh and an FIR has been registered. He said the matter is being investigated.
