BENGALURU: Campaigning for the Karnataka assembly elections, which some analysts call the semifinals before the 2019 Lok Sabha polls, ended on Thursday with all three major parties rallying their forces for one final push. The parties can take up door-to-door campaign to woo voters before polling on May 12. Counting of votes will be taken up on May 15.

 The BJP gathered 35 of its senior leaders for 52 rallies and roadshows across the state, with the list including a battery of Union ministers and three-time chief ministers Raman Singh and Shivraj Singh Chouhan.
 
 
 
 
 The Prime Minister started the day by reaching out to dalits and other backward communities by addressing party workers on his Narendra Modi app where he attacked the Congress, saying the party never respected dalit icon and architect of the Indian Constitution, BR Ambedkar.
 
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
 BJP national president Amit Shah held a roadshow in Badami, from where Karnataka chief minister Siddaramaiah is contesting.
 
 If it wins, the Congress will become the first party after more than three decades to retain power in Karnataka for a second successive term. The polls are also a do-or-die battle for the JD(S), which is contesting with a tie-up with Mayawati, the dalit powerhouse from Uttar Pradesh.
