The court of additional sessions judge,  , also imposed a fine of Rs 1.10 lakh on the convict, and in default of payment of fine to further undergo rigorous imprisonment for one year and two months.

 In September 2015, Division No.7 police had booked the 47-year-old accused — who worked as a driver at a shrine in the city — under sections 376 (rape) and 506 (criminal intimidation) of the Indian Penal Code (IPC), after allegations of raping his co-worker. As per prosecution, the 19-year-old survivor was living with her father and brother after the divorce of her parents. After the second marriage of her father, her stepmother left her and her brother at the shrine. The siblings would live there and serve in that shrine. She added that she used to go to her co-worker’s room to wash clothes, as the washing machine was in that room. During that time, he started teasing her and showing her nude movies.
 
 The complainant said after that, he raped her and continued to physically exploit her for five to six months. She added that after she got married, the accused would call and harass her, after which her parents-in-law lodged a case into the matter. After booking the accused for rape and criminal intimidation, Division No.7 police arrested him. During the trial, the accused claimed innocence and pleaded ‘not guilty’.
 
 The prosecution pleaded for punishment to the accused on the basis of prosecution witnesses, including investigating officer of the case,  , statement of the prosecutorix, and her medical examination report. The court, after taking evidence on record, convicted the accused under sections 376 and 506 of the IPC.
