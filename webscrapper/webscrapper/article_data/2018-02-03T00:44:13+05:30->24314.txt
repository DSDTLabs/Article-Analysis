Salem: The government has taken steps to facilitate organ transplantation surgeries in Salem Super Specialty Hospital, said Dr J Radhakrishnan, state health secretary. “Kidney transplantation surgery will be started within 60 days,” he said. Radhakrishnan was in the city on Friday to inspect activities of health department in the district.

 Talking to reporters, Radhakrishnan said the state government had been working to fill the vacancies of 300 doctors and also nurses across the state.
 
 The state, he said, was placed first in organ transplantation and related surgeries in the country. “So far, we had such facilities only in major cities like Chennai. Soon, it will be extended to other government hospitals,” the health secretary said.
 
 Earlier, Radhakrishnan inspected a multi-slides MR scan installed in Salem Super Specialty Hospital at a cost of Rs 2 crore and also met district health department officials.
 
 Salem collector Rohini R Bhajibhagare, Salem Government Mohan Kumaramangalam Medical College Hospital dean Dr K Kanagaraj, Salem Municipal Corporation Commissioner R Sadheesh and other officials attended the meeting.
