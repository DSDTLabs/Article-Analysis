CHANDIGARH: On a suggestion by chief minister  , the   council of ministers, in a meeting on Wednesday, set up a sub-committee to review the state's mining policy and suggest ways to make it more effective and stringent. 

 The sub-committee comprises local government minister  , finance minister   and rural development minister Tripat Rajinder Singh Bajwa. Amarinder has called a meeting of DCs and SSPs of 14 districts on Thursday to discuss the issue of illegal mining as well as the state's mining policy. 
 
 The DCs and SSPs of the 14 districts of the state where mining takes place have been asked to give their inputs on the mining policy, said a spokesperson, adding that ways to curb illegal mining in the state would also be discussed.
 
 DCs and SSPs have been summoned from Amritsar, Faridkot, Ferozepur, Hoshiarpur, Patiala, Pathankot, Jalandhar and Ludhiana, said the spokesperson.
 
 A mining department spokesperson said 4,285 FIRs had been registered across the state while keeping a check on illegal mining. Teams of police and civil officials are also on the job of tracking all such activity, especially in areas where machines were spotted by the chief minister yesterday.
