MANIPAL: Beant College of Engineering and Technology,  , emerged winner of the ‘Smart India Hackathon-2017’ which concluded at   Institute of technology on Sunday. 

 The team also won a cash prize of Rs one lakh. Another team from Punjab, Lovely Professional University, stood second and Siddaganga Institute of Technology, Tumakuru, third. They bagged Rs 75,000 and Rs 50,000 respectively.
 
 Twenty six teams had worked on problem statements from UGC to find solutions. Zenith from Kasegaon Education Society Rajarambapu Institute of Technology, Sangli, Maharashtra, SMSVNS Titans from School of Management Sciences, Lucknow, Uttar Pradesh bagged fourth and fifth place respectively.
 
  G K Prabhu, pro vice-chancellor Manipal University and director, MIT, presided over the function.
