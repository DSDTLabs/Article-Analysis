VISAKHAPATNAM: Around 4.3 lakh students from first and second years are appearing for the AP   examinations this year which is slated to be held from March 1. While the authorities are doing their best to ensure that the exam system becomes more transparent, students are working hard to come on top.

 For the first time, a jumbling system has been introduced where students will appear for their practical exams in two sessions — from 9 am to 12 pm and 2 pm to 5 pm. 
 
 P Sarathi, a first-year intermediate student from a corporate institute, said, “I wish to get into   and pursue pure sciences. I also wish to pursue a course in engineering to understand technical nuances. The greater the stress on a fair practical examinations, the better the scope for people like me.”
 
 The   on the other hand disclosed that managing the jumbling system would be tough as it has been introduced for the first time. 
 
 A senior member of the board said, “Earlier, we were used to a pattern and now we need to adapt to a change. However, I sincerely believe that this will help student’s prepare better for the practicals and there lies the advantage.”
