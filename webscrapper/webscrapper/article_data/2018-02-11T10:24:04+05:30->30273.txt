PATNA: Chief secretary   on Saturday inaugurated a two-day  , or spring carnival, organised by the IAS Officers’ Wives   at the IAS Bhavan on Saturday. 

 Traders from Kolkata, Jaipur, Hyderabad and Ranchi, among other places, have put up as many as 75 stalls to sell dress-material, accessories, food etc.
 
 Association president Purnima Shekhar Singh said the carnival was aimed at bridging the gap between the bureaucracy and common people. “The association keeps organising programmes to collect funds which are used for social service,” she said and added the association was also working to help underprivileged families and get orphaned children adopted.
