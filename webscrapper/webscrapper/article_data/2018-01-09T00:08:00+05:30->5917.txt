Pune: Bike-borne chain snatchers had a field day on Sunday, striking five times between 7.30pm and 8.30pm in various parts of the city. 

 The police suspect two persons were involved in all five cases and snatched eight chains worth Rs 4.73 lakh. “We have obtained CCTV footage from the spots,” a crime branch officer.
 
 The two persons were wearing helmets and also had covered their faces with handkerchiefs. “The pillion rider, who snatched the chains, was also wearing glasses,” the officer said.
 
 The snatchers first targeted a 44-year-old woman on Wakad Road at 7. 25 pm, followed by a 48-year-old woman near Royal Empress Apartments in Baner around 7.40pm. 
 
 The duo then snatched two chains from a 42-year-old woman at Gujarat Colony in Kothurd at 8.20pm, before targeting a 57-year-old woman in Bavdhan around 8.30pm and fleeing with her two chains. The final strike was in Kothrud at Right Bhusari Colony around 8.35pm and the target was a 36-year-old woman. 
 
 In the meantime, the Warje Malwadi police nabbed a man who had tried to snatch a   from a 46-year-old woman. She had gone on morning walk when the man and his accomplice, riding a blue motorcycle, tried to commit the crime.
 
 The duo, however, fled as the woman caught hold of her chain and raised an alarm. The police later arrested the snatcher, Hassan Sayyad. His accomplice is on the run.
