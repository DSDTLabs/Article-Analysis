COIMBATORE: A chief judicial magistrate (CJM) on Thursday convicted a 72-year-old   of a   near the   for misappropriation of Rs 10.94 lakh church funds. 

 The priest was sentenced to two years of rigorous imprisonment (RI) and a fine of Rs 21 lakh was imposed.
 
 M L Jerald Pubalan, 44, a businessman from Nehru Nagar at Kalapatti was the member of St Sabastian Church located on Civil Aerodrome Road at SITRA. In 2010, he lodged a complaint with the Crime Branch (CB) –CID against the church’s parish priest  . 
 
 In his complaint, he stated that John Bosco, who was working in the church from June 4, 2006 to July 4, 2010, swindled lakhs of rupees that belongs to the church and Sebastian English medium school, which came under the church. John Bosco received donations from abroad, did not maintain accounts and failed to submit accounts to the Coimbatore diocese Bishop every year. He also invested the funds in movable and immovable assets and bought a bus for the school. He further registered the vehicle in his name instead of the school’s name.
 
 Also, John Bosco made transactions worth Rs 27 lakh from 2006 to 2010 in a bank at Singanallur and invested the donated amount in a private firm, the complaint stated. Since CB-CID police refused to register a case against the priest, Jerald Pubalan approached the Madras high court and requested it to pass an order to CB-CID to register an FIR against the priest based on his complaint. Subsequently, the court ordered the CB-CID to register a case.
 
 Based on the complaint, CB-CID police inspector Bharanidharan registered a case against the priest John Bosco and conducted a detailed investigation. Later, he filed a charge sheet before the CJM court. The charge sheet revealed that the priest misappropriated Rs 3,11,303 from the church and school from April 1, 2009 to March 24, 2010.
 
 Further, he had swindled Rs 5, 15,160 from the church and school from April 1, 2010 to August 20, 2010 and misappropriated Rs 2, 68,205 from the church. John Bosco was booked under Sections 409 (criminal breach of trust by public servant or by banker, merchant or agent) and 420 (cheating and dishonestly inducing delivery of property) of the Indian Penal Code.
 
 The case trial was held before the CJM KR Madhurasekaran, who heard both side of the arguments and pronounced the verdict on Thursday.
