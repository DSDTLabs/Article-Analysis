VISAKHAPATNAM: The   (VPT) has registered a growth of 7% by handling 61.02 million tonnes during 2016-17 fiscal as compared to 57.03 million tonnes in 2015-16. The   witnessed an incremental traffic of 4 million during 2016-17 fiscal, coming back on the growth trajectory after a lapse of five years despite severe competition from neighbouring ports and volatile economic conditions.

 Addressing a media conference in the city on Saturday, VPT chairman MT   said despite decline in import of coal due to increase in domestic production as well as decline in fertiliser traffic owing to increase in international prices, the port could achieve the target due to vigorous marketing efforts. He said around 2 million tonnes was realised by attracting new customers to utilise the port during the 2016-17 fiscal.
 
 VPT also achieved an operating surplus of Rs 396 crore in 2016-17 as against Rs 355 crore in 2015-16, he added. He said 11.42 million tonnes of iron ore and pellets was handled in 2016-17 as against 5.98 million tonnes in the previous year at a growth of 91%.
 
 A record quantity of 6.43 million tonnes of container cargo was handled surpassing the previous record of 5.15 million tonnes in 2015-16 achieving a growth of 25%. He said a record number of 3.67 lakh TEUs was handled surpassing the previous record of 2.93 lakh TEUs achieving a growth of 25%.
 
 The VPT chairman said wheat imports have started at the port after a lapse of nearly 10 years. 4.17 lakh tonnes of wheat was imported from Russia, Ukraine and Australia at the port.
 
 Referring to development projects being taken up at VPT, he said that   (NHAI) has received approvals for taking up the dedicated corridor from Sheelanagar to connect to Anakapalli-Anandapuram at a cost of around Rs 400 crore. The NHAI is expected to take up land acquisition for the project, which will be completed in a span of three years.
