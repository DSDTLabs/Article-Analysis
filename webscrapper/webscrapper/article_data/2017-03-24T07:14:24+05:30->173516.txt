JAIPUR: The   sets new targets to save more   dying in the state. Rajasthan is among the states with not so good health indicators. Now, health department has sets targets to improve one of the key health indicators — infant mortality rate ( ).

 According to the National Family Health Survey-4 (2015-16), the IMR in the state is 41 per 1,000 live births. This means means 41 infants die per 1,000 live births under one year of their birth. Over the past 10 years, the state has reported significant drop in IMR. Ten years ago, 61 infants died per 1,000 live births but now it has dropped to 41 deaths. Now, health department has targeted to bring it further down to 23 deaths per 1,000 live births in the next 10 years.
 
 Officials claimed that for the purpose, the health department will ensure full vaccination of the children which will prevent deaths.
 
 Health minister  , while launching rotavirus vaccine in the state on Thursday, said that deaths among children can be prevented by complete vaccination of children. “Health department officials will prepare an action plan to achieve targets of vaccination by 2020. Thirteen percent of children die of diarrohoea which can be prevented by rotavirus vaccine. An action plan will focus on vaccination of those children who were left out from getting vaccinated. Special drive of vaccination will be conducted in the state under mission Indradhanush,” he said.
 
 According to health department, there are 32 neo-natal dies per 1,000 live births in the state before completing 28 days of life. Officials said that the neo natal mortality rate (NNMR) in the state is 32 per 1,000 live births.
 
 Health secretary   on Thursday said that the health department has sets target to reduce NNMR in the state from 32 deaths per 1,000 live births but it will be bring down to 16 deaths per 1,000 live births by 2025.
