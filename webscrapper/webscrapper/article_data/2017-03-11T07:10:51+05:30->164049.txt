CHANDIGARH: Haryana chief minister   on Friday showered sops on the state on the concluding day of the budget session with relaunch of MLA Adarsh Gram Yojana and special sanction of 500 constables each in the traffic wings of Gurugram and Faridabad.

 Khattar said it during the zero hour while announcing the decision of President about adopting 50 villages of Haryana. Last year, President   had adopted five villages of Mewat and Gurugram districts.
 
 Referring to the Vidhayak Adarsh Gram Yojana, the CM said Rs 50 lakh per annum would be given for development of villages having population less than 5,000 and Rs 1 crore per year to those having a population of between 5,000 and 10,000. In case the population of a village exceeds 10,000, the financial assistance would be Rs 2 crore every year.
 
 The CM also announced the decision to pay monthly honorarium to those principals, lecturers and other employees of non-government aided colleges who had retired from service from January 1, 1988, to May 10, 1998, and were not getting pension. The retired principals would get Rs 30,000 monthly, retired lecturers Rs 25,000 per month, Class III employees Rs 11,000 per month, class IV staff Rs 6,000 per month.
 
 Khattar also announced the decision to open Shri Mata Mansa Devi All India Institute of Ayurveda, Yoga and Naturopathy in Panchkula.
