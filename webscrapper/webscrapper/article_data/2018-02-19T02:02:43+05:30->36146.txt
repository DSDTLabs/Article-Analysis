NEW DELHI: As investigations into the Rs 11,400 crore scam at PNB gather momentum, officials at overseas branches of other banks, where the fraudulently issued Letter of Undertakings (LoUs) were encashed, are under scanner, sources said.

 Officials of Hong Kong branches of Indian lenders including Allahabad Bank, State Bank of India ( ), Union Bank,   and   are part of the entire fraud which continued for 7 years.
 
 As per the guidelines, the tenure for the encashment of LoUs for the jems and jewellery sector is usually of 90 days and not 365 days - the maturity period of most of the LoUs linked with the  , sources said, adding that the deviation from the usual practice should have raised alarm in the minds of officials working at the Hong Kong branch of other lenders.
 
 Had someone raised alarm earlier, the quantum of fraud could have been lower.
 
 Among the lenders having a branch in Hong Kong, country's largest lender SBI has already announced an exposure of $212 million to the PNB fraud. Union Bank of India's exposure stood at $300 million, while UCO Bank has to realise $411.82 million. Allahabad Bank suspects it exposure of around Rs 2,000 crore.
