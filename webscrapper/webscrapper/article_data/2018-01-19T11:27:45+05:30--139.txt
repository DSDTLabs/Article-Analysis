KANPUR: More than 48 hours after recovering over Rs 97 crore in demonetised currency notes and arresting 16 persons, investigating agencies, including Kanpur police, are clueless as to how the accused planned to exchange the old notes with new ones. 

 They have pinned their hopes on ' ' of Bengaluru, who, according to those arrested, promised to exchange the demonetised currency at 40% of their face value. SP (east) Anurag Arya said on Thursday the police, after collecting concrete evidence against the accused, would move court to seek their custody. The accused are in judicial custody till January 30. The key accused include Anand Khatri, a garments trader and builder, besides   of Telangana, Sanjeev Agarwal of Kolkata (West Bengal) and Maneesh Agarwal of Howrah (West Bengal).
 
 Arya said the case was "mysterious" for several reasons as none of the 16 accused have revealed the name of the person to whom they were supposed to deliver the old notes. A few among them said the money was to be exchanged through one more "big firm", but they have not named it yet. 
 
 The investigators revealed they operated from six states - UP, Delhi,  , Telangana, West Bengal and Karnataka.
