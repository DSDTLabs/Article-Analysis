 Police said they received a call at 11.15am from a person who claimed to be the woman’s husband. The caller said the woman had switched off her phone immediately after taking his call and couldn’t be reached thereafter.
 
 The woman, Bhavna Thakur, had injury marks all over her body. “She was found lying in bed but there were hardly any blood stains there. We suspect that she was murdered elsewhere and her body was brought home,” said a senior police officer. 
 
 The woman had a son with her previous husband from whom she had been separated. Police were trying to ascertain who the last call was made to. Thakur ran an English coaching institute for foreign studies close to her residence. Neighbours told the police that after she returned from the institute, the woman had stayed indoors.
 
 The woman was rushed to Deen Dayal Upadhyay hospital where she was declared dead on arrival. 
 
 “We have registered a case under Section 302 (murder) of IPC at the Tilak Nagar police station and are trying to trace the person who called us,” said Vijay Kumar, DCP (west).
