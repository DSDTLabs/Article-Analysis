NASHIK: The consistent drop in the maximum temperature in the last 72 hours continued with the city witnessing a day temperature of 32.4 degrees celsius on Wednesday. 

 The drop in temperatures can be attributed to the light rain occurred over parts of Vidarbha, Madhya Pradesh and Chhatisgarh. The weatherman has forecast that the drop in temperature will continue until the regions of Vidarbha, Chhattisagrh, Madhya Pradesh would continue to receive light rain. 
 
 “Nashik’s maximum temperature on Wednesday was 2 degrees below normal. Most of the locations in north Maharashtra have registered below normal day time temperature on Wednesday,” said R B Mankar, a senior official in the   observatory here.
