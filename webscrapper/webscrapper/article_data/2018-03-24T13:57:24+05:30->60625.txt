NEW DELHI: In an overnight operation, security forces gunned down two terrorists in  's Anantnag.

 
 Preliminary investigation suggests that both the killed terrorists were part of Pakistan-based Jaish-e-Mohammed (JeM) group, reported ANI.
 
  "Based on seizures made from the site of the operation it can be confirmed that the two terrorists belonged to JeM," a police official said.
 
 Following the operation, a large cache of arms, including AK47 rifles, grenades and pistols, were also recovered from the site of the operation.
 
 Earlier today, Jammu and Kashmir DGP SP Vaid in a tweet congratulated the soldiers involved in the operation.
 
 "In an encounter in Shishitergam Police station Dooru Anantnag; today morning two bodies of terrorists have been recovered along with two AK47s, pistols, grenades and arms and ammunition. Efforts are on to identify the bodies. Operation concluded," he said.
