MUMBAI: In a case of road rage, a taxi driver and his brother, sister-in-law and sister were assaulted with a helmet by a biker and his wife on Nagardas Road in Andheri on Wednesday. 

 The incident occurred when the biker fell down after his two-wheeler was brushed by another vehicle at around 6.45pm. In a case of mistaken identity, the biker halted Devnarayan Tripathi’s cab.
 
 Andheri   have sought details from the RTO about the biker’s whereabouts who fled from the spot after assaulting Tripathi and others. A case against the biker and his wife, who was involved in the road rage incident, has been registered.
 
  Tripathi, a Virar resident, was heading home when the incident occurred. “On seeing the biker and his wife assaulting Tripathi, his brother Deepankar and sister Shashi tried to mediate, but they too were assaulted,” said a police officer.
 
  In his complaint, Tripathi said, “There are onlookers who witnessed the incident. I was surprised when the biker stopped my car and started banging the helmet on my car’s bonnet. When I asked him why he was doing this, he started beating me up. My brother, sister-in-law and sister were not spared either."
 
 "We are going through the CCTV footage to ascertain what exactly happened in the incident,” said Pandit Thorat, senior inspector of Andheri police station. 
 
  A case under the Indian Penal Code sections 34 (common intention), 325 (grievous hurt), 427 (mischief causing damage), 504 (intentional insult) and 506 (criminal intimidation) has been registered.
