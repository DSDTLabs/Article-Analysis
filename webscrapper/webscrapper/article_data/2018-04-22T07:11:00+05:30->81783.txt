GHAZIABAD: Shifting of power lines and other utility services along Delhi-Ghaziabad-Meerut   Corridor ( ) in the city is expected to begin shortly. 

 The National Capital Region Transport Corporation (NCRTC) has urged agencies like   ( ) and Ghaziabad Municipal Corporation (GMC) to start work on shifting of power transmission lines at the earliest. NCRTC has already released Rs 11.30 crore for the purpose.
 
 “In a survey conducted recently, we came across eight high-tension power transmission lines in addition to 30 underground cables that are coming in the way of the RRTS corridor in Ghaziabad,” said Sudhir Sharma, chief public relations officer, NCRTC. 
 
 “According to the estimate sent by PVVNL and GMC, we have paid the agencies Rs 6.30 crore and Rs 5 crore respectively and have urged them to start work on utility shifting, so that the civil work on RRTS corridor could be initiated at the earliest,” added Sharma. The NCRTC is scheduled to take up work on the RRTS corridor in July 2018.
 
 Apart from GMC and PVVNL, the NCRTC is also in talks with Power Grid Corporation of India and National Thermal Power Corporation (NTPC), and has asked them to send their cost estimate for utility shifting.
