Jaipur: A day after dust storm and rainfall mercury took a dip across the state on Thursday. Majority of the places saw a dip of 4 to 5 degrees Celsius on an average across the state on Thursday, still Bundi remained hottest in the state where a maximum temperature of 42 degrees Celsius was recorded, the met reports said. 

 Attributing the climatic change to the active upper air cyclonic circulation and western disturbances Jaipur met office said that similar conditions will prevail in northern and eastern parts of the state in the next couple of days.
 
 Jaipurities woke up to the pleasant morning as a minimum temperature of 20.2, almost 2 degrees below the normal notch was recorded. It was followed by a comparatively pleasant afternoon as a maximum temperature of 34.8 degree Celsius, almost 2 degree below the normal notch. Heavy dust storm and rainfall late on Wednesday also affected normal life especially in some of the localities of the walled city. 
 
 The unprecedented rainfall also affected the Rabi crops in the farms. Farmers were worried when at many villages in the eastern Rajasthan witnessed the damaging of the harvested crop of Rabi was lying in the farms. “Since it has also introduced power failure at almost 600 villages in the eastern Rajasthan including Dholpur and Bharatpur districts of the state, it has affected the farmers who were not been getting the timely power supply,” said an officer with the irrigation department. 
 
 Bundi was followed by Barmer where a maximum temperature of 40.2 degree Celsius was recorded, Jaisalmer 38.5, Jodhpur 38.0, Kota 37, Sriganganagar 36.2, Bikaner 36.6, Ajmer 35.9, Churu 35.8, Udaipur 35.6 and a maximum temperature of 33.1 degree Celsius was recorded in Pilani. 
 
 In its forecast Jaipur met office said that mild to moderate showers will take place at many places in the northern Rajasthan and some parts of the eastern Rajasthan.
