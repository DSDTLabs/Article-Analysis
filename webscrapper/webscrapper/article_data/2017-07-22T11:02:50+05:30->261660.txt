CHANDIGARH: The Chandigarh district court granted three years and one year five months of imprisonment to two persons in a  . The court of judicial magistrate first class (JMIC) Jagmeet Singh sentenced   a resident of Sector 35 to three years of jail and Samar, an employee of Tricity Autos, Zirakpur- Patiala Highway to little less than one-and-half years of imprisonment. The court also imposed fines of Rs 2500 and Rs 1000 on Dushyant and Samar respectively.

 A case was registered under sections 405 ( criminal breach of trust), 406 (Punishment for criminal breach of trust), 415 (cheating), 417 (punishment for cheating), 420 (cheating and dishonestly inducing delivery of property), 463 (forgery), 464 (making a false document), 465 (punishment for forgery), 467 (forgery of valuable security, will, and others), 468 (forgery for purpose of cheating), 470 (forged- document or electronic record), 471 (using as genuine as forged), 474 (having possession of document as forged) and 120B (criminal conspiracy) of the Indian Penal Code (IPC) at the Sector 36 police station.
 
 The complaint was made by  , branch manager of State Bank of Patiala, Sector 35 C, Chandigarh. The branch had received an application dated November 22, 2014 through Tricity Autos for financial assistance for the purchase of a Swift VDI car by  . He presented all the requisite documents including his pan card, residence proof, salary slip after which SBI sanctioned a car loan in his name for Rs 5.5 lakh. Vinod then purchased the car from Tricity Autos. However, it emerged that the real name of the buyer was not Vinod but Dushyant who worked as a steno grade-II at Western Command, Chandimandir.
 
 Dushyant and Samar of Tricity Autos acted in tandem to defraud the bank by submitting forged documents. Taking note of the same, the court held the employee of the auto company and Dushyant guilty and convicted both of them.
