CHANDIGARH: The special   court on Thursday awarded four-year jail to former zonal director of Narcotics Crime Bureau (NCB) Sunil Kumar   in a bribery case. The court also imposed a fine of Rs 2 lakh on the convict, who was booked by the CBI after being caught red-handed while accepting a bribe of Rs 2 lakh. A CBI team had laid a trap in Prashant Vihar, Delhi.

 The complaint was made by Rajesh Dua, proprietor of Euphoria India Pharmamedicals, Baddi, on September 5, 2009. It was alleged that Sekhri, along with his superintendent, raided their factory and inspected the records on July 23, 2009. After this, Sekhri asked Dua to visit him at his office on July 24 and 25 and demanded Rs 5 lakh for not registering a narcotics case against him. Later, they met at Fun Cinema, Prashant Vihar, Delhi, where the convict again demanded Rs 5 lakh. Finally, the deal was settled at Rs 2 lakh.
 
 Meanwhile, Dua filed a written complaint with the SP, CBI, on September 5, 2009, after which a CBI team, constituted by inspector  , reached Delhi the next day. They contacted Sekhri and laid a trap.
 
 Sekhri was convicted under sections 7 (public servant taking gratification other than legal remuneration in respect of an official act) and 13 (1) (d) ((i) by corrupt or illegal means, obtains for himself or for any other person any valuable thing or pecuniary advantage; or (ii) by abusing his position as a public servant, obtains for himself or for any other person any valuable thing or pecuniary advantage; or (iii) while holding office as a public servant, obtains for any person any valuable thing or pecuniary advantage without any public interest) of the Prevention of Corruption (PC) Act.
