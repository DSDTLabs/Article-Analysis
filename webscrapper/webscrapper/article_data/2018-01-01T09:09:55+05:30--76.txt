

 BENGALURU: If you are one of those complaining about the nip in the air during night, be ready for worse early January. The minimum temperature, especially at night, will dip further this month.
 
 A senior official at the meteorological centre, Bengaluru, said most parts of the state, including Bengaluru, are currently experiencing below normal minimum temperature. “The trend is here to continue and intensify further. Things will be normal only after January 15,” he added.
 
 However, officials don’t see the mercury hitting the record low. L  , scientist, meteorological centre, said Bengaluru will continue to experience fog/mist-covered mornings, with minimum temperature hovering around 15 degrees Celsius. 
 
 “Though the minimum temperature in Bengaluru is set to dip further by a few degrees, the same is not likely to beat the lowest-ever record. The all-time record for the lowest minimum temperature for December in Bengaluru was in 1883, when mercury touched 8.9 degrees C. In 2016, the minimum temperature in December had touched 12 degrees C,” he pointed out.

 “The minimum temperature in most districts is already below 15 degrees Celsius, which is a deviation from normal. Temperature is set to dip further in the range of 1.5 to 5 degrees Celsius in the coming weeks. In Bidar, Vijayapura, Kalaburagi, Kodagu, Koppal, Chikkamagaluru and   districts, mercury levels have dropped to 7 degrees Celsius in the past few weeks,” he added.
 
 Weather department officials said even on Sunday, the minimum temperature remained below normal in most parts of the state. Bagalkot recorded the lowest minimum temperature of 9 degrees Celsius in the northern plains of the state.
