MUMBAI: Mumbaikars woke up to a gloomy Tuesday, which weathermen said, was due to high pollution levels and moisture incursion. In fact, the air quality in parts of the city like   and   was pooer than that recorded during  .

 
 
 
 The System of Air Quality Weather Forecasting and Research (SAFAR) recorded an average air quality index (AQI) of 271. An AQI in the 201-300 range is considered poor and could be harmful to people with sensitive lungs. In fact, among the 10 specific locations monitored by SAFAR, five recorded an AQI in the very poor category of between 301 and 400 ((it had dipped to 319 in Diwali last year).
 
 
  
  
 
 Nitha T, a scientist at IMD, Mumbai, said, “There is also a trough in the westerly winds which is pulling in a lot of moisture. This causes cloudy skies and haze.”
 
 Meanwhile, minimum temperatures remained above normal on Tuesday—21.5 degree Celsius, or 2.1 degrees above normal, at   and 19 degrees—1.9 degrees above normal—at Santacruz. Humidity levels were also high.
 
 
  
  
 
 Residents complained of the haze. “While crossing the Worli Sea Link, the haze was so thick that the skyline had disappeared. I think the pollution levels have risen due to increasing vehicular pollution and digging of roads across the city,” said activist and advocate Godfrey Pimenta.
