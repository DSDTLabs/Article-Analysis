NEW DELHI: An analysis of the contribution details made by   to the   for the financial year 2016-17 shows that the maximum donations made were to the  . 

 These Electoral Trusts, registered with the CBDT, received a total of Rs 325.45 crore from corporates and individuals and distributed Rs 325.27 crore to various political parties. BJP received Rs 290.22 crore or 89.22 percent of the total donations received by all political parties from Electoral Trusts. The other nine political parties - which include Congress, SAD and SP - received Rs 35.05 crore collectively. 
 
 The report put together by   (ADR), says total donations to political parties during FY 2013-14 was Rs 85.37 crore, during FY 2014-15 it was Rs 177.40 crore, during FY 2015-16 donations amounted to Rs 49.50 crore and during FY 2016-17 they climbed to Rs 325.27 crore.
 
 Among them Prudent Electoral Trust contributed the highest 87.18% (Rs 283.73 crore) of the total amount donated to all Electoral Trusts during the financial year 2016-17. It then distributed Rs 283.72 crore to six political parties.
 
 Said the report by ADR, “Fourteen out of the 21 Electoral Trusts, registered with the CBDT, submitted their contribution details for FY 2016-17 to the ECI of which only six declared to have received any donations during that year. One, Janshakti Electoral Trust, was registered only in September 2017.” 
 
 “Prudent Electoral Trust and Samaj Electoral Trust are the only two, who have donated to five or more political parties, the common ones being BJP, INC and RLD,” said the ADR report, adding, “Three Electoral Trusts ( , Paribartan Electoral Trust and Triumph Electoral Trust) have not declared details of cheques given to political parties.”
 
 According to the rules formulated by the central government, Electoral Trusts are required to donate 95% of their total income to registered political parties in a financial year.
 
 DLF Limited contributed the highest amount worth Rs 28 crore amongst all donors of the Electoral Trusts, followed by UPL Limited and JSW Energy Ltd who contributed Rs 25 crore individually.
 
 Only two individuals - Suresh Kotak and Analjit Singh - contributed to Electoral Trusts in FY 2016-17. “Suresh A Kotak is the fifth largest donor amongst all, donating Rs 18.5 crore to Janta Nirvachak Electoral Trust while Analjit Singh donated Rs 1 crore to Prudent Electoral Trust,” said the ADR report.
 
 Prudent Electoral Trust donated 88.90% or Rs 252.22 crore to BJP while Janta Nirvachak Electoral Trust donated 100% or Rs 25 crore to the BJP. Three Electoral Trusts made contributions to only one political party each which includes BJP, AITC and INC.
 
 Between FY 2013-14 and 2016-17, nine registered Electoral Trusts donated a total of Rs 637.54 crore to political parties, of which only two Electoral Trusts (Satya/Prudent and Samaj Electoral Trusts) have donated more than twice.
