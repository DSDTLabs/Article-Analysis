AHMEDABAD: Around 90,000 students from across   will appear for the National Eligibility Entrance Test (NEET) on Sunday, for admissions to   courses. After making   compulsory for admissions to PG medical courses across the country, NEET has now been made mandatory for MBBS admissions as well. The exam will begin at 10 am and conclude at 1 pm. The NEET will be held across 2,200 centres across 103 cities in India, and around 11.35 lakh students will take the exam. Last year, around 8.2 lakh students appeared for the exam from across the country. In Gujarat, apart from Ahmedabad, Vadodara, Surat and Rajkot, the exam will also be conducted in Gandhinagar, Bhavnagar and Anand. The results for the exam will be announced on June 8. This time around, NEET will be conducted in 10 languages, namely, English, Hindi, Gujarati, Marathi, Oriya, Bengali, Tamil, Telegu, Kannada and Assamese. However, this time around, 74,000 students from Gujarat Secondary and Higher Secondary Education Board (GSHSEB) of B-group, along with around 20,000 students of other boards will appear for NEET from Gujarat.
