KOLKATA: The mercury plunged further on Sunday making it the coldest day of the season so far. The minimum temperature at 11.2 degree Celsius was 0.7 degree less than that on Saturday. 

 Apart from the minimum temperature, the day temperature too, dropped four degrees below the normal mark (22.1 Degree Celsius, making Kolkatans feel the chill even during the day. 
 
 In fact In fact the day temperature on Sunday was the second lowest for the day slightly higher than that of Berhampore where it was 21.6 degree Celsius. 
 
 Sunday in fact was the fourth coldest January in the past 10 years. The all time record of being the coldest January was on January 20, 1899 when the city froze at 6.7 Degree Celsius. 
 
 Apart from the city the cold spell gripped the districts too. Coochbehar recorded 5.2 degree Celsius while Sriniketan recorded 6.5 degree Celsius.
 
 The North Westerly wind has been blowing in the chill from the snow laden Himalayan region for the past few days dragging the mercury down. The Met office however expects the intensity of this wind system to slow down. As a result the temperature is unlikely to dip further for now.
 
 "For now there will be no further drop in the temperature, in fact we expect the mercury to start rising by Monday. But the rise will be marginal at least on the first two days," said Sanjib Bandyopadhyay, deputy director general of meteorology at   Centre Kolkata.
 
 According to Met office a western disturbance over Kashmir is posing a threat to the uninterrupted flow of chill wind. Putting further brake on the North Westerly wind will be another western disturbance that is developing over the western Himalayas. The system is likely to be formed by January 17.
 
 "When a western disturbance develops it affects the north westerly wind.This system induces cyclonic circulation over regions in the north and north- west. As a result there is moisture infusion from the   bringing a rise in the temperature," said Bandyopadhyay.
