PORVORIM: Legislators across party lines expressed their happiness at chief minister Manohar Parrikar’s arrival at the assembly complex to present the budget on Thursday and expressed their wishes for his speedy recovery.

 Revenue minister and Porvorim independent MLA Rohan Khaunte said Parrikar returning to the state to present the budget ‘is more than the budget for us’. He also termed the chief minister’s recovery and return to the state as ‘a strong message to those who had been back biting’. “Goa needs him very much. He has to be more cautious with his health. His good   will give us a good Goa,” he added. 
 
 “I welcome Parrikar’s coming to the assembly and wish him well,” former chief minister and Poriem MLA Pratapsingh Rane said. The veteran   functionary also said the chief minister should not ‘strain himself’ and that ‘life is more important than anything else’. “We need persons like him as he is well educated,” Rane said.
 
 Curchorem MLA Nilesh Cabral expressed hope that the chief minister would make a quick recovery. “I only pray to god that he comes back to guide us and lead us. Even if he is not here all the time, his guidance is very important for us,” he said.
 
 Lone NCP MLA  , appreciated Parrikar’s determination and said he prayed for his good health.
 
 “We have always wanted that he should recover and return to the House and today he came to present the budget,” leader of opposition Chandrakant ‘Babu’ Kavlekar said, adding that all his partymen were happy to see Parrikar in the assembly. 
 
 “I pray to god that Parrikar does a complete recovery and returns and works the way he used to,” Kavlekar added.
 
 Congress Navelim MLA Luizinho Faleiro said that he was happy to see the chief minister back in the assembly on the day of budget. “I hope and pray for his speedy recovery and hope that he comes back fast,” he added.
 
 The chief minister returned from Mumbai on Thursday. He was undergoing treatment for mild pancreatitis.
