BHAGALPUR: Bhagalpur SSP Manoj Kumar has constituted an eight-member special investigation team ( ) to probe the murder case of marble trader and contractor Amarjeet Rai alias Bittu.

 The team detained four persons on Friday in connection with the case. “Four suspects have been detained and they are being interrogated. We are also scanning the closed circuit television (CCTV) footage of cameras installed nearby besides looking into the details of call record of the deceased to find out the conversation that the deceased had in the recent past,” said the SSP.
 
 The SIT, led by   (town) Shaharyar Akhtar comprises SHOs of Ishakchak   station, Industrial Area police station, Tilkamanjhi police station, Jogsar police station, Barari police station, Babarganj police station and in-charge of the directorate of investigation unit (DIU) associated with the SSP office. A Forensic Science Laboratory (FSL) team also visited the crime spot and collected samples. 
 
 Bittu was shot from close range by two unidentified criminals near the head post office, when he was on way to his residence at Bose Park locality around 9 pm on Thursday. According to police sources, the deceased had gone to the market to order food for his daughter’s birthday party.
 
 Police sources said Bittu also worked as a contractor and had recently filed tenders issued by the Bhagalpur Municipal Corporation (BMC). Following the murder, people in a large number including his relatives and people from his native village   gathered at his residence on Friday morning.
 
 Bhagalpur Congress MLA Ajit Sharma, who also rushed to the Jawaharlal Nehru Medical College Hospital (JLNMCH), where Bittu was taken after he was shot, demanded immediate arrest of the criminals.
