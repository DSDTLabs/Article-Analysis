AGRA: One man was killed and three were injured in two different incidents of firing in Etah and Farrukhabad districts on Sunday.

 According to police, the incident took place in Jaar village of Etah and Gadiya Baburara village of Farrukhabad over land dispute. The three injured were shifted to government hospital.
 
 In the first incident, a 19-year-old youth identified as Pushpendar Singh Yadav was shot dead, while his two uncles Jabar Singh (40) and Rajeev Singh (45) were gravely injured after a Samajwadi Party leader Harinandan Singh Yadav fired shots at them over land dispute.
 
 The incident took place at around 8:30 am. 
 
 According to Rajnesh Chauhan, station officer of Nawabganj, “After the incident, the local   fled away. Police have sent the body for post-mortem, while the injured ones are been treated in Lohia hospital.”
 
 An FIR against the accused has been lodged under IPC section 302 and 307.
 
 Harinandan Singh Yadav is former district secretary of SP in Farrukhabad and district president of Yadav Mahasabha.
 
 Meanwhile, a local farmer of Jaar, identified as Panna Lal was shot from point blank by his relatives over land dispute.
 
 Station officer of Rijor, Neeraj Sharma said, “The victim’s family has given written complaint against six men including Kayam Singh, the main culprit. He allegedly shot victim in his chest from point blank over land dispute. The victim has been sent to Agra in critical condition.”
