PATNA: The Bihar cabinet on Tuesday gave in-principle approval to the allocation of Rs 1.54 lakh crore for the execution of schemes of its Agriculture Road Map 2107-22 to boost agricultural growth over the next five years.

 While a sum of Rs 25,777 crore has been sanctioned for minor water resources department, Rs 24,624 for water resources department, Rs 52,935 crore for rural works department, Rs 21,612 for the agriculture department, another sum of Rs 5,686 crore has been okayed for the animal husbandry and fisheries department. 
 
 According to special secretary (cabinet secretariat) U N Pandey, the cabinet also approved a urban development and housing department proposal for formation of special purpose vehicles (SPVs) for the smart city mission for Patna and  . 
 
 The SPV for Patna, christened Patna Smart City Limited, has been approved Rs 2,776.16 crore and that of Muzaffarpur, named Muzaffarpur Smart City Limited, has been approved Rs 1,580 crore. 
 
 The cabinet meet on Tuesday also gave nod to the health department’s proposal to give benefits of Dynamic Assured Career Progression scheme to the state government’s Ayurveda, Unani and Homeopath doctors with effect from December 2010. It also approved Rs 7.23 crore for 100 girls’ hostel-cum-model schools and released Rs 151 crore for the mid-day meal scheme for six months. 
 
 It also adopted the Prize Chits and Money Circulation (Banning) Act, 1978 of the central government in Bihar Money Circulation Rule, 2017.
