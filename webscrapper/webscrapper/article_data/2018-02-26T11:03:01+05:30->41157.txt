MADURAI: Manithaneya Jananayaga Katchi   Thamimun Ansari was on Sunday booked under various sections of Indian Penal Code by Madurai city police for holding a press briefing inside the Tamil Nadu circuit house without prior permission.

 Police sources said that the MLA was staying in a room and conducted the press briefing in another hall, for which he did not take prior permission. It was questioned by the building in-charge  , but the MLA and his supporters did not pay heed to him. 
 
 On being alerted, a police team conducted inquiries and deputy commissioner of police V Sasi Mohan visited the spot.
 
 Ansari told the police that he thought his party workers had already obtained permission. 
 
 Later, based on a complaint from Madurai district collector’s Personnel Assistant (general)   a case was registered with Tallakulam police, whose limit the Circuit House falls under. 
 
 Ansari, who won from   constituency on the AIADMK’s party symbol in 2016, was booked under various sections of IPC — 188 (disobedience to order duly promulgated by public servant), 143 (unlawful assembly), 353 (assault of criminal force to deter public servant from discharging duty) and 448 (trespassing).
