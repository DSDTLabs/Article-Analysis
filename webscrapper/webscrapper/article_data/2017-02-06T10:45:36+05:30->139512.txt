DEHRADUN: Intermittent showers lashed Dehradun and adjoining areas in the foothills on Sunday while higher reaches in the state received fresh snowfall.
 
  High-altitude areas in   region including Badrinath, Kedarnath, Gangotri, Yamnotri and Hemkund received snowfall. In Kumaon region, hills of Nainital and Pithoragarh district experienced snowfall as well.
  
  According to Meteorological centre, Dehradun recorded 2.1mm of rainfall, Haridwar received over 2mm of showers while New Tehri received 7.8mm of rainfall. “Most parts of the state witnessed rainfall while places located at 3,500 meters and above were lashed by snowfall,” said  , director, Meteorological Centre.
  
  The Met centre had predicted heavy snowfall at higher reaches in Uttarkashi, Chamoli and Pithoragarh districts and hail at isolated places in plains especially in Udham Singh Nagar, Nainital and Pauri districts.
  
  “The snow and rain occurred under the influence of a western disturbance and cyclonic circulation. The impact will remain till Monday morning and weather will start clearing afterwards. The weather will remain dry till at least February 9,” said Singh.
  
  The temperatures are also set to drop by a few notches in the next few days across Uttarakhand.
  
  On Sunday, the maximum temperature in Dehradun was recorded at 22.6 degrees Celsius while minimum remained at 12.3 degrees. The maximum temperature in Tehri settled at 16.2 degrees Celsius whereas the minimum remained at 3.4 degrees.
