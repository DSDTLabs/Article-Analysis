Vadodara: The city unit of the Congress and the Babu Jagjivanram Akhil Bharatiya Samta Andolan (BJABSA) have demanded that the Vadodara Municipal Corporation (VMC) should install the statue of former deputy prime minister late Jagjivan Ram in a dignified manner. The VMC had removed the statue from the Airport Circle on Sunday raking up a controversy.

 The statue was still covered was placed inside the circle sometime before Sunday morning. VMC initially removed the statue arguing that it was placed there without permission. It later stated that such statues could not be placed at all as per a Supreme Court order in 2013.
 
 BJABSA had on Monday claimed that the demand for the statue was cleared by the general board of the civic body. The organisation was even issued a letter in this regard by the general board. The VMC clarified that the statue should have been placed after signing an agreement with the traffic and public relations departments of the VMC. Also, demarcation of the space where it was to be placed should have been done. But no steps were taken in this direction.
 
 On Tuesday, both the Congress and BJABSA sought that the statue that had been removed should be installed in a dignified manner.
