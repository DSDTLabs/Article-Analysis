CHANDIGARH: The Punjab and Haryana high court on Wednesday stayed the selection of assistant professors in the subjects of political science and public administration for the state-run colleges that was being done by the   (HPSC).

 While staying the selection, Justice   of the HC has also issued notice to the Haryana government and the HPSC seeking their replies on the contentions raised by the  . The case would now come up for hearing on September 20.
 
 The matter has reached before the HC in the wake of a petition filed by  , 61-years old retired academician from Rohtak and several other students and scholars of the subject of political science and public administration. 
 
 Petitioners have sought directions not to consider the applicants having a masterâ€™s degree in political science against the posts declared for the subject of public administration and vice versa.
 
 Petitioners told the court that they are aggrieved from the decision of HPSC, which is recruiting agency for the post of assistant professors for the state higher education department, allowing the applicants having the master degree in the subject of political science to compete for the vacancies in the subject of public administration and vice a versa.
 
 It was further stated that controversy regarding interchangeability of political science and public administration has been pending for a long time.
 
 Petitioners has also referred several documents, received through RTI from various universities, university grant commission and government departments, in which the authorities have clarified that both the subjects are independent to each other and cannot be treated same for the recruitment. On February 16, 2016, HPSC had advertised to recruit on 44 posts of assistant professor in political science and 8 posts on public administration.
