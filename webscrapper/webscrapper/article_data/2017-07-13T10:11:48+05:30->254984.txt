THIRUVANANTHAPURAM: With Onam round the corner, the agriculture department has launched ' ', a scheme which envisages   with the participation of 63 lakh families. These families will be raising vegetables, required for the festival, in their backyards, on terraces and in grow bags. 

 Chief minister   inaugurated the project on the premises of the Secretariat here on Wednesday by handing over a packet of vegetable seeds to an employee, and his cabinet colleagues planted seedlings at the small stretch of land near the Durbar Hall prepared for vegetable cultivation. "Farming has to be promoted as a social responsibility," said agriculture minister   at the inaugural function.
 
 "People from all walks of life should take to re-establish the state's farming culture and to ensure availability of safe-to-eat vegetables," the minister added.
 
 The project, which is aimed to produce vegetables required for Onam in the state in a decentralized manner, is being implemented as part of the Haritha Kerala Mission and with the cooperation of Kudumbashree units, homemakers, students, workers under the employment guarantee scheme, vegetable farmers' clusters, officials and various institutions. As many as 63 lakh packets of seeds, 45 lakh vegetable saplings, and more than one lakh grow bags are being distributed to achieve the target.
 
 As envisaged by the project, each family will be producing a minimum of five types of vegetables which could be used for preparing their Onasadya. The aim is to turn this into a permanent mechanism and to cultivate among people a habit of using safe-to-eat vegetables.
 
 Secretariat employees association general secretary M S Bijukuttan inaugurated the distribution of vegetable seeds among secretariat employees. Senior officials including agriculture principal secretary  , took part in the event, which also kick-started cultivation of vegetables in 1,000 grow bags on the terrace of the secretariat annexe-II building complex.
