BHOPAL: Budget allocations for the department of micro, small and medium enterprises ( ) were slashed by Rs 80.82 crore in the budget presented by finance minister   in the state assembly on Wednesday.

 He announced that Rs 852.44 crore were allocated for the department. The budget allocation for the MSME department was Rs 933.26 crore in 2017-18.
 
 Malaiya announced budget allocation of Rs 939.67 crore for the industry policy and investment promotion department, up from Rs 797.11 crore than the last year’s allocation.
 
 Malaiya said the industrial growth rate again went up to 10.55 per cent in the financial year of 2017-18 after it dipped to 7.38 per cent in 2016-17 from 11.91 per cent in 2015-16. The state’s industrial growth rate is more than the national one.
 
 The finance minister also said development works to the tune of Rs 1,820 crore were being carried out at 22 new industrial estates coming up in the state.
 
 “The work is likely to be completed by the end of this year,” he said.
 
 Malaiya also announced Rs 1,501 crore for skill development, which was 61 per cent more than the last year’s budget allocation.
