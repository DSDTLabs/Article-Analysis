 The CBI had last year registered an FIR in the alleged irregular approval of Rs 305 crore foreign investment in INX Media in 2007 allegedly at the behest of   when his father was finance minister. The agency issued lookout circulars against Karti and others on August 16 and August 18.
 
 On August 10 last year, a single judge bench of the   had stayed the lookout circulars and permitted Karti and others to travel abroad. On CBI’s appeal questioning the Madras HC’s jurisdiction to entertain the petition when the FIR was registered in Delhi, the SC had on August 14 stayed the HC relief to Karti and asked him to submit before the agency for questioning. Karti complied with the order.
 
 On Wednesday, a bench headed by Chief Justice Dipak Misra requested a Madras HC division bench headed by the HC chief justice to decide the petitions filed by Karti and others challenging the lookout circulars within two months. The court said it would be open to the CBI to question the HC’s territorial jurisdiction to entertain the issue when the FIR was lodged in Delhi.
 
 The SC, which had earlier permitted Karti to travel abroad, said if any of the accused in the case facing lookout circular made an application seeking permission to travel abroad, the HC would decide it on merit without being influenced by any previous orders of the HC or the SC.
