Noida:   major   has submitted an application to Noida Authority seeking to surrender nearly 219 acre out of the 466 acre it was allotted for three housing projects in Noida. The plots were allotted to the company in sectors 96, 97, 98, 112 and 117 in 2006-07 for housing projects worth Rs 2,500 crore. 

 According to officials, the company has submitted the application under the UP government’s new initiative— Project Settlement Policy (PSP)—aimed at addressing the issues of the realty sector in Noida, Greater Noida and Yamuna expressway areas.
 
 While the plots in sectors 96 to 98 measuring 340 acre were allotted at the rate of Rs 11,520 per sqm, the land in Sector 113 (54 acre) and in Sector 117 (72 acre) were allotted at the rate of Rs 17,450 per sqm. 
 
 “The realtor has applied to surrender 153 acre in sectors 96 to 98, 36 acre in Sector 113 and 30 acre in Sector 117. The   is chasing a default amount of Rs 4,686 crore,” said Shishir Singh, additional CEO, Noida Authority.
 
 When contacted, company officials confirmed that an application had been submitted to Noida Authority under PSP. “Under the PSP, Unitech will have to forfeit 15% of total amount deposited with Noida Authority. The realtor will get 85% worth of land at rate prevailing at the time of allotment. If the developer wants any extra land, they will have to purchase land at current allotment rate. All outstanding dues of the developer will become zero,” Singh said.
 
 In March this year, Wave Infratech had sent an application to the Noida Authority seeking to surrender nearly 110 acres out of the 152 acres residential-cum-commercial plots allotted to it in sectors 32 and 25A in 2011.
 
 Speaking to TOI, additional CEO Shishir Singh confirmed that the applications from both developers were being processed. “A committee has been formed to peruse the applications,” he said.
