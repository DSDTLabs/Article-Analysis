NEW DELHI: The government has sent to the   for its approval 35 recommendations, mostly candidates from the subordinate judiciary, for appointment as judges to five high courts.

 These recommendations had come from the collegiums of high courts of Madras, Karnataka, Kerala, Jharkhand and Gujarat after which background checks were carried out by the Centre. Together, these five high courts have 97 vacancies as on July 1.
 
 At present, the working strength of the 24 high courts in the country is 665 judges against an approved strength of 1,079.
