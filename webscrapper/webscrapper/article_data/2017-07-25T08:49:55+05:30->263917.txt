GUWAHATI: With the southwest monsoon active over the northeast, the Met department has predicted heavy rainfall in several parts of the region in the next 24 hours. 

 According to the weather forecast issued by the Regional Meteorological Centre (RMC) on Monday, heavy rainfall is likely to occur at isolated places over Assam and   on Tuesday. The Met office said heavy rainfall is also likely to continue in isolated places in Nagaland, Manipur, Mizoram and Tripura.
 
 "Available data indicate that light to moderate rain occurred at most places in Manipur and Tripura,  , Assam, Meghalaya, Mizoram and Nagaland with isolated heavy rain in Assam and Mizoram in the last 24 hours," said a weather statement from RMC.
 
 The Met office warned of rain and thundershowers in Guwahati and its neighbouring areas in the next 24 hours with generally cloudy sky. The maximum temperature recorded in the city on Tuesday was 33.3 degree Celsius, but North Lakhimpur in upper Assam remained the hottest spot in the northeast with a maximum temperature of 34.5 degree Celsius.
 
 The RMC weather bulletin further stated that moderate rain, accompanied by thundershowers is very likely to occur at many places in Arunachal Pradesh, Assam, Meghalaya, Nagaland, Manipur, Mizoram and Tripura.
 
 An official of the Met department said that with 111.0 mm rainfall, tourist hotspot Cherrapunji in Meghalaya received the highest rainfall in the region in 24 hours, while Williamnagar in the hilly state also received heavy rainfall measuring 75.0 mm during the same period.
 
 Dhemaji in Assam recorded heavy rainfall of 92.0 mm followed by Lunglei in Mizoram, which recorded a rainfall of 91.5 mm in 24 hours. Heavy rainfall was also recorded in lower Assam's Goalpara where 78.8 mm rainfall was recorded in 24 hours.
