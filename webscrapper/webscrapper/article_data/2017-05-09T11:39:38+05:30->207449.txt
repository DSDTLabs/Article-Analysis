CHENNAI: Chief minister   on Monday inaugurated a slew of government programmes costing Rs 356.5 crore in PWD, highways and home department.

 He laid the foundation stone for the construction of five flood protection schemes -costing Rs 140 crore -in Cuddalore district, which was severely hit by natural disasters in recent years. The projects will come up in Cuddalore, Chidambaram, Kattumannarkoil and Panruti blocks.
 
 Palaniswami also gave away the best maintained dam award to   dam in   district.The award for the year 2015-16 carries a cash prize of Rs 10,000.About 40 irrigation schemes at an estimated cost of Rs 142.30 crore, including restoration of dams, lakes, supply channels, construction of new dams besides reconstruction of check dams in various districts like Ariyalur and Coimbatore, were launched.Four government buildings were opened in Podhigai complex on Greenways Road in Chennai.
 
 As many as 17 bridges and buildings built by the highways department at a cost of Rs 87.24 crore were inaugurated through video conferencing from Secretariat. A rail overbridge has come up near   railway stations, while a foundation stone was laid for the construction of four-lane 927m long railway bridge near   railway station at a cost of Rs 49.42 crore. For the welfare of police personnel, Palaniswami also opened 488 residential quarters across the state, including 125 in Virudhunagar district. Twenty-five police stations, including one at Royapettah in Chennai, were opened.
