 Rohit Sharma roared back to form and scored his 17th ODI hundred to help India post 274/7 in the fifth one-day international against South Africa at the St George's Park in Port Elizabeth on Tuesday.
 
 After a series of dismal performances in the series, scoring just 40 runs in first four ODIs, Rohit smacked 11 fours and four sixes to score a classy 115 off 126 balls. Rohit led the India innings and added three crucial partnerships - 48 with Shikhar Dhawan, 105 with   and 60 with Shreyas Iyer. But his untimely wicket in the 43rd over stopped India from reaching 300-mark.
 
 Pacer Lungi Nigidi triggered a late collapse for India as he took four wickets, including back-to-back wickets in the 43rd. India lost those wickets for just two runs and in 12 balls, all caught behind by wicketkeeper Heinrich Klaasen. Ngidi finished with figures of four for 51 in nine overs, with MS Dhoni (13) becoming his fourth victim in the penultimate over.
