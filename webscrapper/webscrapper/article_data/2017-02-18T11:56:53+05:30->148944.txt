MUMBAI: Maximum temperatures inched higher on Friday, increasing discomfort among Mumbaikars all through the day. The maximum   recorded touched 37.5 degrees Celsius, making it the second hottest February day since 2012.

 Weathermen said the high maximum temperatures are expected to continue for three to four days. The maximum temperature recorded on Friday by IMD   observatory was almost 8 degrees above normal; the maximum temperature recorded by IMD   observatory was 37.5 degrees Celsius, which was 7 degrees above normal. 
 
 On February 15, the maximum temperature recorded by IMD Santacruz observatory touched 37.2 degrees Celsius. 
 
 V K Rajeev of IMD Mumbai said the easterly winds were responsible for the rise in temperature. “Apart from it, sea breeze has been setting in late,” said Rajeev. The all-time record for the hottest February day stands at 39.6 degrees Celsius recorded on February 25, 1996. The highest maximum temperature recorded on February 23 last year was 33.2 degrees Celsius. Humidity levels recorded by IMD Colaba and Santacruz observatory were 62% and 38% respectively.
 
 Marine Drive resident Mahendra Hemdev said it was barely February and temperatures were rising so high. “This makes one wonder what will happen in April-May. There are many fluctuations in temperature, leading to extreme discomfort. If one is outdoors, the uneasiness is even more,” said Hemdev.
 
 The minimum temperature recorded by IMD Colaba observatory was 23.6 degrees Celsius, which was 3.5 degrees above normal, and IMD Santa Cruz was 20 degrees Celsius, which was 1.8 degrees above normal.
