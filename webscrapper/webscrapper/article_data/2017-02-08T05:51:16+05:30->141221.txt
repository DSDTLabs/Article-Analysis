CHANDIGARH: The   on Tuesday turned down the   of  , an accused in the   of Baljeet Singh, who had allegedly collected around Rs 90 lakh from youths for employment in the Haryana assembly. 

 Justice Inderjit Singh passed these orders while dismissing a petition filed by Balinder, who had approached the HC after a local court in Ambala refused to grant him anticipatory bail.
 
 In his dying statement before police, Baljeet, of Saha Kamleshpur village in Ambala district, had alleged that Balinder had forcefully taken some signed stamp papers from him. He had also revealed names of some other persons, whom he had paid money for getting jobs for some youths in the Haryana asssembly.
 
 Baljeet, who consumed celphos tablets, died during treatment at the Government Medical College and Hospital in Chandigarh on December 24, 2016. 
 
 In his plea, Balinder, a resident of Nanyari village in Saharanpur district of UP, had claimed that he was falsely implicated in this case and booked for abetment to suicide on the basis of the statement made by the deceased.
 
 Earlier on Janaury 12, additional sessions judge, Ambala, Narender Shura had dismissed Balinder’s bail plea, observing that the case related to providing employment in the assembly against consideration and custodial interrogation of the petitioner in this case was must.
 
 “It is not a simple case of repayment of the amount allegedly paid by petitioner. But it is clear that the petitioner, along with other co-accused, have created such circumstances that Baljeet Singh committed suicide. Even if the petitioner’s plea for refund of the amount is accepted in its entirety, the same cannot be endorsed by this court as the petitioner is also involved in illegal act of getting employment against consideration,” the judge held while dismissing his bail plea.
