 The nationwide data of all states suggests that the Southern states are doing well in providing better mobility to their police personal. While the national average of number of vehicles per 100 police personnel was 7.69, the southern states have 12.55 vehicles per every 100 sanctioned police personnel.
 
 Though Andhra Pradesh is having less number of vehicles the average vehicle strength per 100 police personal is better than its counter parts other than Telangana. Andhra Pradesh is having 13.06 vehicles per 100 police personal after Telangana with 14.85 per 100 police personal. This is mainly because the sanctioned strength of Andhra Pradesh police lowest when compared to southern states.
 
 Bihar was the worst state in the country with 0.74 vehicles per 100 policemen followed by   with 3.7 vehicles and Himachal Pradesh with 5.46 vehicles. Dadra Nagar Haveli has 23.23 vehicles per 100 policemen which means nearly one vehicle for 4 persons followed by Lakshadweep 15.17 vehicles and Telangana with 14.85 vehicles.
 
 Mobility is measured on the ability to move the entire police department at once. As the mobility of the police personnel is directly related to overall performance of the department, having better transport facilities is vital for any state government. Quick response by the police in real time, during law and order situations helps save precious lives.
 
 The vehicles needed for police mobility are divided into four categories as heavy, medium, light and two-wheeler. Depending on specific local needs some states have procured special purpose vehicles like riot control vehicles, mine proof vehicles and mobile forensic vehicles.
 
 The major chunk of the police vehicles is occupied by heavy, medium and light vehicles that are trucks, troop carriers, buses, jeeps and cars. They account for 55.23 per cent of the total police vehicles in the country.
 
 The data from 2006 indicates that the police departments in all states have focussed on increasing their fleet of vehicles as the total number of vehicles were increased by 55.74 per cent across the country
