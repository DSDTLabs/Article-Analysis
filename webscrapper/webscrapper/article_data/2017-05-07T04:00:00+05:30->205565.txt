Rajkot: A scorching heat wave swept Saurashtra and Kutch with mercury crossing 42°C at several places.  There is sign of relief from blistering heat in Rajkot city where maximum temperature has remained above 42°C since last one week. According to India Meteorological Department (IMD) officials, the maximum temperature in the city was recorded at 42.9°C while Amreli and Surendranagar baked at 43°C. The rising temperature has also adversely affected people’s health. According to emergency services 108, its staff attended 27 heat-related emergencies on Saturday.  The emergencies included vomiting, abdominal pain, chest pain, fall victim, fainting, dehydration and unconsciousness among others. In all, 417 heat-related emergencies were reported on Saturday in Gujarat. In fact, Amreli and Surendranagar were the hottest in the state. Gandhinagar, the greenest city of India, too scorched at 43°C. Extreme weather conditions have gripped almost all parts of Gujarat, including Saurashtra, Kutch, north Gujarat, central Gujarat and south Gujarat. According to figures of Emergency Management and Research Institute (EMRI) 108, the state has reported 3,508 cases of heat-related emergencies from May 1 to May 5 of which 144 cases were reported from Rajkot city alone.  According to IMD, no respite is likely for at least one week. The maximum temperature is likely to be around 42°C-43°C for next few days in some parts of the state.  Doctors have advised people to remain properly hydrated and avoid going out in the afternoons unless very necessary.
