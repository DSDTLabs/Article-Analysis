NAGPUR: The Common Law Admission Test (CLAT) was held successfully all over the country on Sunday. The test was organized by   ( ), Kochi for admission in 19 Law Universities and 43 other public institutions.The exam was conducted from 3 pm till 5 pm. 

 The test was administered in various colleges of the city including   (YCCE) and Shri Ramdeobaba College of engineering and Managment (RCOEM). 
 
 The exam was conducted smoothly with no issues regarding facilities in any exam centre.
 
 Manan Daga a law aspirant said, “Out of all sections, the mathematics and general knowledge portions were comparatively more difficult and we had to rack our brains to get the solution”. This thought was echoed by many students like   and Achyut Daga. The difficulty level of the exam was overall medium with some questions from logic reasoning being more complex.
 
 The maths section was the hardest said Nihar Bhagat who appeared for the test. Another student   had similar thoughts and stated, “GK was a tough nut but to crack with some tricky questions. But we couldn’t waste time struggling with each question as we had to manage our time to score maximum.” It was reported that the exam procedure required the students to provide valid identity proof but certain documents like ration card were not accepted at the centre.
 
 NUALS will deal with any queries regarding the questions after the answer key is released. NUALS is expected to release the answer key for the same in the coming two or three days. The provincial results will be declared ‪on May 26‬ and the final results will be declared ‪on May 31.‬
