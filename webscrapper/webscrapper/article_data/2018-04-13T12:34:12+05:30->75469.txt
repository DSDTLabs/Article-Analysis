RAIPUR: A day after the death of senior BJP leader and former minister Hem Chand  , chief minister Raman Singh on Thursday made an announcement to rename the state-run   after the departed leader, who had earlier served in his cabinet as higher education minister. 

 Singh made the announcement after attending the last rites of Hemchand Yadav, who died in the early hours on Wednesday at the All India Institute of Medical Sciences (AIIMS) in New Delhi following a brief illness. The mortal remains of the departed leader was consigned to flames and his elder son lit the funeral pyre.
 
 Besides, chief minister Raman Singh, Vidhan Sabha speaker Gaurishankar Agrawal, state BJP president Dharamlal Kaushik, ministers, legislators and senior party functionaries attended the funeral. A large number of people attended the BJP leader's funeral procession. Later, his body was kept at the BJP office where hundreds of people paid homage to the departed leader. 
 
 Yadav was elected to the state assembly of undivided   from   constituency in 1998. After formation of Chhattisgarh, he was elected to Chhattisgarh legislative assembly in 2003 and 2008. Yadav, who represented Durg constituency in assembly, was a minister in Raman Singh cabinet for 10 years from 2003 to 2013 and held portfolios of labour, water resources, panchayat and rural development and education. He lost 2013 assembly elections from same constituency.
