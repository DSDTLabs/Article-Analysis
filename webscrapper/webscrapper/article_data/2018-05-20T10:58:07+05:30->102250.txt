BATHINDA: Patiala MP Dr Dharamvira Gandhi has advocated cultivation of   in  . He was speaking at a programme organized by Unnat Kheti Welfare Society in Bathinda on Saturday.

 Dr Gandhi, the suspended member of the Aam Aadmi Party (AAP), said after the state government strictly prohibited the use of poppy and opium before the last assembly elections, use of chitta (synthetic drugs) started rising among the addicts.
 
 Gandhi had been a votary for allowing poppy cultivation in Punjab. “Youth are hooked to medicinal drugs in the absence of poppy and opium. The synthetic and medicinal drugs are more harmful than poppy. To save people from getting addicted to these harmful and costly drugs, cultivation of poppy seed should be allowed in the state,” said the MP.
 
 He said poppy cultivation was allowed in Rajasthan, Madhya Pradesh and Maharashtra. “The Punjab government should also allow cultivation of poppy in select areas on experimental basis for a year and see the results. Poppy seed is an analgesic, which is used in many medicines. In the early 1950s it was widely used at many places across India,” he said.
 
 Bhartiya Kisan Union (Lakhowal) general secretary Harinder Singh Lakhowal, Ludhiana MLA Simarjit Singh Bains promoted ‘Team Insaaf’ activist Jagmeet Singh were also present on the occasion.
