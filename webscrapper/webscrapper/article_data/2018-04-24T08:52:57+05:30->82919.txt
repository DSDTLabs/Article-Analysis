CHANDIGARH: The Haryana   Commission ( ) is considering setting up its bench or a permanent sitting for the people of southern parts of the state which would save their time and money, commission chairman   (retd) said on Monday.

 Mittal, who was interacting with the media in Chandigarh, said people of south Haryana had to visit Chandigarh for their complaints resulting in loss of time and money. “Therefore, the commission has a plan to set up a bench or permanent sitting after exploring the possibilities,” he said.
 
 He said about 2,500 cases were pending with the commission. These included about 800 new cases which would soon be disposed of. People are made aware about human rights during seminars, which would continue in the future too, he added.
