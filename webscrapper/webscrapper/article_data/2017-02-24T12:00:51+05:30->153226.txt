MANGALURU: In view of the Karavali Souharda rally which will be attended by Kerala chief minister   and a hartal called by the Hindu outfits opposing his speech on communal harmony,   district administration has banned the sale of liquor in the district on Friday and Saturday.

 It will be effective from Friday, 6am till Saturday midnight. Invoking Karnataka Excise Act, 1965, K G Jagadeesha, deputy commissioner of DK has declared two days as ‘Dry Days’. 
 
 He said that the decision to bar sale of liquor was taken in view of the reports from police commissioner M Chandra Sekhar and superintendent of police  . The two officers have asked for bar on sale of liquor in order to maintain law and order on these two days in the district.
