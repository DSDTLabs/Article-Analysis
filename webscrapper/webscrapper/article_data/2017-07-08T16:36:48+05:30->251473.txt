Panaji: As many as 240 motor vehicle   had their permits suspended by the state transport department from April to June this year. The figure marks a substantial increase from the 192 such cases registered during the preceding trimester. 

 Of the 240   suspended from April to June, 102 holders were caught using   while driving and 96 were found to be under the influence of alcohol/drugs. The use of mobile phones while driving presently attracts a three-month licence suspension. Prior to this, motorists were able to get away with a mere payment of fines.
 
 “We had told officers to keep an eye out for those using mobile phones while driving. Following the crackdown, we have observed that motorists have become more disciplined,” a transport official said.
 
 The other violations in the list include  , over speeding and riding without a helmet.
