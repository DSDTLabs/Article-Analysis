NEW DELHI: An increase in expenditure and a decline in the share of total enrollment in private schools are the main takeaways of the education sector, the  , 2016-17, shows. In fact, the city bagged the numero uno position among the states in terms of expenditure on education in 2015-16. 

 The total expenditure (plan and non-plan) on education, including sports and art and culture, increased from Rs 4,799 crore in 2011-12 to Rs 10,690 crore in 2016-17. The share of expenditure on education in the total budget of the   has increased from 18% in 2011-12 to 23% in 2016-17. In fact, the government’s   in 2016-17 was 1.72% of GSDP, the highest among all sectors.
 
 For the first time in five years, Delhi’s investment on education is the highest, upstaging Assam. Assam’s ratio has been in the range of 22-22.6%. Delhi’s share in 2015-16 was the highest at 23.8%, much higher than the national average of 16.4%.
 
 The enrollment figures indicate an interesting trend. The total enrolment increased to 44.30 lakh in 2015-16 from that of 44.13 lakh in 2014-15. The student-teacher ratio also improved to 1:30 in the same period. In the government schools, there has been a marginal decline in the enrolment (from 15.42 lakh in 2014-15 to 15.09 lakh in 2015-16) at secondary and senior secondary levels. There is a marginal increase in enrolment at government aided schools. However, despite a decrease in the number of schools, the highest jump in enrolment has been recorded in the private schools—from 14.71 lakh to 15.26 lakh in the same period. However, individually for the private schools, the share of total enrolment has gone down from 38.34% in 2013-14 to 32.09% in 2015-16.
