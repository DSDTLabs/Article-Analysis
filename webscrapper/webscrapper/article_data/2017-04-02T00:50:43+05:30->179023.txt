Hyderabad: As many as 152 child abuse cases were registered in nine months between May 2016 and February 2017 in the Hyderabad police commissionerate. The victims were below 10 years in 24 of these cases.

 This disturbing data was disclosed by Hyderabad police during a day-long workshop on ‘Consultation on implementation of POCSO’ with different stakeholders like police officers, prosecution officers, Bharosa Support Centre workers and others at the Marri Channa Reddy Human Resource Development Institute on Saturday. 
 
 Metropolitan sessions judge Radha Rani said all the 58 magistrate courts in the city were using video conferencing to record statements & trial proceedings in the Protection of Children from Sexual Offences ( ) Act cases.
 
 “All the children were brought out of trauma through sustained professional counselling and have been rehabilitated according to their needs by the Bharosa Support Centre,” Hyderabad additional commissioner of police (Crimes) Swati Lakra said. “Initiatives like Bharosa Centre have been brought in to support the victim and their family by providing integrated services,” Hyderabad police commissioner M Mahendar Reddy said.
