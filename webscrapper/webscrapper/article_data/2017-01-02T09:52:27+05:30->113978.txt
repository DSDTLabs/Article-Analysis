LUCKNOW: Dense   kept Sun under wraps on the first day of the   on Sunday, intensifying cold conditions in the city and disrupting rail, air and road traffic. Similar conditions are expected on Monday. 

 Thick fog descended on Saturday night, leading to drop in visibility to near zero at many places in the city. Though visibility improved to around 200 metres in the morning, a thick layer of fog remained suspended all day on Sunday. The maximum   could not rise above 17.6 degrees Celsius, four degrees below normal. However, minimum temperature was 9.4 degrees Celsius, two degrees above normal.
 
 State Met director JP Gupta said that icy   winds coming from snow clad mountains led to severe cold conditions in the city last week. The wind pattern, he said, changed slightly on Saturday with the entry of easterly winds coming from the Bay of Bengal. The easterlies also brought moisture which got condensed into dense fog due to low temperatures, he said. Dense fog is expected in the morning on Monday. There can be sunshine during the day. Maximum and minimum temperatures are expected to be around 19 and 9 degrees Celsius, respectively.
 
 Around 26 flights arriving and 25 flights departing from Lucknow were delayed due to fog on Sunday. The delay was from 15 minutes to over four hours. Also, around 34 long distance trains arrived late at Charbagh station in Lucknow. The trains were running late from three to 13 hours.
