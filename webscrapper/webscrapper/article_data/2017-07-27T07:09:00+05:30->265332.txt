The court has listed the matter for further hearing on July 28.

 A division bench of high court comprising acting chief justice Sanjay Karol and justice Ajay Mohan Goel passed the order on a petition taken up suo motu as a public interest litigation (PIL).
 
 There is resentment among people against the Kullu police for its failure to arrest the accused involved in the rape and murder of an 8-year-old girl at Bhuntar in April this year. After incident came to light, Bhuntar residents along with some social organizations had held protest demonstrations but there has been no breakthrough in the case.
 
 With the high court taking note of the incident, it has sought report from the chief secretary, home secretary, director general of police (DGP), Kullu deputy commissioner (DGP) and superintendent of police (SP).
 
 According to sources, the girl lived in a hutment near truck union with her parents, who are migrants from Uttar Pradesh. She had gone missing on April 29 after she had gone to the market. After the parents failed to find her, thsy reported the matter to the police that started the search. The girl was found lying in a pool of blood near Beas river at Parla Bhuntar on April 30. She was taken to the hospital but could not be saved.
