NEW DELHI: A week after   decided to make the washrooms of hotels & restaurants accessible to general public for a charge of Rs 5, the owners and representatives of associations met the corporation officials on Wednesday to discuss the implementation and ramifications.

 SDMC officials claimed that establishments have agreed to allow free usage of their washrooms for women and children. SDMC commissioner P K Goel said, “It has been conveyed to all stakeholders that the women’s safety is of paramount concern and the responsibility will be shared.” The matter will however be reviewed on 1st May, 2017.
 
 “After discussions, National   Association of India tabled concerns of restaurateurs to the SDMC commissioner regarding the matter on Wednesday. The policy will be implemented on a trial basis for a period of 4 weeks,” said Riyaaz Amlani, president of NRAI.
