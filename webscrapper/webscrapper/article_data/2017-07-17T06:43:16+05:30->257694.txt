LUCKNOW: Chief minister Adityamnath   has announced a   of Rs 2 lakh for the family of each patient who died in the Saturday evening fire at  . The hospital administration continued to claim there was no loss of life in the incident. 

 “A compensation of Rs 2 lakh will be given to those who died during evacuation or because of shock in the KGMU Trauma Centre fire incident,” said an official statement by the Uttar Pradesh government. Media reports, however, claimed that eight people, including two children, died in the incident. 
 
 Addressing the media on Sunday, KGMU vice-chancellor Prof MLB Bhatt said there was no death reported in the fire incident. “No one died directly or indirectly in the fire incident,” he said, adding, “At least 12 deaths take place in KGMU daily.” 
 
 Earlier, the chief minister paid a visit to the Trauma Centre on Sunday and met patients and attendants who were shifted when the fire broke out. After reviewing the situation, he said a drive must be initiated to check fire safety in multi-storey buildings. Relevant orders were passed on to director general, fire services. 
 
 The CM advised KGMU authorities to undertake steps to avert repetition of any such incident. On Saturday, he had directed commissioner Lucknow Division to conduct an inquiry into the case and submit a report within three days.
