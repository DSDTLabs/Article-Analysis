MUMBAI: A vacation bench of Bombay high court on Tuesday restrained the civic administration from demolishing   stalls in south Mumbai.

 A bench of Justices P D Naik and MS Karnik were hearing two petitions filed by   and S B Shambharkar and other hawkers who challenged a May 20 order issued by the Brihan Mumbai Municipal Corporation ( ) to revoke licences issued to a list of stalls at Mumbai’s famed Fashion Street on   after revoking the licence issued to them.
  
 The HC looking at the urgency said it was “inclined to grant interim protection’’ and directed the BMC to “maintain status-quo’’ and “restrained from taking action.’’ 
 
 The HC posted the matter for next hearing on June 8.
