 As many as 149 motorists were found driving under the influence of liquor and 256 drivers challaned for over speeding during the drive that started on May 25 and went till May 27.

 As per the available data, UT traffic police have issued 3,352   for drunken driving and 6,356 challans for over-speeding till date in 2018. Traffic police had issued only 1,264 challans for drunken driving and 1,203 for over-speeding during the corresponding period last year.
 
 Traffic police have recommended cancellation of 3,859   from January 1 till date to the authorities concerned.
 
 Chandigarh traffic police would also recommend suspension of driving licenses of violators for over speeding, use of mobile phone, driving vehicle under the influence of alcohol and jumping red light.
 
 According to Section 185 of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, people involved in drunken driving shall be punishable for the first offence with imprisonment for a term which may extend up to six months, or with fine which may extend up to Rs 2,000, or both. Further, as per Section 20 (2) of the  , people driving under the influence of liquor may lose their driving license for period of not less than six months.
 
 As per Section 22 (2) of Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, if a person, who had been previously convicted of an offence punishable under Section 185, is again convicted of an offence punishable under that section, the Court, making such subsequent conviction shall, by order, cancel the driving licence held by such person.
