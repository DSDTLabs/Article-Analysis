NEW DELHI: The   on Wednesday sought to corner the government on the issue of agrarian crisis, highlighting the incident in Mandsaur, Madhya Pradesh, where six farmers were killed in police firing during agitations early last month. The opposition members also demanded implementation of the M S Swaminathan Commission report so that farmers got remunerative prices of their produce.

 The commission had recommended that the minimum support price (MSP) of agricultural produce should 50% more than the total cost of production.
 
 Initiating a discussion on the agrarian situation in Lok Sabha, Congress member Jyotiraditya Scindia blamed the government's policy for the present mess where farmers were not even able to sell their produce.
