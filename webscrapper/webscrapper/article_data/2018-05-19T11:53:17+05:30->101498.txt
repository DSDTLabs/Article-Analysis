AMRITSAR: A US-based NGO, United Sikhs has filed a police complaint against   here on Friday for allegedly used degrading, blasphemous and inflammatory words against the fifth master of Sikhs, Guru Arjan Dev, which according to the NGO’s spokesperson, Mejinderpal Kaur had hurt the religious sentiments of the community. 

 Kaur in a video that went viral, said Das not only questioned the integrity of Gurbani but also demeaned and questioned the martyrdom of Guru Arjan Dev. She added the complaint was filed by Satnam Singh through United Sikh’s advocates Sandeep Gorsi and Neetu Singh for alleged offences under various sections of the Indian Penal Code (IPC). 
 
 The sections are 153-A (promoting disharmony, enmity or feelings of hatred between different groups on the grounds of religion, race, place of birth, residence, language, and others and doing acts prejudicial to maintenance of harmony), 153-B (imputations, assertions prejudicial to national integration), 292 (whoever, to the annoyance of others; (a) Does any obscene act in any public place, or. (b) Sings, recites or utters any obscene song, ballad or words, in or near any public place, shall be punished with imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to three months, or with fine, or with both], 293 (sale of obscene objects to a young person), 295-A (deliberate and malicious acts, intended to outrage religious feelings or any class by insulting its religion or religious beliefs), 298 (uttering, words, and others with deliberate intent to wound the religious feelings of any person) and 500 (punishment for defamation). She added the copies of the complaint had been sent to the Prime Minister and Punjab chief minister.
 
 The Sikh body lodged the complaint after the Jathedar of Akal Takht, Giani Gurbachan Singh had directed Shiromani Gurdwara Parbandhak Committee (SGPC) president and other Sikh bodies to register cases against Das.
