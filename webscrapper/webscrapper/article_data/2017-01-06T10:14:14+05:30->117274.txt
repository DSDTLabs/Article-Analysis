MUMBAI: The market made a solid opening on Friday as the benchmark   gained over 103 points taking support from domestic investors, extending its yesterday's rally on the back of a firm Asian trend.

 Moreover, an appreciating rupee brought more cheer.
 
 The 30-share index advanced 103.48 points, or 0.38 per cent, to 26,981.72, with healthcare, consumer durables, metal, auto, capital goods, oil and gas and banking stocks trading in the positive zone, rising by up to Rs 0.59 per cent.
 
 The gauge had rallied 245.11 points in the previous session.
 
 The NSE index   also recaptured the 8,300 level by inching up 30.05 points, or 0.36 per cent, to 8,303.85.
 
 Brokers said increased buying by domestic financial institutions amid a firming trend in the Asian region gave investors the confidence.
 
 Hong Kong's Hang Seng was up 0.49 per cent and Shanghai Composite gained 0.02 per cent in early trade today. Japan's Nikkei, however, fell 0.40 per cent.
 
 The   Industrial Average, however, ended 0.21 per cent lower on Thursday.
