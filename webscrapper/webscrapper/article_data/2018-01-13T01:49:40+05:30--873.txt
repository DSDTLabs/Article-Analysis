GURGAON: An assistant vice-president (AVP) of   Bank’s Sector 53 branch on   was robbed of his   Ertiga   by three armed robbers in the early hours of Friday. 

 The robbers, who were in a   Suzuki Alto, stopped and overpowered the 50-year-old bank executive in a parking lot near the bank branch as he left it to return home after work. Police have lodged an FIR and are investigating the matter, but haven’t so far found a clue as to who was behind the robbery.
 
 According to police,  , resident of Shivaji Nagar, was about to leave for home at around 1am on Friday, after working overtime, when the incident happened just outside the parking area of the bank branch in Wazirabad village, Sector 53. He later lodged a complaint at the Sector 53 police station.
 
 “I had arrived at my bank branch on Thursday morning and parked my steel-grey Ertiga (2014 model) near the branch, as per my daily routine. Around 1am on Friday, as I left the bank to reach the car, unlocked it and kept my laptop bag in it, three men in their 20s arrived in a white Alto, pushed me aside, snatched the car keys and drove off with my car. The bag contained my laptop and other valuables. They also snatched my wallet, which contained credit and debit cards, PAN card, Aadhaar card, voter ID and Rs 1,000 cash. I called up my colleague Parveen Nangle on the phone and then called 100,” he wrote in his complaint.
 
 A police team led by sub-inspector Mahesh Nagar reached the spot and took down his written complaint. They later registered an FIR as per the complaint, against three unidentified robbers under Section 379A (snatching) of the Indian Penal Code at Sector 53 police station. 
 
 “The case has been registered and probe is on. Today, I visited the bank branch and have sought CCTV footage from the nearby area,” said inspector Arvind Dahiya, SHO, Sector 53 police station.
