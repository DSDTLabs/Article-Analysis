PATIALA: Thousands of people, including religious leaders, ministers of the   cabinet,   MLAs and workers, senior administrative and police officials as well as ordinary citizens from all walks of life, attended the bhog ceremony of Rajmata Mohinder Kaur, mother of Punjab Chief Minister Amarinder Singh at New Moti Bagh palace here on Sunday. 

 Former Prime Minister Manmohan Singh, Union minister Vijay Sampla, SAD president and former Punjab deputy chief minister Sukhbir Singh Badal and senior Congress leader Ghulam Nabi Azad, were among those who were present during the antim ardas.
 
 Azad, leader of the opposition in the Rajya Sabha, described Rajmata as a multi-faceted personality and offered condolences on behalf of Congress president Sonia Gandhi and vice-president Rahul Gandhi.
 
 Sukhbir Badal recalled her service to every section of the society. Punjab BJP chief Vijay Sampla, while conveying the condolences of Prime Minister Narendra Modi and party chief Amit Shah, said Rajmata was a great social reformer. SGPC chief Prof Kirpal Singh Badungar also paid homage.
 
 Born in 1922 at Ludhiana, Mohinder Kaur, the wife of late Maharaja Patiala, Yadavindra Singh, passed away on July 24, 2017 after prolonged illness.
