HYDERABAD: In keeping with the theme for this year's World  , members of the United Federation of Resident Welfare Associations (UFERWAS) along with representatives of the Greater Hyderabad Municipal Corporation (GHMC) sanitation wing,   ( ) and some NGOs, on Friday, conducted a plastic and polythene removal drive in  . 

 According to participants, over 15 tonnes of plastic and other wastes were collected in the drive. This amounted to nearly six truck-loads of waste-of which more than two trucks were filled with plastic waste collected from open areas alone.
 
  
  
 
 "The clean-up drive was conducted outside and in surrounding areas of the AOC driving training centre near Safilguda Railway Station-which has been identified as a vulnerable garbage point by the GHMC," said BT Srinivasan, a resident of Malkajgiri and a member of UFERWAS.
 
 Post the clean-up drive, TSPCB distributed appreciation certificates and cloth bags among participants. 
 
 It is a step in the right direction that the government has taken considering the alarming rise in pollution levels in the city. Now the authorities must work towards ensuring that the plan is effectively implemented.
