PANAJI: The GMC’s medical education cell recently conducted a continuing medical education programme on the issue of medical negligence.

 The lecture was delivered by Dr  , lecturer at GMC’s department of forensic medicine and toxicology, who spoke about unethical practices and acts that constitute medical negligence. 
 
 He highlighted various sections of the Indian criminal law under which a registered medical practitioner can be charged, courts and statutory bodies where such cases are tried or inquired into, procedures of inquiry in the medical council and procedure of settlement of disputes in the consumer forum.
 
 He discussed the scope of coverage of the medical profession under the Consumer Protection Act, yardsticks to decide whether a given case is one of negligence or not, scope of duties of a doctor and various acts covering liabilities in the medical profession.
