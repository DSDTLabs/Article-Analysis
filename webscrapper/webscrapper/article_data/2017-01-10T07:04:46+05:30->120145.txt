HYDERABAD: Students from the city have once again failed to make a mark in the   (CAT) as nobody from Hyderabad secured a rank in the top 20. 

 The results, which were announced on Monday, have seen about 20 candidates score overall 100 percentile in  . 
 
 "There are 20 candidates, who have scored overall 100 percentile in CAT 2016. They are all male candidates and are from the engineering background," Rajendra K Bandi, convener of CAT 2016, said. 
 
 "However, in the sectionals, many non-engineers and woman candidates have scored 100 percentile," he added. 
 
 On December 4, 2016, 1.95 lakh candidates took the test, which was conducted across 138 cities in the country overall. 
 
 On Monday, the candidates were informed individually by text messages about the results and their overall percentile.
 
 "The untiring support of the CAT committee, comprising admission chairpersons of IIM-Ahmedabad, IIM-Calcutta, IIM-Indore, IIM-Kozhikode and IIM-Lucknow, and the tremendous hard work by the CAT 2016 secretariat at the IIM-Bangalore played a big role in enabling us to declare the results on schedule," Rajendra Bandi said.
 
 Convener Rajendra K Bandi also added that the IIM-Bangalore will announce its shortlisted candidates for personal interviews on its website (www.iimb.ernet.in) later in the day.
