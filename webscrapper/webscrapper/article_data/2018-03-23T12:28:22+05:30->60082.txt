HYDERABAD: The bodies of 39 Indians killed in Iraq may take another week to be brought back home. Iraqi authorities have already started the process but no date has been fixed on which the bodies would be flown to India.

 Sources in the   ministry of external affairs said the repatriation may take place even earlier if the authorities speed up the procedure. The forensic department of the Ministry of Health, Iraq is involved in the process. The identity of one Indian is yet to be established while the identities of the remaining 38 have been confirmed through DNA testing.
 
 As announced by Union minister for external affairs  , a special flight from Baghdad will bring the bodies back to India. Union minister of state for external affairs V K Singh is to accompany the bodies in the special flight. The workers killed were from Punjab, Himachal Pradesh, Bihar and West Bengal.
