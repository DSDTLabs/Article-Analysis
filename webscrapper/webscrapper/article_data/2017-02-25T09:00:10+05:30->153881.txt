PUNE: The state government has formed a steering committee to find the cause of death of children staying in Ashramshalas. Former director general of health services in Maharashtra Subhash Salunke will head the committee. 

 Over the past decade, state Ashramshalas have seen 1,000 deaths. The committee will recommend ways to prevent such deaths and the implementation process.
 
 Deputy secretary   of the state tribal development department said that the 11-member committee includes representatives of NGOs, tribal development department, university departments, among others. “The committee’s objective will be to analyse deaths in Ashramshalas and suggest prevention measures. The committee will also analyse various training sessions organised for teachers and students in tribal schools. It would also monitor the implementation of the recommendations in every district,” Shinde shared. 
 
 Activist   had filed a writ petition in the Bombay High Court regarding deaths of children in Ashramshalas and the need to take immediate action. The committee was formed on January 25 following court directives issued to the state government.
 
 The committees’ recommendations would have to be implemented by all district heads in schools under their supervision. The notification also stated that the tribal department should issue directives about implementing the recommendations. 
 
 The state government runs 19 Ashramshalas. As many as 1,077 children have died in Ashramshalas. A majority of the cases were reported from Taloda in Nandurbar, Dharani in Amravati, Kalvan in Nashik and Dahanu in Palghar.
