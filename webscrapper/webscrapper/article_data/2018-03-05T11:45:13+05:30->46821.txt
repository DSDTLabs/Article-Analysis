Mumbai: The   has further tightened the priority sector lending (PSL) norms for   by directing them to mandatorily create sub-targets so that they lend a portion of their loans to small and marginal farmers as well as micro-enterprises from April.

 The move, directed at foreign banks with over 20 branches, will impact the likes of Standard Chartered, Citi and HSBC, which have a much higher branch presence, and will come into force from next financial year.
 
 The PSL norms mandate foreign banks to eventually lend 40% of their total loan book to the priority sector, such as agriculture, rural infra and MSMEs, among others, from April 2020.
