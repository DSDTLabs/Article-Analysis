KOCHI: A 25-year-old youth from West Bengal was arrested following an attempted ATM theft at Aluva on Thursday.

 According to police, the incident happened at the State Bank of India (SBI) ATM at   near Aluva around 1.30am on Thursday.
 
 The attempt was foiled after a security guard of the bank adjacent to the kiosk raised an alarm and caught hold of the youth, when he along with two others was trying to break open the ATM machine using iron rods.
 
 A team from the police station close to the ATM rus hed to the spot and apprehended the accused.
 
 The police said that the ATM was next to one of the busiest banks in the town.
 
 “The accused couldn't open the locker,“ said the police.
 
 The trio after entering the kiosk damaged the CCTV camera and cut the cables to the same. They later used sharp tools to open the plastic covering of the machine, said the police. They also damaged the lock inside the compartment.
 
 The police said that the bank authorities estimated the damage to the tune of Rs 80,000.
 
 An FIR under Sections 457, 380 and 427 of the IPC has been filed against the accused.
