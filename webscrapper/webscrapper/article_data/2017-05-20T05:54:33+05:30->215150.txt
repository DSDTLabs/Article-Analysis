BENGALURU: To counter increasing incidents of attacks on medical professionals, the director general of   has issued a standing order to his force to initiate action against those who harm doctors.

 “Though the   Prohibition of Violence against   and Damage to Property in Medicare Service Institutions Act was enacted in 2009 to prevent violence, there appears to be a lack of understanding on the part of local police in handling such incidents,“ the order said.
 
 The nature of violence includes verbal abuse by attendants of the patients, creating scary situations by a mob, ransacking of the establishment, preventing medical professionals from doing their duty, refusing to pay bills, snatching of medical reports documents.
 
 Police stations will set up a WhatsApp group of medical professionals with beat officers for regular exchange of information.
