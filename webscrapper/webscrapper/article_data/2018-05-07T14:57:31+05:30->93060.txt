CHENNAI: The   and the   on Monday condemned the preventive arrests of government employees and teachers in  . The government employees and teachers have planned to take out a march to Fort St George, which houses the state secretariat, in Chennai on Tuesday to demand payment of arrears under the 7th Pay Commission recommendations and implementation of the old pension scheme.

 In separate statements, the political parties recalled that the state government teachers and employees had given the march call several days ago under the Joint Action committee of Teachers Organisations and Government Employees Organisations (JACTO-GEO). Instead of inviting them for talks, the state government had resorted to preventive arrests to silence them, they said.
 
 “Fisheries minister D Jayakumar had asked the employees to drop their protests plan, without even promising to look into their grievances and demands. On the contrary, he even remarked that the high payment to government employees has left the government in huge debt,” said PMK youth wing president Anbumani Ramadoss. He recalled the more “stringent action” taken against the state government employees by the AIADMK government in 2003 and pointed out that it (government) had to give in to the demands of the employees later.
 
 CPM’s state general secretary K Balakrishnan urged the state government to invite the employees for talks, instead of taking them into preventive custody. He urged the government to release all the arrested employees.
