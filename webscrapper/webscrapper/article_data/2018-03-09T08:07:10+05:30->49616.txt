BATHINDA/FEROZEPUR: Anganwadi workers, who have been protesting in   for over a month for their demands, observed 'black day' on the occasion of the International Women's Day. Hundreds of anganwadi workers and helpers scaled the wall of the Bathinda senior superintendent of police ( ) office and held a protest at the entrance of the deputy commissioner's office. 

 Meanwhile, on the call given by the  , anganwadi helpers and workers observed International Women Day as 'black day' in   on Thursday. Several anganwadi workers, in support of their long-pending demands, protested against the   government in the state by wearing black dupattas and black flags.
 
 Anganwadi workers in   are protesting since January 29, for higher emoluments on the pattern of Delhi and seeking return of children aged 3 to 6 to the anganwadi centres. Holding black flags, anganwadi workers and helpers in Bathinda reached the gate of the SSP's office to hold a protest at the nearby DC office. As the employees shut the gate, the workers had heated exchanges with them and scaled the gate. They forcibly reached the gate of DC's office and protested for nearly an hour.
 
 Anganwadi workers union president   said, "We have not been paid wages for the last six months and are forced to pay rent of the anganwari centres from own resources. 
 
 "We are holding protest for the last 38 days for giving us the rent and to raise the wages. We also want the children to be return to anganwadi centres but nobody is paying."
