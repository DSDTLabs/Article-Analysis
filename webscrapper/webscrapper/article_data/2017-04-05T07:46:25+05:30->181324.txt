 On Tuesday,   conditions continued unabated for the sixth consecutive day. The maximum temperature was 40.5 degrees Celsius, five degrees above normal, and minimum temperature was 21.1 degrees Celsius, two degrees above normal. On Wednesday, sky would be mainly clear in the morning but turn partly cloudy towards afternoon. Maximum and minimum temperatures are expected to be around 39 and 19 degrees Celsius, respectively. 
 
 State Met director J P Gupta said a western disturbance active over hills at present will impact west and northwest UP on Wednesday. It may cause rains or thundershower accompanied by squall and hail at isolated places bordering Uttarakhand and Nepal. This will lead to decline in temperatures in rest of the state as well. 
 
 The impact of western disturbance will be felt in central and east UP from Thursday onward.
