 Dhillon hails from Dahola village in Jind district, and is serving life sentence in Bhondsi jail under sections 302 (murder), 307 (attempt to murder), 452 (assault or wrongful restraint), 323 (voluntarily causing hurt), 325 (voluntarily causing grievous hurt), 201 (causing disappearance of evidence of offence, or giving false information), 120B (conspiracy), 147 (rioting), 148 (rioting, armed with deadly weapon) and 149 (unlawful assembly) of the IPC. His younger sister, Anu (24), tied the knot with Sonu on Sunday. Dhillon had sought permission from jail superintendent to be allowed to attend the wedding ceremony, but was denied.
 
 Maruti Udyog Workers Union general secretary Kuldeep Jhangu said Dhillon’s parents are old and he is their only son. “After he was denied permission to attend the wedding, we did not want his sister to feel his absence. So, all workers came together and contributed money towards the ‘kanyadaan’. The wedding went smoothly on Sunday.” 
 
 The six unions of Maruti and its associate plants contributed Rs 7.97 lakh. “Apart from the six   unions of Maruti Gurgaon, Maruti Manesar, Maruti Power Train, Suzuki Motorcycle, FAMI Auto, and Belasonic Auto Components, worker unions from   and Rewari also contributed another Rs 55,000. These include Honda Motorcycles, Hero Gurugram, Sunbeam, Omex Daruhera, Daikin Neemrana, Munjal Kiriu, Mark Exhaust and Carrier,” he added. 
 
 “We are going to stick with all 13 colleagues and their families through thick and thin. We are united in their support,” Jhangu said.
