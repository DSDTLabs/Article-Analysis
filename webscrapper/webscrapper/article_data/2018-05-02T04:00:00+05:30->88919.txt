Rajkot: Bhavnagar recorded the highest temperature in Gujarat with the city sizzling at 44.1 degrees Celsius on Tuesday. As Saurashtra reeled under severe heat, three other towns also saw the mercury crossing 42 degrees Celsius. Surendranagar baked at 42.7 degrees Celsius followed by Rajkot that recorded 42.5 degrees Celsius and Amreli at 42.2 degrees Celsius.

 Jayanta Sarkar, director of India Meteorological Department in Ahmedabad said that the south-westerly winds usually bring moisture but this is peak summer and high solar radiation is resulting in soaring temperatures.
