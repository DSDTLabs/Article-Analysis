GHAZIABAD: As many as eight persons have been arrested for setting fire to a UP Roadways bus near a village in Industrial Area Site 4 on Wednesday night while protesting against the movie  . 

 
 
 An FIR was lodged at Link Road police station following a complaint by the driver and the conductor of the bus against 30 people, including a local councillor, for allegedly torching the bus near Jhanda Pur village on Wednesday night, police said, adding that 18 among the accused were identified.
 
 Councillor Jaiveer Singh is accused of allegedly provoking his associates into burning the bus to protest against the release of Padmaavat, assistant superintendent of police Anoop Singh said.
 
 Driver Devraj Singh and conductor Kapil Singh claimed that nearly 50 people intercepted the bus when it was on its way to the Anand Vihar bus terminal at around 10pm, hurled stones and set it on fire after dousing it with petrol.
 
 The driver sustained minor injuries in the incident.
 
 SHO, Link Road, Prashant Kumar Mishra said, “The accused were sent to jail on Thursday. The accused have been identified as Saurabh, Gaurav, Amit Yadav, Dinesh Rathore, Bhupesh, Dharampal, Jeetu, Neeraj. There are also 19 unidentified persons in the FIR.”
 
 The FIR has been registered under sections 147 (rioting), 148 (rioting, armed with deadly weapon), 149 (every member of unlawful assembly guilty of offence committed in prosecution of common object), 323 (punishment for voluntarily causing hurt), 427 (mischief causing damage to the amount of fifty rupees), 436 (mischief by fire or explosive substance with intent to destroy house), 3/5 Public Property Damage Act and 7 of Criminal Amendment Act (Whoever with intent to cause any person to abstain from doing or to do any act which such person has a right to do or to abstain from doing, obstructs or uses violence).
 
 Mishra added that councillor Singh is absconding following the incident.
