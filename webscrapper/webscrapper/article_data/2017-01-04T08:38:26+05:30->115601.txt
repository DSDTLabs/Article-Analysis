AHMEDABAD: Not so long ago, on September 5, 2016, the Union department of animal husbandry, dairying and fisheries had declared India free of the dreaded H5 N1 avian flu virus, after a massive culling operation at Humnabad in the Bidar district of Karnataka. After that, Delhi, Keranga village in Odisha on December 26, 2016 and now Ahmedabad in Gujarat on January 1, have reported comebacks by the deadly virus, which, according to health officials has a 60% mortality rate if transmitted into humans.
