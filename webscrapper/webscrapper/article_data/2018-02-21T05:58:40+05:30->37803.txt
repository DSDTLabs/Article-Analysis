MUMBAI: The state has signed over 4,100 memorandums of understanding (Mo-Us) worth over Rs 12 lakh crore with private enterprises from around the world during the Magnetic Maharashtra investors’ summit. The state’s initial target was Rs 10 lakh crore.

 These MoUs have the potential to generate employment for nearly 39 lakh people, he added. Of the total Rs 12 lakh crore worth of MoUs, Rs 5.5 lakh crore was in the manufacturing sector, Rs 3.85 lakh crore in the housing sector and Rs 1.6 lakh crore in the power sector.
 
 Fadnavis was chairing the closing ceremony of the summit, which was organised by the state and the industry trade body, CII.
 
 Fadnavis said that one of the highlights of the meet was that several proposals for which the state signed MoUs were for backward regions like Gadchiroli, Nanded, Nandurbar and Hingoli.
 
 The state is also setting up a Rs 1,000-crore fund to promote the defence and aerospace sectors. Fadnavis said that there is much potential in the state in these sectors and that it also has a competitive edge over other states.
