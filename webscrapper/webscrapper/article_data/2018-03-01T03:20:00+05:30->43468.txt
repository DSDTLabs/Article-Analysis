Panaji: The Indian Medical Association ( )’s initiative of a Bharat Yatra to register its opposition to the National Medical Commission Bill, reached Goa on Wednesday. 
 
 IMA national president, Dr Ravi Wankhedkar, addressing a press conference said that they would oppose the Bill, proposed by the Centre, tooth and nail. 
  
 “The yatra’s objective is to convey to the people how the proposed bill is anti-people. If it is enacted in its present form, it will lower the standard of medical education,” said the IMA president. 
  
 The association is also strongly opposed to the provision in the proposed Bill that allows bridge course.
