MUMBAI: A male leopard that got caught in a wire mesh in Sopwe village of   on Wednesday morning was rescued by the forest department. Locals spotted the leopard entangled in the fence of a chikoo farm belonging to a villager,   and informed the forest department around 11am. 

 Forest officers along with members of the Dahanu-based NGO Wildlife Conservation and Animal Welfare Association ( ) immediately reached the spot with a cage to trap the wild cat.
 
 Forest officials said they cut the net and managed to get the animal into the cage. The big cat, around six years old, was taken to Dahanu divisional office of the forest department. Dr   who heads WCAWA said the leopard was not injured and will be released in the jungle after examination. The leopard did not harm anyone, said officials.
