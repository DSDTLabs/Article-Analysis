LUDHIANA: A woman resident of Kitchlu Nagar Extension had allegedly been posing as an official of the Canadian embassy for the past five months and duping people of lakhs by promising to send them abroad with the held of her “connections”, said police here on Sunday.

 Thirty-one-year-old Ammy Porva’s run came an end on Saturday night, when a team from Haibowal police station descended on her house and arrested her. They also took into custody three cars, Rs 7 lakh, gold jewellery, and passports of her clients into possession. Additional deputy commissioner of police (ADCP) Gurpreet Kaur Purewal said they had received 10 complaints against Porva in the past five months. “We suspect she has duped people of crores of money during this time,” the   said.
 
 A case has been registered against Porva under sections 406 (criminal breach of trust), 420 (cheating) and 120B (criminal conspiracy) of the Indian Penal Code (IPC), and Section 13 of Punjab Travel Professionals’ Regulation Act.
 
 The case has been registered on a complaint by   resident Amarjit Singh. He had accused Prova of duping him of Rs 32 lakh. He alleged that when the woman did not send him abroad or return the money, she told him not to ask for it. He alleged that she also told him that a relative of her was an inspector general of police and threatened to get him booked in a false case.
 
 ADCP Purewal said the woman had been active for three years. “She also used to dress up like an NRI to avoid any kind of suspicion,” said the police officer. 
 
 ADCP Purewal said they expected more people to be involved in the racked.
