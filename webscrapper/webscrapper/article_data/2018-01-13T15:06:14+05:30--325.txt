NEW DELHI: Karnataka chief minister   on Saturday met   president   at his Delhi residence, news agency ANI has reported.

 The timing of the meeting is significant, given the imminent Assembly elections in Karnataka.
 
 Siddaramaiah was accompanied by several other senior party leaders from the state.
 
 After the meeting, the sitting chief minister told reporters that there was "no anti-incumbency" against his government.
 
 "Rahul Gandhi was very happy to know that there is no anti-incumbency against our government, and that all programmes that were mentioned in our manifesto have been completed," he said.
 
 Siddaramaiah also accused the BJP of "raising irrelevant issues".
 
 "Congress president Rahul Gandhi is visiting Karnataka from February 10-12 for the first leg of the election campaign," said the party's state unit chief Dr G Parameshwara.
 
 Gandhi will address meetings, and hold interactions with groups of intellectuals, farmers, women and students.
