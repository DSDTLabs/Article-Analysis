LUCKNOW: The maiden session of the   started on a stormy note on Monday as opposition members led by the Samajwadi Party (SP) disrupted governor Ram  's joint address to the two Houses, throwing paper missiles at him and shouting slogans during his 35-minute speech. 

 However, an undeterred Naik (82) completed his speech even as marshals surrounded him, using official files to   hurled at him by opposition members who had trooped into the well of the  . They were demanding that Naik's speech make a mention of the 'deteriorating law and order situation' in the state.
 
 Carrying banners and placards, the opposition disrupted the House despite being outnumbered by the treasury benches - 78 against 325 MLAs. Since it was a joint session, the opposition strength was bolstered by 66 SP MLCs, many of whom were seen in action in the well of the House.
 
 Naik reminded the legislators that the 'whole country was watching them' as for the first time, the proceedings of the House were being aired live on Doordarshan. But his appeal didn't cut much ice with the belligerent opposition members.
 
 Wearing the party's red cap, almost all the SP MLAs and MLCs jumped into the well of the House moments after Naik began reading the speech. Some of them hurled paper missiles towards Naik's chair while some others led by Irfan Solanki rushed menacingly towards the governor, but were prevented by the marshals. Despite every effort by the marshals, a few paper missiles landed on Naik's table. The SP legislators were carrying banners and placards saying, " Rajyapal vapas jao, vapas jao (Governor go back)" and " Gundagardi ki ye sarkar nahi chalegi, nahi chalegi (We will not let this government led by goons continue)".
