KOLKATA: The core committee of members of the   (SIMTA) and  ' ( ) have unanimously decided to call off the truckers' strike, 10 days after the goods vehicles went off the road, after the government assured to look into their demands.

 SIMTA, that had started the strike in six southern states and has the support of AIMTC, has achieved its objectives, said SIMTA general secretary   in a press release on Saturday. The demands that the core committee had come up with were reduction of third-party insurance premium, stalling of scrapping of old vehicles, reduction of RTO fees and clarification that speed governor is not required for specified speeds below 80kmph.
 
 The state government had issued a circular on Saturday morning, making alternative arrangement for supply of commodities from midnight. However, the strike was called off in the afternoon.
