AMRITSAR: Shiromani Gurdwara Parbandhak Committee (SGPC) — single largest representative body of Sikhs — passed its annual budget of around Rs 1,159 crore for year 2018-2019 with a voice vote on Thursday. It is the first budget presented under the tenure of   president  .

 The budget was presented by general secretary  . The budget was presented in presence of Akal Takht jathedar Giani Gurbachan Singh, Takht Kesgarh Sahib jathedar Giani Raghubir Singh, Takht Damdama Sahib jathedar Giani Harpreet Singh, Takht Patna Sahib jathedar   and   head granthi Giani Jagtar Singh.
 
 SGPC has divided its budget into general board fund, trust fund and education fund and earmarked around Rs 66 crore, Rs 56 crore and Rs 37 crore respectively. Further, the Sikh body has earmarked Rs 76 crore for Dharam Parchar Committee, Rs 228 crore for educational institutions and around Rs 8 crore for its printing presses.
 
 SGPC has also made a budgetary provision of Rs 60 lakh for purchase of an   bus for transporting saroop of Guru Granth Sahib to sangat whereas it has earmarked Rs 1.7 crore and Rs 2.40 crore for installing printing presses for printing of Guru Granth Sahib in India and abroad respectively.
 
 For purchase of langar material, the SGPC has made a provision of around Rs 87 crore during the 2018-19 financial year. These purchases are in addition to donations made by the sangat (community) for the preparation of langar.
