LUDHIANA: Four men assaulted an on-duty cop near   on Saturday evening. The accused are yet to be identified by the police, even though cops have the registration number of the car the accused were travelling in.

 In his police complaint, constable   of   station said they had set up a checkpost at   Chowk on Saturday evening, and during the checking, he had stopped a Maruti Dzire car, with four persons in it, coming from Dugri side. After the driver stopped the car, when the constable asked him for necessary documents, the car occupants started abusing him and also assaulted him. Then, the accused persons fled from the spot after speeding away with the vehicle.
 
 The constable said after the incident, he had informed the senior officers, and added that he had noted down the number of the vehicle before the accused persons fled from the spot. Dugri police later booked the unidentified accused under sections 353 (assault or criminal force to deter a public servant from discharge of his duty), 186 (obstructing a public servant in discharge of public functions), 506 (criminal intimidation) and 34 (acts done by several persons in furtherance of common intention) of the Indian Penal Code (IPC).
 
 Investigating officer of the case, assistant sub-inspector   said though they had the registration number of the vehicle, since it was a Sunday, they could not get details about the vehicle owner, but they would get it from the transport office on Monday, and reach the vehicle owner.
