PATHANKOT: They call it a courtesy meet, but politicians are trying to derive ‘meanings’ from a meeting of Congress nominee for Gurdaspur   by-election   and BJP’s ticket aspirant   at later’s resident on Saturday. “We also have personal life, Jakhar is friend of Vinod and he called on me to express his condolences “ said Kavita Khanna while talking to TOI on Saturday.

 Immediately after the photograph of Jakhar and Kavita’s meeting went viral on social media , the rumour mill started to promulgate gossips and other unsubstantiated claims with many seeing the meeting as ‘congress party’s attempt to woo BJP leaders”. “Vinod Khanna had died several months ago, why didn’t Jakhar expressed condolences earlier and if he did then why especially he called on Kavita before beginning his election campaign” questioned a local BJP leader who didn’t want to give his name.
 
 However, Kavita who went back to Mumbai for a religious ceremony after meeting Jakhar, claimed that there was no politics in their meeting . She said she was a BJP member and would arrive back to Gurdaspur to attend BJP’s rally scheduled for September 26th. “I will perform any duty assigned to me with full responsibility by my party leadership” said she.
 Jakhar couldn’t be contacted for his comment, however his personal secretary said “It was just a courtesy call and nothing more”.
 
 Meanwhile Aam Aadmi Party’s candidate Suresh Khajuria inaugurated his office in Pathankot. “We are reaching out to common people , telling them the poor performance of both SAD-BJP combine and Congress government and what is AAP’s vision for them”.
 
 On the other hand SAD-BJP’s joint nominee Swaran Salaria denied chief minister Captain Amarinder Singh’s claims that he had demanded ticket from Congress party. He informed that senior leadership of BJP including BJP president Amit Shah,   as well as prime minister could visit   constituency to campaign for BJP.
