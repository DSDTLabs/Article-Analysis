 On March 9 too, nearly 40 crore RIL shares, representing about 12% of the company’s equity, had changed hands through block deals among promoter group companies. The block deals were part of a planned transfer of shares within RIL’s promoters. On March 2 the company said that nearly 119 crore shares of the energy-to-telecom major, which was then worth almost Rs 1.5 lakh crore, was set to change hands. Eight companies, which belong to its promoters, will buy out this chunk of shares from 15 other promoter group entities through block deals, the company had said in a stock exchange filing.
 
 Transfers within promoter group entities of a company are called ‘inter-se’ transfers and these do not lead to any change in promoter holding as a whole. In case of RIL too, the promoters will continue to hold 45.24% in the company after this inter-se transfer. The total number of shares being transferred through these transactions constitute 36.7% of RIL’s equity capital, the company had said.
