COIMBATORE: Five people were arrested on Friday on charges of branding a 20-year-old man with a hot iron rod.

 The incident happened on May 13 at   on the outskirts of the city, police sources said. “However, the victim, M Gopinath of   in   district, who claims to belong to schedule caste (Arunthathiyar), lodged the complaint on Saturday. The five men abused him using his caste name,” a police officer said.
 
 Gopinath wanted police to book the men under the Schedule Caste/Schedule Tribe (Prevention of Atrocities) Act.
 
 The complainant was working in a hardware shop at Somanur, the officer said. “On May 13, around 4pm, he was approached by one Saravanan, who asked him to visit a house near the shop. But, once he reached the house, he was kept under wrongful confinement and was beaten severely by five people, including Saravanan. He was also branded with a hot iron rod before freed,” the officer said.
 
 Gopinath, who was admitted in the Coimbatore Medical College and Hospital (CMCH) for treatment, lodged the complaint with Karumathampatti police on Friday. Police registered a case against B Prabhakaran, N Dhanesh, K Dharmarajan, M Sathiyamurthy and M Saravanan, all hailing from Somanur under Sections 147, 148, 323, 342, 324 and 506 (ii) of IPC and arrested them.
 
 However, the arrested men told police that Gopinath tried to enter Saravanan’s house by removing glass roof tiles. He was beaten up after he was caught, they said.
