BENGALURU: Police detained two Bangalore   employees on Monday night for allegedly selling 32 sites in various layouts using forged documents.

 Acting on a complaint of fraud filed by   officials, Seshadripuram police detained two clerks,   and  .
 
 The alleged scam came to the attention of the BDA brass during an internal audit. It was found that the two staffers had sold sites al ready allotted to other parties. The internal audit was ordered after the original allottees approached the BDA asking for their sites, sources in the agency said.
 
 “The sites were reserved for allotment under the Economically Weaker Section category . The signatures of the BDA commissioner and deputy secretary were allegedly forged, and these sites were sold,“ police said. Sources said more people could be involved in the scam.
