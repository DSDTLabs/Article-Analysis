NEW DELHI: Delhi witnessed shallow fog in the morning with the minimum temperature settling at 9.9 degrees Celsius.

 In the last 24 hours, maximum temperature of 26 degrees Celsius has been recorded, a notch higher than expected, said a MET official.
 
 As many as 22 trains were cancelled, 32 delayed and two were rescheduled in Delhi, due to operational reasons and low visibility caused by a dip in the temperature.
 
 In the next three days, the minimum temperature is expected to range between eight and 10 degrees Celsius with fog density ranging from shallow to dense.
 
 The minimum temperature on Tuesday rose in comparison to previous few days and settled at 9.8 degrees Celsius.
