BENGALURU: The number of   (in the age group of 18-19 years) in Karnataka has crossed 15 lakh mark, which is double the number of young voters registered prior to the 2013  

 Karnataka chief electoral officer   on Saturday said as for the draft   of 2018, the number of young voters stood at 15.4 lakh as compared to 7.2 lakh voters in 2013, a rise of 113%. The number could go up a bit as still there is time for inclusion of names on electoral rolls.
  
  Sanjeev Kumar attributed this massive jump to various communication activities taken up by the  . “Of the 15.4 lakh voters, 7.7 lakh have been enrolled during the special summary revision undertaken from November 30, 2017. During this period, we managed to contact nearly 2 crore people,” Kumar added.
 
 
 
 
 The number of women voters to every thousand men voters has also gone up significantly. While the electoral rolls in 2013 had 958 women voters for every 1,000 men, now the ratio has increased to 972: 1,000.
 
 “We are also in the process of identifying people with disabilities and enrolling them as voters with the help of women, child and disability welfare department,” he added.
