MUMBAI: A 17-year-old Kalyan boy who the police said was obsessed with owning an   has been arrested for using fake notes to buy the device. The notes used to pay for the phone had ‘Children Bank of India’ written on them.

 The boy found the second-hand iPhone for sale on an online shopping portal. “The boy contacted the phone owner after seeing his number and finalised the deal for Rs 30,000,” a police officer said. 
 
 The police said the boy met the seller on DN Road in Fort on Wednesday, paid him and took the phone, but the seller noticed that the notes of Rs 2,000 denomination were different in size and had ‘Children Bank of India’ written on them. “He ran after the boy and caught him,” said the officer.
