KOLKATA: Calcutta   has decided to dedicate itself for a social cause and to begin with, a social service project has been undertaken by the university. On Monday, syndicate - the highest administrative decision making body of   has resolved to start a community service involving dwellers of 28/1 Giribabu lane slum under ward 44.

 
 "We have decided to embark upon doing community service since we are going towards achieving excellence in higher education. Yet we found that in the nearby slums financially backward students are trying to find assistance in academics. In a society it is the collective responsibility that can uplift the backward," said vice chancellor of CU,  .
 
 The students at the slum will be given educational support, books will be distributed, they will be helped to do the homework. The slum dwellers will be given tips on basic health amenities, regula health checkups will be conducted, they will be provided garments twice in a year and students are to be taken to excursion every year. Food will be distributed twice every week by CU. The students from the slums will be taken to pandal hopping during Durga Puja.
 
 "Four students are to be selected based on interview who will be associated with the project. They shall be paid a stipend of Rs 2000 every month. This will be a community service for outreach. The equilibrium otherwise can't be maintained in the society," said the CU vice-chancellor.
 
 CU selected students will hold special classes for the students in the slum at a local school which holds classes till 12 noon. Thereafter it will be converted into a special class.
