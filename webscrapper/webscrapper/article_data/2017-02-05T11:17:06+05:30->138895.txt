COIMBATORE: The city police registered a case against an unidentified person for criminally intimidating the resident medical officer (RMO) of   and Hospital (CMCH) over telephone on Friday. 

 The unknown caller introduced himself as  , a personal assistant to Union minister for state  , and verbally abused the RMO for not taking proper action against the CMCH staff, who allegedly demanded bribe for releasing a body to kin of the deceased, a few days ago. 
 
 The RMO told the caller that the concerned staff was placed under suspension. He then filed a complaint with the city police, as the caller criminally intimidated him. 
 
 A sanitary worker, Senthil, was suspended by the CMCH authorities two days ago, after he was caught on camera demanding a bribe of 2,000 to release a body on January 31. The worker had however alleged that he had only asked for money to purchase a new white cloth to cover the body, as per her family's request.
 
 Meanwhile RMO, A Soundarvel received a phone call to his office landline from an unknown person on Friday at 1.21 pm, who threatened him for not taking proper action against the staff and also alleged that the RMO would face action from the Union government. The caller also said that Union minister Pon Radhakrishnan would speak with the RMO soon.
 
 "I answered to the unknown caller very respectfully. But he use bad language and also threatened me. I noted down the mobile number and have lodged a complaint with the Race Course police," said RMO Soundarvel.
 
 Based on his complaint, Race Course police inspector,  , has registered a case against an unknown caller under Section 353 (assault or criminal force to deter public servant from discharge of his duty) and 506 (i) (criminal intimidation) of the Indian Penal Code.
