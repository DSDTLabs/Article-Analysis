 “The master plan and annual plans were not prepared in the absence of which targets were not set and performances could not be ascertained. Delay in procurement and obsolete equipment adversely affected delivery of medical services and academic activities in the institute,” read a para of the audit report.
 
 “The trauma centre and mother and child hospital constructed at the cost of Rs 56.59 crore could not be put to use due to non-purchase of equipment and furniture. Slow implementation of Pradhan Mantri Swasthya Suraksha Yojana, Centre of Excellence and pilot programme for the prevention of burns delayed accrual of benefit of the schemes pertaining to patients,” the report further read.
 
 According to information, the audit team carried out the work in March and April last year. The team had surveyed the audit performance between the years 2011 and 2016.
 
 “A performance audit of the functioning of the institute during the period of 2011 to 2016 brought out deficiencies which impaired its ability to achieve its overall objective of providing quality health services and medical education in the state,” the audit report said.
 
 Pandit Bhagwat Dayal Post Graduate Institute of Medical Sciences (PGIMS), which was elevated as the health university, was one of the premier projects initiated by the   Hooda-led Congress government in Haryana in 2008.
