VISAKHAPATNAM: The state government is to set up eight mega food processing parks of which five are expected to come up in the four   of Srikakulam, Vizianagaram, Visakhapatnam and East Godavari.

 Three others are being planned in the Rayalaseema districts of Chittoor, Kurnool and Anantapuram. The parks will be set up by private firms, finance minister Yanamala Ramakrishnudu announced while presenting the state budget in Amaravati on Wednesday.
 
 “Nearly half of the states in the country do not have a food processing policy. But AP is keen to develop food processing parks,”   CEO YS Prasad told TOI. 
 
 The state has 75 shrimp processing units with exports of around USD 2 billion, he said. This year, the budget allocation for the development of food processing parks and cold chain projects is around Rs 274 crore. 
 
 As per the AP Food Processing Policy, the state government plans to set up one processing park in each district. The central government had also allotted three mega food processing parks in AP at Chittoor, Bhimavaram and Mylavilli. Apart from this, the state had given permissions to Nellore, Srikakulam, Ongole, Chitoor, Kadapa and Kurnool to set up one food processing park each and three at Viziangaram, including the Patanjali food processing park that would come over 170 acres, sources said.
