GURUGRAM: A   carried out in a Sector 30 bar in the early hours of Saturday turned violent after its owner allegedly thrashed a policeman for being asked to shut down the place after midnight as per the orders of the state government.

 Cops said bar owner Rohit Kumar thrashed police constable Sajjan Singh and tore his uniform. He was later arrested, produced in a city court and sent to judicial custody.
 
 The night patrolling team of Sector 40 police station, led by SHO inspector Sudhir Kumar, visited Bottles and Barrels, a bar located on the second floor of Star Tower in Sector 30, post midnight and found that it was still open, despite the state order that prohibits eateries, restaurants, bars and pubs from operating after 12am.
 
 Bar owner Rohit was told to close the bar. As ASI Mangal Singh was preparing to serve him a notice, Rohit, who was inebriated, attacked constable Sajjan Singh.
 
 “When we tried to serve him the notice, Rohit refused to accept it and started thrashing one of our constables and even tore his uniform,” said inspector Kumar. 
 
 “The constable is in hospital, but he is stable,” he added. 
 
 An FIR was registered against 34-year-old Rohit, a resident of Sector 4, Dwarka, under sections 323 (causing hurt), 332 (voluntarily causing hurt), 353 (assault), 186 (obstructing public servant in performing duties), 187 (omission to assist public servant when bound by law to give assistance) and 188 (disobedience to order duly promulgated by public servant) of IPC and Section 68/1/14 of the Excise Act.
 
 “The accused was produced in a city court and sent to judicial custody,” said Kumar.
