MUMBAI: IndiGo and GoAir together cancelled 48 flights on Wednesday, two days after the Indian aviation regulator ordered the grounding of 11 A320neo (new engine option) aircraft over safety concerns.

 While the focus has been on A320neos powered by   and Whitney 1100 engines of a certain make, a writ petition filed in the Delhi high court on Wednesday sought the grounding of the entire A320neo fleet on the grounds that these aircraft have been facing engine problems since introduction in India in March 2016.
 
 On Tuesday, the day after the  , the two airlines had cancelled 65 flights.
 
 Among the 42 flights cancelled by IndiGo are those to Mumbai, Kolkata, Jaipur, Srinagar, Bhubaneswar, Chennai, Delhi, Dehradun, Amritsar, Bengaluru and Hyderabad. A GoAir spokesperson said they cancelled six flights. In an announcement on its website, GoAir said it would cancel 18 flights, including 8 daily ones, from March 15 to 24. These include flights from Cochin, Bengaluru, Chennai, Hyderabad, Bhubaneswar and Kolkata.
 
 The petition in Delhi high court by advocate   seeks the grounding of entire fleet of A320neos till the technical problems are solved. India operates the largest fleet of A320neos with PW1100 engines. Of the 155 A320s in the IndiGo fleet, for instance, 45 are A320neos, out of which 11 are currently grounded.
