NEW DELHI: Equity markets opened on a buoyant note on Monday as the Street looked to shrug off the extended spell of correction.

 30-share BSE   opened with a gain of 198 points on 34,203.34 while the broader 50-share NSE   rose 63 points to start trading on 10,518.20. The indices extended their gains and Sensex was up 268 points at 9.30 am. Both the benchmark indices were gaining 0.80 per cent.
 
 All sectoral sub-indices with the exception of Nifty PSU Bank were trading in the green, with metal and pharma sectors leading the rally.
 
 Tata Steel, ONGC,   and Hero MotoCorp were the major gainers while SBI, BPCL and   were the laggards.
