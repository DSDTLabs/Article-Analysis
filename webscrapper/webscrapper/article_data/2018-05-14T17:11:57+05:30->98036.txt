* Voting for the single-phase panchayat polls is underway on Monday across West Bengal amid tight security.

 * Ministry of home affairs has sought a report from the West Bengal government regarding incidents of violence during panchayat election in the state (ANI)
 
 * Overall 56% polling recorded till 3 pm: ANI
 
 
 * Women vote in large numbers in Cooch Behar.
 
  
  
 
 * Two CPM men - Apu Manna and Jogeswar Ghosh - have been killed in Nandigram.
 
 * In less than two hours since the polling began at 7 am, the State Election Commission (SEC) received complains of violence from at least four districts -- North 24 Parganas, Burdwan, Cooch Behar and South 24 Parganas -- and has asked police to take action.
 
 
 
 * A TMC worker named Arif Gazi was shot dead in Kultali area of South 24 Parganas district, reported ANI.
 
 
 * In Dinhata area of Cooch Behar, 20 voters were injured after clashes broke out between two groups outside a polling station. A crude bomb explosion in Sadhanpur in North 24 Parganas wounded 20 people. In Burdwan, CPI(M) and   accused the ruling   (TMC) of terrorising voters and hurling bombs outside polling stations. TMC denied these allegations.
 
  
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
 * TMC minister Rabindra Nath Ghosh got into an altercation with a BJP supporter at a polling booth in Cooch Behar, a video shared by news agency ANI showed. Ghosh was accused of slapping a BJP polling agent and forcing him out of the polling premises.
 
  
  
 
 * CPI(M) worker Debu Das and his wife were charred to death after their house in North 24 Parganas was torched Sunday night. CPI(M) alleged TMC workers were behind the attack.
 
 
 
 * Today's elections are being held in 621 zilla parishads and 6,157 panchayat samitis, besides 31,827 gram panchayats. Counting of votes will take place on May 17.
 
 
 * Around 1,500 security personnel from Assam, Odisha, Sikkim and Andhra Pradesh are in the state to reinforce security for the polls. They are aiding around 46,000 personnel of the state police and 12,000 of the Kolkata Police.
 
 
 * The run-up to the panchayat election was marked by intense campaigning. The ruling TMC was engaged in a bitter war of words with the opposition Congress,   Front and BJP over the violence that erupted during the filing of nominations for the polls.
 
 
 * The opposition had accused the ruling TMC of letting loose a reign of terror during the nomination process. The TMC rubbished the allegations and had said the opposition do not have a mass base and are trying to evade the polls.
 
 
 * The filing of nominations, which was held from April 2 to April 9 and then again on an extended date on April 23, was marked by violence and clashes between the ruling TMC and opposition parties that led to several deaths.
 
 
 * Political parties in West Bengal are viewing the three-tier panchayat polls as a warm up match ahead of 2019 general elections.
