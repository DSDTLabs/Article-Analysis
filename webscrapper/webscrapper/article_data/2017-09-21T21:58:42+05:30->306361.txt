Ludhiana: The surveys conducted by the investigation wing of the income tax department on five locations in the city on Tuesday have proved to be a big success for the department. According to sources, the wing has so far detected black money and unaccounted cash worth Rs 10 crore in these surveys which were conducted on businessmen, doctors and even a city-based chartered accountant firm. Principal director of income tax (Punjab and J&K) Parneet Sachdev refused to comment on the development. But sources revealed that all the surveys ended by Wednesday morning with departments officials taking into their possession several documents and even data from computers of the firms for further investigation.
