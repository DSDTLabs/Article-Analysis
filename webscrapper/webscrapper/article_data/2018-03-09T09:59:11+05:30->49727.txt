VELLORE: Directorate of Vigilance and Anti-Corruption (DVAC) sleuths on Thursday arrested K Kulandaivelu, deputy collector of the special flying squad of   in Salem region, for taking bribes from supervisors and salesmen at Tasmac liquor outlets in   and surrounding areas. They seized Rs 3.41 lakh unaccounted money from him.

 A team led by additional superintendent of police of DVAC M Balasubramanian tracked the movement of the official and caught him red-handed when he was on a bribe-collecting spree.
 
 Kulandaivelu’s job was to check the irregularities at Tasmac outlets in Nammakal, Dharmapuri, Salem, Krishnagiri,   and Tiruvannamalai districts that falls under the Salem region. 
 
 He told supervisors of Tasmac outlets that he would take action against them for their ill-gotten benefits if they didn’t give him  . 
 
 “We have been tracking his activities. Today (Thursday), we followed him and caught him with Rs 3.41 lakh bribe amount that he had collected from Tasmac supervisors and salesmen in and around Vellore,” said a senior officer in the wing. He was arrested in the presence of two government officials as witnesses.
 
 He had collected bribe from supervisors and salesmen of Tasmac outlets in Tiruvannamalai district and in and around Katpadi in Vellore district and reached Ambur by 7pm. “He went to Cuddalore for a personal work. On his way to Salem, he collected bribe from salesmen and supervisors in Tiruvannamalai. He reached Ambur after collecting the bribe from the outlets in and around Katpadi and Vellore. He has been collecting Rs 1,500 to Rs 5,000 from each person every month,” said a police official.
 
 A case would be filed against him under the provisions of the Prevention of Corruption Act, said the officer and added that they would also conduct a detailed inquiry to unearth irregularities involving Tsmac employees.
