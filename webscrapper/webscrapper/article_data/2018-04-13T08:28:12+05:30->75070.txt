AHMEDABAD: After the results of the first round of admissions to   were declared on Tuesday, 225 PG medical seats and 62 PG dental seats remained vacant.

 According to officials, 2,533 candidates had provided their choices for admission and 1,152 were given admission to PG medical courses. Officials further said that this year, the cutoff mark has been higher than last year.
 
 Similarly, in dental courses, 61 seats were vacant after the first round of admissions. Candidates who have secured admission in the first round will have to pay their fees between April 15 and April 23 and verify documents by April 23.
 
 Officials said the second round of admissions will be conducted on April 21, after first round of allocation of seats for the all-India medical quota.
