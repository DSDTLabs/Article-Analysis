CHANDIGARH: The Chandigarh district court sent one person to jail for three years in a cheating and forgery case after he forged the logo of telecommunications company   in an e-mail.

 The court of civil judge Parveen Kumar, a resident of Panchkula, to three years each under Sections 420 (cheating and dishonestly inducing delivery of property), 419 (punishment for cheating by personation), 467 (forgery of valuable security, will etc), 468 (forgery for purpose of cheating) and 471 (using as genuine a forged document) of the IPC and imposed a fine of Rs 12,000.
 
 The complainant in the matter is Ramanpreet Singh, who is relationship executive, Hero Mindmine Institute, Sector 34, Chandigarh. The case was registered on his written complaint. 
 
 As per his allegations on August 31, 2009, he received a call from convict Arvind Goyal, who claimed to be assistant manager of store operations and planning from   and was looking after the company’s Karnal office. Goyal mentioned that he required customer care executive backend operations and trainees and that is the reason he contacted Ramanpreet, who shared his official e-mail ID details and asked him to send the requirement over mail.
 
 On the same day, he received an e-mail from Arvind Goyal’s personal Gmail account with a proposal for manpower requirement for Bharti Airtel operations which carried a logo of the company too.
 
 They exchanged regular mails and Goyal also selected two women candidates from Mohali on their selection, he asked them to deposit Rs 5,000 each in his ICICI bank account and the former did exactly that.
