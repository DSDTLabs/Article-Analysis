Rajkot: There was no respite for the people of Rajkot from the scorching heat as the temperature remained above 40°C and touched maximum of 44°C on Tuesday. Heat wave conditions too prevailed in most parts of Saurashtra and Kutch regions forcing people to remain indoors during the day.  The main roads of Rajkot wore a deserted look in the afternoon as people avoided going out. According to India Meteorological Department (IMD) officials, on Tuesday, maximum temperature in Rajkot was 44°C while Amreli and Surendranagar recorded 44°C and 45°C respectively. Surendranagar was the hottest city in the state on Tuesday. The rising temperature also adversely affected people’s health in Rajkot. According to emergency services 108, it has attended around 249 heat-related emergencies from May 1 to May 8 in Rajkot itself. These emergencies included vomiting, abdominal pain, chest pain, fall victim, fainting, dehydration and unconsciousness among others.  Extreme weather conditions gripped almost all of Gujarat, including Saurashtra, Kutch, north Gujarat, central Gujarat and south Gujarat. According to IMD, respite from the extreme heat is not likely for at least one week. Maximum temperature will hover around 42-43°C over next few days in many parts of the state.
