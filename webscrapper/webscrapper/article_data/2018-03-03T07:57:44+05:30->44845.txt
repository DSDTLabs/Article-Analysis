MADURAI: The Madurai bench of the Madras high court on Friday directed the commissioner of municipal administration to file a report regarding the safety measures taken for the welfare of scavengers. 

 A division bench headed by justice M Sathyanarayanan gave this direction on a petition filed by district secretary of   (AAP), Sivaganga, C Thirugnanam and adjourned the case to April 2. 
 
 The petitioner’s counsel said that the authorities failed to implement the provisions of the Prohibition of Employment as Manual Scavengers and their Rehabilitation Act, 2013. 
 
 He wanted a direction to the commissioner of municipal administration to strictly adhere to the provisions of the Act and to take action against the commissioner of   selection grade municipality for failure to implement the provisions.
 
 The municipality is looking after maintenance of drainages through tender. But, the successful bidder did not provide safety equipment to the sanitary workers. Due to shortage of workers, many places in the municipality are unclean, the counsel said.
 
 Besides, manual scavenging is taking place across the municipality. 
 
 The municipality’s action is against the directions of the   in   case. Further, all local bodies in the state including corporations are using manual scavengers, most of whom are not provided mechanical devices for sewage and septic tank cleaning, the counsel said.
