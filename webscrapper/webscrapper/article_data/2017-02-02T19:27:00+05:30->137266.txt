MUMBAI: Maharashtra A produced an excellent performance to beat top-ranked Petroleum Sports Promotion Board (PSPB) 3-1 in the women’s team championship semi-final of the 78th Senior Nationals at the Manesar Sports Club, in Manesar on Thursday.

 They will take on  , who had finished runners-up in the last edition, in the final.
 
 PSPB, however, ensured their berth in the men’s final and will clash with hosts Haryana who, too, had ended their campaign last year with the silver medal. PSPB and Haryana thrashed Gujarat and West Bengal by an identical 3-0 margins in their semifinals.
 
 In the women’s semi-finals, it boiled down to that third singles which Maharashtra won. When left-handed Shruti Amrute, a junior player, beat an in-form Mousumi Paul it was all over bar the shouting for PSPB. But then Pooja Sahasrabuddhe, who had given Maharashtra a flying start when she defeated Madhurika Patkar, continued with her good showing to score against the tricky veteran   in the fourth rubber to seal Maharashtra’s berth in the final.
