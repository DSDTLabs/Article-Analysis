CHENNAI:   chief minister Edappadi K Palaniswami on Monday laid the   for the construction of five flood protection schemes -- costing Rs 140 crore -- in   district, which was severely hit by the natural disasters in the recent years. The projects will come up in Cuddalore, Chidambaram, Kattumannarkoil and Panruti blocks.

 Palaniswami also gave away the best maintained dam award to Sathanur dam in Tiruvannamalai district. The award for the year 2015-16 carries a cash prize of Rs 10,000.
 
 An official release recalled a statement of former chief minister J Jayalalithaa announcing permanent measures such as widening of channels, strengthening of water bodies and embankment of rivers.
 
 Palaniswami launched 40 irrigation schemes at an estimated cost of Rs 142.30 crore, including restoration of dams, lakes, supply channels, construction of new dams besides reconstruction of check dams in districts like Ariyalur, Coimbatore, Dharmapuri, Dindigul, Kancheepuram, Kanyakumari, Karur, Krishnagiri, Pudukottai, Salem, Sivaganga, Thanjavur, Theni, Tuticorin, Tiruvannamalai, Trichy and Vellore.
 
 Bridges across the Varaganadhi in Periakulam block and surplus channel in Illupur block of Pudukottai were inaugurated. A service lane has also been built along the Vaigai river in Paramakudi block of Ramanathapuram district.
 
 The chief minister opened four government buildings in Podhigai complex on Greenways Road in Chennai.
