LUDHIANA: A court here has sentenced a 70-year-old man to seven years in prison after finding him guilty of the rape of a 16-year-old girl. 

 The court of additional sessions judge   also imposed a fine of Rs 60,000 on the convict and in default of payment of fine to further undergo rigorous imprisonment for a year. 
 
 The court ordered that Rs 50,000 of the Rs 60,000 fine, if paid by the convict, be given to the survivor under provisions of the CrPC. The court also directed the district legal services authority also provide compensation to the survivor.
 
 On April 8, 2015, Sadar   police on a statement by the survivor’s mother had booked the accused, a resident of Amloh, under Section 4 of the Protection of Children from Sexual Offences Act (POCSO).
 
 The complainant alleged that her husband was at work and she had gone to   on the day of the crime, while all four of their children were home. 
 
 Around this time, the accused, who was known to the survivor’s family, came to the house and asked the remaining three children to go to the market. He then bolted the door from inside and raped the teenager. 
 
 The father was the first one to have gotten to know about the crime, and he phoned the mother around 3pm. 
 
 Later, the family complained to the police and the man was arrested on April 10, 2015.
 
 During trial of the case, the accused claimed innocence and being falsely implicated in the case. The additional public prosecutor for state, S S  , pleaded for exemplary punishment to the accused on the basis of statement of prosecutrix, her mother and other prosecution witnesses apart from her medical examination.
