HYDERABAD: Nearly half the vehicles in the city are not fixed with high-security   plates (HSRP), which was made mandatory by the transport authority.

 “Over 30 lakh vehicles ply on city roads. Of those, 50% are not fixed with the high-security registration plates, including both old and new vehicles,” said the joint transport commissioner  .
 
  
  
 In 2013, fixing high-security registration plates was made mandatory for new vehicles in united  . However, most of the vehicles in the city flaunt the rule by using fancy and customised  .
 
 As per rules, once the registration and inspection of a vehicle is done, the owners receive a message asking to visit the designated   centre to get the HSRP fixed.
