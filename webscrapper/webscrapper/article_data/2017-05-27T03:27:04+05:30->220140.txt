Mumbai: The fishing community in   has sent a letter to the Centre not to publish the proposed coastal regulation zone notification without holding consultations with them.

 The Maharashtra Machchimar Kruti Samiti has demanded that the government take measures to enact a comprehensive Coastal Zone Regulation Act on the basis of consultations with fishing communities. The letter states that fishing communities are shocked and alarmed that the government plans to ease coastal rules, allow land reclamation for commercial use. The draft of the proposed notification was obtained by environmentalist Meenakshi Kapoor of the Delhi-based think-tank, Centre for Policy Research, through the Right to Information Act.
 
 Rajesh Mangela, a research analyst for the Samiti, said the draft proposes to allow tourism activities in ecologically sensitive areas along the shores and setting up of fish-processing units.
