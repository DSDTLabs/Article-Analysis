PANCHKULA: A 58-year-old man was killed after the scooter he was riding was hit by a speeding car near Panchkula’s   on Sunday evening.

 Gurwinderpal Singh Batra, a resident of Dhakoli who was a   by profession, was heading back home from his office in Sector 11, Panchkula, on his scooter when the car hit his scooter from behind and sped away. Such was the impact of the collission that the victim was flung in the air and left in a pool of blood. 
 
 Batra was rushed to Panchkula civil hospital in Sector 6 by passers-by and a PCR team that reached the spot. He was referred to the PGIMER, Chandigarh, due to his critical condition, and breathed his last there on Monday. The body of the deceased has been handed over to his family after the post-mortem.
 
 A case against under Section 304-A (causing death by negligence) and 279 (rash driving or riding on a public way) of the IPC has been registered at the Sector 14 police station. However, as the police have not received any inputs about the car involved in the accident, they are yet to identify the accused.
 
 “We are carrying out investigations to trace the car,” said the SHO of Sector 14 police station,   
 
 Family members informed that the deceased was survived by a son who lives in Australia, a daughter and his wife. His wife is the principal of Ballistics Vidyalaya, TBRL, Ramgarh.
