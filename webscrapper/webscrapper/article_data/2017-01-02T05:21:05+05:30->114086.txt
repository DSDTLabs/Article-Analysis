NEW DELHI:   are taking the joy out of celebrations. Sunday was no different as commuters spent hours on the roads leading to India Gate and Connaught Place.

 Traffic police said there were long snarls due a sudden increase in traffic volume in central Delhi. The CHexagon was choked as people, many armed with selfiesticks, started gathering for a gala time.
 
 Like every year, police had issued traffic advisory .This time, though, there appeared to be no stopping people who wanted to be at India Gate. Police had their hands full as they tried to direct the traffic. As a cascading effect, most of Lutyens' Delhi came to a standstill by evening.
 
 Approach roads to CP were choked. Those who drove to Sarojini Nagar, Karol Bagh, Chandni Chowk, Sadar Bazaar, South Extension and Lajpat Nagar either had to turn back disappointed or were stuck for hours. Long jams were reported along Ring Road, Outer Ring Road, Vikas Marg, Mathura Road, Aurobindo Road, Bhishma Pitamah Marg, NH-24 and ITO. The next two days are expected to be equally difficult.Police have advised people to use public transport as market parking lots are expected to be filled to capacity. There were long snarls outside Akshardham Temple, Lotus Temple and Bangla Sahib Gurdwara. The rush at Akshardham stretched to Noida border. “More than 10 lakh vehicles from NCR enter the city during the festive season, adding to the usual 40-50 lakh that ply daily,“ said a policeman.
