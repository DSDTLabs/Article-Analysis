MADHUBANI: Governor   on Tuesday said Bihar enjoyed a very important place in Indian story. “It is here that Prince Gautam attained enlightenment, became the Buddha and the great religion of Buddhism was born. It is here also that Lord Mahavira, the founder of another great religion, Jainism, was born and attained nirvana (death). Tenth Sikh Guru Gobind Singh was also born here,” the governor said while addressing a select gathering after unveiling the statue of late Nilambar Chaudhary, MLC, on the premises of the college named after him at Benipatti. 

 Invoking Mahatma Gandhi, he said the real objective of education should be the inculcation of creativity and sensibility so as to enable human being to serve the society in general, irrespective of caste, creed and religion. “As the constitutional head of the state, I always consider myself as the head of the family. The doors of   are always open for the people of Bihar,” he said.
 
 The governor lauded the services rendered by late Nilambar Chaudhary and exhorted the people to follow in his footsteps. “Only a good human being can be a good teacher or a good administrator or a good politician,” he said.
 
 Earlier, Dilip Chaudhary, MLC and son of late Nilambar Chaudhary, welcomed the guests. Prominent among those present on the occasion included Sitaram Yadav, MLA and Sang Kumar Chaudhary, educationist. While Ram Audit Yadav, former MLA from Benipatti proposed the vote of thanks, DPRO Jaishankar Prasad conducted the proceedings.
