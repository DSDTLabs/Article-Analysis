Music maestro L Subramaniam’s latest composition, Bharat  , was recently premiered at the Chicago World Music Festival, which is one of the largest festivals in the US.

 The symphony was written to mark India’s 70th year of independence, and has been influenced by significant eras in Indian history. Written especially to suit the unique voice of his playback singer wife  , the composition was premiered by three generations of female musicians — Kavita, his daughter   (singer/songwriter and educator), and his six-year-old granddaughter  .
 
 Additionally, his son and violinist   received two standing ovations for his performance alongside Alam Khan, musician and son of sarod maestro,  .
 
 The family is also slated to play the Bharat Symphony at the Sydney Opera House.
