NEW DELHI: Two incidents of fire were reported in the capital in the last 24 hours wherein three persons including a woman and a child died and three were injured.

 Over a 100 people were rendered homeless after fire broke out at a slum area in South Delhi’s Malviya Nagar on Sunday. The cause of fire, police officials said, is yet to be ascertained. 
 
 Fire officials said that they received a call about the incident at 2.50 am, soon after which 25 fire tenders were pushed into action. They said that all people, apart from one who had been charred to death by the time they reached there, were rescued. The person was identified as Ashok Gupta (50). Two others were injured in the incident as well. 
 
 Arvind Kejriwal and Manish Sisodia reached the slum area and declared offering Rs. 2 lakh to the deceased’s family and Rs. 25,000 each for the families of those who have been injured. 
 
 The other incident of fire was reported in outer Delhi’s  , where a major fire broke out inside a house on Saturday night. Two persons, a woman and a child died in the incident. They were identified as Aditi (28) and Anjali (12). Another child, Tavishka, was injured and is being given treatment
 
 Fire officials said that they received call about the incident at 9 pm, after which 16 fire tenders were sent to the spot. Others who were stuck up inside the house were rescued safely and the fire was doused within an hour. 
 
 Police said that the fire began due to a short circuit on the ground floor of the house. They said that the house where fire broke out belonged to the deceased's woman’s parents and she had come here to attend her brother’s marriage on Sunday.
