MADURAI: The Madurai bench of the Madras high court on Thursday said that the   health secretary has to file all documents pertaining to the setting up of All India Institutes of Medical Sciences (AIIMS) in the state on August 1, failing which the secretary should appear before the court on August 7. 

 The division bench of justices K K Sasidharan and G R Swaminathan gave this direction on a public interest litigation filed by one B R Basker seeking to set up AIIMS in Madurai.

 The central government wrote a letter to the Tamil Nadu government requesting it to identify three or four suitable alternative locations for AIIMS as approximately 200 acres of land is required to set up an AIIMS type super-speciality hospital cum teaching institution. The location should have road connectivity, water and electricity facilities, the ministry had told.
 
 It also told that the state government has to identify and select the appropriate place as per the criteria and to communicate the same to the health ministry. Thereafter, the ministry will carry out the necessary examination of the sites, finalise the most suitable site and take further action for the establishment of the institution. The health ministry is waiting for the response of the state government, the under-secretary had said.
