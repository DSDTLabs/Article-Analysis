KOLHAPUR: The district and sessions court in Kolhapur, hearing the Govind Pansare  , granted bail to Virendrasinh Tawade on Tuesday on a surety bond of Rs 25,000. 

 Special public prosecutor  , who represented the state, said, “While granting bail, the court also put some conditions on Tawade. He is not allowed to enter the jurisdiction of Kolhapur district, except during the hearing of the case. He will have to be present before the SIT from 11am to 1pm every Saturday... He should not try to tamper evidence or threaten the witnesses. He has to surrender his passport to the court. He should not go out of Maharashtra and should reveal his address of residence to the court. He should not change his address of residence until the court grants him permission for the same.”
 
 “We will appeal against the court’s decision in the Bombay high court soon,” he added.
 
 Sameer Patwardhan, who represented Tawade, said, “The conditions put on Tawade are the same as that on  , who was released on bail earlier. Tawade will abide by these conditions and will co-operate with the investigating agencies. The bail granted to Tawade indicates that the court has accepted the facts we presented.”
