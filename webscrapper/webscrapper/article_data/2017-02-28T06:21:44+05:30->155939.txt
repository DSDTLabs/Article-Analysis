RANCHI: All the 13   (GPOs) in Jharkhand started   (SGB) bonds on Monday. This is a part of the national scheme to issue gold bonds at the   fixed at Rs 2,893 per gram by the   (RBI) based on average gold prices last week. 

  Chief post master general, Jharkhand, SP Mandal, said the customers can invest anywhere between one gram and 500 grams of gold, bonds for which would be available till March 3. 
 
  For buying the bond, the customer should submit a KYC (Know Your Customer) form and have a valid identity proof like voter i-card, Aadhar card, or passport and pan card. 
 
  "The government has fixed a term of 8 years for maturing of gold bonds and will pay taxable interest at the rate of 2.5% annually," he said, clarifying that the option for withdrawal would be available from the fifth year onwards. The bond has a nomination facility as well. 
 
  Mandal said investors can obtain application forms from any of the GPOs and submit them with necessary papers and money in cash, draft or cheque. 
 
  Speaking about the benefits of SGB, one of the officials at the Ranchi GPO said the quantity of gold for which the investor pays, is protected as he receives the ongoing market price at the time of redemption. 
 
  "The SGB offers better alternative to holding gold in physical form as the risks and costs of storage are eliminated," he said. 
 
  "Investors are assured of the market value of gold at the time of maturity and periodical interest," the official added.
