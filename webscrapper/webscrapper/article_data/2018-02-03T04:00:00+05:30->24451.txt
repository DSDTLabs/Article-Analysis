Surat: Nine employees of a cash management firm were booked by the police on Friday for cheating a nationalized bank to the tune of Rs 44.04 lakh in a period of one year from 2015 to 2016. These employees were tasked with collecting and refilling cash in the ATMs of Bank of Baroda (BOB) in the city. 

 Manager of CMS Info System Limited Chandrabhan Chaubey filed a complaint against the employees when the bank audit exposed the fraud. Rahul Patel, Jayesh Nadkar Pal, Kalpesh Patel, Harish Maru, Jay Patel, Tejas Patel, Piyush Rana, Tushar Parmar and Prashant Marathe were booked under IPC Sections 406, 409, 420 and 114. They had been employed to collect cash deposited by customers in the ATM machines and fill up them with cash currency brought from the bank chest. 
 
 A police officer said, “They collected the cash from the ATMs of BoB over a period of time. We have called the people concerned to take forward our probe.” tnn
