NEW DELHI: One   jawan was killed and five others were injured after suspected militants opened fire at a CRPF convoy in Srinagar's   area on Monday.

 Unidentified gunmen fired at a civilian passenger vehicle carrying CRPF jawans of 97 Battalion at Sempora in Pantha Chowk area. Two civilians, including a 10-year-old girl, were also injured in the crossfire.
 
 The wounded jawans were rushed to a hospital and two of them are reportedly in critical condition. One of the injured personnel, Head Constable Basappa, later succumbed to his injuries.
 
 "Our companies were going from Jammu to Srinagar, when we were fired upon by terrorists. Six personnel were injured and have been taken to the hospital," CRPF PRO B Chaudhury told ANI. 
 
 A police official told PTI that the militants managed to flee from the area by taking advantage of the ensuing chaos.
