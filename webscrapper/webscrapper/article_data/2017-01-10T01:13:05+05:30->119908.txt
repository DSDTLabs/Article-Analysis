CHENNAI: Class 10 and 12 exams for all CBSE-affiliated schools will begin on March 9. The   said in a press release on Monday that in view of the coming assembly elections in Punjab, Goa, Manipur, Uttarakhand and Uttar Pradesh, the exams had to be scheduled for a later date than usual. The exams, which should have begun a week earlier, had to be rescheduled with a "reasonable time gap maintained between all major subjects." 

 Class 10 exams will go on till  , while class 12 exams will conclude on April 29. According to the board, the dates of entrance exams like the JEE (Joint Entrance Examination) for engineering and NEET (National Eligibility cum Entrance Test) for medicine were also given due consideration before choosing a date, which would effectively allow students to "complete the CBSE examination ahead of these entrance exams".
 
 This year, class 10 exams will be taken by 16,67,573 students in the country, a 10.56% increase from the number that took the exam last year. A total of 10,98,420 students will take the class 12 examination, a 3% increase from last year.
