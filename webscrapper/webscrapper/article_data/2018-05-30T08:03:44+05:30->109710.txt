LUDHIANA: The police on Tuesday claimed to have busted a gang of robbers who struck at yarn factories, with the arrest of seven.

 Sazmi of Badera village in Saharanpur; Muhammad  , also of Saharanpur; Muhammad Nadeem; Muhammad Sonu; Muhammad Mubarak; Deepak Sharma, a driver and resident of Tibba Road; and Gurmeet Singh of Nakodar were arrested on a tip-off. 
 
 Commissioner of police   said a check post was set up at Seedha road T-point at 7am on Tuesday. A tempo was stopped and on checking 3,000 kilograms of yarn was recovered from it. Besides, sharp-edged weapons were recovered from the possession of Sazmi, the kingpin, and Afzal. 
 
 Police said the gang would strike at hosiery factories at night and hold their employees hostage. 
 
 Deputy commissioner of police (crime) Gagan Ajit Singh said with their arrest, 13 cases of robbery of yarn from factories have been solved. Their three buyers — Arif Muhammad, Saleem Baggar and Muhammad Gulzar, all residents of Tibba Road — were yet to be arrested. 
 
 “The gang had been active in the city for the past five years. It was involved in several cases of robberies in factories in Sahnewal, Focal Point and Meherban. A CCTV footage procured from a unit helped identify them,” the DCP added. 
 
 As many as 36 boxes and 23 bags of looted yarn have been seized. The mini truck they used to commit the crimes has also been impounded. 
 
 Several loot cases were registered in Ludhiana against Sazmi and Afzal, who had been declared proclaimed offenders.
 
 A case under sections 379-B (snatching), 380 (theft in dwelling, house etc), 457 (lurking house-trespass or house-breaking by night in order to commit offence punishable with imprisonment) of the Indian Penal Code (IPC) has been registered.
