In a nod to gender empowerment, the government has increased allocations for women by 27% to Rs 1.84 lakh crore in 2017-2018 from Rs 1.56 lakh crore in 2016-2017. 

  Saying that "Sabka Saath Sabka Vikas begins with the girl child and women",   Arun Jaitley announced establishment of   at village level with an allocation of Rs 500 crore in 14 lakh anganwadi centres. This is expected to be one stop convergent support service for empowering rural women with opportunities for skill development, employment, digital literacy, health and nutrition. 
 
  "Setting up of institutions like Mahila Shakti Kendras will increase access of essential services for women and this is welcome. There is also an increase in employment opportunities for women with their participation increased from less than 48% to 55% in MGNREGA. 
 
  But a significant increase in funds in the absence of specific proposals runs the risk of remaining under-utilised like the Nirbhaya fund," said Ranjana Kumari, Director of Centre for Social Research. 
 
 L ast year, the government had announced a nationwide scheme for financial assistance to pregnant women. Under this scheme, Rs 6,000 will be transferred directly to the bank accounts of pregnant women who undergo institutional delivery and vaccinate their children. 
 
  To facilitate this move, allocation under the maternity benefit scheme has been increased from Rs 400 crore to Rs 2,700 crore.
