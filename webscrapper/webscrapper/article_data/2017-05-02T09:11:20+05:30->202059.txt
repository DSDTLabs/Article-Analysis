VEIN PUIN (TARN TARAN): Grief and anger pervaded the village of Naik Subedar   of 22   who was killed in an ambush by the   more than 250 metres inside   on Monday morning. Reports of the mutilation of the Army man's body, who was a source of inspiration for the youth of this area, had the residents of his village in Tarn Taran district of Punjab brimming with anger against the neighbouring country. 

 "I want the government to take decisive action against Pakistan this time. Let there be a full-fledged war, our jawans and villages are ready for it," said Ranjit Singh, the braveheart's brother while talking to TOI on Monday evening. Singh is survived by his wife, three children, including two daughters, and his elderly parents.
