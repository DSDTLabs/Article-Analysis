 A police team headed by senior inspector Sanjay Patil of social security cell said the police had booked seven persons, including a reputable businessman from Pune, for allegedly running the hookah parlour illegally.
 
 The police found 25 youths and a minor girl smoking hookah in the premises. Seven hookah pots, loudspeakers and tobacco flavours totally worth Rs62,500 were seized from the spot.
 
 The minor girl was 17 years old, while the others were aged between 19 and 36 years. A majority of them were students of different city colleges staying at Kalyaninagar, NIBM Road, Nigdi Pradhikaran, Model Colony, Wanowrie and other areas. “The students are witnesses in the case,” Patil said.
 
 The police rescued the minor waiters, too, and conducted their age determination test at the Sasoon General Hospital. “On May 5, we received the medical reports that revealed the waiters were all 17. Based on this we invoked charges of Juvenile Justice Act against the owners,” he said.
 
 All the three boys came from Uttar Pradesh looking for jobs in the city. They belong to poor families. “The hookah parlour owners were aware of their age but employed them on minimum salaries,” Patil said.
 
 The case has been handed over to the Koregaon Park police for further investigation.
