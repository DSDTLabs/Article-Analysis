 Based on information given by a complainant, CBI sleuths laid a trap near Kendriya Shulk Bhavan at Basheerbagh on Tuesday evening. They caught superintendent (anti-evasion) of Hyderabad-II commissionerate S Gopala Krishna Murthy red-handed when he was taking Rs 4 lakh cash from Jagdish, representative of Ms KM Plastics.
 
 During interrogation, Jagdish confessed that Murthy inspected his company K M Plastics on March 1 with his team and made a panchnama about certain tax irregularities. The central excise superintendent then demanded Rs 10 lakh as bribe to ensure that Jagdish was not booked for excise duty evasion. Jagdish agreed to pay the bribe and the confidential informant alerted the CBI about the entire episode.
 
 The complainant also told CBI that Murthy has been regularly collecting bribes from owners and proprietors of various companies. Jagdish told CBI sleuths that he had earlier in the day paid `6 lakh bribe to Murthy separately at the same spot. CBI registered a case under sections 120-B of the IPC rw section 7, 12, 13 (2) rw 13 (1) (d) of PC Act, 1988. The duo were produced before the court and remanded in judicial custody .
