GHAZIABAD: A day after an Ola car driven by a drunken man ploughed into a weekly market injuring 16 people, police claimed that the driver, an alcoholic, might have pressed the accelerator instead of the brake. The accident took place on Saturday 11.15pm during the weekly ‘shani bazaar’ at   in Khoda area.

 The car, a Datsun GO, was being driven by Rakesh Yadav alias Chhotu, who was allegedly under the influence of alcohol at the time of the incident. “He must have pressed the accelerator instead of the brake,” said Jai Prakash Chaubey, SHO, Khoda police station. Yadav was produced before a magistrate who sent him to judicial custody.
 
 Shani bazaar is a weekly farmers’ market that remains open from 6.30pm till midnight. “Customers who were there to purchase vegetables and other groceries were among the injured,” Chaubey added. 
 
 Eyewitnesses said the speed of the car was such that it did not stop despite colliding with several vegetable carts parked on the roads and dragged people along with it. It finally crashed into the concrete stairs in front of a shop. The car had to be manually lifted to its side, and five persons were rescued from beneath it. The injured include six women and three children.
 
 The injured were rushed to hospital in autos, scooters and cars of residents. 
 
 “Someone told me that the car had dragged people for almost 200 metres,” said Lalit Kumar, an eyewitness. 
 
 An FIR has been registered against Yadav under sections 338 (causing grievous hurt), 279 (rash driving) and 447 (criminal trespass) of IPC at Khoda police station.
