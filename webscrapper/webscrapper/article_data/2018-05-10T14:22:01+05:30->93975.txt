SALEM: Urging people to boycott movies like the recently-released Iruttu Araiyil Murattu Kuththu, which hit the headlines for its double meaning dialogues and scenes, cadres of the   on Tuesday sent used   to   officials and its director  .

 Earlier, the film director had asked the movie goers to bring tissue paper to cinema halls for his movie. “Iruttu Araiyil Murattu Kuththu, which hit the screens across the state last Friday, is full of double meaning dialogues and scenes. It has broken all the standards set by the Tamil cinema world,” said N Praveen Kumar, Salem district secretary of DYFI.
 
  About 100 DYFI cadres on Tuesday gathered at the head post office in the city, wiped their faces with tissue papers and parcelled them to Chennai.
