NEW DELHI: Deputy chief minister   on Wednesday urged Delhiites to continue fighting against air pollution and keep up the good work, after the latest data released by the   (CPCB) revealed that Delhi’s annual average PM2.5 and PM10 levels had fallen for the first time in the last 5 years. The pollution levels showed a drop in 2017 as compared to the levels in 2016.

 While average PM10 levels reduced to 260 micrograms per cubic metre in 2017 as compared to an average of 310 micrograms per cubic metre in 2016, Delhi’s average PM 2.5 levels also fell down to 101 micrograms per cubic metre compared to 120 micrograms per cubic metre in 2016.
 
 “The drop wouldn’t have been possible if people hadn’t stepped forward to fight pollution,” said Sisodia, along with Delhi environment minister  .
 
 “The consumption of diesel reduced by 16% in 2017 as compared to 2016. This is no small reduction and it shows that people are switching to cleaner fuels. The capital also saw a significant drop in the registration of new vehicles. In addition, Delhi’s green cover has increased by 0.2% over the last couple of years which roughly translates to 600 hectares of land,” said Sisodia.
 
 The government also said strict implementation of measures like stopping of construction activity or banning of diesel generator sets under the Graded Response Action Plan (GRAP) has helped Delhi’s air quality.
