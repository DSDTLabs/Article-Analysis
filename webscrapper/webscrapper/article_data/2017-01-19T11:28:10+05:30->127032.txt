AGRA: At least four people were killed and more than six were injured after a bus carrying American tourists rammed into a tractor on Agra-Jaipur national highway (NH-11). Though, the agony of the victims did not end there, as the  , ferried the uncovered corpses in a pickup truck for 30 kms.
 
  The accident took place at around 1:30pm when a tractor trolley ferrying at least 12 passengers was hit from behind by a speeding tourist bus. The accident took place 2 kms ahead of Korai toll plaza in   area.
  
  Speaking to TOI, station officer of Fatehpur Sikri, Vinod Yadav said, “We have booked the bus driver,  , under Section 279 (Rash driving or riding on a public way), 304A (Causing death by negligence), 337 (Causing hurt by act endangering life or personal safety of others) and 338 (Causing grievous hurt by act endangering life or personal safety of others) of the IPC.”
  
  “None of the American tourists have been injured, but those travelling in tractor trolley suffered grave injuries. The tourist bus was coming from Jaipur,” Yadav said.
  
  The deceased have been identified as Kuldeep, Devi Das and Durga. Police officers are yet to identify the last corpse. The injured were ferried to S   in ambulance but the deceased were kept at the back of a pickup truck and their corpses were brought to mortuary uncovered.
  
  When TOI tried to contact project director of NHAI Rajasthan, Subhash Janu, he did not respond to any calls. Later, Korai toll plaza incharge Ajitabj Jha confirmed that corpses were ferried uncovered in a pickup truck. “Since the injured were taken to hospital in one ambulance, the dead were sent in a pickup truck,” he said.
