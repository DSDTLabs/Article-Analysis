KANPUR: Labour department has issued three notices against   leader   in building collapse case and ordered to pay Rs 75 lakh as compensation to the labourers who lost their lives. 

 Ten labourers had died when the under-construction building owned by Mehtab Alam collapsed on February 1. The department had calculated compensation of Rs 75 lakh and it might be increased if compensation to injured labourers is also included.
 
 The source said department had issued two notices earlier against Alam but he did not give any reply. Therefore, fresh notices have been issued. Deputy labour commissioner Rajesh Mishra said that department would initiate recovery proceedings if building owner failed to respond the notices issued to him.
