BHOPAL: The day as well as night temperature came down marginally in the state capital on Friday. There is also a possibility of thundery development in the city and parts of the state.

 Officials of meteorological department, Bhopal circle, attributed the slight dip in temperature to moisture incursion from Bay of Bengal, Arabian Sea and an upper air cyclonic circulation over southwest MP. Besides, there is western disturbance in Jammu & Kashmir, which is leading to blowing of cold winds, officials added.
 
 Day temperature in the city was recorded at 34.0 degrees Celsius, which was 3 degrees above the normal mark while the minimum temperature was recorded at 15.5 degrees Celsius, which was normal. A day earlier, the day temperature was recorded at 35.3 degrees Celsius, which was four degrees above the normal mark while night temperature was recorded at 16.8 degrees Celsius, which was 2 degrees above normal.
 
 Meanwhile, the wind direction in the city was westerly and the average wind speed was 10 kilometres per hour. The highest temperature in the state was recorded in Raisen at 36.2 degrees Celsius while the lowest temperature in the state was recorded at 12 degrees Celsius in Ujjain and Rewa.
 
 In the forecast for Bhopal on Saturday, the weatherman said there would be a clear sky with a possibility of it becoming partly cloudy during afternoon. There is also a likelihood of thundery development. Average wind speed will be around 8 to 10 kilometres per hour while the day and night temperatures will be at 33 degrees Celsius and 15 degrees Celsius, respectively.
 
 In the forecast for state, the weatherman said there is a chance of dust storm/light rain /thundery activity at isolated places over Rewa, Shahdol, Mandla, Seoni, Jabalpur, Narsinghpur, Katni, Damoh, Panna, Chhatarpur. Met department also issued a warning of hailstorm at isolated places over Rewa, Sidhi, Singroli, Katni, Umaria, Shahdol Mandla and Dindori.
