HUBBALLI: Chief minister   said the results of elections in Tripura, Meghalaya and Nagaland will not have any impact on the   assembly elections. 

 He told reporters here on Saturday the election results of one state won't have an impact on another state. "The   had no illusions about winning in Tripura and Nagaland as we were not there. Hence, the results are not shocking, We were the single-largest party in Meghalaya," he said.
 
 Asked if the rise of the BJP in north-eastern states is a defeat for secular parties, Siddaramaiah said there are small and regional parties in those states. "Those parties cannot be called secular parties. Hence, their defeat is not the defeat of secular parties. In Karnataka, JD(S) is calling itself secular. Is JD(S) a secular party? It was secular when we were in it. It's not secular anymore," he said. 
 
 Reacting to BJP leaders' claim that Karnataka will also be Congress-free, Siddaramaiah said they've been saying it for many years. "The time will come when the BJP will vanish," he said. 
 
 He said the party favours conducting assembly elections with ballot papers. "Many countries tried conducting elections with EVMs but switched back to ballot papers," he said.
 
  
 
  Siddaramaiah said a committee set up by the minority commission to look into the demand for a separate Lingayat religion has submitted its report to the commission. "The report is yet to reach me. We'll take a call once the report is submitted to the state government," he said.
