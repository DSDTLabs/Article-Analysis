  : Former DMK town panchayat chairman’s wife and her brother were arrested for illegal sale of Indian Made Foreign Liquor in their house at Veeraganur here on Saturday late evening.

 According to a source, Salem superintendent of police (SP) P Rajan got a tip about the illegal liquor sale at DMK man P Akilan’s house and ordered Veeraganur police station inspector Muthusamy, 45, and sub-inspector  , 48, to take action.
 
 The duo reportedly conducted a raid at Akilan’s house on Saturday morning and informed the SP that there wasn’t any case of illegal sale of liquor. Not convinced, the SP directed assistant superintendent of police (ASP) P Chandrasekar to look into the issue.
 
 The ASP alerted the prohibition enforcement wing (PEW) police in  , who raided the DMK’s former Veeraganur town panchayat chairman P Akilan’s house on Saturday evening.
 
 During the search, officials found 1,200 bottles of Indian Made Foreign Liquor worth around Rs 1.5 lakh and arrested Akilan’s wife Maathu, 45, and her brother K Subramani, 42.
 
 “The duo has confessed to their crime. They bought liquor from Tasmac outlets and other states, and sold them at exorbitant rates,” the police said.
 
 The Attur division PEW registered a case against the duo and they were remanded in judicial custody. The two were later lodged in the   prison.
 
 The SP, meanwhile, transferred Muthusamy and Veeramuthu to the Armed Reserve in Salem district for not following his instructions and also providing him false information. He has also ordered a departmental inquiry against the duo.
