During the four years, when he served as MD, Dr K Rathnam, the 55-year-old officer, had implemented four major projects including that of starting a cattle feed plant at Kapadwanj and milk processing units at Punjab and Guwahati apart from starting Amul’s operations in United States through tripartite agreement. When he took over the reins of Amul from his predecessor Rahul Kumar, Amul Dairy’s turnover was Rs 3,440 crore which is expected to touch Rs 6,250 crore in financial year 2017-18. Also, the dairy union’s milk procurement which was around 63 crore litres per year touched 110 crore litres a year in the last four years.
