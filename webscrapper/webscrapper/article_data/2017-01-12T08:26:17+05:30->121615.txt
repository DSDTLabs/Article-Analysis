NEW DELHI: Four youths have been arrested for allegedly abusing and thrashing a policeman for stopping them at a traffic intersection in south Delhi's  . The accused - Vishal Sharma, Harish Lohia, Yatin Norang and Navjyot Sehrawat, all 23 years old - were allegedly drinking in a moving car when they were stopped.

 They were arrested from their residences after police first traced the address of Lohia, who was the owner of the car, and then held his three other friends in separate raids.
 
 Constables Mohammad Alam, Prithwiraj and Sushil were on picket duty at Masudpur near Malik Hotel at 11pm when a speeding Skoda narrowly avoided the barricades. “When Alam told them to drive carefully , they came out of the car and thrashed him before fleeing,“ said a police officer.
 
 Alam was rushed to hospital for treatment.
 
 Police said the accused were returning from a birthday party and were heavily drunk. All of them are unemployed.
 
 “A case was registered and investigations taken up,“ said Chinmoy Biswal, DCP II (south). A case under Sections 186 (obstructing public servant in discharge of public functions), 353 (assault or criminal force to deter public servant from discharge of his duty), 332 (assault or criminal force to deter public servant from discharge of his duty) and 34 (acts done by several persons in furtherance of common intention) of IPC was registered at Vasant Kunj (South) police station.
 
 In October, the Delhi government had banned drinking at public places and imposed hefty fines and a jail term for such violations.While the fine for drinking is Rs 5,000, the offender could face double the penalty and a jail term for creating nuisance.
 
 The government had also run a campaign to dissuade people from drinking in open and make them aware of the rules.
