CHANDIGARH: A district court here held a 24-year-old fruit seller guilty in the murder case of another vegetable vendor in May 2017. 

 The court of district and sessions judge Balbir Singh pronounced the order against   Kumar, who is a resident of Indira Colony. The quantum of sentence will be pronounced on March 26.
 
 The matter dates back to the intervening night of May 25 and May 26, 2017, when complainant  , along with his brother Sukhpal, were on duty at a mandi in   of Manimajra. 
 
 At 12:30am when Vijay was guarding his fruits from cattle, an argument broke out between Kumar and Sukh Pal over space. 
 
 The fight took an ugly turn after Chattarpal and his brother Sukhpal decided that they would report the matter to the police. 
 
 However, as per the complainant, the accused Vijay continued to follow them to Manimajra police station, hurling abuses at the duo. He also threatened to kill them if they reported the matter to the police.
 
 When the brothers reached Manimajra police station's entrance gate, Kumar caught hold of Sukhpal and struck his head in his chest. 
 
 As a result, Sukhpal received some injury and fell down. 
 
 His brother rushed him to a civil dispensary in Manimajra. He was told Sukhpal was in a criticial condition. Sukhpal passed away at the dispensary later. The doctors said that the victim had injured his head due to the fall.
 
 Police arrested Vijay the same night and registered a case under Section 302 (punishment for murder) of the Indian Penal Code (IPC) at the Manimajra police station.
