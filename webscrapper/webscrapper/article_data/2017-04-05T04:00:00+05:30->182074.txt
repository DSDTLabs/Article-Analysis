Vadodara: Rural police seized Indian Made Foreign Liquor (IMFL) worth Rs 2.04 lakh from Mohammedpura village near Kapurai outpost late on Monday night.

 Police said that liquor stock belonged to bootlegger Vikram Chavda. This was the third seizure on Chavda’s liquor stock by police in last two days.
 
 Police said that during routine patrolling they had received a tip-off that some persons are unloading alcohol bottles in the bushes in Mohammedpura village. 
 
 When the police reached the spot, Sabbir Rathod, a resident of Tarsali, and Sanjay Mahida, a resident of Vadodara taluka, were caught loading IMFL stock in an MUV.
 
 Cops arrested Rathod and Mahida after which they told the cops that the stock belonged to Chavda.
 
 On April 2, the special operations group (SOG) of city police had intercepted a van near national highway-8 in Tarsali and seized IMFL worth Rs 1.5 lakh. While on April 3, SOG sleuths raided Chavda’s liquor godown near Kapurai crossroads and seized alcohol worth Rs 2.41 lakh.
 
 Police said that Chavda has been booked seven times in the past in prohibition cases and was also detained under Prevention of Anti-Social Activities Act (PASA) three times in 2012 and 2013.
