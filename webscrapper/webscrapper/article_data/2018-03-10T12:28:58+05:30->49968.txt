TRICHY: Five people, including two Iranian nationals, were on Friday sentenced to 20 years jail by a Pudukkottai court for selling narcotic drugs. The accused Iranian nationals,   49,   30, came to India in 2013 and rented a house owned by one Kaliammal at Sirukoodalpatti village, Karaikudi in   district. 

 They were engaged in producing Methamphetamine, a highly addictive stimulant that affects the central nervous system since then. They were selling it though Shavkat Ali, 55, of Chennai and Nasud Atthar, 38, in New Delhi.
 
 Tracing the duo from Delhi, a team from Narcotics Control Bureau (NCB) raided the house at Sirukoodalpatti on March 23, 2013 and recovered 52 packs of Methamphetamine, each weighing half a kilogram, worth Rs 10 crore. 
 
 The duo was arrested along with Kaliammal and the NCB seized the equipment used to make Methamphetamine. 
 
 The essential commodities act special court Pudukkottai, while hearing the case on Friday, sentenced the five accused including Shavkat Ali and Nasud Atthar, who were arrested later by NCB, to 20 years of imprisonment. The court also slapped a penalty of Rs 2 lakh on Nasud and Rs 3 lakh each on the four other convicts.
