BHOPAL: Three   players of   have brought laurels to the country by winning silver medal at the Asian championship organised in Okinawa, Japan, on Saturday. 

 Defeating Sri Lanka in the team kata category, MP players Gargi Singh Parihar, Saloni Patidar and Vandana Yadav won the silver medal. Sports minister Yashodhara Raje Scindia congratulated the trio for their feat. "We have been working hard on our junior players. This medal is a proof of that. More medals will come soon in other events also," said Scindia.
 
  Around 48 players from India, had gathered at Bhopal's Tatya Tope Stadium last month to attend the national camp. These players went to Asian championship after the camp. The national camp is being organised under the guidance of internationally acclaimed coach Ahmed Safi from Iran. Karate has emerged as an important event after   announced that the game will be introduced in 2020 in Tokyo, Japan. Due to this reason, the ongoing national camp is being seen as an important activity. 
 
 In September 2015, games like karate, baseball, softball, skateboarding, surfing and sport climbing were shortlisted for inclusion in the 2020 Summer Olympics. In June 2016, executive board of International Olympic Committee ( ) announced that they would support the proposal to include all shortlisted sports in the 2020 Games. Finally on August 3, all five sports were approved for inclusion in 2020 Olympic programme.
