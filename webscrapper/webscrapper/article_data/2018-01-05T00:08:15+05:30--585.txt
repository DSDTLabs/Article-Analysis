Jaipur: There was no respite from shivering cold conditions in the state for the third consecutive day on Thursday when at least six places in the state witnessed its minimum temperatures on or below 5 degrees Celsius. Alwar remained coldest in the state where a minimum temperature of 0.4 degree Celsius was recorded. 

 People in Alwar not only witnessed the coldest day in the state of the season so far they also witnessed early morning fog. It is rare when a district like Alwar is recorded to be coldest in the state as usually Churu and Sriganganagar remain coldest during winters. 
 
 Attributing the continued dip in the mercury to the active northerly winds Jaipur met office said that similar conditions will prevail in the state in the next couple of days. Dense to moderate fog will also be witnessed at many places in the state. 
 
 Jaipurities woke up to another chilly morning as a minimum temperature of 6.4 degrees Celsius was recorded in the city. The continued dip in the mercury has also affected the daily footfall of the morning walkers at the central park. During the early morning hours roads gave an empty look as people confined themselves to their respective homes. Moderate fog was also witnessed in the outskirts of Jaipur which also affected the road traffic. A few early morning trains to Jaipur also arrived late due to heavy fog in the neighbouring Harayana and Delhi. 
 
 In its forecast Jaipur met office said that many places in the state would witness its minimum temperature on or below 4.0 degrees Celsius. 
 
 Alwar was followed by Churu where a minimum temperature of 2.7 degrees Celsius was recorded, Sriganganagar 3, Pilani 3.5, Udaipur 3.6, Jodhpur 6.4, Ajmer 6.6, Kota 6.8, Barmer 8.7, Bikaner 9.9 and a minimum temperature of 10.3 degrees Celsius was recorded in Jaisalmer.
