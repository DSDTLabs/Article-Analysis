MUMBAI:   of Resqink Association of Wildlife Welfare ( ) were overjoyed to find that at least 15 eggs of a checkered keelback snake they rescued last month have been successfully hatched.

 The   with 70 eggs were rescued from a drain in Vikhroli on February 7. “Digging and cementing work had been going on there,“ said Pawan Sharma of RAWW. “Our zoologist, Chinmay Joshi, had started taking care of the snake and her eggs at his Mulund house. So, we were all very happy when 15 of these eggs hatched on Saturday .“
 
 Of the 70 eggs that had been found in the drain, many were visibly damaged while about 30 were in intact. “We had informed the range forest officials concerned who also encouraged us to save the eggs. Due to the recent rise in temperatures, some more eggs had perished. These 15 baby keelbacks will be carefully released into the fo rest, close to a water body,“ said Sharma.
 
 Checkered keelback, a non-venomous snake, is known as Asiatic Water Snake. They are usually found near ponds, springs. wells and rivers.
