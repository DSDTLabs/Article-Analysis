NEW DELHI: Nine students of a government school in Devli fell ill on Thursday morning after a   was found in the midday meal. The children, four aged below 14, were rushed to Madan Mohan Malviya hospital where they have been kept under observation for the night. They are said to be out of danger.

 “The students complained of minor stomach ache and are being treated for it. There has been no nausea or vomiting,” said Dr Ramesh Chugh, medical superintendent at the hospital.
 
 An official said the mid-day meal distribution was stopped as soon as the dead rat was discovered. “But by that time a few children had already consumed the food. We rushed them to a hospital as a precautionary measure. The others were asked to throw away the   served to them,” he said.
 
 Education minister Manish Sisodia said a complaint has been registered against the vendor. The kitchen of the school is going to be taken over by the government and the vendor will be blacklisted. A showcause notice has been sent to the vendor.
 
 Senior BJP leader Ramesh Bidhuri and Congress leader Kiran Walia also visited the children at the hospital. “The school authorities gave us a complaint about the children falling ill after consuming the mid-day meal,” said DCP (southeast) Romil Baaniya.
