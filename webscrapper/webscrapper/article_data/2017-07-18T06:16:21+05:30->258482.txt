AHMEDABAD:   (SAC), Isro, Ahmedabad, recently released a video showing the launch of India’s mapping satellite CartoSat-2E from Isro’s workhorse Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV) (C-38). The video, shot by the camera developed by  , shows CartoSat detaching from the PSLV on June 22 this year.

  The satellite is on a five-year mission and will collect imagery for urban planners, emergency response, agricultural purposes, and for security establishments.
 
 The PSLV had launched the 712kg Cartosat-2 series satellite for earth observation, and the 30 co-passenger satellites weighing together about 243 kg at lift-off, into a 505km polar Sun Synchronous Orbit (SSO). The total weight of all these satellites carried on PSLV-C38 came to about 955kg.
 
 PSLV-C38 was launched from the First Launch Pad (FLP) of   Space Centre (SDSC) SHAR, Sriharikota. This is the seventeenth flight of PSLV in 'XL' configuration (with the use of solid strap-on motors).
 
 In the June 22 launch, along with the CartSat 2E, there were 29 nano satellites from 14 countries namely, Austria, Belgium, Chile, Czech Republic, Finland, France, Germany, Italy, Japan, Latvia, Lithuania, Slovakia, United Kingdom and United States of America. One nano satellite (NIUSAT) from India was also among them.
 
 The 29 international   satellites were launched as part of commercial arrangements between Antrix Corporation Limited (Antrix), a government of India company under department of space (DOS), and the commercial arm of Isro and international customers.
