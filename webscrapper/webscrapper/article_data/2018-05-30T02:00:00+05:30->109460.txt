PATNA: The   has cancelled at least three pairs of passenger trains either originating from or passing through the jurisdiction of the East Central Railway (ECR) in Bihar from May 29 to June 3. The step has been taken due to track doubling and non-interlocking (NI) work being carried out at three different stations on the Janghai-Varanasi section of the Lucknow division.

 According to ECR’s chief public relations officer (CPRO) Rajesh Kumar, the railways cancelled the Rajendra Nagar Terminal-Jammu Tawi Archana Express (12355/12356) from May 29 to June 2. On its return journey, the train will remain cancelled on June 3. Similarly, the Howrah-Amritsar Mail (13005/13006) and the Puri-New Delhi Nilanchal Express (12875/12876) will also be cancelled.
 
 Meanwhile, the Puri-New Delhi Nilanchal Express, which originated from Puri on Tuesday, was diverted to the Varanasi-Sultanpur section.
 
 Plastic-free zone: The railways has taken a policy decision to make the Patna Junction (under the Danapur division of the ECR) and the Muzaffarpur station (under the Sonepur division) free from the use of plastic.
 
 “The use of plastic bags at these stations has been banned in order to make their premises clean and pollution free. Vendors and tea stall owners have been asked to use eco-friendly materials like earthen cups for selling tea or coffee on platforms,” the CPRO said.
