COIMBATORE: A 38-year-old man was found   with his face completely burnt in a vacant land at   near   here on Sunday early morning. 

 The deceased was identified as L Kalimuthu, 38, a native of Arupukottai in Virudhunagar district. He was employed with a spinning mill at Pallapalayam near Sulur and stayed in a room on its premises along with his co-worker Ragunathan, 24, son of Pannerselvam from Thiruvidaimaruthur in Thanjavur.
 
 Police suspect the role of Ragunathan in the crime. According to the police, the duo planned to consume liquor as they got salary ( 21,000) on Saturday night. They bought liquor from a Tasmac shop and left for the vacant land at Kannampalayam for drinking. But they did not return to the company till Sunday morning.
 
 On Sunday around 8am, local people found a body with its face charred in the vacant land and immediately alerted the Sulur police, who rushed to the spot. The police identified the victim from the Aadhaar card and the driving license found from the shirt pocket of the deceased.
 
 The police said the murderer attacked Kalimuthu using a boulder and later burnt his face. The whereabouts of Ragunathan is not known. Hence, the police suspect that he might have murdered Kalimuthu. The police said Ragunathan was married with three daughters and his family members were in Thanjavur.
 
 The Sulur police have registered a case under Section 302 (murder) of the Indian Penal Code and further investigation is on.
