Visakhapatnam: As many as 7,000 students from 21 social welfare residential schools performed a record   dance on the occasion of Ambedkar Jayanti at AU Engineering College grounds on Tuesday and are hoping to make it to the Guinness Book of Records. The event was attended by chief minister N Chandrababu Naidu.

 Addressing the gathering after the performance, the CM said the state government has allotted Rs 100 crore for the development of Kuchipudi and it is the people’s responsibility to protect our heritage. “Physical and mental strength can be gained through Kuchipudi, besides personality development,” he added. 
 
 The CM further requested all parents to encourage their children to take up Kuchipudi and the government will soon prepare an action plan to provide training in the dance form in all state high schools. He also said the government is planning to set up a Kuchipudi academy in Amaravati. 
 
 Later, the CM participated in the 191st birth anniversary celebrations of Jyotirao Phule at the AU convocation hall. He praised Phule for contributing extensively to the society. “The government is following Phule’s footsteps by implementing many schemes for the backward communities including the NTR higher education supporting scheme and NTR foreign education supporting scheme,” he added. 
 
 The CM further said he has included eight ministers from backward communities in his cabinet and also allotted Rs 10,000 crore through 37 departments under the BC Sub-Plan this fiscal. 
 
 He also offered a gold crown to Goddess Nookambica at Anakapalli and inaugurated a 150-bed mother and child care centre in the city.
