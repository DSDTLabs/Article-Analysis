COIMBATORE: A joint director of agriculture department and his assistant were caught red-handed taking a bribe of Rs 1.5 lakh from a farmer for converting his agricultural land into plots here on Tuesday.

 The arrested were identified as  , joint director (agriculture), and his assistant Murugan. The two fell into the trap set by the Directorate of Vigilance and Anti-Corruption (DVAC) sleuths based on a complaint lodged against them by  , a farmer from Puliyakulam.
 
 According to a DVAC sleuth, Ashok has 10 acres agricultural land on the outskirts of the city and he wanted to convert the same into plots for sale. “He approached the director of agriculture, Sukumar, at the office of the joint director (agriculture) on  , for getting no-objection certificate (NOC) to convert the land,” the officer said.
 
 Sukumar advised the farmer to meet his assistant for further procedures and Murugan demanded a bribe of Rs 2 lakh from Ashok on behalf of Sukumar to issue the NOC. The farmer finally agreed to pay Rs 1.5 lakh.
 
 Ashok then approached DVAC deputy superintendent of police  , who gave him chemical-coated currency notes, asking him to hand over the same to the officials.
 
 On Tuesday evening, Ashok went to the office of the joint director and met Murugan. Later, he met the joint director and handed him over Rs 1.5 lakh. While accepting the cash, DVAC sleuths caught Sukumar and Murugan and arrested them. Further investigation is on.
