CHANDIGARH: A 36-year-old man allegedly committed suicide in the servant quarters of the house of a former Armyman in Sector 2 in the early hours of Saturday, days after he was accused of stealing jewellery and cash from a house in Sector 11.

 Varinder Gill, a resident of Sector 11, had registered a case of   against Amarnath, the victim, and sacked him as his driver. Family members alleged Amarnath was falsely implicated. When he had gone to pick up his daughter from school on Friday, he overheard women of some students discussing about the theft case. After returning from school, Amarnath was upset and took the extreme step, his mother said.
 
 Around 5 am, the owner found Amarnath hanging from a ceiling fan in the backyard of the house. He informed Amarnath’s wife Saroj and police. Police took Amarnath to Government Multi-specialty Hospital, Sector 16, where doctors declared him brought dead. No suicide note was recovered from the spot, police said.
 
 The victim’s wife works as a domestic help in the house of the retired Armyman. He and his wife had been living in the house for last 20 years.
 
 Police registered a case and started investigation. Police said preliminary investigation revealed there was no foul play, but the exact cause of death would be known only after the post-mortem report.
 
 On Friday, a 24-year-old woman allegedly committed suicide by hanging herself from a tree in a Sector 9 house. Later, police arrested her husband on charges of dowry death.
