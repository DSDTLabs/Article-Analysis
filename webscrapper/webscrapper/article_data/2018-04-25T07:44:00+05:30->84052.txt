Chandigarh: The Panchkula CBI court on Tuesday adjourned the hearing of Rohtak’s Apna Ghar case to April 27 for announcing the quantum of sentence for all the nine convicts, including Jaswanti Devi. They were held guilty on various charges last week and were produced in the court on Tuesday.

 They all had got their statements recorded wherein they had requested the court for taking a lenient view. On April 18, the special CBI judge Jagdeep Singh had held guilty Jaswanti, owner of Apna Ghar shelter home in Rohtak. The judge had also held Jaswanti’s daughter Sushma alias Simmi, brother Jaswant Singh, son-in-law Jai Bhagwan, cousin Sheela and two other accused Veena and Satish, guilty.
 
 In August 2012, CBI had charged the accused under various sections of the IPC, including 313 (abortion carried out forcibly/without consent), 120-B (conspiracy), 323 (criminal assault), 374 (forcing a person to indulge in labour work unlawfully), 376 (rape) and 506 (criminal intimidation).
