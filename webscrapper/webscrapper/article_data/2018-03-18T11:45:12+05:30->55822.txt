MADURAI: As a first step towards cleaning up the  , the   is constructing an underground drainage system along the river banks to prevent sewage mixing with river water. The river is being beautified and developed under the smart city project.

 Vaigai River is usually dry for most of the months in the year, but sewage water is let into the river from wards in the city that border the   banks. Following many representations to stem the flow of sewage into the river the corporation started the work at a cost of Rs 30 crore under the Jawaharlal Nehru National Urban Renewal Mission (JNNURM).
 
 Some areas near the  , where the Lord Kallazhagar enters the river, has a steady flow of sewage into the river. Apart from these, sewage from overflowing drainages, which have been clogged, enters the river. The corporation is now in the process of revamping them with its funds. Corporation commissioner   said that they will construct more manholes on the stretch to prevent sewage overflowing into the river.
 
 In addition to it, areas which do not have a proper drainage system and drained its sewage directly into the river will get an underground drainage system under JNNURM. The sewage would be carried to the pumping station in Thathaneri, for which work has begun in   and Aruldosspuram. 
 
 The corporation is also in the process of constructing toilets in houses and public areas near the river banks to prevent open defecation. The Vaigai river banks will be developed and beautified at a cost of Rs 93 crore and the new drainages will help the corporation make headway in this regard.
 
 The corporation commissioner said that the river would be completely free of sewage once this project is completed.
