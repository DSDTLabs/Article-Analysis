NEW DELHI: On Wednesday, a day after government data showed that inflation for August hit a five-month high of 3.36 per cent, the equity markets opened on a flat note.

 30-share BSE   opened with a gain of 30.29 points on 32,188.95 and the broader 50-share NSE   started trading on 10,099.25, up by 6.20 points.
 
 The sedate sentiment in the markets came after two consecutive days of handsome gains which saw Sensex and Nifty go past the psychological marks of 32,000 and 10,000 respectively.
 
 The Indian rupee opened higher by 6 paise at 63.98 per dollar on Wednesday against the level of 64.04 on Tuesday.
 
 Reliance, TCS,   and   were the major gainers while Larsen & Toubro, ITC and Aurobindo Pharma lost the most.
