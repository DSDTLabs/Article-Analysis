JAIPUR: At 44.2 degrees Celsius, Monday was the hottest day in April that the city experienced over the last nine years. 

 This was four degrees above normal. The city last reported a maximum temperature higher than this for April in 2009, when it stood at 44.7 degrees Celsius.
 
 
  
 
 There was some relief in the evening though, after a scattered rainfall. Heat wave conditions further aggravated in the state on Monday when majority of the places witnessed maximum temperatures above normal. With a maximum temperature of 46.5 degree Celsius, Bundi was the hottest in the state. 
 
 Similar conditions prevailed at many places in Kota, Sawai Madhopur, Bundi, Bikaner, Sriganganagar and   districts.
 
 Attributing the continued rise in temperatures to active northerly winds,   office said that with the passing of western disturbances, the state would witness a slight dip in day and night temperatures in the coming days.
 
 Asked about the mean temperature in the month of April, an officer of the Jaipur met office said, "The average maximum temperature of April has been 40.0 degree Celsius."
 
 In the evening, the city experienced heavy dust storms followed by scattered rainfall, which brought a much-needed respite from the scorching heat. 
 
 The ongoing spell of heat wave has affected drinking water supply and resulted in power cuts in Jaipur and other parts of the state.
