HYDERABAD: Nearly 5,000 Jains in the city, under the aegis of Shri Vardhaman Sthanakvasi Jain Shravak Sangh, assembled at Ranigunj in Secunderabad to chant 'Navkar Manthra' one of the most   in Jainism over 36 lakh times.

 The event, titled 'Navkar Maha Manthra Jap' was part of aslew of peace-building initiatives that were kicked off earlier this week by the Shri Vardhaman Sthanakvasi Jain Shravak Sangh, Secunderabad, in collaboration with the Sri Jain Seva Sangh--an apex body of about 3 lakh Jains spread across Hyderabad and Secunderabad. 
 
 The japam was undertaken by Jains present, under the guidance of jain monk   Upadhayaya. “Darkness cannot drive out darkness; only light can do that. Hate cannot drive out hate; only love can do that.Peace is something we must all work for, every day . It begins with smile.  , but hug other religions too. Let us all give peace a chance. I urge apeople to take a step for peace. I appeal to people to be part of the peace building process. Our objective behind these initiatives is to sensitize people towards a culture of peace,“ said Praveen Rishiji Upadhyaya.
 
 Parasmal Dungarwal, president of Shri Vardhaman Sthankavasi Jain Shravak Sangh, Secunderabad, meanwhile, informed that the Jain Samaj will embark on another initiative aimed at   within the community . “1,008 Jains with their monks will go on an 8day fasting, between July 17 and 24, in Ranigunj.
 
 Along with them, 18 leprosy patients will also go on the fast.They will commence their fast in Chennai on July 17 and arrive in Hyderabad by flight on July 23 to join 1,008 Jains,“ said Dungarwal.
