HYDERABAD: With ATMs running dry and cash crunch in  , the Cash Management Committee led by   has decided to airlift currency to Hyderabad from bank chests in cash-surplus locations like Kochi, Chennai, Mumbai and Bhubaneswar.

 Official reports submitted to the Telangana government state that only 52% of 8500 ATMs of various banks across Telangana are functional, implying the remaining 48% are either shut or dry. 
  
 
 Speaking to TOI, SBI chief general manager (Hyderabad Circle)   said, “With RBI permission, we will airlift currency from our bank chests in cash-surplus states to Hyderabad on an emergency basis to deal with current situation.”
