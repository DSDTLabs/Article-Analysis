DEHRADUN: In a major crackdown against   ahead of assembly elections, the Uttarakhand Special Task Force (STF) on Friday seized 4kg charas, meant to be used in the assembly elections, worth Rs20lakh in international market from a peddler at Khatima area in Udham Singh Nagar district.

  The peddler has been identified as Sukhwinder Singh, resident of Banbasa in Champawat district. He was caught by STF and Khatima police while he was smuggling the contraband in his bike. The charas was hidden inside specially made space beneath the bike's seat for the purpose.
 
 Senior Superintendent of Police (SSP) STF, Renuka Devi, said that STF had received prior information regarding smuggling of large quantity charas at Khatima after which a team was formed to nab the culprits.
 
 The arrested drug smuggler has told STF that he had brought the charas from Nepal for supplying in Udham Singh Nagar for use in forthcoming assembly elections. A case has been registered in Khatima police station and he has been booked under section 8/20 of NDPS Act.
 
 According to STF officials, they are gathering more information from Sukhwinder about his network and people involved in the smuggling of drugs in the area.
 
 The Director General of Police (DGP) MA Ganapathy has announced a cash reward of Rs10000 for the STF team which nabbed the peddler and seized charas from his possession.
