GURUGRAM: Four people have been booked for the   of a 35-year-old man, Parvesh Bhati, who was killed on Saturday evening in front of the JBM factory in Mohammedpur  . The accused, who were in a pick-up van, made Bhati stop his car and shot at him, while his daughter and brother were in the car.

 Cops said the accused fired multiple shots at Bhati, of which two hit him directly, after which they fled the spot. Bhati’s brother took him to a private hospital where he was declared brought dead. An FIR was registered at Sector 37 police station, under sections 302 (murder) and 34 (common intent) of the IPC, and 25, 54 and 59 of the Arms Act. Bhati’s family has named a certain Giriraj, who is out on parole after being in jail for murdering Bhati’s uncle 10 years ago. The family told police that Giriraj could have held a grudge against the family.
 
 “The accused are absconding, and we are making all efforts to arrest them,” said Inspector Amit Kumar, SHO, Sector 37 police station.
