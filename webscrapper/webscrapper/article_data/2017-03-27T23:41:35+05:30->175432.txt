Hyderabad: Dubai-based low cost carrier (LCC)   on Monday announced that it has signed an agreement with IIIT-Hyderabad for setting up flydubai’s Aviation Co-Innovation Lab at the institute’s campus in Hyderabad.

 The agreement was signed by flydubai’s chief information officer (CIO) Ramesh Venkat and IIIT-Hyderabad director PJ Narayanan. As per the agreement, flydubai’s domain experts and engineers will work together with faculty members and researchers of IIIT-Hyderabad to provide deeptech inputs in the areas of data mining and extraction, natural language processing, machine learning and artificial intelligence. 
 
 flydubai’s Aviation Co-Innovation Lab will work on three levels of engagement. Firstly, IIIT-Hyderabad’s research centre will work on flydubai’s research challenges, secondly, flydubai’s engineers will work on aviation challenges with deeptech inputs from IIIT-Hyderabad’s research labs and finally, the lab will also work on prototyping new technologies from the start-up ecosystem.
 
 Commenting on the announcement, flydubai’s Venkat said, “We look forward to a fruitful association in incubating ideas and building full-fledged products.”
 
 flydubai currently operates five flights a week to Hyderabad from Dubai in addition to flights to seven other destinations in India, including Ahmedabad, Chennai, Delhi, Kochi, Lucknow, Mumbai and Thiruvananthapuram.
