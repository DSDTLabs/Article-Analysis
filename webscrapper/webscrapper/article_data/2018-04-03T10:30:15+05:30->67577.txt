MADURAI: Government doctors in Madurai went on a token strike on Monday, protesting against the   ( ).

 More than 200 doctors of the   (GRH) sat on a protest in front of the hospital premises, demanding that the NMC bill be withdrawn. State president of the  , Dr K Senthil, said that they would continue their protest if the bill was passed in the Parliament.
 
 Terming most of the clauses in NMC as draconian, they said that it should not be passed at any cost. The bill is proposed to replace the Medical Council of India (MCI) and will have a panel of five elected and 12 ex-officio members, including a government nominated chairman and members. The doctors are demanding the formation of a panel under the existing structure of the MCI that has only medical doctors as council members.
