LUCKNOW: A strong   lashed the city on Friday evening, giving people a break from high daytime temperatures. Weather office said the storm was a result of the local continuous high temperatures. 

 On Saturday, the city is likely to witness partly cloudy sky with a drop in daytime temperature. Met officials said the maximum and minimum temperatures are expected to be around 36 and 19 degrees Celsius, respectively.
 
 "The maximum temperature will go down by a degree due to Friday duststorm," said Met director  . He said the duststorm has given only temporary relief from hot weather and the temperatures will soar again as no rainfall is predicted in the coming days. 
 
 On Friday, the maximum temperature in the city was 37.2 degrees Celsius, two degrees above normal, while the minimum was 19.3 degrees Celsius.
 
 Lucknow has been witnessing daytime temperatures as high as 38 degrees Celsius for the past two days. The temperatures are relatively higher as compared to the last week. "Continuous high temperatures led to a rise in warm air from the earth's surface creating a vacuum in the lower atmosphere. A strong air circulation to fill the vacuum from all directions led to the duststorm," said Prof   of  's climate monitoring station. 
 
 Rain and thundershowers are expected at isolated places in east UP while the weather will be dry in the western part of the state.
