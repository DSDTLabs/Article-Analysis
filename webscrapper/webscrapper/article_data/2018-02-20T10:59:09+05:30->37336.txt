NEW DELHI: Union Public Service Commission (UPSC) has recently released an important notification regarding revision in the eligibility criteria for the upcoming   Civil Services Preliminary Examination 2018. 

 The UPSC had released the notification regarding the Indian Administrative Serivecs (IAS) examinations 2018 on February 7, 2018. 
 
 The candidates interested to appear for the IAS examination are advised to read all the details given in the notification before filling the online application forms. 
 
 Those candidates who have filled or are planning to fill the application form should also go through another notification released by the UPSC regarding changes in the relaxation in the upper age limit under the eligibility clause of the recruitment process. 
 
 The notification is available on the official website of UPSC - upsc.gov.in. As per the notification, the provisions for extending the upper age limit are to be read as up to a maximum of five years if a candidate had ordinarily been domiciled in the State of Jammu and Kashmir during the period from the 1st day of January 1980 to the 31st day of December 1989. 
 
 It is further notified that the candidates belonging to the Scheduled Castes (SC) and the Scheduled Tribes (ST) and the Other Backward Classes (OBC) are also covered under any other clauses of Rule 6(b) above, viz. those coming under the category of Ex-servicemen, persons domiciled in the State of J&K
 
 (a) blindness and low vision
 
 (b) deaf and hard of hearing
 
 (c) locomotor disability including cerebral palsy, leprosy cured, dwarfism, acid attack victims and muscular dystrophy
 
 (d) autism, intellectual disability, specific learning disability and mental illness; and
 
 (e) multiple disabilities from amongst persons under clauses (a) to (d) including deaf-blindness etc. will be eligible for grant of cumulative age-relaxation under both the categories.
