Pune: The 113th convocation ceremony of the Savitribai Phule Pune University will be held at 5pm on June 1 on the varsity campus. 

 Well-known diplomat Dnyaneshwar Mulay, who is currently serving as secretary in the ministry of external affairs (MEA), will deliver the convocation speech. Vice-chancellor Nitin Karmalkar will be the chief guest at the event. A total of 11 students will be awarded gold medals.
 
 According to the University Grants Commission (UGC) guidelines, a total of 7,242 students passing out in the 2016-17 academic year or earlier will be given degrees. 
 
 A total of 104 PhDs, 54 M Phils, 3,006 postgraduate degrees, 63 postgraduate diplomas, 3,994 graduate degrees and 21 diploma certificates will be awarded.
 
 SPPU was established in 1949 under the Poona University Act. It occupies an area of about 411 acres.
 
 The university houses more than 40 academic departments. It is popularly known as the ‘Oxford of the East’. 
 
 It has about 307 recognized research institutes and 612 affiliated colleges offering graduate and under-graduate courses.
