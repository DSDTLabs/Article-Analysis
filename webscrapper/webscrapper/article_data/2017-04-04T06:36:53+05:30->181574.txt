Mumbai: A Gujarat resident, Kamleshkumar Patel (31), has been deported on March 30 from Frankfurt airport to Mumbai for travelling on forged documents. 

 Patel managed to skip the immigration check at the city’s international airport and boarded the flight to Frankfurt on March 29. 
 
 He was supposed to fly to Canada from Germany. The Sahar police are investigating Patel’s case. A travel agent, who helped Patel in getting the fake passport and forged documents, is on the run.
 
 “Patel skipped the immigration check at the Mumbai airport. An inquiry is trying to ascertain how he managed to board the flight directly. We are trying to track down the agent who helped him get the forged passport,” said BT Mukhedkar, senior inspector of Sahar police station.
 
 Patel sold bicycles in Junagadh district. He has been booked under the Indian Penal Code sections 419 (cheating by impersonation), 420 (cheating), 465 (forgery), 468 (forgery for purpose of cheating). He has been remanded in police custody till April 5.
