PUNE: State health authorities on Wednesday confirmed that there was no negligence involved in the treatment of the woman who died of multi-organ failure at a city hospital on Monday afternoon.

 The woman’s relatives had alleged that the woman’s previous doctor had sought help from a priest who performed some rituals to save her life before she died at the Erandwane hospital’s intensive care unit (ICU). 
 
 The relatives had later lodged a complaint with the police under the provisions of the Maharashtra Prevention and Eradication of Human Sacrifice and other Inhuman, Evil and Aghori Practices and Black Magic Act. Police have booked the medical practitioner and the priest.
 
 “We had asked the health officials of   (PMC) to carry out investigation. The civic officials submitted a primary report that clearly states that the woman was a patient of a severe auto-immune disease. The cause of her death was sepsis with multi-organ failure. Besides, there was no negligence involved in her treatment,” said  , deputy director, state health department, Pune circle. 
 
 Vaishali Jadhav, assistant medical officer, PMC, said, “We have submitted our primary report. We have also told our health officials to visit the other hospital at Swargate and the surgeon from whom she had sought treatment before being admitted to the Erandwane-based hospital. Their report is awaited.”
 
 The woman suffered from systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE), also known as lupus. It is an autoimmune disease in which the body’s immune system attacks healthy tissues in the body.
