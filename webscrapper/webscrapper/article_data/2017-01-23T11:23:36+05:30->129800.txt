MUMBAI: The benchmark   fell by about 71 points to slip below the key 27,000-mark in the opening trade today, on sustained foreign fund outflows amid weak Asian cues as investors await US President  's economic plan details.

 The 30-share index was 70.92 points or 0.26 per cent lower at 26,963.58 in early trade with sectoral indices led by IT, healthcare, banking and capital goods, trading in the negative zone, falling by up to 0.78 per cent.
 
 The gauge had lost 274.10 points in the previous session on Friday.
 
 The National Stock Exchange   too dropped by 22.15 points, or 0.26 per cent, to 8,327.20.
 
 Laggards from the Sensex kitty included ICICI Bank,  , Bharti Airtel, L&T, Hindustan Unilever, Lupin and Asian Paints, dragged down the benchmark.
 
 Among other Asian markets, Hong Kong's Hang Seng was down 0.03 per cent and Japan's Nikkei fell 1.06 per cent in early deals. China's Shanghai Composite index too fell 0.47 per cent.
 
 The US   Industrial Average had ended 0.48 per cent higher in Friday's trade.
