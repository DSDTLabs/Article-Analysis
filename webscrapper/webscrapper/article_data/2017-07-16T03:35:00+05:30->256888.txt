Panaji: Union minister for road transport Nitin Gadkari on Saturday said that the National Highway Authority of India (NHAI) is doing the necessary investigation into the CDM Smith bribery case. Speaking on the offset of NDA’s presidential candidate Ram Nath Kovind’s visit to Goa, Gadkari said that the inquiry orders have already been issued. “The matter pertains to 2011, when the UPA was in power. Whatever is found will be revealed. I cannot do state specific investigations as that is under the jurisdiction of the state government,” he said.  The bribery case has brought in PWD ministers and secretaries during 2011 to 2015 in the state under scanner. The Aam Aadmi Party has demanded the anti-corruption bureau to file an FIR in the case.
