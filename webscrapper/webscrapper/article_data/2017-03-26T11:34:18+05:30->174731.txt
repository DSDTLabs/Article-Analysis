PATNA: Railways has decided to cancel at least 10 pairs of mail and express trains mostly originating from Bihar and   due to non-interlocking (NI) work to be carried out at   and Pachhim Koiripur in Lucknow division of   from March 30 to April 5. 

 According to Danapur PRO R K Singh, Howrah-Dehradun Upasana Express (12327/12328) will remain cancelled from March 31 to April 4 while Howrah-Haridwar Kumbha Express (12369/12370) will be cancelled from March 30 to April 2. The premier Rajgir-New Delhi Shramjeevi Express (12391/12392) will remain cancelled from March 31 to April 5, he said, adding that   (12331/12332) will also remain cancelled from March 31 to April 4.
 
 Similarly, Howrah-Amritsar Express (13049/13050) will be cancelled from March 30 to April 4 while   (13237/13239) will not run on March 31 and April 4 from Patna end. This train will be cancelled on April 1, 3 and 4 from Kota end, Singh said, adding that Patliputra-Chandigarh Express (13255/13256) will also remain cancelled on March 30, April 2 and 3 due to NI work in Lucknow division. 
 
 Raxaul-Delhi Sadbhawna Express (14015/14016) will not run between March 31 and April 2 and Harihar Express (14523/14524) originating from Muzaffarpur will remain cancelled from April 1 to 4, he said. Delhi-Malda Farakka Express will also remain cancelled from April 1 to April 3.
 
 Meet held: Railways organized a meeting with different railway union representatives under the participation of railway employees in management (PREM) scheme at the ECR headquarters at Hajipur on Friday. The railway union representatives discussed arrangements of safe drinking water and other amenities at stations during summer, sources said.
