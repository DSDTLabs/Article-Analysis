MUMBAI: HDFC Bank has reported a net profit of Rs 4,799 crore for the quarter ended March 2018, an increase of 20.3% over Rs 4,642 crore during the same period last year. 

 
 The bank’s net profit for year ended March 2018 was Rs 17,486 crore, up 20.2%, over Rs 14,549 crore in the year ended March 31, 2017. The bank’s board of directors recommended a dividend of Rs 13 per equity share, which has face value of Rs 2, for FY18, as against Rs 11 per share for the previous year. 
 
 The country’s most valuable bank, managed to increase its market share in both deposits as well as advances during the year as most other banks struggled with non-performing assets.
 
 Total deposits as of March 31, 2018 were Rs 788,771 crore, an increase of 22.5% over March 31, 2017. Total advances stood at Rs 658,333 crore, an increase of 18.7% over March 31, 2017.
