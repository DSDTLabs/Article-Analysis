PANAJI: A Goa   van rammed into a house and four parked two-wheelers in the wee hours of Wednesday near Azad Maidan in the capital city, after being hit by an unidentified speeding car from behind, police said. 

 According to   police, the driver of the police van, who the police has refused to identify, lost control under the impact and rammed into a house before bulldozing two scooters and two motorcycles parked on the side. 
 
 The impact of the   was so strong that the van continued to drag the two-wheelers for nearly 100 metres before coming to a halt.
 
 “We have registered a case of rash and negligent driving against an unidentified person. A part of the car’s number plate has been found near the accident site,” a police official said.
 
 Police said they managed to identify the vehicle’s owner based on the registration, but is yet to nab the accused. 
 
 “By tomorrow, we will be able to locate the vehicle,” an official said.
 
 A person living nearby, who rushed to the accident site, said the driver of the car had allegedly sped away after the accident. Police said nobody was injured.
