KOLKATA: The   ( ) is likely to have veteran actor Sabitri Chatterjee as the new chairperson.

 Chatterjee confirmed to TOI she had been spoken to on this matter but when contacted, Atri Bhattacharya, director general of KIFF, declined to comment. However, sources told TOI that the decision had already been taken.
 
 “Someone had got in touch with me regarding this,“ Chatterjee said. “I have to speak to the chief minister personally on this. I have some questions and need to first understand what is expected of me. I have always worked in the industry with my head held high and want to continue to function that way .“
 
 Director Goutam Ghose served as the chairperson for 22nd KIFF, and before him, actor Ranjit Mullick was the chairperson.
 
 Chatterjee is regarded as one of the finest actors of Bengali stage and cinema. In 2014, she was conferred Padma Shri and the year before that, she was bestowed Banga Bibhushan, Bengal government's highest civilian award. Currently working in two TV serials, Chatterjee has consented to work in two short films, where her co-actor will be Soumitra Chatterjee.
 
 Speaking about KIFF, Chatterjee said, “There was a time when I wouldn't even be invited to attend the festival. I had asked for invitation cards too, but they never reached me.“ But the scene seems to have changed over recent years. “I am being respected now. People call me up to invite me over. In fact, the authorities have even arranged for my conveyance so that I can attend the festival,“ she said.
 
 Chatterjee loves watching   films. “I used to catch morning shows of films by  . What a splendid performance she came up with,“ she said.
 
 The 76-year-old actor, who has a career spanning over 50 years, was last seen on the big screen in Nandita Roy and Shiboprosad Mukherjee's `Praktan'. Her other recent films included Sudipto Chattopadhyay's `Highway' and Srijit Mukherji's `Hemlock Society'.
