KOLHAPUR: The city police have registered a case against six persons for cheating a builder to the tune of Rs 9 lakh and harassing him.

 The case was registered following a complaint made by the builder on Saturday, police said.

 Both cheques were bounced following which the builder demanded the money but the suspects abused him and denied the payment.
 
 In September 2017, one of the suspects met Sakhalkar and demanded one flat from the building for a political party. When Sakhalkar did not pay attention to his demand, he brandished a revolver at Sakhalkar and threatened him stating that his demand should be accepted.
 
 It was alleged that between September and November 2017, the suspect, along with five others, have stolen steel, bricks and other material required for construction of building, all amounting to Rs 7.5 lakh from Sakhalkar's construction site at Devkar Panand area and caused to damage to building amounting to Rs. 4.5 lakh. Following this Sakhalkar lodged complaint with   police station.
 
 "Based on the complaint, we have registered a case against the suspects under several sections of IPC including section 420, 143, 147, 452, 323, 504, 506, 427 and section 379. We have also invoked section 3/25 and section 27 of Arms Act against the accused," said a police officer attached with Juna Rajwada police station.
 
 "The probe is going on and we will soon nab the accused after gathering constructive evidences against them," he said.
