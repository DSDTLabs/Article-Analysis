NEW DELHI: An Indian Engineering Services (IES) officer has been arrested for allegedly sexually assaulting and stalking a   student. 

 Police said that the accused works in the ministry of road transport and highways and has been sent to 14 days in judicial custody.
 
 In her complaint at   police station, the survivor said that the accused has been abusing her since 2012. Since the girl was a minor when the assault began,   has added sections of Protection of Children from Sexual Offences (POCSO) in the case. Police said that she has refused to undergo a medical examination for now.
 
 The survivor told the police on Monday that the accused was known to her family. He would visit her house and sexually assault her. He had also allegedly made a video of the assault and threatened to upload it on social networking sites if she did not concede to his demands.
 
 She said that the youth would sometimes come to   library with friends and on one occasion he even tried to attack her. She added that their families too had fallen out with each other due to this. The survivor told the police that he hacked her social media accounts as well.
 
 She had tried telling her roommate, friends and brother about the sexual abuse, but was scared of the social stigma. The student said that she had informed the JNU security to stop the youth from entering the campus, but he had still managed to reach her hostel.
 
 The accused used to boast about his position and even told her that nothing could harm him. The survivor stopped stepping out of her hostel in fear of the accused.
 
 A case under IPC sections 354 (assault or criminal force to women with intent to outrage her modesty), 354A (making sexually coloured remarks), 354D (stalking), 509 (word, gesture or act intended to insult the modesty of a women, 506 (criminal intimidation) and relevant sections of POCSO has been registered against the accused.
