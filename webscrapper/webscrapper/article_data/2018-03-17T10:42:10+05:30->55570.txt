KANPUR: Tension gripped   area of   in   on Friday after a statue of   was allegedly pulled down by some people. According to the police, the trouble mongers were those who were against holding ' '--to celebrate birth anniversary of BSP founder Kanshi Ram. 

 "As per the complainant, the lumpen elements did not spare even women, elderly and children, who were attending the discourse, and thrashed them badly. They threatened and abused supporters of Ambedkar and Kanshi Ram. They warned the locals not to hold such an event in future or else face dire consequences," said a senior police official. 
 
 Before the police could arrive at the spot, the miscreants managed to escape.
 
 "When the followers of Ambedkar and Kanshi Ram resisted the protest by some local youths, the latter attacked them and resorted to violence. They pulled down the statue of Ambedkar and raised anti-dalit slogans. This caused tension, and at that time there was no police in the area", said a local. 
 
 The police, after coming to know about the incident, rushed to the spot and tried to pacify the agitated mob but all in vain. Seeing the people seething in anger, more police force was sent which managed to nab one of the five accused.
 
 "Four accused are still on the run, and they will be arrested soon. An FIR has been registered against five people in this case", said DSP, Unnao, Swatantra Singh while talking to TOI.
