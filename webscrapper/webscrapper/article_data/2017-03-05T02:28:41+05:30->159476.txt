NOIDA:  , former diplomat and MP, known for his role in the   case and his opposition to the   Masjid demolition, died following prolonged illness at a Noida hospital on Saturday.

 Shahabuddin (82) was suffering from chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) and was admitted to the hospital on February 18, sources told TOI.
 
 “He was suffering from a terminal illness and died of COPD at 5.45am on Saturday,” a source said.
 
 Shahabuddin, who breathed his last in the presence of family members, was buried at the Punjpeeran Qabaristan in Delhi’s Nizamuddin around 1.30pm where Vice-President Hamid Ansari, former Haryana MLA Swami Agnivesh and MP Bajruddin Ajmal were among the main attendees.
 
 Navaid Hamid, president of the All India Muslim Majlis-e-Mushawarat (AIMMM) and an associate of  , informed well-wishers about his demise through a message posted on his Facebook wall.
 
 An officer of the Indian Foreign Service (IFS), Shahabuddin took voluntary retirement in 1978 and was subsequently nominated by the Janata Party to a Rajya Sabha seat in 1979. Later, from 1984-96 he represented the   constituency in Lok Sabha.
 
 Shahabuddin was best known for his role in galvanising Muslim leadership after the Babri Masjid demolition in 1992 and taking the issue to the streets. He took part in most of the meetings and discussions that had taken place between the Babri Masjid Action Committee/Muslim leadership and the Sangh Parivar.
 
 Netas from different political hues including BJP’s Subramanian Swamy and AIMIM’s Asaduddin Owaisi expressed their condolences.
