MANGALURU: The multi-state cooperative Central   and   (Campco) Limited has welcomed the notification (35/2015-2020), New Delhi dated January 17 issued by the Director-General of  , department of commerce, Union ministry of commerce and industry. The notification pertains to enhancement of minimum import price of areca nut to Rs 251/kg, as sought by domestic   and traders. 

 Influx of import of areca nut into domestic market was causing continuous destabilisation in the areca nut market causing great distress to domestic farmers produce. Campco along with other like-minded co-operatives had submitted various representations to union ministry of commerce outlining the market situation. Nirmala Sitharaman, union minister for commerce and industries understanding the plight of the farmers took up their cause with the DGFT.
 
 Based on her spontaneous recommendation, the DGFT has hiked the existing minimum import price of areca nut from current Rs 162/kg to Rs 251/kg. This move, S R Satishchandra, president, Campco said will go a long way in curbing the import of areca nut as well as boosting the morale of the domestic areca farmers as the arrival of new stock of the (areca nut) crop into the market has just commenced, he said thanking the minister for her affirmative intervention.
 
 Incidentally, a delegation from Campco had met Nirmala Sitharaman at Puttur on January 12 when she had come to take part in a function organized by Vivekananda Vidyavardhaka Sangha and reiterated their demand to hike the minimum import price. The minister had assured the delegation that the DGFT was seized of the matter and they could expect good tidings soon. As per this notification, areca nut imports below Rs 251/kg is banned and will prop up domestic rates.
