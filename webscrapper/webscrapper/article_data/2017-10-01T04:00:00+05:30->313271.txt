 According to details of the case, Dabhi, a resident of Khodiyarnagar near Magandada ni Wadi in Botad, owns a diamond polishing unit in Khodiyarnagar. Four years ago, a 12-year-old girl joined his unit as a diamond polisher. 
 
 “Dabhi, who is crippled in both legs and married, coaxed the girl and raped her on September 2, 2013. He also recorded a video of the act and uploaded on internet. Since 2013, he raped the girl several times and threatened to kill her if she did not comply with his wishes. Eventually, the girl gathered courage and approached the police with a complaint,” an official from Paliyad police station, where the case against Dabhi has been registered, said. 
 
 After arresting Dabhi, police booked him under Sections 376, 504 and 506 (2) of Indian Penal Code (IPC) and Section 3-4-8 of Protection of Children from Sexual Offences Act (Pocso Act) 2012. The case was transferred to the additional sessions court in Bhavnagar, which sent Dabhi to jail for 14 years. 
 
 Under Section-4 of Pocso Act 2012, Dabhi was sentenced to 14 years of rigorous imprisonment with penalty of Rs 20,000. Six months will be added to the sentence if he fails to pay the fine. Under Section 506 (2) of the IPC, he was sentenced to three years of jail with Rs 5,000 fine. And under Section 67 of the IT Act, he has been sentenced to three years of jail and Rs 75,000 fine. More six months in prison if he fails to pay the fine. All the sentences will run concurrently, the court said.
 
 The court also ordered that from fine amount of Rs 80,000, compensation should be given to the rape survivor.
