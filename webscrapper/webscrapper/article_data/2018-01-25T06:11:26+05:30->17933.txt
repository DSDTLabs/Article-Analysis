GHAZIABAD: Infrastructure projects worth Rs 222.39 crore, undertaken by various government agencies in the district, were unveiled on the occasion of UP Day on Wednesday. 

  MP V K Singh and mayor Asha Sharma dedicated a railway under bridge (RUB) near Sahibabad railway station and a project by Ghaziabad Municipal Corporation (GMC) to supply potable water to various areas of   areas of the city.
 
 Foundation stones of various other development projects, including those undertaken by UP Housing Board, UP Power Corporation Limited (UPPCL), Ghaziabad Development Authority (GDA) and GMC were also laid during the function. The function was held in Hindi Bhawan of Lohia Nagar and was also attended by senior administration and police officials.
 
 According to the officials, around 50 projects were unveiled in the function. These include 29 projects by GMC and six projects by GDA. 
 
 The total cost of projects by GMC is Rs 90 crore, GDA is worth Rs 80 crore, UPPCL is worth Rs 45 crore, UP Housing Board is worth Rs 2.39 crore, police department is worth Rs 2.5 crore and health department is worth Rs 2.5 crore.
 
 “The   project was completed by funds under the AMRUT Yojana. A shopping complex on Ramte Ram Road was also inaugurated. Foundation stones for a number of other works including roads, parks and tube wells were also laid,” municipal commissioner Chandra Prakash Singh told TOI.
 
 “Foundation stones were laid for installing solar power plants at the City Forest, sewage treatment plants (STPs), community halls and for a 45m road to be constructed near Morti in Raj Nagar Extension,” said GDA vice-chairperson Ritu Maheshwari. Foundation stones were also laid for the development of the Mohan Nagar T-point at a cost of Rs 1.25 crore and two U-turns near the Raj Nagar Extension crossing worth Rs 4.48 crore.
