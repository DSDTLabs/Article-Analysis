PATNA: At least 150 academics and scholars from across the globe will attend the final silver jubilee international conference of Asian Development Research Institute ( ) on ‘Bihar and Jharkhand: Shared history to shared vision’, in Patna from March 24 to 28. 

 President Pranab Mukherjee will open the event on March 24, which will also be attended by Bihar governor Ram nath Kovind, CM Nitish Kumar, deputy CM Tejashwi Prasad Yadav and education minister Ashok Choudhary, among others. 
 
 Jharkhand governor Droupadi Murmu will preside over the valedictory session on the concluding day, at which Jharkhand CM Raghubar Das will be the chief guest.
 
 Scholars from prestigious universities like Oxford, Cambridge, Yale, London School of Economics and National University of Singapore, among others, will deliver talks and present papers at the conference. They will include French economist J J Boillot, Prof Anand Yang of the University of Washington, Lord Meghnad Desai and Prof Christopher V Hill of the University of Colorado.
 
 Select academics from ten universities in Bihar and five universities in Jharkhand will share their views on the path that the sister states will traverse in the near future apart from speaking on their shared histories of development, agitation, decline and divergence.
 
 “Spread over five days, the conference will have 28 silver jubilee lectures and 20 technical sessions, apart from two special lectures,” ADRI’s member-secretary Shaibal Gupta told newsmen on Friday, adding the conference will be dedicated to the memory of eminent social scientist Late Arvind N Das, whose writings on Bihar inspire many scholars.
 
 The conference has been organised in association with Rashtriya Uchchatar Shiksha Abhiyan and supported by the higher education council of Bihar as well as that of Jharkhand.
