Panaji: From a registered unrecognized party,   Party (GFP) has now been recognized as a state party in Goa by the Election Commission of India (ECI). 

 The ECI granted the party this status on the basis of a review of its poll performance and after finding that it fulfilled the conditions laid down in para 6 A (iii) of the Election Symbols (Reservation & Allotment) Order, 1968.
 
 The condition stated that the party was required to win at least three percent of the total number of seats in the legislative assembly at the last general election or at least three seats in the assembly, whichever is more. The party has decided to reserve the coconut as its party symbol in the state.
