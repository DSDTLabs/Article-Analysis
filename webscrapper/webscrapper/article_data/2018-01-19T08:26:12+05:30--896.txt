 In this plant, modern, methodical composting is used as a multi-step, closely monitored process with measured inputs of water, air, and carbon- and nitrogen-rich materials.
 
 The decomposition process is aided by shredding the plant matter, adding water and ensuring proper aeration by regularly turning the mixture. The experts said this compost is rich in nutrients and can be used in gardens, landscaping, horticulture and agriculture. The compost itself is beneficial for the land in many ways, including as a soil conditioner, a fertilizer, addition of vital humus or humid and a natural pesticide for soil. In ecosystems, compost is useful for erosion control, land and stream reclamation, wetland construction and as landfill cover.
