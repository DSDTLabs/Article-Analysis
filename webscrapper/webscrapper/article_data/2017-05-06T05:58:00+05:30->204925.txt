Gurgaon: There is still no ign of Brownie, the missing pet mongrel from DLF Phase-2 who, it’s being alleged, was kidnapped, killed and then eaten by a group of youths. Now, another pet dog of the same breed as Brownie is also reported missing from DLF Phase-2.

 Motu, an Indian dog which belonged to Sonia Bhardwaj of DLF Phase-2’s Block N, went missing on April 11. After searching in vain, the owner lodged a complaint with the police on Thursday. An FIR has been registered against unidentified person(s) under Section 379 (theft) of IPC at DLF 2 police station.
 
 Brownie went missing on April 1. An NGO, People for Animals (PFA), filed a complaint on Tuesday, accusing a group of youths in Sikanderpur Ghosi village of stealing the pet dog and killing and eating it.Police had detained Philip, the prime suspect in the case. But during questioning, he claimed to have eaten dog meat, but not Brownie’s. 
 
 An FIR was registered on Tuesday against unidentified persons under sections 379 (theft) and 429 (mischief by killing or maiming cattle) of IPC and Section 11 of the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals Act at DLF-II police station on the complaint of Amit Chaudhary, president of PFA-Gurgaon, for killing and eating Brownie.
 
 The complainant claimed that he had submitted all evidence, including five video clips that showed a group of men, including Philip, eating dog meat.
