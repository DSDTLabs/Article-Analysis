PUNE: Maharashtra registered a pass percentage of 88.41 in the higher secondary certificate (HSC, std XII) board examinations. The results were declared on Wednesday by the Maharashtra state board of secondary and higher secondary education. State board chairman Shakuntala Kale announced the results at a news conference at the board headquarters in Pune on Wednesday morning.

 Science stream pass percentage was recorded at 95.85 while commerce recorded 89.50 arts recorded 78.96 and minimum competency vocational course recorded 82.18 pass percentage.
 
 
 
  
 
 
 Over 14 lakh students from Pune, Mumbai, Nagpur, Kolhapur, Konkan, Latur, Amravati, Nashik and Aurangabad divisions in the state. Girls outshined boys registering 92.36pass percentage while boys recorded 85.23 pass percentage.
 
 
 Among the nine divisions of the state board, Konkan division topped with 86.13 passing the exam followed by Kolhapur 87.44 division came last among all nine divisions with Nashik 86.13 pass percentage.
