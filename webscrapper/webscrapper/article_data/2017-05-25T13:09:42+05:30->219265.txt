AMARAVATI: To facilitate sharing of technology for advancements in healthcare, the state government has entered into a memorandum of understanding (MoU) with   and  . 

 Through this pact, the health, medical and family welfare department will work towards ensuring high-level hygiene at public hospitals in the state.
 
 As per the MoU exchanged between both the parties in the presence of chief minister N Chandrababu Naidu through a video link on Wednesday, health minister Kamineni Srinivas, who was also present at the meeting, explained that the hospital linen, through which chances of spread of infections is possible, will be changed every day.
 
 "Seven colours of linen have been designated for all the seven days of the week. So, the bed sheets, pillow covers and blankets will be changed every day and will be uniform across the state," he said. 
 
 "This ensures hygiene and the initiative will be implemented under the public private partnership (PPP) mode," he added.
 
 In the first phase, the colour-coded bed-sheets and blankets will be made available at 11 teaching hospitals and district hospitals.
 
 The health minister said the code will be implemented for 13,200 beds at various categories of hospitals in the state. In the second phase, 12,000 more beds will be given colour-coded linen, Kamineni said and added "We will provide 200 sheets and blankets for every 100 beds to ward off infections." 
 
 Kamineni explained that the new blankets and bed sheets will be washed in Sodium Hypochlorite and that the key performance indicators will be used to know how the hospitals are utilising the linen.
 
 Principal secretary of medial, health and family welfare department  , representatives from Bill and   Foundation and other officials were present.
