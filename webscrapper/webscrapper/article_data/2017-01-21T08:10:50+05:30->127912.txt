PUNE: The city unit of Bharatiya Janata Party ( ) will come out with its final draft of the election manifesto within a week.

 Besides ward-wise development plan, the draft will comprise inputs from public, sources from the party told TOI. “We have shared the draft with the citizens and have invited their views. We will take some time to fine tune the agenda, following which it will be published,” they said.
 
 When contacted, BJP city unit chief   said the party will announce the elease date of the manifesto in next couple of days. “We are planning to publish it either on January 25 or 27. We will check the status of work and announce the final date in a day or two,” he said.
 
 As per the plans of the party, discussions would be carried out with different groups of people, including lawyers, doctors, engineers, sports personalities, writers, Ganapati mandals, public transport commuters and PMC employees, before finalising the manifesto.
 
 In the draft manifesto, the party has stated that old public health projects such as setting up of  , Primary Health Centres, OPD and ICUs in PMC hospitals will be taken up. The party has also committed to improve the education board, creating gardens for senior citizens and training programmes for the youth.
 
 ‘Chai Pe Charcha,’ BJP’s pet initiative tried during the   elections, will also be repeated during the civic elections to woo voters.
 
 As many as 10 municipal corporations, including Pune, will go to polls on February 21 and the results will be declared on February 23. Over 26.43 lakh voters have been registered for this election, which will elect 162 corporators. The voting will be conducted in 41 panels across the city. Each panel will have voters’ population between 60,000 and 85,000.
