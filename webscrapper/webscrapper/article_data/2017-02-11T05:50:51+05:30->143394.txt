 "We make it clear that if there is no adequate parking space available, authorities shall forthwith shut down the restaurants/eateries; only when they provide parking space, would they be entitled to function," said the first bench of Chief Justice Sanjay Kishan Kaul and Justice M Sundar. 
 
 Concurring with the submissions of senior counsel A Sirajudeen, the bench slammed the restaurants/eateries for having tried to duck court orders by not even trying to put a valet parking system in place and identify parking space in their 'reasonable proximity.' 
 
 The closure order comes two years after proceedings triggered by a public interest litigation filed by Raghu of Vyasarpadi.
