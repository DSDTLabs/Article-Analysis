AHMEDABAD: In a fitting tribute, one of the city's prominent arterial roads, Sabarmati riverfront west bank, has been named after  , the late spiritual guru of Bochasanwasi Akshar Purushottam Swaminarayan Sanstha (BAPS) of Swaminarayan sect. 

 He passed away in August last year. The 11.5km long road will be called the Pramukh Swami Maharaj Marg. At an elaborate ceremony organized on the riverfront on Saturday to honour the late guru, among the many dignitaries present were the deputy chief minister Nitin Patel, Mahant Swami Maharaj, cabinet minister Bhupendrasinh Chudasama, MoS Home Pradipsinh Jadeja and political heads of the   (AMC).
 
 Towards the beginning, a ritual was performed by offering holy waters from 108 rivers of India into river Sabarmati at 4.30pm by Mahant Swami Maharaj and deputy CM Patel. Brahmavihari Swami praised Pramukh Swami's personality and his association with the   and the riverfront project, saying, "Pramukh Swami Maharaj blessed Sabarmati river by doing pujan of the waters of the Narmada river on August 28, 2002. He had also blessed the Riverfront Project in 2004 on its commencement". Thereafter, mayor Gautam Shah said, "Pramukh Swami Maharaj's monumental life and work has been an inspiration."
 
 Deputy CM Patel expressed his regards to Pramukh Swami Maharaj, saying, "By naming the Riverfront road as 'Pujya Pramukh Swami Maharaj Marg', it will forever be associated with not only Ahmedabad, Gujarat or India, but with the entire world because the international kite festival and many fairs are held on this place".
