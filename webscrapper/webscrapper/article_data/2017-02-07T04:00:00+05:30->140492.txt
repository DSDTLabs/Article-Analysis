Tube Investments of India (TII) has reported a 67% year-on-year (y-o-y) increase in net profit in the third quarter (Q3) of the current financial year on a standalone basis. The company’s board has declared an interim dividend of Rs. 1.25 per share (62.5%).

 Revenue for the quarter was higher by 8% on a y-o-y basis and stood at Rs. 1041 crore. “The domestic turnover of the company across sectors and the profits were adversely affected due to demonetisation announced in November,” said L Ramkumar, managing director, TII. “PAT (profit after taxes) was higher than last year due to savings in finance charges,” he said. Exports grew by 48% y-o-y over corresponding quarter in the previous year.
