Vadodara: With no signs of relief from the scorching heat, mercury in eight towns hovered around 42-43°C on Friday. The rising temperature also adversely affected people’s health with over 2,765 cases of heat-related emergencies reported in the state from May 1 to 4.

 According to India Meteorological Department (IMD), Kandla airport station was the hottest place in the state at 43.6°C. Gandhinagar followed at 43.2°C. In Vadodara, the maximum temperature shot up to 42.6 degrees Celsius, which was three degrees above normal. The minimum temperature too remained at 27.2 degrees Celsius, one notch above normal. Despite the cloudy weather, humidity level ranged around 50% on Friday.
 
 According to figures of Emergency Management and Research Institute (EMRI) 108, the state reported 2,765 cases of heat-related emergencies from May 1 to 4. In Vadodara, 152 cases of heat-related emergencies were reported during the same period. The complaints included abdominal pain, blood pressure problem, chest pain, bleeding from ear and nose, fainting, dehydration, unconsciousness, fall and vomiting. 
 
 On Friday, there were 26 heat related cases in city. According to IMD, no respite is likely from the scorching heat for at least one week.
