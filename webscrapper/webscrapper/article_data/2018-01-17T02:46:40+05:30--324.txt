NEW DELHI: A 40-year-old businessman was mowed down by an unknown vehicle in west Delhi’s Inderpuri on Tuesday morning.

  Police said that the man was dragged for several metres before he finally got stuck near a tree trunk. Parts of his bike and his helmet were found by the cops. A special team has been formed to scan   footage of the area to ascertain which vehicle hit the man.
 
 According to police, a passerby made a PCR call around 11.30am, informing about the incident. A team came to the spot and the man was taken to Ram Manohar Lohia Hospital, where he was declared brought dead.
 
 Police suspect that the man was hit by the   and then dragged for several meters, as a long trail of blood was found on the stretch.
 
 The victim, Nirmal Singh, lived in central Delhi’s Baljeet Nagar. His family members told police that he had left home just a few minutes before the accident took place and was heading to work.
 
 They told police that the man was always very particular about using helmets and always pointed out the importance of helmets to the commuters he saw travelling without it.
 
 Police said that they have registered a case and have formed a team to identify and nab the accused driver.
