LUCKNOW: The brief spell of rains and thundershowers on Monday brought down the day and night time temperatures in many parts of the state on Tuesday. 

 The minimum   in the city dropped from 28 degrees Celsius to 21.3 degrees Celsius, three degrees below normal, while the maximum temperature was 38.5 degrees Celsius. 
 
 Weather office said rains and thundershowers are expected in isolated places across the state, while thunderstorm may come in parts of east UP. Also, cloud cover is likely to prevail in most parts of the state for next 24 hours, but this may raise humidity. On Tuesday,   increased from 68% to 75% in the city.
 
 After Monday showers, temperatures also dropped in most parts of UP. In Allahabad and Sultanpur, maximum temperatures dropped to below 40 degrees Celsius and 36 degrees Celsius, respectively. 
 
 But   remained dry as heat wave conditions continued in the region. Jhansi and Banda in Bundelkhand recorded maximum temperatures of 44 and 43 degrees Celsius, respectively.
