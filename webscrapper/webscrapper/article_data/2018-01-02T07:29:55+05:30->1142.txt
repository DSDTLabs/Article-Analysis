BENGALURU: In order to cater to the needs of increasing number of commuters taking the Namma   services, the Bangalore Metro Rail Corporation Limited ( ) will introduce 10 additional trips on the Purple Line (Byappanahalli to  ) and three on the   (Nagasandra to  ) starting Tuesday. 

 In an official media communication, Mahendra Jain, managing director, BMRCL, said the frequency of the trains will also be increased and there will be trains at 3.5-minute gap during peak hours on the Purple Line. "On the Green Line, the frequency has been increased to six minutes during peak hours. The number of train services has been increased during non-peak hours too," he added.
 
  Metro commuters have been complaining about crammed rides in the trains, especially during peak hours, owing to a large number of people using the service. 
 
  On an average, around 2.5 to 3 lakh people use the Metro network every day.
