RANCHI: The   government has banned the Kerala-based Popular Front of India ( ) in the state saying “members of this organisation are influenced by the   terror group (IS)”.

 
 The ban comes after police claim that there were pronounced activities of the group in Pakur and   districts of the tribal-dominated Santhal Pargana region.
 
 The government issued a communique saying that in course of its probe, the home department was convinced that some members of PFI from south Indian states had visited Syria and are working for the IS.
