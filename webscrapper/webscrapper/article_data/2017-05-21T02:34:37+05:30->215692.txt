MUMBAI:The Nagpada police late on Friday filed a case against ex-MLA Ramesh Kadam for criminal intimidation and threatening a police officer. A   showing Kadam abusing on-duty assistant police inspector Manoj Pawar, went viral on Friday. 

 Kadam, who is lodged in Byculla Jail in a Rs 365-crore scam, was being taken to J J hospital for a medical check up around 10.45am on Thursday when he   Pawar who was escorting him out of the jail. 
 
 After the incident, Pawar approached the Nagpada police and informed them about the incident. No FIR or NC was registered on Thursday. Senior police officials ordered an inquiry. “Prima facie, the incident that police officer narrated in the police station, had happened. We have registered a case,” said Deven Bharti, joint commissioner of police (Law or Order). 
 
 The Nagpada police booked Kadam under sections 186 (Obstructing public servant in discharge of public functions), 189 (Threat of injury to public servant), 504 ( Intentional insult with intent to provoke breach of the peace) and 506II ( punishment for criminal intimidation) of the Indian Penal Code. 
 
 In the video, Kadam is seen abusing Pawar. 
 
 Kadam was arrested in August 2015 for his suspected role in a scam involving the Lokshahir Annabhau Sathe Development Corporation (LASDC), of which he was chairman then. A constable recorded the incident on his mobile.
