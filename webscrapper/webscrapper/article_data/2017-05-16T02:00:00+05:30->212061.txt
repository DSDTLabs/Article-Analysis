PATNA: A   court on Monday awarded 30 years’ rigorous imprisonment to automobile dealer Sudhir Kumar and fined him Rs 54.72 lakh for embezzlement of public money worth Rs 1.06 crore. 

 CBI counsel Akhtar Hussain Khan and Rajeev Nandan Singh said the judge Manoj Kumar Singh ordered confiscation of the convict’s property if the failed to pay the fine.
 
 According to the prosecution, a sum of Rs 1.06 crore was shown to have been disbursed as loan to 126 farmers. While many of these farmers did not exist in reality, documents for others were forged.
 
 Madhya Bihar Grameen Bank’s Ushari (Arwal) branch manager Bagish Nath Dubey transferred the amount to the bank account of Anand Auto’s proprietor Sudhir between August 2014 and December 2014. They were hands in glove with Sudhir, his brother Ajeet Kumar and state government’s revenue clerk Ran Vijay Kumar of Kaler circle, said the prosecution.
 
 The manager was dismissed from the bank while the clerk, suspended by the government, was still evading arrest.
 
 The CBI had lodged an FIR in connection with the fraud on May 26, 2015. CBI inspector Alay Vats submitted the charge-sheet in the case on April 12, 2016 against Bagish, Sudhir, Ajeet and Ran Vijay. The trial against Bagish is going on while Ajeet was arrested from Arwal six days back.
 
 Sudhir was awarded five years’ RI and fined Rs 10,000 each under Sections 120B (criminal conspiracy, read with sections 420, 467, 468, and 471), 420 (cheating and dishonestly inducing delivery of property), 468 (forgery for purpose of cheating) and 471 (using as genuine a forged document or electronic record) of the IPC.
 
 He was also awarded 10 years’ RI and fined Rs 54.32 lakh under Section 467 (forgery of valuable security). The court said all the sentences would run one after another.
