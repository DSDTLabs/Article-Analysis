LUCKNOW: Shahi Imam of the historic Teele Wali Masjid, Maulana Fazlur Rah man Waizi breathed his last on Mon day. He was 75 and battling with chronic liver disease for the past few months. Hundreds from all walks of life and across the state, including chief minister   reached to mourn the demise and pay tributes to the priest who was popular among all religious communities and hailed as a bridge between Shia and Sunni sects in the city.
Born in 1942, he rose to fame after leading the funeral prayer of Shia cleric Maulana Kalbe Abid in 1986. His two sons, Maulana Qari Syed Shah Fazlul Mannan Waizi, who is present Imam of Teele Wali Masjid and Syed Wasif Hasan Waizi said that on Sun day night, the maulana complained of stomach ache and was rushed to SGPGI but despite intensive care could not recover. At 12:15pm on Monday, he passed away .
 
 Maulana Fazlul Mannan told TOI that after completing his studies from Nadwatul Ulema university of Islamic studies in 1964, the maulana was appointed Imam of Teele Wali Masjid by his father Maulana Syed Waiz Hasan in 1964. He was also appointed Qazi of Koda Jahanabad, of Fathepur district. In 1995, he was declared Shahi Imam, the post he served on for 50 years.
 
 In 1980, when police opened fire on Muslims at Eidgah in Moradabad district, he led a march from Lucknow to Delhi. A relative of Shahi Imam, Maulana Mohammad Rafeeq Qasmi said, “He always took a stand for the rights of every human being.“
