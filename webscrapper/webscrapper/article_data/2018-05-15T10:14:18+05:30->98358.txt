JAIPUR: Udaipur and Jodhpur’s   laboratories got Rs 50 lakh each for construction work from the Centre under Strengthening of Food Testing System in the Country Including Provision of Mobile Food Testing Labs. Under the scheme, the two existing laboratories will get a total of Rs 10 crore in phases for construction and purchasing high-end equipment for testing food items. The upgrade and strengthening of food laboratories will expand the coverage of food items and liquids for testing. 

 The state government had sent the proposal to upgrade Jodhpur and Udaipur laboratories, which was deliberated by the   in its 4th meeting held on January 30. After the meeting, Food Safety and Standard Authority of India (FSSAI) had sent a team to assess the feasibility of upgrading Jodhpur or Udaipur Laboratory. 
 
 The release of grant was depending on the recommendations by the FSSAI team visited the state. But, now after the inspection, the FSSAI has released Rs 50 lakh each for the two food laboratories. “The release of Rs 50 lakh each for Udaipur and Jodhpur will spend on construction and civil work,” said Dr  , additional food commissioner. 
 
 Under the scheme, a total of 45 State   (at least one in each State/UT and two in larger states) to create a network of modern Food Testing Laboratory across the country at state level. 
 
 Now, in Udaipur and Jodhpur, three high-end equipment (Inductively Coupled Plasma Mass Spectrometer (ICP-MS), Gas Chromatograph Tandem Mass Spectrometer (GC-MS/MS) and Liquid Chromatograph Tandem Mass Spectrometer (LC-MS/MS) will be installed to test metals and other such substances, which are not good for health. 
 
 There are six food testing laboratories in the state in different districts including Jaipur, Ajmer, Udaipur, Jodhpur, Kota and Alwar. But, there are five more in the pipeline for Jalore, Churu, Bikaner, Banswara and Bharatpur.
