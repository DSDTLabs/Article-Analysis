PHAGWARA: Three cars were damaged in the main bazaar of Hadiabad locality of   by miscreants on Wednesday late night. Initially some residents alleged that it was a casteist incident as the youths who damaged the cars were Dalits. However, police said the miscreants were a mixed lot and that as least one among them was an upper caste youth.

 Sources said around 10 youth started damaging the parked cars and hurling abuses at a person from upper caste. However, Phagawara superintendent of police (SP)   said the miscreants damaged the cars without any provocation and violence was not on caste lines.
 
 Satnampura station house officer (SHO)   said one accused, Shivam alias Shibbu, has been identified and named in the FIR. “Preliminary inquiries reveal that even as some of them were from the Dalit community, youths from other communities were also among them. We have procured a CCTV camera footage to identify the accused,” he said.
 
 “Apparently the accused were drunk and damaged cars to create fear among people. Owners of three damaged cars have reported to the police,” he added.
 
  community leader  , too, denied that it was a caste-based incident as eyewitnesses revealed that the group was made of Dalit and upper caste youths. 
 
 Police have registered a case under sections 143 (whoever is a member of an unlawful assembly, shall be punished with imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to six months, or with fine, or with both), 147 (punishment for rioting), 427 (mischief causing damage to the amount of Rs 50), 290 (punishment for public nuisance in cases not otherwise provided for), 506 (criminal intimidation) and 510 (misconduct in public by a drunken person) of the Indian Penal Code.
