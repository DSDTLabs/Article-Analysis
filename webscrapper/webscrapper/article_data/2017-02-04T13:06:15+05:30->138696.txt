THIRUVANANTHAPURAM: More than 40% KSRTC bus services remained off road on Friday during the 24 hour-long token strike called by a section of the employees. The strike by the Kerala State Transport Employees Union, Transport Democratic Federation and Transport Employees Sangh was in protest against the delay in payment of salaries and pension. 

 Their other demands include withdrawal of  , reduction in tax charged on purchase of diesel, cancellation of permits of all parallel services and government takeover of the responsibility of the debt of the corporation.
 
 The striking unions claimed that only around 2,000 of the 5,069 services were operated. The most affected were the inter-district services.
 
 In some depots, the striking unions prevented other employees from operating services.
