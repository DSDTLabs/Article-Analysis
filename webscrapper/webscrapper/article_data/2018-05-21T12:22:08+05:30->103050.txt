NEW DELHI: Non-banking finance company (NBFC)   made a decent debut today on the bourses. The share of the company and got listed at Rs 600 on the National Stock Exchange, a 4.90 per cent premium to the issue price of Rs 572. On Bombay Stock Exchange too, the stock was listed at Rs 600. 

 
 At 12.02 pm, the scrip was trading at Rs 589, almost 2 per cent below the listing price. The broader market was flatter in comparison down 0.40 per cent.
 
 The company recently underwent an Initial Public Offering ( ) worth Rs 1,844 crore and was oversubscribed 6.80 times during May 9-11. 
 
 The IPO was of fresh issue of up to Rs 700 crore and an offer for sale of up to 2 crore equity shares (including anchor portion of 9,671,328 equity shares). Price band for the offer was fixed at Rs 570-572 per share.
 
 The category reserved for qualified institutional buyers (QIBs) was subscribed 16.08 times, non institutional investors 6.91 times and retail investors 1.48 times.
 
 JM Financial, Kotak Mahindra Capital Company, Morgan Stanley India, Motilal Oswal Investment Advisors and Nomura Financial Advisory and Securities (India) managed the IPO.
