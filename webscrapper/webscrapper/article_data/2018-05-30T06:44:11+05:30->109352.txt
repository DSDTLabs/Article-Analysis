 Jaipurites too had no respite as a maximum temperature of 44.9 degree Celsius was recorded in the city. Roads were empty in the afternoon as people confined themselves to their respective homes and offices. Earlier in the day a minimum temperature of 29.5 degree Celsius was recorded in the city. Continued rise in the mercury has also aggravated the drinking water crisis and introduced abrupt power cuts. “The power failure complaints are on the rise in the city for the past five days. On an average we are getting more than 100 calls every night,” said an executive of Jaipur Vidyut Vitaran Nigam Limited.
