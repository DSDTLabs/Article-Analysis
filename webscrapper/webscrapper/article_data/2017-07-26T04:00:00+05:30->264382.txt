Chennai: Private sector lender Lakshmi Vilas Bank on Tuesday said its profits for the April-June quarter rose 8.95% year-on-year at Rs 66.12 crore as provisions for bad debts rose steeply and so did bad loans or non-performing assets (NPAs).

 “We have had a satisfactory year,” said Parthasarathi Mukherjee, MD & CEO of the bank, adding that the slippages for the year was Rs 250 crore largely due to legacy accounts where the bank was a consortium lender.
 
 Gross advances witnessed a 15.13% YoY rise at Rs 23,236.19 crore while total deposits witnessed a 13.39%. Net interest income rose 22.62% at Rs217.73 crore. The bank’s provisions for bad debts rose steeply to Rs 82.60 crore for the quarter as against Rs 34.10 crore in the corresponding quarter last year. The Gross NPA witnessed a jump to 3.78% from 2.14% while Net NPA more than doubled from 1.3% to 2.84%. The bank’s capital adequacy ratio for the quarter was 11.67%. “We have enabling resolution to raise equity by issuing upto 5 crore equity shares. We will raise it at an appropriate time depending on the market situation,’ Mukherjee said.
 
 The stock price of the bank was down nearly 2% at Rs 195.10 at the BSE when the benchmark sensex ended the day flat.
