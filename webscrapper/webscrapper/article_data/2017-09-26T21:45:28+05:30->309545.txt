Nagpur: The stage is set for celebrations of 61st Dhammachakra Pravartan Diwas from September 27 to 30 at Deekshabhoomi. Addressing the media on Monday, secretary of Dr Babasaheb Ambedkar Smarak Samiti, Sadanand Fulzele said that this year the ritual of giving deeksha will be performed on September 28 from 9am onwards by Bhadant Arya Nagarjun Surei Sasai, the former president of Akhil Bhartiya Bhikkhu Mahasangha. Fulzele said that earlier in the day, guardian minister Chandrashekhar Bawankule had taken a meeting of all departments concerned and the district administration to ensure that the entire three day event, which sees lakhs of pilgrims coming to the city, remains peaceful and incident free. Dhamma Chakra Pravartan Diwas is observed every year on Dussehra to mark the anniversary of Dr Ambedkar embracing Buddha religion at this venue in 1956. The committee has made a special request to NMC and Zilla Parishad schools in the vicinity of Deekshabhoomi to accommodate the pilgrims in case of rains. A similar request has also been made to all the Buddha Vihars in city to provide accommodation. Since the celebrations involve repeating the sequence of events as they happened on October 14, 1956, Fulzele said that at on September 30, Bhadant Arya Nagarjun Surei Sasai will lead the Buddha Vandana at 9am and added that all the Buddha Vihar’s in the city should also do the same at the same time. Play ‘Bhima Tujhya Janam Muley’ will be held at 6pm at Deekshabhoomi on September 28. A cultural show will be presented by members of Dr Babasaheb Ambedkar Smarak Samiti Sakhi Manch on September 29. For the main event to be held on at 6pm, September 30, the chief guests will be chief minister Devendra Fadnavis, union ministers Nitin Gadkari, Radhamohan Singh, Thawarchand Ghelot, Ramdas Athavale, guardian minister Chandrashekhar Bawankule, state minister Rajkumar Badole and Mayor Nanda Jichkar.
