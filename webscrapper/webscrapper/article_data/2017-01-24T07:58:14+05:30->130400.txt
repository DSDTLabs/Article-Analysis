 Experts pointed this out after sewage samples drawn in   a few months ago showed the presence of Type II polio virus.
 
 India shifted to bivalent polio vaccine in April last year. While trivalent oral vaccine fights three types of polio viruses (Type 1, 2 and 3), the bivalent one is effective only against Type 1 and 3. "However, even after the shift, a section of doctors and pharmacists continued to use the trivalent vaccine, which resulted in Type 2 polio virus escaping into the municipal drains through human waste," the experts said.
