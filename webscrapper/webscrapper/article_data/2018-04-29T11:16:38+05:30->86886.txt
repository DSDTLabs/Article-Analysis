CHANDIGARH: The UT police on Saturday submitted chargesheet against Federation of Sectors Welfare Associations of Chandigarh( ) president B S Bittu and four others in an   case filed last year. Navdeep  , A S Bhatia, Pardeep Chopra and Sandeep Bhalla along with Bittu were booked on the basis of former president P C Sanghi’s complaint. The matter would come up for hearing on May 15, when the copy of the chargesheet would be given to them.

 
  
 
 The sections under which the chargesheet is filed are sections 147 (punishment for rioting), 149 (every member of unlawful assembly guilty of offence committed in prosecution of common object), 323 (punishment for voluntarily causing hurt), 341 (punishment for wrongful restraint) and 506 (criminal intimidation) of the Indian Penal Code (IPC).The matter dates back to November 4, 2017, when a day before election of the resident welfare association of Sector 21, 82-year-old P C Sanghi alleged that he was assaulted by five persons. Sanghi has been two-time Foswac president and former councillor as well. 
 
 As per Sanghi’s complaint, he along with Aman Mittal, had gone to the residence of  , who was the returning officer for RWA   of Sector 21. The five accused were already outside his house and started threatening him in case he entered the house. They then allegedly assaulted Sanghi outside Toor’s residence. He then lodged a complaint with police against all five of them. Sanghi even alleged that Bittu threatened him with a pistol. 
 
 Bittu and the four other accused were then booked under relevant sections of the IPC. Bittu had refuted all the allegations and said Sanghi wanted to extend the polling date of RWA elections and that’s why created all those drama.
