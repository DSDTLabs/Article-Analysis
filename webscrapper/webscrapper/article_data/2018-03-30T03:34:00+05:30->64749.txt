Panaji: Health minister Vishwajit Rane said the government will continue to charge outstation patients and added that he expects an annual “revenue of over Rs 12 crore” from these patients.

 Rane said GMC’s revenue collection for January-February was Rs 1.26 crore. “The hospital is therefore averaging around Rs 50 lakh a month without charging others,” he said. 
 
 Following Rane’s discussion with Maharashtra CM Devendra Fadnavis on Wednesday, the later resolved to empanel Goa Medical College in the health insurance scheme, Mahatma Jyotiba Phule Jan Aarogya Yojna (MJPJAY). 
 
 “Through this system of insurance scheme, we will recover all our money,” Rane said, adding that most of the cases that come from “there are of cardiac emergencies, accidents, etc, which were done free”. Now, the total amount will be reimbursed to us, Rane said.
