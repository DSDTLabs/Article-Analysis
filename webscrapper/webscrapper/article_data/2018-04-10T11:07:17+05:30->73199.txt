CHANDIGARH: The Union tourism ministry on Monday gave approval in principle to the detailed project reports (DPRs) worth Rs 99 crore prepared by the Punjab government for beautification of the historical cities and ensuring adequate facilities for the tourists as well as devotees. 

 Punjab tourism and cultural affairs minister  , on Monday, attended the meeting of mission directorate at New Delhi. The meeting was attended by the joint secretary of the union tourism ministry Suman Billa and the Punjab's tourism secretary Vikas Pratap.
 
 The Punjab government prepared the DPRs regarding the project and with the in-principle approval to these projects, the religious cities of Punjab namely, Anandpur Sahib, Fatehgarh Sahib and Chamkaur Sahib, Hussainiwala and   (connected with  ),   at Ferozepur, Jallianwala Bagh at Amritsar and Kalanaur (where Mughal Emperor Akbar was coronated) would undergo transformation.
