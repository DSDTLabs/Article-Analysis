COIMBATORE: Two stone plaques depicting trade relations between ancient Rome and the   have been retrieved from the  . Though the artefacts were excavated three decades ago but were later abandoned. 

 Environmentalists and conservationists, who conducted a cleaning drive at the lake recently, spotted the plaques. They informed a group of amateur archaeologists, who work towards identifying and preserving artefacts of historical significance. 
 
 The plaques have engravings depicting trade relations of the 13th century kingdom. "They have been engraved with an antennae sword and a camel, depicting the trade ties between Rome and the Kongu region," a member of the group, S Anand Kumar said. Traders from Rome sold camels and horses to Pandyas for jewellery, especially pearls from Gulf of Mannar.
