CHANDIGARH: Pakistan high commissioner to India Sohail Mahmood on Tuesday said that the challenge is to talk to each other rather than talk at each other for smooth flow of communications. 

 Mahmood urged to bring about paradigm shift in the framework of South Asian nations through researchers, academicians, and public intellectuals, saying they can be instrumental in the change of mindset to interact in a better way despite the "constraints". In a 70-minute meeting at  , Mahmood met vice-chancellor Prof Arun Kumar Grover and other professors. Earlier in the day, he met CMs of Punjab and Haryana.
 
 Without directly naming the Narendra Modi government, sources said he emphasized in the meeting that the situation has turned worse in the four years since dialogue has been frozen between the two countries.
