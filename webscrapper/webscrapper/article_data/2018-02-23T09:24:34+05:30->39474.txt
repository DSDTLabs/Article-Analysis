A virtual reality (VR) film on the   was unveiled at the   on Thursday.

 Made by   in partnership with   MGIEP (Mahatma Gandhi Institute of Education for Peace and Sustainable Development), the film gives a virtual tour of the architectural marvel on VR headsets through Samsung smartphones.
 
 President Ram Nath Kovind and chief minister Yogi Adityanath were shown the film along with Union finance minister Arun Jaitley, governor Ram Naik, industries minister Satish Mahana, among others.
 
 To be launched on Friday, the film will be available on YouTube and other social media platforms where anyone with a VR headset can watch it.
 
 The film will also be shown to students of Jawahar Navodaya Vidyalaya (JNV) schools where Samsung runs its ‘smart classes’. The company runs around 80 JNV schools in UP. The film will be provided to the Union tourism ministry and used under the Incredible India campaign. Eventually, tourists at the monument might be able to take the VR tour, too “Tourism and education are priorities for us. We are delighted that Samsung India has contributed on both fronts and has come forward with its VR film on the Taj Mahal,” said Yogi Adityanath.
 
 “We are happy to partner with UNESCO to offer experiential educational content with the effective use of technology. Today, there is a need for children to acquire deep understanding of our national heritage sites. We believe this partnership will help provide many more schoolchildren easy access to such content in an engaging and exciting format such as VR,” said Samsung India vice-president for CSR Deepak Bhardwaj.
