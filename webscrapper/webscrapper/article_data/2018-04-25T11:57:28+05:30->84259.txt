VELLORE: The Vellore fast track mahila court has sentenced a 23-year-old man to 10 years   for sexually assaulting a  . The court slapped a fine of Rs 6,000 on him.

 Judge P Mathusuthanan on Monday pronounced Annamalai guilty of sexually assaulting his neighbour’s five-year-old girl. 
 
 The incident took place on April 19, 2015 when the girl was playing alone. The youth, who was 20 years then, raped the girl after luring her to a defunct public toilet saying that he would buy her chocolate, according to public prosecutor S Priya Lakshmi.
 
 Following a complaint from the victim’s family member, the all-woman police in Vellore registered a case against him under various sections of the IPC and also invoked the Prevention of Children from Sexual Offences Act 2012.
 
 The judge awarded one-year rigorous imprisonment and Rs 1,000 under provision 363 (punishment for kidnap), two years RI and Rs 2,000 under Section 366 (kidnapping or inducing woman to compel her marriage) of the IPC. The judge awarded 10 years RI and imposed a fine of Rs 3,000 under Section 6 (punishment for aggravated sexual assault) read with 5 (m) (punishment for penetrative   on a child below 12 years) of the POCSO Act. The judge stated that the imprisonment would run concurrently.
 
 It was the third conviction in the last one week.
 
 On April 20, the judge awarded 10 years RI for a 27-year-old man for sexually assaulting an 11-year-old girl.
 
 On April 17, the court sentenced a 54-year-old man to 10 years rigorous imprisonment for sexually assaulting his relative’s six-year-old daughter.
