Mumbai: Ahead of BMC elections, resident associations in various parts of the city are trying out every trick in the book to get more of their members to come out and exercise their right to vote. 

 From Cuffe Parade to Khar, most of the advanced locality management (ALM) groups are taking steps to make it convenient for the elderly or infirm members to reach the polling booth on February 21. 
 
 Taking the case of plush Cuffe Parade, a 30-storey tower plans to set up a help desk on Tuesday to assist residents know their voting booths and read the voter slips. “The voter slip is known to give out details in Marathi and some of our members are not fluent in the language therefore in such cases members at the help desk will guide them,” said Parag Udani, chairman of Venus society in Cuffe Parade. The society also plans to ferry its senior citizens to the polling booth whenever required. 
 
 Members of the Cuffe Parade Residents Association have put up posters outside the elevators of buildings in their locality, requesting members to go out and cast their vote. In the 2012 elections, areas under the A ward recorded the lowest voter turnout of 34.15%.
 
 Some resident associations are keeping vehicles ready to ferry senior citizens. In the western suburbs, the Gulmohar Welfare Societies Association in Andheri and Khar Residents Association (KRA) will be getting vehicles stationed at places wherever required to ferry the elderly. 
 
 Some of the resident organizations recently organized a ‘meet your candidates’ interaction of their members some aspirants from their ward.
