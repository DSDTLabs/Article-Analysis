NAGPUR: The Nagpur bench of  , on Wednesday, directed the   (NMC) to punish its errant employees found guilty of misappropriation of funds and financial irregularities.

 A division bench comprising justices Bhushan Dharmadhikari and   sternly asked NMC commissioner Ashwin Mudgal to file civil or criminal suits against such employees and even lodge police complaints if needed.
 
 The court’s directives came while hearing a plea by Mukesh Shahu pointing out a string of irregularities by the civic officials while awarding tenders or contracts between years 2002 and 2011. Union secretary for rural development, state secretary for   (GAD), NMC commissioner, and superintendent of anti-corruption department, among others were made respondents.
 
 Citing audit reports of 2012-13, the petitioner stated that about 170 cases of misappropriation of funds were found while the civic body failed to recover Rs22 crore advance granted to its employees. Even the contractors were paid extra VAT of 4%, instead of 2%. 
 
 Shahu claimed that embezzlement in various projects under NMC’s transport, education and standing committees also came to fore in audit reports. He prayed for a departmental enquiry against the errant officials and punishment to guilty.
