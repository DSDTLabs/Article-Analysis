Madurai: Every resident in Madurai city generates 500 grams of garbage in a day on an average, said Madurai city corporation commissioner Aneesh Sekhar. He along with Madurai district collector K Veera Raghava Rao took part in the conclusion event of special sensititation program on the Swachh Bharat cleanliness ranking program being conducted throughout the country. The event took place in Arappalayam in the city on Thursday. Aneesh Sekhar said that keeping the city clean is a challenging work. Around 650 tonnes to 700 tonnes of garbage accumulates in the city every day. Segregation of the garbage into biodegradable and non-biodegradable is even more challenging. He urged the city residents to segregate the waste at the source level to help the corporation to manage the garbage in an effective way. He also said that the civic body is taking various steps to emerge as top city in the swachh Bharat ranking.  Residents of the locality were also handed over with two types of garbage bins in blue and green colours, to dispose the biodegradable and non-biodegradable separately. The collector urged the public to be ambassadors of cleanliness in making the city clean. People should cooperate in keeping the River Vaigai clean by preventing the garbage dumping at the river bank. He also urged them to stop defecating in the open places and use toilets.
