AMRITSAR: It was on February 20, 1920, that nearly 150 Sikhs had laid down their lives at the birthplace of the founder of Sikhism, Guru Nanak Dev, for reforms at gurdwaras spread across the country. Facing thousands of bullets fired from the guns of Pashtun mercenaries hired by the mahant who then controlled Nanakana Sahib, the Sikh jatha stood without fear.

 Ninety-seven years on, a three-day function was organized in the memory of those killed in the massacre, which has come to be known as Saka Nankana Sahib, at the revered shrine near Pakistan’s Lahore city on Tuesday.
 
 Pakistan   ( ) chairman Siddiq-ul-Farooq informed TOI on Tuesday that religious rituals were held at Nankana Sahib in the memory of a group of reformist Sikhs who were massacred at   on February 20, 1912.
 
 Sources informed that Pakistan Sikh Gurdwara Parbandhak Committee’s controversial general secretary Gopal Singh Chawla, a close confident of terror mastermind Hafiz Sayeed, also attended the religious function. Chawla had led anti-India protests at Nankana Sahib in recent past.
 
 In India, a function was held at Shaheed Sikh Missionary College, Amritsar, and was attended by Akal Takht former jathedar Joginder Singh Vedanti. Vedanti informed about the Saka Nanakan Sahib and hailed Lachman Singh Dharowali, one of the martyrs who had entered the shrine at 5.45 am but became prey to bullets which were sprayed form the rooftop of a guest house.
