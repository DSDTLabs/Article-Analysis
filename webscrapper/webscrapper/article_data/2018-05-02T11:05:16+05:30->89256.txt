HYDERABAD: Three hundred and fourteen social and tribal welfare students from   excelled in the joint entrance examination ( ) (mains), the results of which were declared on Monday. 

 As per a press release from Telangana Social Welfare Residential Educational Institutions Society ( ), 179 social and 135 tribal welfare residential students qualified for the advanced exam, to be held on May 20. "Among those who qualified are sons and daughters of daily wagers, farm labourers, masons, vegetable vendors and vulnerable primitive tribal communities," read the press release.
 
 Officials said this is the first time that 314 students qualified for JEE (advanced). "Credit goes to students and teachers for their hard work," said RS  , secretary, TSWREIS.
