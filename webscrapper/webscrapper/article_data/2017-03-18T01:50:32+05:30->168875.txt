 The estimated fiscal deficit is expected to be Rs 35,031 crore, lower than Rs 37,950 crore in 2015-16. Fiscal deficit is the difference between what the government spends and what it earns.
 
 “The fiscal deficit and debt figures cannot be seen in isolation. We are well within fiscal limits and in a better position than many other states,” said state finance minister  .
 
 However, with the state’s debt rising, interest payments are expected to rise by 7.6% over last year. Annual interest payments are expected to rise to 28,220 crore from 26,217 crore in 2015-16.
 
 Revenue expenditure of the state, which comprises salaries and wages, pension, and interest payments, has risen steadily over the years. From Rs 1,38,736 crore in 2012-13 it has soared to Rs 2,24,455 crore in a span of four years. Outgo on salary and wages now account for more than one-third of total revenue expenditure—in 2016-17, it is estimated to touch Rs 79,941 crore. Expenditure on pension is estimated at 24,370 crore, which is more than 10% of total revenue expenses. 
 
 Open market borrowings and securities from the National Small Savings fund contribute to 75% of the debt stock, according to the economic survey. 
 
 The internal debt of the state at Rs 2.89 lakh crore accounts for 81.3% of the state’s debt, according to the economic survey 2016-17.
