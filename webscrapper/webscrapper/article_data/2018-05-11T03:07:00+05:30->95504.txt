Goa’s Uvena Fernandes became the first Indian to get officiating assignments at two World Cups. Having officiated the final of the Fifa U-17 Women’s World Cup between Japan and DPR Korea as an assistant   in 2016, Uvena has now been selected to officiate at the Fifa U-20 Women’s World Cup to be held in France from August 5-24.

 Komaleeswaran Sankar is the only other referee to do duty in a World Cup in 2002.
 
 Early this year, Uvena was honoured with the Jivbadada Kerkar Award, Goa’s highest recognition for achievements in sports.
