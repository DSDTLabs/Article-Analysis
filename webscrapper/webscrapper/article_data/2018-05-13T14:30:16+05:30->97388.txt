BENGALURU: Voting in   held on Saturday has broken many past records. Office of the chief electoral officer   on Sunday released final figures on voter turnout at 72.13%.

 
 
  
  
 Dr BR Mamatha, Additonal chief electoral officer, Karnataka told TOI that the turnout is most likely highest ever voter turnout for the state since the 1952 assembly elections. “We will come out with the analysis of polls results over the years soon,” she added.
 
 
  
  
 Out of the past six assembly elections in the state, 2013 polls had recorded highest voter turnout of 71.45 %. While in 2008 and 2004, the figure was a low at 65%, in the elections of 1989 and 1994, the turnout was 69% each.
