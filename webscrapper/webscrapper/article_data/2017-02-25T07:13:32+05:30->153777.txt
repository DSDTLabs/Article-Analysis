SALEM: Burglars broke into two houses of an apartment located near the New Bus Stand here on Friday and decamped with gold ornaments weighing 125 sovereigns and Rs 15,000 cash.

 According to the  , burglars broke open the main door of K G Muthuraj’s house and made away with gold ornaments weighing 80 sovereigns.
 
 At the time of the incident, Muthuraj, 54, dean of a private engineering college in Ariyanoor, along with his 50-year-old wife Shaji, was attending to his son Praba Dev, 25, who was admitted to a private hospital in Nethimedu following an eye surgery.
 
 “The couple had been staying with their son in the hospital for the past two days,” the Alagapuram police said.
 
 The burglars also broke into Muthuraj’s neighbour V Shankar’s house and looted gold ornaments weighing 45 sovereigns and Rs15,000 cash. Both Shankar, an anesthesia doctor, and his wife Saranya, professor of a  , were at work when the incident occurred.
 
 Around 4pm, their maid J Seetha, 47, came to the apartment and found main doors of both the houses broken open. She immediately alerted both the house owners, who rushed to the spot. They subsequently alerted the Alagapuram police. The police reached the spot and initiated investigation.
 
 Forensic experts also visited the crime scene and lifted finger prints. The police have registered a case and further investigations are on.
