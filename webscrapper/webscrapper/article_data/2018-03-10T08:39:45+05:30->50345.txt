CHANDIGARH: Karnal police on Friday busted a racket of drug peddlers and recovered 480g high quality heroin having a worth of Rs 2 crore in international market from four persons. The arrested accused include: final year student of Ayurveda medicine course in a college of Rohtak Vijay Kumar, Kishore Kumar, Rajinder Singh and Kartar Singh.

 All the accused are in their early 20s. A case under the Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances (NDPS) Act has been registered against them at the   police station.
 
 Police said all four accused were travelling in a white colour hatchback coming from Delhi. Karnal superintendent of police (SP) Jashandeep Singh Randhawa said acting on a tip-off, a team of the crime investigation agency (CIA) staff and local patrolling team stopped them while they were crossing Karnal. The accused attempted to escape after their car was stopped at the check post.
 
 “Our team, however, overpowered them and during search it spotted the consignment of drugs. They confessed to have procured the consignment from New Delhi and were supposed to supply it in Ambala, Kurukshetra and nearby areas in Punjab,” said Randhawa.
 
 He said during investigations it was found that Vijay was heading the team and they would target the youngsters mainly students to supply narcotics. “Further investigations are in progress to ascertain the sources of procurement and other network of this gang,” the SP said.
