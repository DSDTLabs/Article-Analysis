BHAGALPUR: The   district administration has lodged an FIR in connection with alleged irregularities and procedural lapses in the allotment of shops/warehouses on the Kirshi Utpadan Bazar Samiti premises. 

 Former   (sadar) Kumar Anuj is among the five accused named in the FIR. The others are Mukesh Kumar Sinha and   of the SDO office, Bazar Samiti functionary Narayan Das and one Vivek alias Pintu Choudhary. 
 
 The FIR in the   police station was lodged on a complaint made by executive magistrate Rajeshwari Kumari Poddar on Tuesday evening. Poddar along with the complaint has attached the 20-page inquiry report of a five-member probe team, which was headed by DDC Amit Kumar. 
 
 According to sources, the FIR has been lodged under Sections 120B (criminal conspiracy), 406 (criminal breach of trust), 417 (cheating), 418 (cheating with knowledge that wrongful loss may ensue to person/s whose interest the offender is bound to protect) and 420 (cheating and dishonesty) of the IPC.
 
 The sources said the probe into the alleged irregularities and misappropriation of money besides bungling in allotment of shops and warehouses at the Bazar Samiti was initiated after RTI activist   made a complaint to the divisional commissioner. 
 
 Bhagalpur DM Adesh T said on Wednesday the probe committee found the former SDO guilty of criminal misconduct, procedural lapses and other irregularities. “We will act in the interest of the state as well as common people,” the DM said and added he was forwarding the probe report to the state government for further action.
 
 On the other hand, those denied shops and warehouses are planning to demonstrate before CM Nitish Kumar at Patna. 
 
 Anuj, who was transferred from Bhagalpur recently, could not be contacted for his comments.
