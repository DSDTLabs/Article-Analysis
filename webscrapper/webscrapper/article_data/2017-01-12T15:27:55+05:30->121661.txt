THIRUVANANTHAPURAM: The state government has decided to give a compensation of Rs 10 lakh to the family of Jishnu Pranoy, the student of Nehru Engineering College at Pampady in Thrissur who committed suicide allegedly following harassment by the college management. 

 This was announced by education minister C Raveendranath after the state cabinet meeting here on Wednesday.
 
 
 
 
 The government also decided to increase the sanctioning power of the working groups of each department which sanctions the plan fund. For the financial year 2017-18, these working groups will have the power to give administrative sanction up to Rs 10 crore from the plan fund for various projects. This was Rs 5 crore till now.
 
 The government decided to create new 400 posts of police constable drivers in the home department. A new lecture post has been sanctioned for the mathematics department at the   and two lecturer posts in the zoology department of   at Pathanmathitta.
 
 To commemorate the 60th anniversary of the first elected government in the state, the state cabinet decided to constitute a committee to plan a detailed programme for celebrating it. Tourism minister A C Moitheen will be the convener of the committee in which ministers A K Balan, C Raveendranath, Ramachandran Kadanappally, A K Sassendran, Mathew T Thomas and E Chandrashekaran will be the members.
 
 The 60th anniversary of EMS Namboodiripad government falls on April 5, 2017 and the state government plans to celebrate the event in a big way.
