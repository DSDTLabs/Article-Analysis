About eight relief camps were opened in Kollam, Kochi,   and Thiruvananthapuram for the temporary rehabilitation of the affected. "Of the eight relief camps opened in the state, four camps are functioning in Thiruvananthapuram where 180 people are accommodated. One camp is opened in Kollam (6) and one camp in Alappuzha (14). Two relief camps in Ernakulum were closed on Monday evening after the people went back to their homes when the water receded," said officials of the state disaster control room. 

 Now, the weathermen and disaster management experts have warned that the sea would be rough near shores and in low-lying areas in parts of Kerala coast till 11.30 pm on Tuesday because of high swell waves.
 
 Kerala State Disaster Management Authority (KSDMA) and Indian National Centre for Ocean Information Services (INCOIS) have also warned that sea could be rough near the shore. 
 
 Indian meteorological department centre director K Santosh said thunderstorms accompanied with gusty winds were likely in Kollam, Kottayam, Idukki, Malappuram,   and Thrissur districts. Rains or thundershowers were likely in parts of the state till April 27. Fishermen had been warned from venturing into the sea, he said.
 
 In the last 24 hours, Munnar in Idukki, Kuppady in Wayanad and Konni in Pathanamthitta district have recorded 1 cm rain each.
