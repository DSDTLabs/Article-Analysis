Mumbai: Chief minister Devendra Fadnavis on Thursday appointed Sampada Mehta, an IAS officer of the 2008 batch, as   city   in place of Ashwini Joshi, who took over as the excise commissioner.

 The state government also appointed retired bureaucrat Gautam Chaterjee as the first chairman of the Maharashtra Real Estate Regulatory Authority. Another bureaucrat, Vijay Satbir Singh, and district judge B D Kapadnis will be the members of the authority. 
 
 An IAS officer of 1982 batch, Chaterjee retired as additional chief secretary (transport), while Singh, IAS officer of 1984 batch, is serving as additional chief secretary (public health). Singh is due to retire on June 30.
