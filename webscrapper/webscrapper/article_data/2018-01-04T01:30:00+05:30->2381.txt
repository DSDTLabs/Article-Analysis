Hyderabad: The Telangana government is planning to set up an Information Technology (IT) cluster at Budwel near Rajendranagar, IT minister K Taraka Rama Rao announced on Wednesday. 

 Nearly 30 IT firms, including some multi-national companies, are keen on setting up shop there and are expected to generate 1.25 lakh jobs in the next five years, the minister said.
 
 K T Rama Rao along with officials of Telangana State Industrial Infrastructure Corporation (TSIIC) as well as the IT and revenue departments inspected the land for the proposed IT cluster at Budwel and Rajendranagar on Wednesday. IT and industries principal secretary Jayesh Ranjan was also present on the occasion.
 
 He said chief minister K Chandrasekhar Rao will be laying the foundation stone for the IT cluster soon and the state government will soon be entering into Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) with the firms that have evinced interest. The IT minister directed the TSIIC officials to complete the infrastructure facilities and amenities at the IT cluster. “Some institutions located in Rajendranagar have agreed to hand over unutilized land to the state government and the mutation process would also be completed for the land,” KTR said.
 
 The minister said a meeting would also be convened by the chief secretary with officials of IT, TSIIC as well as collectors of Hyderabad and Ranga Reddy districts on land alienation issues.
