Gurgaon: Three men were arrested on Tuesday for allegedly trasferring Rs 62, 400 from the bank account of a Gurgaon resident by cloning his ATM card. They were produced in a city court and sent to two days’ police remand on Wednesday.

 Police said the accused are involved in  . They have been identified as Dhanraj, a resident of Chavdi village in Alawar district, Joginder, a resident of Jaipur in Rajasthan and Abhijeet, who is originally from Mumbai.
 
 According to the police, Shubham Dhingra, a resident of Sector 23, lodged a complaint at Palam Vihar police station on March 16 saying that unknown persons had transferred Rs 62,400 from his ICICI bank account. After investigation, the accused were arrested on Tuesday around 7.15pm in a raid near Atul Kataria Chowk.
 
 6 ATM cards in different names, suspected to be cloned from originals, have been recovered from their possession. 
 
 “The case is being investigated by the cyber crime cell. The accused were involved in card cloning are being interrogated,” said Sumit Kuhar, DCP (crime), Gurgaon.
