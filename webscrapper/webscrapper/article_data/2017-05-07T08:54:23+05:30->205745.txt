 The decision to conduct the special adalat was taken based on complaints about the delay in getting their pensions. 
 
 Applications of beneficiaries who have not been getting pensions since a year will be taken up at the adalat. 
 
 The local self-government department has directed all the self-government bodies to conduct adalat at the earliest to resolve grievances of various pensioners availing social welfare pensions in its limit.
 
 According to the corporation office, a total of 52,000 beneficiaries have been levying pensions under various schemes. 
 
 A few months ago, councillors had complained that around 10,000 applicants were not getting pensions after they failed to submit self-affidavit disclosing their income owing to lack of knowledge about it. 
 
 Anitha Rajan, social welfare standing committee chairperson, said that the adalat would be conducted exclusively to clear pending files of various social welfare pensions.
 
 “We have been receiving numerous complaints from pensioners who have not been getting monthly pensions since a year. The civic body will receive the applications till May 11,’’ she said.
 
 The civic body would extend the day of the adalat on the basis of number of complaints, she added.
 
 The corporation office has already started getting petitions for the adalat. Those who would like to participate in the adalat need to submit an application form which can be obtained from the corporation office along with their aadhaar number,’’ she said.
