NEW DELHI: All NGOs who had applied for renewal of foreign funding licence but not uploaded their annual returns for the period 2010-11 to 2014-15, now have a month to do so.

 The government on Tuesday announced that all associations/organisations which have applied for renewal of their registration under the Foreign Contribution (Regulation) Act, 2010 ( ) but not uploaded their annual returns from financial year 2010-11 to 2014-15, may upload the same, along with the requisite documents, within a period of 30 days, starting from May 15, 2017 to June 14, 2017.
 
 “No compounding fee will be imposed on them for late   during this period,” the   said in a press release.
 
 The exemption is a onetime measure and available to those associations who upload their missing annual returns from FY 2010-11 to FY 2014-15 within this period. 
 
 The renewal of registration under FCRA cannot be granted unless the annual returns are uploaded by the organisation.
