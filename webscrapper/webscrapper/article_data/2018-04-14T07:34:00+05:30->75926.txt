AMRITSAR: Homage was paid to the martyrs of the Indian freedom struggle on the 99th anniversary the   massacre here on Friday. A large number of people had arrived at Jallianwala Bagh, many from various parts of the country to remember and pay respect to the freedom fighters shot dead by the British government on April 13, 1919.

 The function organized by Jallianwala Bagh Memorial Trust was attended by family members of those killed in the incident besides leaders of different political parties and representatives of various NGOs.
 
 To mark the occasion, the Artists Welfare Society staged a show detailing about the atrocities committed during the British regime and how Shaheed Udham Singh shot dead Michael O Dwyer, Lieutenant Governor of Punjab at the time of Jallianwala Bagh massacre, at Caxton Hall, London on March 13, 1940.
