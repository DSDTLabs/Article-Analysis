SURAT:   will be launching its inaugural flight service connecting Surat and Bhavnagar under UDAN (regional connectivity scheme) from April 16.

 Air Odisha, a joint venture of Gujarat State Export Corporation Limited (GSEC), Monarch and Air Deccan from Odisha, has started online bookings for Surat and Bhavnagar services. 
 
 Earlier, the airline was supposed to launch the interstate air connectivity in January, but due to some technical issues and permissions, it got delayed. The airline has received permission from Directorate General of Civil Aviation (DGCA) and   (AAI) for operating services on   route, while the other routes like Ahmedabad, Diu, Jamnagar and   will be added on later dates. 
 
 As per the plan, Air Odisha is looking at connecting Ahmedabad with Bhavnagar, Surat, Mundra, Jamnagar, Mithapur (Dwarka) and Diu. All these destinations will have return direct flights to and from Ahmedabad. 
 
 As per the operating schedule, Air Odisha will launch its flight services from Surat to Bhavnagar on April 16. The airline will be using 19-seater Beachcraft aircraft on Surat-Bhavnagar route. 
 
 The aircraft will depart from Bhavnagar at 8am to reach Surat at 8:35am and in return journey, the flight will depart from Surat at 8:55am to reach Bhavnagar at 9:25am. 
 
 Sources said Air Odisha has been awarded 50 routes in different states in the country under the UDAN scheme. The airline has single largest share of routes under the scheme. 
 
 “It is a proud moment for the people of Surat as low cost flight services under Rs2,000 is going to start on Surat-Bhavnagar route by Air Odisha. At present, a private airliner is operating nine-seater aircraft on Surat-Bhavnagar route and charging anything above Rs3,500,” said Rasik Patel, a diamond trader, who travels to Bhavnagar thrice a week.
