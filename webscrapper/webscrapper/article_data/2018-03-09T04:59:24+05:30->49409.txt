 Replying to another question Singh said that the government has approved setting up of nine research parks one each at IIT Madras, IIT Kharagpur, IIT Bombay, IIT Gandhinagar, IIT Delhi, IIT Guwahati, IIT Kanpur, IIT Hyderabad, and IISc Bangalore. 
 
 IIT Madras research park, which is fully functional with 43 R&D clients, four incubators, 55 startups, and five centres of excellence was established at a total cost of Rs 447.66 crore, which includes grants-in-aid of Rs 137 crore from the government. 
 
 The research parks at IIT Kharagpur and IIT Bombay are under construction at a cost of Rs 100 crore each, out of which IIT Kharagpur has got its full allocation and IIT Bombay has received Rs 33 crore so far. 
 
 IIT Gandhinagar Research Park currently under construction has been approved at a cost of Rs 90 crore with full funding from the department of science and technology, which has released a sum of Rs 40 crore as on date.
