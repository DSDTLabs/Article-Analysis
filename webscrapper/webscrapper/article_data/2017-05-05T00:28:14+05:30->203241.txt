Pimpri Chinchwad: To check allegations of contractors repainting and reusing old tree guards, the Pimpri Chinchwad Municipal Corporation (PCMC) has decided to change the   of the tree guards every year.

 The standing committee on Wednesday approved a draft annual budget of tree conservation with a total outlay of Rs 17.61 crore.
 
 PCMC municipal commissioner Shravan Hardikar said, “Changing the shape of the tree guards every year is an easy and effective solution to this problem. The shape of the   will tell us the year in which it was made.”
 
 Seema Savale, chairperson, standing committee, PCMC said, “There are complaints from corporators and citizens that the contractors reuse the old tree guards after repainting and repairing them. The civic body is cheated in this way. So Hardikar’s suggestion is an easy solution to stop such malpractices and save the taxpayers’ money.”
 
 Savale further said, “The civic body will plant 6,000 big trees at a cost of Rs 75 lakh. We have directed the garden department to take help from Ganesh mandals, schools and women self-help group to create public awareness about the need for tree plantation.”
 
 “The garden department will spend Rs 15 crore on administrative expenditure and on the hired contractors, which will leave around Rs 2 to 2.5 crore for the department to conduct new development works and maintain existing infrastructure,” Savale added.
 
 Suresh Salunke, chief garden superintendent, PCMC said, “The major highlight of the annual budget is the planting of 50,000 trees in the city.”
