 The ED’s request is based on a non-bailable warrant issued against the two accused who failed to appear before the agency even after repeated summons issued to them. The ED had registered a money laundering case against them on February 14. The ED’s request will be processed through CBI to be taken up with the Interpol headquarters in Lyon, France.
 
 Once an RCN is issued, the Interpol seeks to arrest the person concerned in any part of the world and notifies the country of origin to take his or her custody for further action. Earlier this month, a special Mumbai court had issued non-bailable warrants against Nirav Modi and Mehul Choksi on the request of the ED.
 
 Responding to ED summons, both Modi and Choksi had expressed their inability to depose citing business engagements and the fact that their passports had been revoked.
 
 The ED has attached assets worth Rs 1,200 crore of Mehul Choksi and questioned the   CEO relating to irregularities in issuing him lettes of undertaking (LoUs).
 
 The agency has seized assets worth over Rs 5,800 crore belonging to Nirav Modi and his associated companies. It has also attached several of his properties, considered to be worth over Rs 1,000 crore. Modi’s wife Ami and his brother Neeshal are the other accused in the PNB case which has been registered under the Prevention of Money Laundering Act.
