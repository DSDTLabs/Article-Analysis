JAIPUR: In a big boost to the  , the   ( ) has given its approval to the ambitious Rs 30,000-crore   (ERCP).

 The project proposes to bring Chambal River water to 13 parched districts of east Rajasthan.
 
 The project, envisaged on the lines of Indira Gandhi Canal in west Rajasthan, proposes to makes use of the excess water from Chambal River which is at present being wasted.
 
 “We have received the green signal from the CWC for the ERCP. The detailed project report (DPR) of the project will be completed in the coming weeks. We will send a proposal to the Union government to declare it a project of national importance,“ said Sriram Vedire, chairperson, Rajasthan River Basin and Water Resources Planning Authority .
 
 He said the Centre's approval would help to take care of a major part of the project's funding. Vedire said the mega project, announced by chief minister   in the 2017-18 Budget, aims to make available Chambal water for people in 13 districts to meet their drinking and irrigation requirements.
 
 This is a permanent solution to the water problem in eastern Rajasthan, he said.
 
 The 13 districts include Bundi, Kota, Bharatpur, Dholpur, Karauli, Sawai Madhopur, Baran, Jhalawar, Jaipur, Tonk, Dausa, Sikar and Alwar. “The project will help to irrigate nearly 10 lakh acres of land, giving a boost to agriculture in the state,“ the official said.
 
 A radar feasibility survey of the project has already been carried out, he said.
 
 The official said the state has cleared the first step by getting hydrology clearance from the CWC.
 
 Vedire said the project, which would be implemented in three phases, would also boost the Delhi-Mumbai Industrial Corridor (DMIC) in Alwar district and generate employment for youths of the state.
 
 The first phase of the project will extend from Galwa Dam to Dholpur, the second from Galwa to Bisalpur-Isarda and the third from Galwa to Alwar.
