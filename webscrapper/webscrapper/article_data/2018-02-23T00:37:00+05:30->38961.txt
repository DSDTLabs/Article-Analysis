Madurai: A minor fire broke out at the warehouse of the Tamil Nadu Civil Supplies Corporation at Kappalur on the city outskirts on Thursday evening. The fire was first reported to the Thirumangalam fire station at 4.15pm. Sensing that it could quickly spread to other areas, the Madurai fire station was alerted for support. Vehicles from these stations rushed to Kappalur and the city corporation too rushed its water tanker trucks to the spot. “We received the call at 4.15 and reached the spot quickly. The water tankers provided by the city corporation was of great help,” said a fire department official. Sixteen fire and rescue department personnel led by Thirumangalam station officer, Rajendran fought the fire and doused it after one hour. Quickly action from the department saved the powerhouse and warehouse in the proximity. Preliminary investigation found that discarded cigarette butts could have triggered the fire. Further investigation is underway by police department, sources said.  Madurai collector, K Veera Raghava Rao inspected the mishap site later in the evening. He said that the fire which occurred in a bus stand, spread to warehouse premises, consuming trash materials like plastic sacks and four trees were also damaged. There were, however, no injuries or loss of property, collector said. He urged the public to keep their premises tidier considering the arid climate that could contribute to sudden fire. He also appreciated the efforts of the fire and rescue, revenue and civil supplies workers, who acted swiftly and put out the fire.
