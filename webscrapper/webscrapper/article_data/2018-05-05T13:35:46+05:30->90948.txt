Indore: Indore cell of state cyber police on Friday arrested another   involved in operating international child pornography  .

 The research wing of the state cyber police had tracked down a WhatsApp group which was known for circulating child pornographical content.
 
 People from 28 countries were added in both the groups. Most of the people in both the WhatsApp groups were from India – 131 in the first group and 74 in the second group whereas 78 people were from Pakistan. The administrator of the group was from Kuwait, said police.
 
 The group members were tracked down and four people were found to be from Madhya Pradesh and Chhattisgarh. Three of them were from Dhar whereas one from Chhattisgarh. The three accused were arrested and taken in for questioning on April 17.
 
 “During sustained interrogation, information about one more 19-year-old accused was received following which he was arrested on Friday. Rajeev Kumar Sahu, a resident of Jabalpur, accepted that he was involved in sending and receiving   on the group,” said SP   while speaking to media persons on Friday. 
 
 He added that the group had obscene videos of children participating or being forced into sexual acts.
 
 Under Section 67 B of the IT Act, any person who is caught publishing, transmitting, creating texts, collecting, seeking, browsing, downloading, advertising, exchanging or distributing any material in any electronic form depicting children in obscene or indecent or sexually explicit manner is punishable with five years of imprisonment and will be fined Rs 10 lakh.
