PATNA: Altogether 104 examinees were expelled for using unfair means and 21 impersonators arrested across the state on Friday, the third day of   examinations being conducted by the Bihar School Examination Board (BSEB). Nearly 17.7 lakh examinees wrote science paper in two sittings at 1,426 examination centres across the state on Friday. In Patna, 82,839 examinees wrote papers at 74 examination centres.

 Among the arrested impersonators, five were from Banka, four from Nalanda, three from Khagaria, two from Jamui and one each from Munger, Katihar, Darbhanga, Patna, Saharsa, Lakhisarai and Gopalganj. A BSEB official said one of the impersonators was caught at the College of Commerce, Science and Arts examination centre in the state capital.
 
 Altogether 27 examinees were expelled in Saran, 10 in Bhojpur, seven each in Buxar and Gaya, five each in Nalanda and Madhepura, four each in Munger, Sheikhpura, Bhagalpur and Jehanabad, three each in Jamui, Begusarai, Supaul and Saharsa, two each in Nawada, Arwal, Gopalganj and Khagaria and one each in Araria, Patna, Aurangabad, Katihar, Vaishali, Madhubani and Samastipur.
 
 “At least 687 candidates in Patna failed to appear for the exam on Friday – 299 in first sitting and 379 in second sitting,” said an official of the exam board.
 
 BSEB chairman Anand Kishor has asked centre incharges to maintain visitor entry register during the examinations.
