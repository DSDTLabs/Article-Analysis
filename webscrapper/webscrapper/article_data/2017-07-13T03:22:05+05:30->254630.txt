Mumbai: Taxi unions have petitioned the   seeking   for kaali-peeli taxis and   at five the entry-exit points to Mumbai.

 BJP city vice-president and union leader Krishna Hegde said, “We are demanding toll waiver for taxis so that hundreds of passengers are benefitted.” A letter has already been sent to chief minister   and the unions have requested him to take up the issue in the general interest of both taximen and passengers.
 
 Passengers not only pay toll for one-way journey, but kaali-peeli drivers also demand 50% return fare with the toll amount, a commuter said. Many taxi drivers refuse to ply to Navi Mumbai as they complain that they do not get a return fare.
