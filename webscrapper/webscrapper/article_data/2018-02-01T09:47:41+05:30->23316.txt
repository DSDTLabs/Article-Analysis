TIRUPUR: The state government has decided to fund the   ground water recharge scheme completely and execute it without any help from the central government.

 Though the   government had earlier sought financial assistance from the   under the  , it changed the mind after farmers from four western districts mounted a huge pressure on it to begin the project without any further delay, said a source with the   (PWD).
 
 “Earlier, our plan was to set up open water canals from Pillur dam. But in order to cut the cost, we have modified the plan and decided to pump water from   canal of Bhavani river by setting up underground pipelines,” a senior PWD official told TOI.
 
 The PWD said it was considering a solar power generation scheme near Kalingarayan canal at a cost of Rs 60 crore to provide electricity for the water pumping project.
 
 The official further said the PWD has prepared a new detailed project report with an estimated cost of Rs 1,850 crore. Earlier, the project was estimated to cost Rs 3,500 crore.
 
 “Since the project has been modified, the state government won’t wait for the clearance from the Union ministry of environment and forests. The government is also likely to terminate the services of  , which was assigned to prepare environmental impact assessment report,” another PWD source said.
 
 The state government had earlier announced to complete tender process and lay foundation stone on December 31. Now, it has assured Avinashi-Athikadavu Project struggle committee to commence the project at the earliest.
 
 Once the project is implemented, it will benefit more than 1.5 acres of agricultural land across Nilgiris, Coimbatore, Tirupur and Erode districts.
