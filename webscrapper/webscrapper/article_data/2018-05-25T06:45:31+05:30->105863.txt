JAIPUR: As talks between the district administration and villagers living near   (STR) remained inconclusive, the dharna (mahapadao) at the main gate continued for the fourth day.

 
 Villagers of 60 villages who are participating in the protest have submitted a 25-point memorandum of their demands. Though, the dharna continued no initiative was taken from the district administration on Thursday.
 
 Sources at forest department said, “It is difficult to fulfil most of their demands as it is not possible to amend the   rules regarding tiger reserves.”
 
 The protesters alleged after the death of tiger ST-11 and tigress ST-5 went missing, the forest department is committing atrocities on the villagers. They further demanded to withdraw all criminal cases against them which were registered by the forest department in the recent past. The protesters stressed that state government should give right to panchayats to issue lease deeds of land in villages falling under   where ban is imposed under Forest Rights Act 2006. It was demanded that state government should pay compensation to the villagers, if their crop is damaged by wild animals. Moreover, forest department should prevent wild animals from entering the fields. As most of the villagers are dependent on cattle for their livelihood, the protesters demanded to allow grazing in Sariska area.
 
 They also demanded that forest department should construct a concrete road that connects villages in Sariska and villagers should not be restricted to go to villages and temples, which are situated inside forest area.
