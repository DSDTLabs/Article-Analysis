PATNA: The state   has decided to introduce   as a subject in senior secondary classes of government and government-aided schools in 27 districts during the 2018-20 session.

 According to education minister  , the aim of the two-year   will be to train students to help improve agricultural productivity by using technology. “This course will expose senior secondary students to different problems faced by farmers owing to natural calamities and highlight ways to tackle them,” he said and added the syllabus and study materials had been prepared by the agriculture department.
 
 The vocational subject will be introduced in districts like Araria, Arwal, Aurangabad, Banka, Begusarai, Bhabhua, Bhagalpur, Bhojpur, Buxar, East Champaran, Gopalganj, Jenhanabad, Khagaria, Kishanganj, Lakhisarai, Madhepura, Madhubani, Nalanda, Nawada, Sasaram, Saharsa, Samastipur, Saran, Sheikhpura, Sheohar, Sitamarhi and Siwan.
 
 Verma told this reporter, “On a temporary basis, agricultural experts will teach students. Later, the education department will appoint permanent teachers as well.”
