NEW DELHI: School of Open Learning students at   staged a   on Wednesday against the college administration for framing discriminatory, sexist rules. The students met the principal and submitted a memorandum demanding an immediate annulment of the rules. She will address the students on January 15. 

 Principal   said two student representatives met her. “We had a long chat on the issue, and I told them that I would like to speak to them on Sunday and address their grievances.” 
 
 The students decided to protest following a notice that was allegedly put up on November 27, threatening suspension if the girls were found clicking selfies, combing their hair or “modelling” in the gallery. The principal, Pratibha Jolly, had told TOI on Tuesday that the notice was unsigned and issued keeping the safety of the students in mind.
