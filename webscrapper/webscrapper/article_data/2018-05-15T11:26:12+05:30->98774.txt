CHANDIGARH: The   on Monday night arrested a sub-inspector for allegedly taking a bribe of Rs 5,000 for releasing the impounded motorcycle of a resident. 

 Munna, a resident of Colony Number 4, had lodged a complaint against sub-inspector Sewa Singh posted at the Industrial Area, Phase I, police station, three days back. He said the   had stopped him at a naka and found him not having some documents. Sewa Singh demanded Rs 8,000 to release the bike. Later, the deal was struck at Rs 5,000.
 
 After receiving the complainant, CBI started recording conversations of sub-inspector with   over phone. Sewa Singh called the complainant to pay the bribe near the police station. He sent his accomplice, Ajay, to collect the amount. The CBI laid a trap and caught Ajay. Later, CBI arrested Sewa Singh. The officials also searched the room of the accused in the police station and seized some documents.
 
 On October 24, 2017, the CBI had arrested sub-inspector Mohan Singh of Sector 31 police station while accepting a bribe of Rs 2 lakh from a Ram Darbar-based property dealer. The deal was struck for Rs 8 lakh and the SI was arrested while taking the first installment of the bribe near the sector 31 police station.
 
 On May 23, 2017, a head constable of the UT police, posted at the Sarangpur police station, was arrested by the CBI while allegedly taking a bribe of Rs 10,000 from a woman.
