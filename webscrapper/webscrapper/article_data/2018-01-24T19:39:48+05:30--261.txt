HYDERABAD: A 14-year-old son of a watchman hacked the social media accounts of Hyderabadi international cricketer,   and sent ‘I love You’ messages to several of his friends. The incident came to light after the pace bowler from First Lancer lodged a complaint with Cyber Crime sleuths on Tuesday.

 
 Mohammed Siraj realised about the hacking on Monday after several of his friends contacted him about the ‘I Love You’ messages posted from his instagram account. The quick bowler, who recently made his début for Indian team in T20 cricket matches played against New Zeland, approached the Cyber Crime sleuths of Hyderabad commissionerate on Tuesday.
 
 “The social media accounts of Md Siraj including his facebook, instagram, gmail and twitter accounts were hacked by the unknown person. The   sent ‘I Love You’ messages to friends on social media accounts,” said a Cyber Crime official.
 
 After receiving the complaint, police immediately swung into action and tracked the details of the hacker. They have realised that the hacker replaced the cricketer’s password with his own password and checked the details. “The hacker gave a cell phone number as the new password to access the social media accounts of Siraj. We have obtained the details of the cell phone number and realised that it is being used by a 14-year-old boy from Begumpet who is the son of a watchman,” said an investigating official.
 
 Cops went to the boy’s house and questioned him in the presence of his family members. A case was also registered under the IT Act. During the preliminary probe, police came to know that the boy’s brother is a friend of the cricketer and they are questioning him to know how the boy managed to hack into the accounts of Siraj.
