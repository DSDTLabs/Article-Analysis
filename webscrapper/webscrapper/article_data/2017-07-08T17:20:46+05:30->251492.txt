DARJEELING: Army was called on Saturday amid   in Darjeeling after death of a youth allegedly in a police firing, PTI quoted Army sources as saying, even as chief minister Mamata Banerjee appealed for talks. 

 Fresh agitations started on Friday night when a young man named Tashi Bhutia died in Darjeeling. On Saturday, a rally was organised with the body of Bhutia, a GNLF supporter. 
 
 Police lathicharged agitators and used tear gas to control the situation while superintendent of police Akhilesh Chaturvedi appealed for normalcy.
 
 Gorkha Janamukti Morcha (GJM) leader Bimal Gurung said that on Friday at around 11pm, Tashi was returning home after purchasing medicine when police fired at him and he allegedly died on the spot. "We don't have any reports of police firing as of now. We are looking into the incident. We can give you details in the afternoon," the news agency quoted a police official as saying.
 
  
  
 
 Meanwhile, chief minister Mamata Banerjee appealed to agitators in Darjeeling to withdraw shutdown and come for talks. 
 
 Darjeeling and adjacent areas are again under the grip of violent agitation by GJM supporters, with agitators setting afire Sonada rail station. It was already burnt few years back. Incidents of hooliganism were also reported from Sonada police station area.
 
 Morcha supporters burnt down a traffic police kiosk at Sonada, while Sonada police station was vandalized by the agitators. One police vehicle was ransacked near Darjeeling railway station. Darjeeling food and supply office and DSP town office were also vandalized by Morcha supporters.
