PANAJI: The district magistrate, North Goa on Tuesday issued an order extending ban imposed on   Chief   by two more months saying his statement may lead to violence.

 The order also bans the entry any of its associate for the same period. The district magistrate’s order, said “the step has been taken due to their aggressive statement or speeches that could hurt the sentiments of certain groups of the public and instigate violence, thus adversely affecting public peace and tranquility.”
 
 The ban on Muthalik’s entry into Goa has been in force since 2014.
 
 In March, Hindu   (HJS) had demanded that the Goa government withdraw the ban imposed on the entry of Sri Ram Sene chief into the state in view of his acquittal in the Mangaluru pub attack case.
