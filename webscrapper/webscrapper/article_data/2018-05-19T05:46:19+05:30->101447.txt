NAVI MUMBAI: Two men posing as customers assaulted a jewellery store owner who was about to shut for the day with a chopper and fled with all gold ornaments on display in   on Thursday night. Senior inspector   said, “The duo aged 2530 had visited New Balaji Jewellers, Tirupati Complex, in sector 36 the previous day and purchased a gold ring worth   1,200, as they wanted to do a recee of the store. 

 
 They befriended the jeweller and learnt he was alone as his colleague had gone to his native place.” Naik said, “Around 9.30pm, jeweller   was tallying his accounts and gold stock, when the two entered. They pulled down the store’s shutters from inside and one of them assaulted the jeweller. 
 
 The jeweller injured his right palm. One of them then made him sit on a chair, while the other collected all the ornaments. They took away around 2,000 gm of gold jewellery worth Rs 65 lakh and Rs 1 lakh cash from the counter. 
 
 
 The duo fled on foot, which has been captured by   cameras of the building. But they have taken away the DVR device of the surveillance at the store.” Naik added, “We have registered an FIR under section 394 (voluntarily causing hurt in committing robbery), 342 (punishment for wrongful confinement), 452 (house-trespass after preparation for hurt, assault or wrongful restraint) and 34 (common intention) of IPC.
