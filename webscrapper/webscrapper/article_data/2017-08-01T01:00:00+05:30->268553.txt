Last Saturday morning, grown-ups and kids joined the   commandos at the   in   to promote Swachh Bharat Abhiyan. Around 800 people participated in the cycle rally while some 200 participants ran to cover a distance of 7km.

  Deepa Mishra, a school student who participated in the rally, said, "We should keep our environment clean, something that we are taught in school too. Commuters stopped to look at us and even enquired about our rally. It was an effective way to send the message of Swachh Bharat across." Another student,   said, "We have not participated in a rally before and it was an enjoyable experience. We were wearing green T-shirts so that we could make people aware of the cause of the rally. It was fun cycling with the commandos."
 
  
  School students were also a part of the rally, which was organised to spread the message of Swachh Bharat Abhiyan
 
 
  The CISF officials told us that they will be conducting such campaigns and rallies regularly. OP Singh, DG CISF, who was the chief guest at the event, urged people to keep the surroundings clean and be an active participant in Swachh Bharat Abhiyan. Raghubeer Lal, DIG, CISF (Metro), also interacted with the school children.
