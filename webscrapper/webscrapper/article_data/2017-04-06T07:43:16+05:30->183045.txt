DHARWAD: The Karnatak Vidyavardhakh Sangha will organise the seventh edition of   (convention of Kannadigas residing outside Karnataka) at Hyderbad on April 8 and 9.

 The KVS has been organising this convention to discuss the problems of the Kannadigas residing outside the state and exert pressure on the state government to solve their problems.
 
 KVS president   said the two-day meet will be held at Moulana Azad Urdu University and as many as 16 pro-Kannada organizations in Hyderabad have joined hands with KVS in organizing the event. Nearly 500 delegates will participate in the meet.
 
 Poet K S Nissar Ahmed will inaugurate the meet on April 8 at 10:30 am. Vice chancellor of Moulana Azad University Mohhamad Aslam Parvez, Tejeswi Kattimani and M Mahadevappa will be the chief guests.
 
 This will be followed by a session on growth of Kannada during Nizam’s rule. At 4:30 pm, women’s meet with Shanta Imrapur as the president will be held. Cultural events will follow the women’s meet. 
 
 On April 9, Leela Kalkoti will preside over the first session wherein poets will recite their compositions. This will be followed by an interaction session on border issues and language. Writer Mohan Nagammanavar will be the moderator. The valedictory will be held at 3:00 pm. Karnataka minister for higher education Basavaraj Rayraddi will be the chief guest.
