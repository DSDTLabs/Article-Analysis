VISAKHAPATNAM: M Roopvanti from SFS School Seethamadhara, Vishakhapatnam is the city champion of Classmate Spell Bee Season 9, India’s largest spelling competition. She emerged winner from the city in the third level of the competition in an online test. 

 A city winner does not automatically qualify for the national finals. Only 16 students with the top 16 scores across India qualify purely on a score merit. 
 
 This week, the online semifinals of Classmate Spell Bee Season 9 was conducted for Vishakhapatnam. The notebook brand Classmate and Radio Mirchi 98.3 FM had launched season 9 of Classmate Spell Bee three months ago. The theme for this year is ‘Every Child is Unique and So is Every Word.’ The competition this year is powered by The Times of India, Times NIE is the education partner,   is the television partner and   is the event partner for the property.
 
 Vishakhapatnam is one of the 30 cities in which such semifinals were conducted. In the initial phase, an onground exercise was conducted across 20 schools in Vishakhapatnam where children were given spelling tests. Top 15 scorers from each school competed in the city finals round. The top performers from the city finals then competed in the semifinal stage. 
 
 The culmination of Classmate Spell Bee Season 9 will see a tough competition between the top 16 scorers of India to win the title of Classmate Spell Bee Season 9’s National Champion. The national finals will be telecast on The  , Discovery Kids and Discovery Tamil. The National Champion of Classmate Spell Bee Season 9 will win Rs 2 lakh apart from the opportunity to witness the prestigious   with a parent in Washington DC, USA in an all-expenses paid trip. The four semifinalists will win a cash prize of Rs 50,000 each.
