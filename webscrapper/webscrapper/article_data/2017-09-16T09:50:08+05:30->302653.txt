The bank officials could not be contacted for their comment on this. Earlier, the state government, amid reports of indebted small and marginal farmers receiving loan waiver certificates of amounts as low as Rs 5, had admitted there were at least 5,000 beneficiaries of the ambitious scheme who received compensation between Re 1 and Rs 100 in the first phase covering 12 lakh farmers.
 
 The state government said the low waiver amount was a result of repayment already made by farmers over a period of time. The loan waiver scheme, which is projected to cover around 86 lakh farmers, envisages to waive crop loans of up to Rs 1 lakh taken by small and marginal farmers till March 31, 2016.
 
 The state government had also said that in all, the first phase of scheme covered 11.27 lakh farmers, of whom 4,814 received loan waiver benefit of Rs 1 to Rs 100.Another 6,895 farmers got remaining loan amounts between Rs 100 and Rs 500 waived. Likewise, 5,553 farmers received a loan waiver sop of Rs 500 to Rs 1,000. There were 41,690 farmers who received a farm loan waiver of Rs 1,000 to Rs 10,000. The total amount of loan waived was Rs 7,371crore.
 
 UP government's admission comes after it faced an embarrassing situation when farmers were reported to have received loan waiver certificates for amounts as low as Rs 10.
