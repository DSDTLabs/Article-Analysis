 The commission has asked the   to make a list of especially abled students who need facilities like writer and extra time in the exam centres. The panel was referring to the complaint of a class 10 student with   in Balaghat district, in which he said he was not provided with a writer and extra time in exam, as per the rules, due to which his two academic years were wasted. MPHRC has directed the district collector to make necessary arrangements for him.
 
 The commission has termed it as the violation of right to equality. The complainant had stated that he had filed an application in 2013 that he suffers from writing disability and extra time and writer should be provided to him as per the rules. Despite the request, the exam centre head did not allow him the facility. The student is appearing in the Class 10 exam since 2015, but could not clear it “due to negligence of the officials” concerned.
 
 The MPHRC has directed the Balaghat district collector to make adequate arrangements so that student could get the facility of a writer and extra time in the exam this year. MPBSE secretary has been called to the commission for discussion on the issue, stated MPHRC officials. On February 14, the MPHRC had issued a notice to secretary of Madhya Pradesh Public Service Commission (MPPSC), asking whether required arrangements were made for physically challenged people during the MPPSC exam held earlier this month.
 
 The commission has also taken cognisance of an incident wherein an especially abled woman had a tough time climbing two floors to reach the hall at Ashok Technology Institute in Vidisha, an exam centre for the MPPSC. The incident had gone viral after the exam.
