MARGAO: Chairperson of   Municipal Council ( ),   Angle Prabhudessai, on Wednesday said that, within 50 days of initiating the   recovery drive, the civic body has netted a revenue of Rs 1.77 crore by way of house tax arrears and sanitation tax. 

 Besides, MMC has also succeeded in getting a couple of fuel stations in the city to pay their rentals arrears for the municipality-owned land occupied by them. “With this, the total revenue recovery figures have gone up to Rs 2.34 crore,” Prabhudessai said, adding that demand notices have been sent to fuel stations that have failed to clear dues. Fuel station rentals will now be increased by 10% annually, she said. MMC will enter into fresh lease agreements with petrol pump dealers, following approval from the directorate of municipal administration. 
 
  Chief officer Johnson Fernandes said that he has set a targeting revenue arrears to the tune of Rs 5 lakh. 
 
 “Next, we will target trade tax and rentals,” he said. 
 
 Admitting that the single window system started by MMC has evoked poor response, Prabhudessai said that action would now be taken against those found plying their trade without licence. “We will also work out a mechanism to issue trade licences on as-is-where-is basis on temporarily to help them regularize their trade,” she said. A number of party halls operating in the city will also be targeted by MMC soon to bring them under the trade tax net, she added. 
 
 As regards the GIS survey that is under way, Prabhudessai said that nearly 70% of the work is complete and is likely to be completed by February-end. The data collected will be interpolated with GPS mapping to determine the exact location of a unit.
