NOIDA/GHAZIABAD: The winter break, which started in the third week of December for all classes in  , has further been extended till January 15, while schools in neighbouring Ghaziabad reopened as scheduled on Thursday. 

 The Gautam Budh Nagar district administration on Thursday ordered that the classes in all schools up to Class XII be shut till January 15 owing to the cold weather.
 
 District magistrate N P Singh said the decision to extend the winter vacation has been taken due to continuing   in the past few days. "All the schools affiliated to the boards of CBSE, ICSE and UP government shall remain closed. We will monitor the weather conditions and issue further directions in this regard," he told TOI. 
 
 Earlier, the administration had ordered closure of classes from nursery to Class VIII till January 11. The extension of holidays is important as the city is witnessing freezing cold in the past few days. On Thursday, Noida recorded the minimum temperatures at 5.3 degrees Celsius and maximum at 16.7 degrees Celsius.
 
 The school managements confirmed having received the directive on closure from the district magistrate's office. "We will close the school and reopen on January 16," said an official of Amity International School, Noida.
 
 Parents welcomed the move, saying it was necessary.  , president of Gautam Budh Nagar parents' association, said it was a welcome step.
 
 On December 12, 2016, the district administration in Noida ordered all schools to reschedule their timings and open from 9.30am-2.30pm owing to the foggy weather. The original timings were revived on December 16.
 
 On January 9, the administration shut the schools till January 11. In Ghaziabad, the schools reopened on Thursday. The Ghaziabad district administration had ordered a shutdown of all schools, up to Class VIII for the past 13 days. "No further instructions regarding school closure have been issued," Ghaziabad district magistrate,  , told TOI.
