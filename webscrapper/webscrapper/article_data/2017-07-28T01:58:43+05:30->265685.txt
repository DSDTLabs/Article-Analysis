NEW DELHI:Hot and sultry conditions returned to the capital on Thursday with mercury touching 35.4 degrees Celsius, one degree above normal. However, it was the ‘sticky’ weather which continued to cause an inconvenience to Delhiites with humidity levels touching 85%. According to Met officials, there has been a delay in the monsoon trough advancing northwards, with the next two days also unlikely to see any rain in the capital, followed by a spell of showers from July 30 to August 1.
