SHIMLA: Chief Minister Virbhadra Singh said that one time lump-sum grant to the recipients of Param Vir Chakra,   had been enhanced from Rs 25 lakh to Rs 30 lakh and Mahavir Chakra from Rs 15 lakh to Rs 20 lakh whereas rates of annuity of these categories of Gallantry awards winners had been enhanced from Rs 1.25 lakh to Rs 3 lakh from Rs. 1 lakh to Rs 3 lakh and Rs 1 lakh to Rs 2 lakh respectively.

 He said that rates of annuity to the other recipients of Gallantry Award Winners and Distinguished Services Awardees of Armed Forces that is Army, Navy and Air Force had also been enhanced from 1st April 1 last year.
 
 He said that Himachal government was committed to the welfare of serving soldiers, ex-servicemen, war widows and their dependents. He said that next of kin of soldiers working in the   belonging to state had also been declared as defence personnel and were entitled for the payment of ex-gratia grant from the Chief Ministerâ€™s Sainik Welfare Fund from January 4 last year.
 
 An amount of Rs Two crore had been provided for the repair and maintenance of Sainik Rest Houses, he added.
 Virbhadra Singh said that state had 1,10,202 ex-servicemen, 1050 war widows and 33,099 other widows besides large number of officers and Jawans serving the nation. He said that state government had constantly improved its efforts for ensuring the welfare of these families.
 
 He said that War Jagir had been increased from Rs 2000 to Rs 5000 per annum from April 1, 2014 and an amount of Rs 8.90 crore had been provided for the establishment of War Memorial Museum at Dharamshala. He said free travel facility had been extended to the Gallantry Award winners in deluxe and ordinary buses of HRTC within the state. He said financial assistance to the second World War veterans had also been increased from Rs 2000 to Rs 3000 per month from April 1, 2015.
 
 He said that the ex-gratia grant had been revised from May 25, 2015 and the beneficiaries would now get an ex-gratia @ Rs 5 lakh to the next of kin of battle casualty, disabled soldiers during operation @ Rs. 1.5 lakh (50% disability or above) and Rs 75,000 below 50% disability) and members of Para Military Forces, who die in active operations @ Rs 1.5 lakh and physical casualty @ Rs. 35,000.
 
 Virbhadra Singh said that annual income limit criteria had been enhanced from Rs 15,000 to 35,000 per annum (excluding the income of MNREGA) for availing the benefits of financial assistance ( ) being paid to ex-servicemen and their widows aged above 60 years and not getting any kind of pension.
