SHIMLA: Even as family members of Himachal Pradesh chief minister Virbhadra Singh’s wife Pratibha Singh nephew Akansh Sen, who was run over by a BMW, have decided to meet Union home minister  , Virbhadra’s son Vikramaditya Singh has alleged that one of the accused, Harmehtab Singh Farid was hiding in Gurgaon.

 Vikramaditya Singh said he was informed by five to six people about where these accused were hiding but Chandigarh police have no information about them. He alleged Farid was hiding in his friend’s house in Delhi and when police team went there, he fled. He said he was now hiding somewhere in Gurgaon. He said mobile locations of two accused have been found in Delhi, Gurgaon and Bangalore, which itself showed they were roaming freely while Chandigarh police were not arresting them despite having all information.
 
 Farid had studied at Bishop Cotton School (BSC) of Shimla, where Vikramditya Singh and his father   had also studied. 
 
 Vikramaditya said they would meet Rajnath Singh to demand formation of a inter-state or inter-city coordination committee of police officials to ensure early arrest of accused.
 
 Vikramaditya said on Tuesday, lawyers of two accused pleaded in the court that it was an accident but they maintained that it was a murder. “Our lawyer informed the court that it was not culpable homicide but murder,” he said.
 
 Family members of Akansh Sen claimed that call locations of two accused have been found in Delhi, Gurgaon and Bangalore. Akansh Sen was the son of Rana Arun Sen, who is the brother-in-law of Himachal Pradesh Chief Minister Virbhadra Singh.
 
 Not happy with the investigation of Chandigarh police, chief minister Virbhadra Singh too said that mater would be taken up with Rajnath Singh to constitute a special investigation team to speed up the investigation as attempts are being made to give the impression that Akansh Sen’s death was an accident.
