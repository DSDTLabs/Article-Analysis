PATNA: A galaxy of   biggies will attend the week-long Bodhisattva International Film Festival, scheduled to open at the city’s Adhiveshan Bhavan on Thursday.

 According to the organizers, altogether 102 films from 40 countries will be screened at the festival which will kick-start with ‘Bihar Ek Virasat’, a cultural programme by children of the state.  , a popular band, will also perform at Patna for the first time at the inaugural function of the film fest.
 
 The celebrities who have consented to grace the Bodhisattva include actor-turned-politicians Shatrughan Sinha and Manoj Tiwari, along with filmmaker Adoor Gopalakrishnan, Padma Vibhushan Dr Sonal Mansingh, Mahesh Bhatt, Govind Nihalani, Sudhir Mishra, Goutam Ghose, Leena Yadav, Saurabh Shukla, Vinay Pathak and Tannishtha Chatterjee.
 
 The movies, to be screened at the three auditoriums at Adhiveshan Bhavan, have been divided into different categories like Feature Film, Documentary, Short Film, Homage to   and World Cinema. “We will also have a panel discussion with the celebrities for the audience,” said  , chairman of the festival organising committee.
 
 Festival adviser and film critic Premendra Mazumder said the film festival is an attempt to push Bihar as a destination on the international film map. “The foreign films will help the Patnaites understand the social mores and manners of other countries,” he said. 
 
 The films lined up for the festival ara ‘Angry Indian Goddess’, ‘Parched’, ‘Jaane Bhi Do Yaaro’, ‘Dharavi’ and ‘Emma’.
