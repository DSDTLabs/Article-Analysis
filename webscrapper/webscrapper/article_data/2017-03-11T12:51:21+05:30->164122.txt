HYDERABAD: Fund-starved   ( ) has decided to mobilize Rs 3,500 crore through the issue of   and term loans from various agencies. While Rs 1,000 crore will be raised through municipal bonds, Rs 2,500 crore will be mobilized from various banks.

 The civic body requires the funds for taking up various schemes and projects such as the state government's flagship programme two-bed room housing project, the Strategic Road Development Project (SRDP) and other infrastructure projects.
 
 GHMC commissioner B Janardhan Reddy held a prebid meeting with nine bankers, empanelled by the Centre, for floating municipal bonds. Speaking to the bankers, the commissioner said the state government had taken up various programmes to make Hyderabad a global city without enhancing property tax and other taxes.
 
 He said only few municipal corporations that raised municipal bonds in the country and the GHMC would take their experience into consideration before floating the bonds. Janardhan Reddy said the civic body will engage merchant banks that quote less charges.
 
 Representatives from SBI Capital, Yes Bank, ICICI Bank, Axis Bank, AK Capital Services, ICRA management and other bankers and financial institutions were among those who attended the meeting.
