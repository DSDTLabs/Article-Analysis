YAMUNANAGAR: Police have arrested former minister and senior Congress leader   and his associate Chhota Aslam for allegedly opening fire following an altercation with a mining contractor and his workers over right to passage to a mining site in Khizrabad area of the district. 

 Khizrabad police station incharge sub-inspector Bal Raj said that so far they have arrested only two accused and a case was registered under various sections of the IPC, including 148 (rioting, armed with deadly weapon), 149 (unlawful assembly guilty of offence), 323 (voluntarily causing hurt), 506 (criminal intimidation), 307 (attempt to murder) and 395 (dacoity) against 10 persons. He said the other accused were being tracked and they would be arrested soon.

 Nirmal was an MLA from Naggal constituency of Ambala district in 1982, 1991, 1996 and 2005. The incident took place on April 24, when Nirmal and his associates fired at the owner of a screening plant and workers of the mining zone when they started using land which the former minister claimed to be in his name.
 
 In his complaint, Karan Singh of Mandoli Gagat village said Nirmal's land was connected via the common pathway from Belgarh to Lakkar. Karan alleged Nirmal and his associates had closed the way by digging trenches a few days back.
