 This was disclosed in the affidavit filed by Sukhbir before the   in Jalalabad on Thursday.  , including movable and immovable, have declined from Rs 76 crore in 2012 to Rs 72.4 crore in 2017.
 
 In the affidavit, he has shown himself as an agriculturalist. His overall annual income is Rs 2.1 crore which includes Rs 24.28 lakh earnings from farming. His movable assets, including money in nine bank accounts, bonds and jewellery, is Rs 19.25 crore as compared to Rs 27.58 crore in 2012. While his cash in hand has dropped from Rs 5 lakh in 2012 to Rs 25,000 in 2017. Sukhbir doesn’t own a single car but has two tractors.
 
 His immovable assets, including residential, agricultural and commercial properties, have risen from Rs 48.88 crore to Rs 53.13 crore, an increase by roughly Rs 5 crore. His properties range from land leased out to a petrol pump to commercial land in Rajasthan and Haryana.
 
 Among his new property purchases are two commercial buildings, one each in Ludhiana and Jalandhar for Rs 2.65 crore and R 2.63 crore. Both were purchased on March 26, 2013, a year after his last government was sworn in.
 
 The affidavit discloses that the rate of Jalandhar property has sharply declined from its original price of Rs 5.4 crore.
 
 Sukhbir has also disclosed that he owns shares worth Rs 14.25 crore in various firms, including those owned by him. They are Orbit Resorts, Falcon Properties, Metro Eco Green Resorts, Punjab Animal Breeders and Orbit Aviation Limited.
 
 According to the affidavit, his wife and Union minister Harsimrat Kaur Badal’s overall assets have risen by Rs 15 crore from Rs 14 crore in 2012 to Rs 29.5 crore.
