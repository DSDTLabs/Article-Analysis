Punjab’s most-wanted gangster Vicky Gounder as well as one of his aides were killed in an encounter by   police in   district on the Rajasthan-Punjab border on Friday. 

 Acting on a tip-off, Punjab Po lice personnel tracked the gangster to Hindu Mal Kot village in Sri Ganganagar district. “When confronted, Gounder opened fire on the cops and an encounter began. Gounder and his aide were killed in the gunfight while two cops sustained injuries,” a Rajasthan police officer said. Gounder was also wanted by Rajasthan police. 
 
 “He had been involved in gang wars in Punjab. We were investigating his role in a loot case in Rajasthan’s Hanumangarh,” the Rajasthan police officer said. The 27-year-old gangster was accused of breaking out of a high-security jail at   in 2016 and carried a reward of Rs 25 lakh.
