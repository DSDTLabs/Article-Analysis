Shimla: Six people, including three women, were killed on the spot while two others were seriously injured when the Bolero Camper vehicle (HP-08A-0635) they were travelling in skidded off road and fell into a 300-feet-deep gorge near Ranvi village of Chopal subdivision in Shimla district on Thursday. The accident spot is located about 30 km from Nerwa town and the ill-fated vehicle was on its way from Paouria to Nerwa.

 Those killed in the accident have been identified as Sunil of Gajtari, Rajinder Gajta of Burech, Kalawati of Ranvi, Shanta Devi of Poran, Devi Singh of Ranvi and Kalpna of Poran while the injured are Paras and Arun (driver), both from Gajtari.
 
 In his statement to the police, Ram Lal Thopta of Ranvi stated that the accident occurred around 10: 55 am. A case of rash driving, causing hurt by act endangering life or personal safety of others and causing death by negligence has been registered at Nerwa police station.
 
 After receiving information of the accident, Chopal DSP Santosh Sharma, Nerwa tehsildar Rishabh Sharma along with police and revenue officials reached at the spot. Immediate relief of Rs 10,000 each was given to the families of the deceased while Rs 5,000 each was given to those injured.
