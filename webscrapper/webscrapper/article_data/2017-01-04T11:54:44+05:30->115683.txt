NAGPUR: Former head of the department of pharmaceutical science of   Naresh Gaikwad on Monday said that he was inspired by Dr Babasaheb Ambedkar who laid stress on study and fight for rights come what may. It was necessary to not give up and follow the ideals set for oneself, he added.

 Replying to the felicitation after receiving ‘Jeevangaurav Samman’ at a function held at a city hotel on Monday, Gaikwad gave the credit of his academic excellence to Dr  , vice-chancellor of Krishna Institute of Medical Sciences, Karad and professor VV Parashar. 
 
 He said that the department of pharmaceutical sciences was accredited by National Board of Accreditation-All India Council of Technical Education, New Delhi, mainly because of the support he got from his mentors and friends.
 
 SP Kane, vice-chancellor of NU,  , pro-vice-chancellor, Puranchandra Meshram, registrar, Sudhakar Deshmukh, MLA and GS Parashar, vice-chancellor of Indian Maritime University, Chennai, were present.
 
 In his introductory speech, Mohan Kale, co-chairman of the organizing committee, spoke on how Gaikwad played an important role in the life of students, staff and their colleagues by motivating, guiding and encouraging them. 
 
 Maya Gaikwad was felicitated by  , head of the department of pharmaceutical sciences of NU.
