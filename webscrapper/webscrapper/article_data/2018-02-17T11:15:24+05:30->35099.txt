CHENNAI: On a day when a section of   unions went on strike as announced earlier in connection with wage dispute, the Madras high court has said that it would take up the issue for detailed hearing on Monday. 

 The first bench headed by Chief Justice   said so when  , a resident of Chennai, made an urgent mention about the issue.
 
 He wanted the court to interfere and stay the strike as it would cause inconvenience to public at large.
 
 A section of   workers affiliated to CITU, BMS and a few other trade unions went on strike from Friday as the management failed to finalise the wage agreement which was due in January 2016.
