 Mathura ADM Ravindra Kumar said that the candidate had uploaded a picture of an EVM machine with his finger pressing the party’s poll symbol in violation of the model code of conduct. He added that mobile phones are not allowed inside the polling booth and hence the candidate had also breached security. Kumar told TOI that Mathura returning officer (RO) has been directed to register an FIR against the candidate.
  
 When contacted, Divedi however claimed that the “so-called selfie” that he uploaded wasn’t clicked by him. “I had posted a collage of some pictures on my Facebook page stating that my wife and I had cast our vote. One of the pictures in the collage, showing a finger next to the BSP party symbol, was received by me on WhatsApp,” he added.
  
 Admitting that he had committed a mistake inadvertently, Dwivedi said that he would file his response to the EC as and when he receives a notice.
