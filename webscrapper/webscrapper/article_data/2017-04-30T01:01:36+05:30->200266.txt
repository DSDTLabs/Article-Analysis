MUMBAI: The state government has approved a   development plan worth Rs 350 crore for the Mumbai  . The plan was approved by Vinod Tawde, the guardian minister for Mumbai   and the funds will be used as per the district planning and development council guidelines. These funds will be used by the BMC for undertaking projects recommended by the council.

 Political representatives of the area attended the meeting and discussed the allocation of the funds as well as the monsoon preparedness of the district before the rains. Tawde said as work on the Metro-3 project is going on, precautions must be taken to ensure that the commuters and motorists are not hassled because of it. 
 
 “There is a huge problem of water logging in these areas, there should be adequate pumping stations at the spot to pump out the access water,” said Tawde.
