NEW DELHI: It was a pleasant morning in the national capital Thursday as rains brought the mercury down, with the minimum temperature settling at 21.5 degrees Celsius.

 According to a MeT department official, the   observatory, recording of which is considered the official figure for the city, received 0.8 mm rainfall, while Palam recorded 3.2 mm rainfall.
 
 Humidity levels were recorded at 52 per cent in the city.
 
 The maximum temperature is likely to hover around 38 degrees Celsius with clear skies throughout the day, the weatherman said.
 
 Wednesday, the maximum temperature settled at 35.8 degrees Celsius, while the minimum was recorded at 22.4 degrees Celsius.
