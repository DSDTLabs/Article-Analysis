 Replying to a question by   MLA Anil Joshiyara, the government disclosed these figures . 
 
 Finance minister Nitin Patel said that Gujarat owes Rs 1,82,098 crore according to the revised estimates for 2015-16, an increase of Rs 18,647 crore from the previous year. 
 
 In the last financial year, the state’s public debt was Rs 1,63,451 crore. 
 
 Patel said the government borrowed Rs 19,454 crore in 2014-15 and Rs 24,852 crore in 2015-16.
 
 In 2014-15, the state government paid Rs 13,061 crore in interest and Rs 5,509 crore of the principal on its borrowings.
 
 In 2015-16, the state paid Rs 14,495 crore as interest and Rs 6,205 crore of principal, Patel’s reply stated.
 
 In reply to a question by Congress MLA from Dariapur Gyasuddin Shaikh, Patel said that the government through the Reserve Bank of India has raised loans of Rs 14,920 crore from the market in 2014-15 and Rs 16,260 crore in 2015-16 (revised estimates).
