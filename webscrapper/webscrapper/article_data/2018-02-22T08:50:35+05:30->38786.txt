HYDERABAD: In yet another case of corporal punishment, a five-year-old LKG student was beaten up by her class teacher at  ,  .

 The victim, B Pushpanjali, a resident of Patancheru and a student of   was beaten up by her teacher on Tuesday.
 
 “Pushpanjali did not speak about the incident to her parents. When her mother was getting her dressed, she noticed injury marks on her hand and back. On Tuesday night, the parents approached police and filed a complaint against the teacher,” said Praveen Kumar Reddy, sub inspector Patancheru police station.
 
 The accused was identified as Meghna (38), who taught all subjects for LKG classes at the school.
 
 According to police, Pushpanjali is an active student. She picked up a fight with her classmate and beat him. Following which the teacher punished her by beating her with a stick. The five-year-old sustained injuries on her hand and back.
 
 “ We are yet to question the teacher as she is in the hospital, getting a treatment for her depression. We will investigate the case and take action accordingly,” said SI Praveen.
 
 Following this, a case was registered under Section 324 (Voluntarily causing hurt by dangerous weapons or means) of IPC against the teacher.
 
 Soon after the case came into light, Balala Hakkula Sangham, a city-based child rights NGO, has demanded police officials to book a case against the school management also, stating that care and protection of the children is the responsibility of the management.
 
 According to the NGO, there have been nearly 30 cases of corporal punishment till now this year.
 
 “There are over 100 cases of corporal punishment in other districts as well. Corporal punishment, not only affects students physically but also mentally,” said Achyuta Rao, honourary president, Balala Hakkula Sangham.
