Panaji: The directorate of vigilance’s anti-corruption branch ( ) has summoned leader of opposition Chandrakant ‘Babu’ Kavlekar to appear before it in connection with the Rs 4.78 crore disproportionate assets case, SP Priyanka Kashyap told STOI.

 Kavlekar had told the ACB that he had got about Rs 5.5 crore through unsecured loans from some Mumbai-based companies, and that the loans were interest-free.
 
 On September 15, 2017, ACB registered a first information report (FIR) in the disproportionate assets case under Section 13(2) read with 13(1)(e) of the Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988.
 
 The ACB had also booked Kavlekar’s wife, Savitri, under Section 109 (abetment) of the Indian Penal Code (IPC) as she is a director of Kavlekar's companies. ACB had then conducted searches at Kavlekar's residence at Quepem as well as his office and commercial offices at Quepem and Margao, respectively.
 
 In 2013, ACB received information that Kavlekar, then chairman of Goa Industrial Development Corporation (GIDC), had acquired various properties in Kerala.
 
 Accordingly, ACB started preliminary investigations and conducted a verification drive into his entire tenure, right from the time he was elected MLA till 2013. ACB found that Kavlekar served as GIDC chairman for six-and-a-half years. It was also revealed that he purchased around 14 different properties worth crores of rupees in Kerala.
