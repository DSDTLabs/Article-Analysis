JAIPUR: From March, children of the state will get a shield to protect themselves from fatal pneumococcal pneumonia.

 The Pneumococcal Conjugate   (PCV) will be launched for children to bring down the mortality rate due to pneumonia which is a vaccine preventable disease.
 
 The training workshop for health department officials will be organised in the first week of February for sensitising and informing them about PCV.
 
 “In the first phase, nine districts have been identified for PCV launch in March. These districts are Barmer, Jaisalmer, Jalore, Pali, Sirohi, Udaipur, Banswara, Dungarpur, Chittorgarh,” a senior health department official said.
 
 According to officials, the mortality rate due to pneumonia will be reduced significantly following the launch of the vaccine.
 
 In the first phase, the vaccine has already been introduced in Bihar, UP and  .
 
 The vaccine protects children against pneumococcal pneumonia, which is the leading cause of pneumonia in the country.
 
 Health department officials claimed that PCV will help in reducing mortality rate among kids below five years. “A lot many children die before attaining the age of five due to pneumococcal pneumonia in the state. There was no protection such as vaccine was available with the government in the state, but now it will be added to immunization programme of the state soon,” the official said.
 
 According to   (WHO), “Diseases caused by streptococcus pneumoniae (pneumococcus) constitute a major global public health problem.”
