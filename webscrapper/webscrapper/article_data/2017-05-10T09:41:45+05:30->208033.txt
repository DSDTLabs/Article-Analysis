 The court of civil judge Rekha Chaudhary granted bail to the two women booked under sections 332 (voluntarily causing hurt to deter public servant from his duty) and 353(assault or criminal force to deter public servant from discharge of his duty) of the Indian Penal Code. They were released on bail after they furnished bail bonds of Rs 35,000. Incidentally, one of the women got bail on the day of her mehandi ceremony. She is to get marriend on May 11.
 
 Police arrested the women on the night of April 8, 2017. A case was registered at the Sector 36 police station on the complaint of constable Manjit Kumar of the Chandigarh Police. They allegedly misbehaved with the official on duty. 
 
 According to the complainant, the two women, who were in a car, jumped the red light near Judicial Academy, Sector 43, Chandigarh. A police official signalled them to stop and when they did, he asked them to produce documents. At this, they allegedly created a scene and women constables had to be called to bring things under control. Both women were allegedly under the influence of alcohol. 
 
 The FIR says the women misbehaved with the public servant on duty, tore his uniform and created obstruction in the discharge of government duty.  , SHO, Sector 36 police station said medical reports confirmed that they were under the influence of alcohol.
 
 The women were visibly relieved at getting bail. The court took note of this marriage factor and accepted the bail applications.
