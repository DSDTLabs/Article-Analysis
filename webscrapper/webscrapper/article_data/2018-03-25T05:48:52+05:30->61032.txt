TRICHY: Aimed at fostering creativity among school students and to expose them to innovation and technology,   (ATL) will be established in all 32 districts in  . 

 Participating in an interactive session with incubatees on TREC-STEP campus on Saturday, R Ramanan, mission director, Atal Innovation Mission,  , said that they planned to establish the state-of-the-art laboratories equipped with tools and learning kits in at least 5,000 schools across the country this year.
 
 Through the facilities available in ATL, students studying between Class VI and Class XII will be exposed to the tools and do-it-yourself kits inculcating innovation to find solutions to real-time problems. 
 
 “ATL will be established in schools in about 93% of the districts in the country, including all the 32 in Tamil Nadu. Gadgets to support augmented virtual reality, 3D printers, and electronic equipment to learn robotics will be available in such model labs,” R Ramanan said. 
 
 Close to 2,400 schools across India have been identified so far, of which at least 170 schools from Tamil Nadu have found a place. Facilities would be provided to each of the ATLs by Niti Aayog. tnn
