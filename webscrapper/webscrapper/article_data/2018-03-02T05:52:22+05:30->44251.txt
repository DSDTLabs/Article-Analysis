HYDERABAD: Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI) sleuths conducted searches at the house of M Jaipal Reddy, cashier of Telangana Grameena   in Moinabad, and unearthed 57 lakh assets disproportionate to his known sources of income. After the raid,   registered a fresh case against Reddy. 

 Based on a complaint lodged by M Ravinder Reddy, regional manager of the bank, CBI had registered a case against the bank's Aziznagar branch cashier M Jaipal Reddy and 10 others for defrauding the bank and its customers to the tune of 8.9 crore on February 19. 
 
 CBI conducted searches at Jaipal Reddy's residence in Moinabad on Feb 20 and an inventory of assets was prepared.
 
 According to CBI, the approximate income of Jaipal Reddy and his wife,  , during April 1, 2011 and February 20, 2018 was 39.6 lakh and the value of assets possessed by the couple was approximately 73.4 lakh. "It is learnt that the accused is living lavishly and his expenditure between April 1, 2011 and Feb 20, 2018 is likely to be 23.4 lakh. The accused is learnt to be in possession of assets disproportionate to his known sources of income to the tune of 57.2 lakh in his and his family members' names, which he cannot account for," the CBI's   stated. 
 
 After the searches, CBI on Wednesday registered a case against Jaipal under Sections 13(2) r/w 13(1)(e) of the Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988.
 
 Earlier, a bank customer,  , lodged a complaint on Jan 31, 2018, alleging that 10 lakh deposited by him was missing. "Vishwanath deposited 10 lakh for a fixed deposit and Jaipal Reddy gave him receipts for it. But he did not enter details in records and siphoned off the amount. We had registered a case under Sections 120-B (criminal conspiracy), 420 (cheating) and 409 (criminal breach of trust) of the IPC and transferred the case to CBI," Moinabad inspector   told TOI.
