PATNA: Improved visibility due to clear weather notwithstanding, the railways has decided to continue with its arrangement   to keep 38 passenger trains cancelled on different days.

 These trains were cancelled from December 17, 2016 to February 15, 2017 due to poor visibility in foggy weather conditions. Besides, 14 passenger trains running on different routes of East Central Railway (ECR) in Bihar will remain cancelled every day till February 28, said ECRâ€™s chief public relations officer (CPRO) A K Rajak on Wednesday.
 
 According to Rajak, the Howrah-Patna Janshatabdi Express train will remain cancelled every Thursday till February 28 while the Patna-Ranchi Janshatabdi Express (12365-12366) train will not operate every Friday. The Patna-New Delhi Sampoorna Kranti Express (12393-12394) train will not run every Wednesday from Patna side and every Thursday from New Delhi end.
 
 The Gaya-New Delhi Mahabodhi Express (12397-12398) train will not originate every Monday from Gaya side and every Tuesday from New Delhi toll February 28. The Jaynagar- New Delhi Swatantrata Senani Express (12561-12562) train will be cancelled every Thursday from Jaynagar and every Friday from New Delhi end while the Barauni-Lucknow Express (15203-15204) train will remain cancelled every Tuesday, the CPRO said, adding the   (15209-15210) train will remain cancelled every Sunday from Saharsa side and every Friday from Amritsar side.
 
 The   (15211-15212) train will continue to be cancelled every Wednesday from Darbhanga and every Monday from Amritsar. The Singrauli-Varanasi Intercity Express (13345-13346) train will remain cancelled every Tuesday while the Danapur-Anand Vihar Jansadharan Express (13257-13258) train will remain cancelled every Wednesday from Danapur side and every Thursday from Delhi end.
 
 The 14 trains, which will not run on any day till February 28, include Sitamarhi-Anand Vihar Lichchvi Express, Muzaffarpur-Maduadih Express, Saharsa-Barauni Express, Sealdah-Delhi Express, Hatia-Anand Vihar Express, Guwahati-Anand Vihar Express and   trains.
 
 Though no specific reason was given by the railways for continuing with the cancellation of trains till February 28, sources attributed it to non-availability of rakes of these passenger trains at their originating stations. 
 
 Needless to say, the railways is suffering huge revenue losses due to cancellation of trains in such a large number. A Railway Board official said that railways was yet to assess the exact loss of revenue due to foggy weather.
