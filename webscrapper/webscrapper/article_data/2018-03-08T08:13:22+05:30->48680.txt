RAJKOT: Three traders were booked on Wednesday for running a   racket in Amreli. Mahesh Tilavat, Chandresh Rawal and  , all  , were booked after the scam was unearthed by the district food and civil supply officer. 

 Amreli mamlatdar Manilal Jadav lodged a police complaint against the trio, following which the accused may be arrested soon. During inspection, the district food and civil supply officer stumbled upon food coupons issued in the names of ration card holders. 
 
 “During investigation, it was revealed that the coupons were bogus and were generated fraudulently,” said an official from Amreli police station. 
 
 “This is how the whole scam got busted. It was revealed that the accused would generate bogus food coupons in the names of ration card holders and buy food grains and kerosene under the Annapurna scheme by the state government for providing essential commodities to those living below poverty line and sell them at higher prices,” said the official. “For example, they would buy 1kg wheat grain at Rs 2 from the fair price shop and sell it at Rs 25 or Rs 30 per kg in the market,” he explained.
