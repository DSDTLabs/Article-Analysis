NEW DELHI: Talking on the mobile phone while driving in   will now cost you your licence. The Jodhpur bench of Rajasthan high court has directed the traffic police to obtain photographs of such violators and forward their credentials to the regional transport office for cancellation of driving licence. 

  The HC passed the order after the ACP (traffic) told the court that a large number of four- and two-wheeler drivers used cellphones while driving, which was not permissible under law and caused accidents. 
 
  A bench of Justices   and   ordered the RTO and transport department authorities to initiate proceedings to cancel licences after giving an opportunity for hearing. 
 
  According to WHO, those using phones while driving face four times higher risk of crash. Accidents and deaths due to the growing menace has shot up across the world.
