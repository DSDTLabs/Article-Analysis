LUCKNOW: Chief minister   has set up a three-member inquiry committee to determine the cause behind the collapse of an under-construction bridge in Varanasi on Tuesday evening. 

 The incident claimed 17 lives while a few more were feared trapped under the debris. Three persons were pulled out from the debris by the   (NDRF). Deputy chief minister   and minister of state Neelkanth Tiwari rushed to Varanasi to oversee the rescue and relief measures.
 
 The government has announced ex gratia of Rs 5 lakh each for the families of the deceased and Rs 2 lakh for the injured.
 
 Relief commissioner   said the bridge that collapsed was being constructed by the UP State Bridge Corporation. "It is a 2,261 metres long bridge which is being constructed at a cost of Rs 129 crore. About 56% of the work has been completed and the span that collapsed on Tuesday had been laid in February this year," he said.
 
 "The inquiry team is led by agricultural produce commissioner Rajpratap Singh, with chief engineer of the irrigation department Bhupindra Sharma and Jal Nigam MD Rajesh Mittal as other members. It has been asked to submit report within 48 hours. The CM has asked the district administration and departments concerned to ensure rescue and relief at warfooting. 
 
 Seven teams of the NDRF, comprising 325 workers, have been deployed for the rescue mission," said a government spokesperson.
 
 Expressing grief at the incident, PM   tweeted: "Extremely saddened by the loss of lives due to the collapse of an under-construction flyover in Varanasi. I pray that the injured recover soon. Spoke to officials and asked them to ensure all possible support to those affected. I spoke to UP CM Yogi Adityanath Ji regarding the situation due to the collapse of an under-construction flyover in Varanasi. The UP Government is monitoring the situation very closely and is working on the ground to assist the affected."
