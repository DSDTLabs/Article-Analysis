CHENNAI: A meeting of the   district secretaries held in Chennai on Sunday asked   chief minister Edappadi K Palaniswami to intervene and bring the transport workers’ strike to an end. It wanted the district review meetings being held by Governor Banwarilal Purohit to be stopped immediately. 

 As many as 11 resolutions were passed in the meeting held at the party headquarters, Anna Arivalayam. DMK working president M K Stalin chaired the meeting held on the eve of the assembly session. 
 
 The meeting, which was the first to be held after the DMK’s R K Nagar drubbing, decided to hold the party’s zonal conference in Erode on March 24 and 25.
 
 One of the resolutions congratulated party president M Karunanidhi’s daughter and Rajya Sabha member, Kanimozhi, and former Union minister for telecom A Raja for coming out clean in the 2G spectrum case. Another resolution wanted the Centre to remove fear in the minds of Muslims over the Triple Talaq Bill.
 
 The meeting also resolved to urge the Centre to immediately release the full quantum of relief sought by Tamil Nadu to undertake relief works in areas affected by recent cyclone  . 
 
 Yet another resolution passed at the meeting expressed concern over the increasing debt burden of the Tamil Nadu government and wanted an expert committee to be formed to study the state’s precarious financial situation.
