CHENNAI: As part of its efforts to clean the Ganga from pollution, the National Mission for Clean Ganga (NMCG) will soon introduce   for surface cleaning of the river.
 
 Trash skimmers would be introduced in six more cities in the first week of this month, an official statement by the Union ministry of water resources, river development and Ganga rejuvenation said on Monday. 
 
 “The cities are Rishikesh, Haridwar, Garh Mukhteshwar, Sahibganj, Kolkata and Navdweep,” the statement said, adding that urban local bodies will be the nodal agency to monitor this work. State Programme Management Groups (SPMGs) and collectorate office will be supervising these works under the aegis of the NMCG at the state and district levels, respectively.
 
 River surface cleaning (RSC) work was introduced in Allahabad, Kanpur, Varanasi, Mathura-Vrindavan in Uttar Pradesh and Patna in Bihar last year. Trash skimmers were deployed in the foregoing locations and tonnes of floating waste was collected and disposed of in a proper manner. 
 
 This task was performed under the CSR head and proved to be extremely effective. In the coming days, more towns would be identified for carrying out similar activities, the statement said.
