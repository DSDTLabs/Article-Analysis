CHENNAI: The   on Monday instructed the   government to immediately release due compensation to the families of three men who died while doing   in   on February 14. 

 The first bench of Chief Justice Indira Banerjee and Justice Abdul Quddhose passed the instruction in a public interest litigation (PIL) moved by A Narayanan of Change India. The bench then posted the plea to next week for further hearing. 
 
 While requesting the bench to take up the plea for urgent hearing, Narayanan said though adequate awareness was being created in line with the Prohibition of Employment as Manual Scavengers and their Rehabilitation Act, 2013, such incidents continued. 
 
 On February 14, three men were asphyxiated while cleaning a septic tank at Ponthur near Sriperumbudur. The incident took place around 2.30pm when Murugesan and Mari cleaning the tank at a hotel on the Oragadam Road. Murugesan slipped and fell into the tank after inhaling the fumes, followed by Mari.
 
 Ravi, who was supervising the work on behalf of the hotel management, tried to rescue them, but he also fell into the tank and died.
