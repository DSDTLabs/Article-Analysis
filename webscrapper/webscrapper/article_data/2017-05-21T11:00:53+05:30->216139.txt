 A few vehicles skidded because of the oil spill on the road.“We have registered two complaints against the tanker drivers with the Khopoli police station. The tankers were overloaded and oil was spilling on the road, much to the risk of the highway users. The drivers told us that the tankers were overloaded following the directions of the owners in Mumbai,“ a Highway Safety Patrol (HSP) officer said.
 
 He added that police will from now file complaints against drivers and owners of trucks and tankers “if they cause nuisance on the highway that triggers traffic snarls“.
 
 HSP officials said the two oil tankers were headed for Pune from Mumbai around 8am.“The tankers were full and oil started leaking through the lids. Both the tankers continued to move towards Pune for nearly 2km, from Baur ghat to Amrutanjan bridge, before some motorists stopped those,“ an official said.
 
 “At least four car drivers trailing the tankers lost control and their vehicles skidded.Fortunately , no accident occurred,“ the officer said.
 
 Expressway authorities, HSP and local agencies stopped vehicular movement towards Pune near Baur tunnel. Sand trucks and water tankers reached the spot and the oil was washed out.
