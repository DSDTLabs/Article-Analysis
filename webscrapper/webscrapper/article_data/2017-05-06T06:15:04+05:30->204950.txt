BERHAMPUR: With six new cases of diarrhoea reported from   town in   district, the number of affected persons has gone up to 82 in the last 10 days. Consumption of contaminated water is suspected to have led to the outbreak, health officials said. 

 "Most of the patients were discharged after recovery. Only six patients, who were admitted on Friday, are undergoing treatment at a community health centre in Kabisurya Nagara. Their condition is stable," said additional district medical officer (public health)  . He visited the affected areas on Thursday and reviewed the measures taken to tackle the situation. 
 
 He said their team, including anganwadi,   and health workers continued to be deployed in all 18 wards of the NAC town and create awareness about the use of boiled water among the people.
