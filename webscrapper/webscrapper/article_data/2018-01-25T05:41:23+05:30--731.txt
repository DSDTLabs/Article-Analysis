GURUGRAM: A 26-year-old man was arrested on Tuesday for allegedly raping a   IX student twice in the last three   in Palam Vihar. The accused, Rahul Raj, who is a water tanker driver originally from Aligarh in Uttar Pradesh, called the girl inside his rented room and   her, first in November last year, and then on January 4.

 According to police, Raj met the girl for the first time in November last year near her house, where he would go to fill up tanker from a borewell. Once they became acquainted, he lured her into an autorickshaw one day, on pretext of showing her around the city, but took her to his rented accommodation instead. After raping her, he promised to marry her, but also allegedly threatened to kill her if she told anyone of the incident. On January 4, the accused again took her to his room and raped her. 
 
 The girl narrated her ordeal to her mother on Tuesday, and they approached police. After a complaint was registered, the minor was taken to Civil Hospital for medical examination, where doctors confirmed rape. An FIR was then registered against the accused under Section 506 (criminal intimidation) of IPC and Section 6 of Protection of Children from Sexual Offences Act at Palam Vihar station. 
 
 The accused was arrested from Palam Vihar. He later confessed to his crime. 
 
 “He was sent to judicial custody,” said Vikram Nehra, SHO, Palam Vihar police station.
