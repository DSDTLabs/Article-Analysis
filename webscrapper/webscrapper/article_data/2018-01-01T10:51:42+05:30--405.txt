 That’s not all. The number of drug-related cases registered in Chandigarh rose in 2017. While 149 cases were registered under the Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances (NDPS) Act in 2016, this number rose to 220 in 2017.
 
 In 2015, 174 NDPS cases were registered in Chandigarh.
 
 Heroin seizures were also high, with 1.118kg of the drug being caught by the police this year. On the streets, this drug costs nearly Rs 2,000 a gram, according to sources.
 
 When it came to opium (afeem) and poppy husk (bhukki), police seized 2.102kg opium and 1.217 kg poppy husk. Only 72 grams of smack were seized during the year.
 
 During the year, police arrested 18 women drug peddlers. Police stations in Maloya, Maulijagran, sectors 39 and 36, and Manimajra arrested more than 50% drug peddlers. Police said easy availability was the main reason behind addicts using buprenorphine. “During interrogation of peddlers arrested with buprenorphine, we found they procured these vials for anything between Rs 50 and Rs 80; they used to get these vials from Ambala, Zirakpur, Baddi, and rural areas of Panchkula,” DSP (crime) Pawan Kumar said.
