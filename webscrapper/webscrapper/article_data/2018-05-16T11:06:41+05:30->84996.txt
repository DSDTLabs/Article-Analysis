NEW DELHI: The Election Commission (EC) on Tuesday, May 15, 2018, declared the   Result 2018. 

 
 In the Karnataka assembly elections, the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) with 104 seats emerged as the single largest party in the state but failed to cross the half-way mark of 112 in the 224-member assembly constituency. 
 
 The Indian National Congress is at the second position with 78 seats while the Janata Dal-Secular (JD-S) became the third party with 38 seats. 
 
 Both Congress and JD(S) have decided to come together to form a coalition government.
 
 However, BJP national Vice President Vinay Sahasrabuddhe on Wednesday said that he is confident that Karnataka Governor Vajubhai Vala will respect the party's position as the single largest one and invite it to form the government.
