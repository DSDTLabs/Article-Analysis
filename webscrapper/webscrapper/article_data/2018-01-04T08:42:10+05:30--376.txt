AHMEDABAD: A magisterial court at the   rural court campus has punished   scamster   with 18-month imprisonment and imposed a fine of Rs 30,000 in a cheque bounce case.

 The complaint against Gandhi was filed by  , who had invested in a scheme floated by Gandhi. In 2011, Desai asked Gandhi for return on his investment. Gandhi handed him cheques worth Rs 1.65 crore, but none of them could be realized. On the other hand, Gandhi came to be arrested in the same year on charges of embezzlement of Rs 380 crore.
 
  
  Upon dishonour of the cheques, Desai filed a complaint under Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act through advocate S M Qazi.
 
  The court on Tuesday punished Gandhi for the cheque bounce and ordered him to return the amount. The court also imposed a fine of Rs 30,000. 
 
 Gandhi expressed inability to pay the fine at first, but deposited it later. 
 
 After awarding punishment, the court granted bail to Gandhi for one month to file an appeal against the verdict in an appellate court.
