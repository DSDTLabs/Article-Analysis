BHUBANESWAR: Chief minister   on Wednesday presented a cash award of Rs 1 lakh to Sesha Kisan, a chemical   who rose from humble beginnings in a Sambalpur village to obtaining a post at the University of Gottingen in Germany. 

 "Delighted to meet Sesha Kisan, whose journey from grazing cattle in a village in Sambalpur to obtaining a position at the prestigious George August University of Gottingen, Germany, is an inspiration to all. Felicitated him and wished him the best," Naveen tweeted after felicitating Sesha here at the state secretariat.
 
 Sesha hails from Balasingha village in Naktideoli block of the district. The 24-year-old, who joined the German university as a research associate, earns Rs 2 lakh as a monthly stipend, said an official statement issued by the chief minister's office.
 
 The chief minister, in his Facebook post, said Sesha was born in an agricultural family. He lost his mother at a tender age and his father in his teens. "Despite immense struggles and suffering, he did not lose sight of his dreams and worked hard to achieve them," Naveen said.
