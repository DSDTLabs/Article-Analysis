ALLAHABAD: A   (BSP) leader on Monday night filed a police complaint against cabinet minister Nand Gopal Gupta ' ' for allegedly comparing party national president and former chief minister Mayawati with Ramayana character Shurpanakha. 

 The complaint, filed by party's zonal coordinator for Allahabad and Mirzapur, Ashok Kumar Gautam, at   police station states that "Nandi has degraded the dignity of a woman of lower caste and has also hurt the religious sentiments of people belonging to Scheduled Caste and Scheduled Tribes."
 
 Nandi, however, declined to comment on the issue on Tuesday.
 
 As part of the election related campaign, Nandi had made the alleged remarks at a rally at Durga Puja Park, Preetam Nagar in the city on March 4. Chief minister Yogi Adityanath was also present with other senior leaders at the rally.
 
 SSP Akash Kulhari told TOI, "The complaint against cabinet minister Nand Gopal Gupta 'Nandi' has been accepted by the Dhoomanganj police. We are now seeking legal opinion related to the case and steps will be taken accordingly."
 
 A social activist and lawyer, Charlie Prakash, also filed an online complaint with the Election Commission of India (ECI) alleging that 'Nandi' violated the model of conduct by delivering inflammatory speech and passing derogatory comments against   patron Mulayam Singh Yadav, former CM Akhilesh Yadav, former cabinet minister   and BSP president Mayawati at the election rally.
 
 Prakash demanded "stern action against Nandi by debarring him from further canvassing and making public speech till the elections are over".
