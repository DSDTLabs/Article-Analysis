CHANDIGARH: Chandigarh administration has received a total 60 of objections to the draft parking policy. The UT administration has invited objections and also suggestions from the people of the various wards before finalising the draft policy.

  The UT administration has set January 15 deadline to receive objections to the policy.
 
 The UT administration has received 10 objections, which were submitted physically by the residents. The rest was sent via email. After the lapse of the deadline, a committee will be formed to study the objections and suggestion. 
 
 Also, if required changes will be made in the draft rules on the recommendation of the committee.
 
 Various industrial associations, resident welfare association (RWA),   (CBM) and architects have submitted their objections and suggestions. 
 
 The proposed stringent provisions in the policy aimed at putting a check on the sale of cars have evoked strong response from the city residents.
 
 In the draft, the administration has proposed to put a cap on the number of cars to be sold in one quarter and charge road tax up to half of the price of the cars costing Rs 10 lakh and above and on the second car purchased.
