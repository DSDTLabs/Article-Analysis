AGRA: Even though the Uttar Pradesh Power Corporation Limited had set 100 days deadline to provide 1.45 lakh electricity connections in 21 districts of   jurisdiction, it has managed to give only 58,000 new electricity connections till date.

   , managing director of  , who was on a one-day day visit to  , which is a subsidiary of UPPCL, on Saturday said, “For 100 days, we had set a target of providing 1.45 lakh electricity connections in Agra, Mathura, Mainpuri,  , Hathras, Etawah, Etah, Farrukhabad, Firozabad, Kanpur city, Kanpur rural, Banda, Jhansi, Kannauj, Auraiya, Jalaun Urai, Hamirpur, Mahoba, Lalitpur, Chitrakoot and Kanshiram Nagar. So far, we have provided 58,000 connections. Action will be taken against those officials who fail to complete the task.”
  According to the MD, out of 7,500 hamlets, 2721 have power connections now.
 
 DVVNL is also working to expedite the upgradation of transformers. “Out of 2200 transformers, the UPPCL subsidiary has managed to upgrade 775 to provide glitch-free services. Aligarh has performed exceptionally well and managed to recover over 85 % revenue from pending bills while districts like Firozabad and Agra have performed poorly in this regard,” the MD added.
