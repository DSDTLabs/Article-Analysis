GUWAHATI: With two more deaths in Golaghat and Hojai districts on Friday, the flood toll has touched 75. At least 14 districts and 1.65 lakh people remain affected. 

 Meanwhile, actor   has urged his fans to provide relief to the flood-hit people here. Asking them to help the state, Kapoor tweeted, "Lakhs of families in Assam urgently need your support. Donate now." 
 
  
  
  
  
 
 
 Lakhs of people have been affected in Lakhimpur, Biswanath, Nalbari, Barpeta, Bongaigaon, Dhubri, Goalpara, Morigaon, Nagaon, Golaghat, Jorhat, Sivasagar, Karimganj and Cachar districts, the Assam State Disaster Management Authority (ASDMA) said.
 
  
  
 
 
 A Comptroller and Auditor General (CAG) report has observed several shortcomings in the country's flood management programme and its execution and noted that there has been a 60% shortfall in release of central funds to Assam for implementing those programme.
 
  
  
 
 
 On Assam, the CAG noted that the Centre was to share over Rs 2043 crore with the state for implementing 141 projects between 2007-08 and 2015-16. Of this, it however released Rs 812.22 crore - a shortfall of Rs 1230.97 crore.
 
 The animals in the Kaziranga National Park were badly affected and were running for their lives as the floods disrupted their habitat. The half of Kaziranga National Park was submerged and over 70 animals have died due to the floods. 
 
 Across the state, 1,23,357 animals and poultry are affected by the flood waters at present.
