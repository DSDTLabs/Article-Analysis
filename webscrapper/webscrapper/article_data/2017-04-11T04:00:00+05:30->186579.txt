Ahmedabad: Gujarat government has agreed to give 51 acres of land to the Gujarat Technological University (GTU) for setting up its campus. The university had demanded 120 acres of land at the Vishwakarma Engineering College.

 Earlier, the university officials had written to the state government seeking 120 acres of land in Gandhinagar for setting up its campus.
 
 In November last year, the government had announced to allot land to the university. According to GTU officials, the 120 acres included 10 acres for riverfront.
 
 The officials said that once the process of allotting the land begins, the GTU will be able to apply for affiliation from the University Grant Commission (UGC) and the All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE).
