CHENNAI: A training session for personnel of the intelligence section to use the 'mPassport'  , inaugurated by chief minister   on January 4, began at the   commissionerate on Wednesday.

 The digital application, to be used by police for field verification, will reduce the number of days of receiving the   by 10, said a press release. The training is being given by experts of an IT firm.
 
 The applications and documents that are submitted to the respective passport offices will be transferred to the intelligence department and then to the respective intelligence personnel of the area. "Since this takes a lot of time as most of the transfer is either postal or manual, the department introduced the mPassport," said Chennai city police commissioner  .
 
 The initiative which was started in Chennai will soon be started in Coimbatore and Madurai, the commissioner said.
