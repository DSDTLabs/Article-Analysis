CHENNAI: A former MP of the   has withdrawn his PIL against the nomination of V K   for the post of party  . 

 The PIL petitioner, K C Palanisamy, had sought nomination of a neutral person by the Election Commission of India to conduct the party poll for election of general secretary. 
 
 It was filed at the height of power tussle between Sasikala and former chief minister P   after the latter was made to resign for the former to be the CM. 
 
 The first bench of acting Chief Justice Huluvadi G Ramesh and Justice R Mahadevan, however, said it was not convinced with the submissions of the former MP in the writ petition. After the bench made it clear that it intended to dismiss the PIL, counsel for the petitioner sought to withdraw it.
