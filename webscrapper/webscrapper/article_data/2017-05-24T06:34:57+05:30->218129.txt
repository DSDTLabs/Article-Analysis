COIMBATORE: The body of a 54-year-old man was found in a highly decomposed state in a house at Athipalayam Pirivu here on Tuesday morning.  The deceased has been identified as Manu, 54, a native of Thrissur. He was employed in a lathe and stayed alone in the rented house for the last five years. He was not regular in going to work and spent most of the time at home.
 
 The neighbours had noticed him at the house on Sunday afternoon. On Tuesday, a foul smell began emanating from the house. The neighbours opened a window and found him dead. They alerted the Saravanampatti police who rushed to the spot and broke open the front door of the house.
 
 Police held an inquiry with the neigbours and said Manu hanged himself two days go. The door was bolted from inside. 
 
 Preliminary inquiries revealed that he was staying for five years at Maniyakarampalayam and later shifted to Athipalayam Pirivu. He rarely visited his home town in Thrissur district. Neighbours knew nothing about his family.
 
 “He had obtained Aadhaar card and other identity proofs in the Coimbatore address. So we could not trace his family members and relatives in Thrissur. We have sent his photographs to the Thrissur district police to trace the family members,” said a police officer.
 
 The body was sent to the Coimbatore Medical College and Hospital (CMCH) for postmortem. The Saravanampatti police registered a case under section 174 CrPC (suspicious death). Investigations is on.
