BHOPAL: Of the 21 loss-making PSUs of the  , the three   accounted for a whopping 96% of the losses — to the tune of Rs 5,126 crore, says the latest   

 And of those three companies, Madhya Kshetra Vidyut Vitaran Company Limited, which supplies power to Bhopal, alone accounts for over Rs 2,700 crore (51%) of the losses.
 
 The report of the AG says, “As per latest finalised Accounts, out of 58 working PSUs (including three statutory corporations), 31 PSUs earned profit of Rs 729.34 crore and 21 PSUs incurred loss of Rs 5,321.92 crore. Five working PSUs prepared their accounts on ‘no profit no loss’ basis and one working PSU did not finalise their first report on PSUs for the year ended 31 March 2016. The losses were mainly incurred by Madhya Pradesh Madhya Kshetra Vidyut Vitaran Company Limited (Rs 2,766.08 crore), Madhya Pradesh Pashchim Kshetra Vidyut Vitaran Company Limited (Rs 1,207.01 crore) and Madhya Pradesh Poorv Kshetra Vidyut Vitaran Company Limited (Rs1,161.58 crore).”
 
 What’s more, accumulated losses of PSUs doubled from Rs 15,348.27 crore in 2011-12 to Rs 31,609.10 crore in 2015-16, says the report. The major contributors in these accumulated losses were the PSUs distributing power in central, western and northern parts of the state. The Madhya Kshetra Vidyut Vitaran Company Limited was again the worst, accounting for a shocking Rs 13,998.21 crore of the accumulated losses, the report points out. 
 
 The report also points out that these power distribution companies are eating up a large chunk of the state Budget in the form of subsidies, loans and grants.
 
 The AG report says, “The Budgetary outgo towards equity, loans and grants/subsidies has increased from Rs 8,921.46 crore in 2014-15 to Rs 9,908 crore in 2015-16. The budgetary outgo of Rs 9,908 crore during 2015-16 included support of Rs 7,870.18 crore to three PSUs — Rs 3,268.72 crore to Madhya Pradesh Pashchim Kshetra Vidyut Vitaran Company Limited, Rs 3,007.37 crore to Madhya Pradesh Poorv Kshetra Vidyut Vitaran Company Limited and Rs 1,594.09 crore to Madhya Pradesh Madhya Kshetra Vidyut Vitaran Company Limited by way of equity, loan, subsidy and grants.”
