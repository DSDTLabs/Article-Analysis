NEW DELHI: In an effort to help MPs keep an eye on progress of developmental work in states, the   will launch a mobile application for parliamentarians on Wednesday. The app called -UPaAI (unified planning and analysis interface) or 'Solution' will provide an integrated platform for data on infrastructure and social indices for each constituency, information on about 50 Central schemes and   ( ) fund.

 The move is expected to provide district-wise information to the MP on his/her constituency and help him/her take better decisions related to MPLAD funds. It is also aimed at improving coordination at the district level. MPs often complain that state and district authorities keep them out of the loop while making decisions about their constituency.
 
 The app is being monitored by the   and developed by non-profit swaniti initiative and  . In the next phase, it will be extended to include state schemes, and bring district magistrates and members of legislative assemblies on same platform.
 
 Ank Aha director Shantanu Agarwal said, "Elected representatives will now have data on their constituency like literacy levels, number of schools, hospital beds among others, the amount of resources available to them in the palm of their hands. This will streamline decision-making related to development work.''
 
 About 20 MPs have already started using the app and the rest will be encouraged to use as it is launched. Ank Aha will also conduct training workshops during the Parliament session to bring MPs up to speed.
