GURUGRAM: One of the owners of Ipsa Club in Sahara Mall, Shyambir, approached police on Monday against his ex-business partner’s brother-in-law and 4-5 of his friends. 

 He alleged that the accused forcefully entered the club on the intervening night of Friday and Saturday, and robbed him of Rs 50,000 in  , a gold chain and beat him up. Shyambir said that they also pointed a pistol at him, threatening to kill him. 
 
 An FIR was registered against all accused persons at Sector 29 police station. 
 
 Shyambir, who is a resident of Delhi’s Fatehpur Beri, said, “Our ex-partner’s brother-in-law Neeraj came with his friends and thrashed me. One of them snatched my gold chain and took Rs 50,000 cash from the counter. He demanded more money from me, saying I must pay him if I want to run the club.”
 
 An FIR was registered against all accused persons under sections 323 (causing hurt), 380 (theft), 452 (trespassing), 506 (criminal intimidation), and 34 (common intention) of the IPC and Section 25-54-59 of the Arms Act at Sector 29 police station. 
 
 “The entire act was captured on the CCTV footage of the club. We will take action as per the law after verifying the facts,” said ASI Rajesh Kumar, the investigating officer.
