KOLKATA: Calcutta high court on Wednesday expressed its unhappiness over the progress made by the CID in its probe into the January 17  . Two persons were killed in the violence on that day. On February 8, Justice   had observed that the court will monitor the CID probe. On Wednesday, the state submitted that it had been established that the bullet found in one of the bodies was fired from an improvised firearm. 

 “But do you know who killed him,” Justice Bagchi wanted to know from the state counsel. On receiving an answer in the negative, the court wanted to know: “Then what is the investigation’s progress? You need to find out the identity of the man behind the death. Such things shouldn’t be delayed.”
