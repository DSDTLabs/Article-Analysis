LUCKNOW: Directing officials to provide necessary details at the earliest to the   for the preparation of the 'Infrastructure Framework Plan', chief secretary Rajive Kumar has said that the report needs to be ready by June this year. 

 The report, said an official, would suggest ways of increasing capital investment in the state, creating job opportunities, preparing quality infrastructure and providing support to macro, medium and large scale industries. "The June deadline was set by the chief secretary in a meeting held with the   on Monday. Kumar has also asked officials to prepare a plan for improving quality of potable water, electricity supply and last mile connectivity in the 18 districts that have been identified as potential employment zones," said an official.
 
 The 18 districts have been divided into six zones by ADB.
