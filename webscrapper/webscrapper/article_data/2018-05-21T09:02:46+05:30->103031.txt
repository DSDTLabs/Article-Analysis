PANCHKULA: A day after cops booked two persons and seized a truck, JCB and tractor-trolley for illegal sand mining, another person was nabbed on Sunday while carrying out   in fields of Raipur Rani without a permit.

 As per the officials of the mining department , A K Chauhan, a mining guard was informed by the local villagers that some persons were spotted excavating earth from a field located in   village in Raipur Rani following which he immediately filed a complaint with the cops.
 
 Acting swift on the plaint filed by Chauhan, a team of mining officials along with cops conducted a raid and found a Kobelco machine specifically modified for mining operating in the field. When the officials asked the person to show mining permit, he failed to produce it.
 
 Subsequently, the cops seized the vehicle and booked the person under section 188 (Disobedience to order duly promulgated by public servant) and 379 (punishment for theft) of the Indian Penal Code (IPC) and section 21(4), 4(1) and 4(1)-A of Mines and Mineral Act in Raipur Rani police station.
 
 Mining officer Chauhan, said, “Local villagers informed me about the illegal activity going on in the area. Acting on the basis of a complaint filed by the locals, we constituted a team and with the help of cops stopped the illicit act.”
 
 Balram Singh, head constable and invesitgating officer, said, “Machinery and truck has been seized and further investigations in the case are underway.”
 
 The cops have   a total of 26 persons since January for illegal mining. All the accused have been nabbed from mining-prone areas of Pinjore, Raipur Rani and Barwala blocks of the district.
