THIRUVANANTHAPURAM: The special investigation team probing the telecast of a sleaze audio tape by a vernacular news   visited the channel’s office and recorded the statements of its employees, on Sunday afternoon.

 The team also took a detailed note of the functioning of the channel and initiated steps to examine the controversial audio tape.
 
 Meanwhile, channel CEO A Ajith Kumar, the second accused in the case, is learnt to have approached the court seeking anticipatory bail.
 
 According to the FIR registered by police, the accused, as part of the channel’s marketing publicity, conspired to defame former minister A K   and telecasted the sleazy audio through the television channel and its Facebook page on March 26.
 
 Police have invoked Section 67A of the Information Technology Act and Section 120B of the IPC in the case. Section 67A of the IT Act says, whoever publishes or transmits or causes to be published or transmitted in the electronic form any material which contains sexually explicit act or conduct shall be punished on first conviction with imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to five years and with fine which may extend to 10 lakh rupees.
 
 News channel chairman Sajan Varghese is the first accused in the case. The list of accused also includes coordinating editors M B Santhosh and Rishi K Manoj, investigation team leader   alias S Narayanan, news editors Lakshmi Mohan, Firoz Sali Mohammed and S V Pradeep and the girl who was involved in the tape. State police chief Loknath Behera said that an impartial investigation would be conducted in the matter.
