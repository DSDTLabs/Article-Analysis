Chennai: With the markets on a steady rise, top honchos of leading financial services companies are offloading their stakes on the bourses. Keki Mistry, vice chairman and CEO, Housing Development Finance Corporation (HDFC), the country’s largest mortgage lender, Chanda Kochhar, managing director (MD) and CEO of ICICI Bank, the country’s largest private sector bank by consolidated assets and Shikha Sharma, MD and CEO of Axis Bank are among those who are on a selling spree. These three honchos have disposed shares to the tune of Rs 23 crore so far in March. Mistry sold a total of 1.15 lakh shares in HDFC in two separate transactions on March 14. The sale earned him Rs. 16.29 crore. The HDFC scrip hit a 52-week high of Rs 1,464.5 on the BSE on March 20. The scrip has surged 15.5% so far in 2017. The benchmark Sensex and the broader Nifty have gained 11.4% and 11.9% respectively during the timeframe. Kochhar offloaded 1 lakh shares in ICICI Bank on March 14 that earned her around Rs. 2.86 crore. The scrip has gained about 7.8% in the current year. Vijay Chandok, executive director, ICICI Bank sold 15150 shares in the bank in three separate transactions between February and March, BSE data showed. Sandeep Batra, a director in the bank, has sold 48500 shares since February 27.  Shikha Sharma disposed 75,000 shares in Axis Bank, the country’s third largest private sector bank, in three separate transactions in March for a total consideration of about Rs. 3.87 crore. The Axis scrip has increased 12.1% so far in 2017. Incidentally, Shikha has been a big seller of the Axis scrip having disposed 2.75 lakh shares during the second quarter of 2016-17 that netted Rs. 16.07 crore. While Uday S Kotak, the vice chairman and MD of Kotak Mahindra Bank, offloaded 2.76 crore shares for about Rs 2,255 crore on March 3 in compliance with RBI’s directions to reduce promoter holdings, C Jayaram, a director in the bank, was another big seller during the month. Jayaram disposed 55000 shares on the bank for about Rs 4.44 crore on March 3. The Kotak Mahindra Bank scrip touched a 52-week high of Rs 861.8 on March 20 and has surged 18.8% in 2017.
