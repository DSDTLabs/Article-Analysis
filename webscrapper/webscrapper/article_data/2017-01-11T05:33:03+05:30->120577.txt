 On the first day of a two-day orientation programme, about 276 drivers were tested of whom 109 were diagnosed to be diabetic, a medical check by Toyota Kirloskar Motor (TKM) revealed. The medical camp also brought to light that many cab drivers have poor vision which results in poor driving on the roads.
 
 The tests revealed that drivers are under tremendous stress caused by various problems, including long working hours and lack of nutrition. 
 
 TKM officials also said that they are providing training to cab drivers on various aspects like etiquettes to be followed with the passengers, problems of drink driving and steps to be taken to handle accidents that occur on highways.
