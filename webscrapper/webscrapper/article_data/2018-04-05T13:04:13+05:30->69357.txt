JALANDHAR: Around 30 cases were registered in different police stations of four districts of   region on Wednesday for blockade of roads and unlawful assembly. Police have also invoked provisions of the national highway Act in cases where protesters resorted to road blockades. 

 However, all cases have been registered against unidentified persons. 
 
 Around 10 cases each were registered under the jurisdiction of the   police commissionerate and Jalandhar (rural) police where protesters blocked traffic at several places. Cases have also been registered in Kapurthala, Nawanshahr and   districts. 
 
 “Around 30 cases have been registered at different police stations of Doaba region,” said a police official. Police have invoked sections 145 (joining or continuing in unlawful assembly, knowing it has been commanded to disperse), 188 (disobedience to order duly promulgated by public servant), 283 (danger or obstruction in public way or line of navigation) and 431 (mischief by injury to public road, bridge, river or channel) of the IPC and provisions of the National Highway Act pertaining to blocking of roads.
