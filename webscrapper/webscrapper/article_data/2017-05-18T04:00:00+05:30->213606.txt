 The high court issued notice to the state government, seeking an answer to the question of constitutional validity of certain provisions of the fee regulation law. Further hearing shall be on June 12, when many other petitions filed by private schools are scheduled to be heard.
 
 Private self-financed schools have taken strong objection to the diktat that schools charging fees above than the fixed caps — Rs 15,000 for primary schools, Rs 25,000 for secondary and higher secondary schools in general stream and Rs 27,000 for higher secondary in science stream — should submit a proposal for approval of their fee structure to the fee regulation committee. 
 
 The state government’s fee regulation law has been challenged by city-based Prakash School, a federation of CBSE schools, Anand Niketan School as well as ICSE board-affiliated schools. Many more private schools are planning to file petitions in the high court.
