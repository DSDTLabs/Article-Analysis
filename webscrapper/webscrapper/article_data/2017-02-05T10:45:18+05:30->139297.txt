INDORE: Over 1,200 people on Saturday were caught riding their two-wheelers without helmet. Traffic cops collected fine of around Rs 3 lakh from violators. 

 Traffic police authorities are running a special drive to reinforce compulsory helmet rule in city. Officials said that they have once again launched the drive to curb motorists from riding without helmets on city roads. They said that around 11 checking points have been made in and around the city to check on motorists without helmet.
 
 The list of checkpoints included Bengali square, Madhumilan square, Khajrana square, Star square, Pipliyahana square and Radisson square in the east part of the city while in the west of the city areas like Mhow Naka, Gangwal Bus Stand, Yashwant Road,   Square and Transport Nagar have been included.
