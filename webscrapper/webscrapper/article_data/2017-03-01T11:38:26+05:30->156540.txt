PATNA: A   court in Patna on Tuesday awarded three-year   to a retired central excise and customs superintendent, Mohd Qasim Ansari, for amassing assets disproportionate to his known sources of income.

 Special court-II judge Sarvajeet also slapped a fine of Rs 10 lakh on the 64-year-old, who was present in the court at the time of judgement.
 
 Special PP Akhtar H Khan said the CBI had lodged an FIR against Ansari on September 19, 2000 in connection with the disproportionate assets case. It filed a charegesheet on December 2, 2002.
 
 “Ansari had made immovable property and had bank deposits worth Rs 24.65 lakh during the scrutiny period from January 1, 1981 to September 20, 2000 against Rs 12.23 lakh calculated on the basis of his known sources of income,” Khan said.
 
 During the period, Ansari was posted as an inspector in the Patna collectorate and was later elevated as the superintendent.
 
 The court simultaneously granted interim bail to Ansari. He is a resident of   under the Shastri Nagar police station area in Patna.
 
 In another judgement, the special CBI court-I of judge Manoj Kumar Singh on Tuesday awarded five-year RI to Samastipur Kshetriya Grameen Bank’s suspended cashier Arvind Kumar Singh for his involvement in embezzlement of Rs 25 lakh 25 years ago. 
 
 The court also slapped a fine of Rs 30,000 on the convict. In addition, the court ordered him to deposit Rs 1.72 lakh with 6% interest from the date of institution of FIR on July 13, 1993.
 
 According to special PP Khan, another cashier Arun Kumar Singh was acquitted by the court for lack of evidence. Yet another accused, Shailesh Bhushan, the then branch manager, died during the course of trial, Khan said, adding the trio embezzled Rs 25.25 lakh by forging account books of the bank’s branch at Shahpur Undi in Samastipur district.
 
 The convict was forwarded to jail after the judgement.
