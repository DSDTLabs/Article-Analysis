AHMEDABAD: Indian   rescued a patient onboard a merchant ship off   coast of Saturday night. The patient was administrated the first aid and shifted to a private hospital. 

 According to   sources, they received a distress signal from Maritime Rescue Co-ordinator, Mumbai, about the ailing crew member Awhad Pranav Somnath. The ship was then about 144 nautical miles off Porbandar coast. 
 
 The ship was asked to move towards the coast and in the meantime an ICG interceptor was dispatched with medical crew. 
 
 “About eight nautical miles off Porbandar coast, the patient was shifted and brought on the coast,” added a source.
