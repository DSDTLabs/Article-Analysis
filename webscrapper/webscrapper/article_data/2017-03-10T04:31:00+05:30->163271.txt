Gandhinagar: Grants for police modernization decreased by around 70% in the state. The government admitted in the state assembly that the centre government has released Rs 22.47 crore in 2015-16 against Rs 72.65 crore in 2014-15.

 In a written reply to a question by Danilimda MLA Shailesh Parmar, the government said that the state government in 2015-16 requested for a grant of Rs 28.46 crore, of which the Centre allocated Rs 22.47 crore, for police modernization. The government further said that it has so far spent just Rs 13.60 crore on the modernization. This was 70% less than the previous year.
 
 In reply to another question by Ranjit Gilitwala, the Surat East MLA, the government said it has installed CCTV cameras in 610 police stations in the state at the cost of Rs 62 crore. Pradipsinh Jadeja, minister of state for home, said that in all 7,237 CCTV cameras have been installed in police stations.
