 Attributing the cloudy conditions to the active western disturbances, Jaipur Met office said that some places in the state will see a dip in mercury. In some parts of northern and eastern Rajasthan, mild to moderate showers are likely in next couple of days while weather would be mainly dry in rest of the state. 
 
 Jaipurites woke up to a warm morning as a minimum temperature of 29.8 degrees Celsius, almost 4 degrees above normal, was recorded. Jaipur on Sunday witnessed the hottest day of the season so far as a maximum temperature of 44.5 degrees Celsius, almost 5 degrees above normal, was recorded. 
 
 However, post afternoon heavy dust storm and mild showers lashed many parts of the city. High velocity winds also hit power supply in many localities. Several sign boards got damaged while a few trees were uprooted. 
 
 Churu was followed by Bikaner where a maximum temperature of 46.2 degrees Celsius was recorded, Jaisalmer and Barmer logged 46 degrees Celsius each, Sriganganagar and Kota 45.5 degrees Celsius each, Pilani 45.1, Jodhpur 44.3, Ajmer 43.8 and a maximum of 41.5 degrees Celsius was recorded in Ajmer.
