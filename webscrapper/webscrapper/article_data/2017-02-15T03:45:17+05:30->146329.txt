GURUGRAM: A 27-year-old man allegedly threatened to throw acid on a   as well as molest her on Monday while she was on her way to Khandewla village, 21km from Gurgaon. The accused was thrashed in public when the girl raised an alarm. 

 He was arrested the same day and produced in city court on Tuesday and sent to judicial custody.
 
 According to the police, the accused is Manish, a resident of Rampur village. The girl, who resides in Khandewla, was returning home from her college in Gurgaon by train. When the student alighted at Pataudi and headed towards her house, Manish started stalking her. He allegedly stopped her and threatened to throw acid on her if she opposed him. He also allegedly threatened to kill her and her family. Somehow, the girl managed to flee and raised an alarm after reaching the main road.
 
 Shopkeepers and passersby gathered, caught the accused and thrashed him before informing the police. 
 
 An FIR was registered against Manish under sections 342 (wrongful confinement), 354 (molestation) and 506 (criminal intimidation) of the Indian Penal Code at Pataudi police station. “The accused said he had been stalking the girl for many days,” said assistant sub-inspector Pawan Kumar, the investigating officer of the case.
