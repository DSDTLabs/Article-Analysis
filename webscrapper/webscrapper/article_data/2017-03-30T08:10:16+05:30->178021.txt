 Officials feel the spurt was a fallout of  . "Last year, the total tax revenue in the region was 51,490 crore. This year, till November, we had the income disclosure scheme (IDS) and after that Pradhan Mantri Garib Kalyan Yojana (PMGKY) till March 31. Because of the two schemes, the increase in tax so far is 15%," a senior I-T official told TOI. 
 
 The Tamil Nadu region is the fourth highest in generating tax revenue. 
 
 Top on the chart is Mumbai, followed by New Delhi and Bangalore. "There are around 27 lakh tax payers in TN and Pondy region. Out of them, 20 big companies and high networth individuals contribute a major share. The maximum tax of 1,800 crore was paid by a company," said the official. 
 
 Apart from the two Central government schemes, the department was better placed this year, thanks to a new software that has made tax evasion difficult. 
 
 "We got the list of deposits from all banks only on January 31. But within 16 days, we were able to detect high-level cash deposits in bank accounts. We found that several doctors, jewellers and other businessmen had deposited huge amount of cash soon after demonetisation," the official said.
 
 The department sent notices and many of them replied and also paid tax through online. "But some of them did not reply. We summoned them and asked them to show their source of money. Many of them joined the  ," he said.
 
 Linking Aadhaar with PAN would make the job of tax officials in detecting black money easy, the official said.
 
 Meanwhile, Tamil Nadu region also leads in the country in terms of filing cases against companies and individuals for tax evasion. In 2016-17 alone, 170 cases were filed against companies and individuals who did not pay tax.
