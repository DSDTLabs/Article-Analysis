PANAJI:   Mining Corporation, a subsidiary of  , has asked around 1,800 staff employed at its Surla, Codli and Sonshi mines not to report to work with immediate effect.

 The conglomerate has stated that the workers were no longer required for extraction and transportation of iron ore from the three mines following the closure of iron ore mining in the state. 
 
 A few days ago, the company had issued a similar order to workers at its Bicholim mines. No one has been laid off. 
 
 Vedanta has attributed the notice to “circumstances beyond control” and because of government instructions to stop ore extraction on account of the SC order. A spokesperson said the notice was aimed at reducing operating costs.
