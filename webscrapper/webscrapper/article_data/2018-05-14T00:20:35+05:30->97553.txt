Kolkata: Three days after the Calcutta HC order, banning   festivals on the occasion of Folharini Kali Puja between May 12 and May 16, various government departments, agencies and the citizens of Bengal came together in curbing the illegal hunting of wild animals protected under wildlife protection act in various parts of south Bengal.

 In Howrah’s Deulti, foresters arrested one Manish Chowdhury, who’s from Bihar, and found carcasses of a palm civet, a juvenile changeable hawk eagle (protected under Schedule I of the Wildlife Protection Act, 1972) and a few small birds. Additional divisional forest officer of Howrah, Srikanta Ghosh, said that he would be produced in court on Monday.
 
 Over 50 civic volunteers on behalf of Zero Hunting Alliance came forward to help the forest department and the Railway Protection Force on Sunday. The HC, on May 10, instructed all relevant authorities to help foresters to make sure that no hunting takes place on the occasion of Folharini Kali Puja.
 
 Sources said the hunting activities are expected to pick up pace from Tuesday after voting for panchayat polls gets over on Monday. Forest department had put up posters against hunting festivals at various places in East Midnapore and Howrah and also did miking to raise awareness.
