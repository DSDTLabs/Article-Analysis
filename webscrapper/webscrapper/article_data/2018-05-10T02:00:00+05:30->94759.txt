 At a review meeting which was also attended by ADG (CID) Vinay Kumar, the DGP directed the Patna police to prevent incidents of loot taking place especially in Kankarbagh and Kadamkuan localities of Patna. 
 
 “The DGP said the   should be disposed of and accountability fixed for such large number of pending cases,” the ADG said.
 
 According to official sources, 17,193 cases were pending in Patna till December 2017 while this figure was 13,241 till December 2016. By the end of 2015, 11,270 cases were pending.
 
 ADG Kumar said prohibition cases were only one of the reasons for pending cases. “There are other factors too,” Kumar said.
 
 The ADG said the number of pending cases must not be higher than three times the number of cases instituted during a month at end of that month. “This is the standard rule,” he said.
 
 Sources said around 2,500 cases, on an average, are lodged every month in Patna. 
 
 The DGP and ADG also directed the Nalanda police to ensure speedy investigation and trial of communal cases. “Officials were instructed to seek sanction from the government for prosecuting accused in communal cases. Section 153A of the Indian Penal Code makes government sanctions mandatory for prosecuting such accused,” he said.
