NAGPUR: About 160 prisoners of Nagpur Central Jail appeared for the Gandhian thoughts exam on Sunday, which is jointly conducted by  , Mumbai Sarvoday Mandal and Sarvoday Ashram every year.

 Ravindra  , trustee of Sahyog Trust, who initiated the concept, said about 130 men and 30 women inmates appeared for the exam. These included inmates on death row as well the ones awaiting trial in special cell billet and barracks.
 
 Some under-trials chose to appear in English medium apart from the standard Hindi and Marathi. Study material was provided to inmates by the trust which included books written on Mahatma Gandhi and his way of life.
