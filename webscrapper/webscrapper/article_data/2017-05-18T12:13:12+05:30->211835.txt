 ’ saw a phenomenal run at the Indian box-office upon its release in December 2016. With the film having released in China in the first week of May, the Nitesh Tiwari-directed film has seen a rise in its worldwide collection once again. 
 
 ‘Dangal’ has collected $59.70 million (Rs 382 crore) in ten days, according to a report on Boxofficeindia.com. The report further stated that the business of the film till date stands at Rs 1100 crore (in all languages) adding that the worldwide total is difficult to predict at this point.
  
 ‘Dangal’, which tells the story of Indian wrestler and coach   and how he trained his daughters   and Babita Kumari to become world-class wrestlers, also became the first   film to cross the 360-crore mark and is the highest grossing film in Hindi cinema. The film also stars Fatima Sana Shaikh, Sanya Malhotra, Sakshi Tanwar, Zaira Wasim and Suhani Bhatnagar.
