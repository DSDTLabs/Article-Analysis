CHENNAI: The combined storage levels of 31 major reservoirs in five south Indian states has dropped by 2% in a week. The water storage has declined to 22% from 24% a week ago.

 There are 31 reservoirs in  ,  ,  ,   and   under the monitoring of the Central Water Commission (CWC). They have total live storage capacity of 51.59 BCM. 
 
 The current total live storage available in these reservoirs is 11.31 BCM which is 22% of total live storage capacity of these reservoirs, according to an official release from the Union ministry of water resource.
 
 The storage during corresponding period of last year was 23%, and average storage of last ten years during corresponding period was 39% of live storage capacity of these reservoirs. 
 
 "Thus, storage during current year is less than the corresponding period of last year and is also less than the average storage of last ten years during the corresponding period," the statement added.
  
 Eight days ago, the total live storage available in these reservoirs is 12.19 BCM, which was 24% of total live storage capacity of these reservoirs in the southern states.
