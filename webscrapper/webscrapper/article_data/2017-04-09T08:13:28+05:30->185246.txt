ROHTAK: Three months after the murder of two Indian National Lok Dal (INLD) workers in Chautala village of  , Haryana chief minister Manohar Lal   on Saturday accepted the demand of getting the case investigated from the   ( ).

 Two party workers, Amit and Satbir, were shot dead at the kinnow waxing plant of Pardeep Godara, a close aide of senior INLD leader Abhay Singh Chautala, on January 11, 2017. To arrest the killers, a five-member police team headed by additional superintendent of police (ASP) Himanshu Garg was formed, who found that the deceased had an old enmity with notorious gangster Chhotu Bhat.
 
 Police said they had arrested three persons of Chautala village in connection with the murders but the main conspirator was still at large. Recently, a meeting was held at Chautala village in which residents of the village, which is also the native village of former CM Om Parkash Chautala, demanded a CBI probe.
 
 The panchayat formed an 11-member committee to prepare stratergy to put pressure on the government. The panchayat also had warned of not sending their wards to governmetn schools if the government did not order CBI inquiry in the high-profile case.
 
 On Saturday, residents gathered at the Barnala road to meet CM Khattar who was on his visit to Sirsa. However, they were stopped from moving close to the CM by police. A delegation of 11 members was permitted to meet Khattar at the faculty house in Chaudhary Devi Lal University (CDLU), Sirsa after some time.
 
 Sirsa deputy dommissioner Sharandeep Kaur and superintendent of police (SP) Satinder Gupta were also present on the spot. Senior Congress leader K V Singh, who was officer on special duty (OSD) to former CM Bhupinder Singh Hooda, led the delegation and put forward their demand to get the case probed by the premier investigation agency. Accepting their demand, CM Khattar promised them of high-level probe in the case.
