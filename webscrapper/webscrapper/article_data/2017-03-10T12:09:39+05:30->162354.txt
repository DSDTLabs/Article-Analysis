MADURAI: The Madurai bench of the Madras high court on Thursday directed the central and state governments to complete   of the super speciality wing of the  , Madurai within six months. 

 The   headed by   gave this direction after it heard a petition filed by one  .
 
 During hearing, the petitioner’s side said that under the Prime Minister Swathya Suraksha Yojana phase – II, the super speciality wing was going to be set up at a cost of Rs 150 crore with 300 beds. The construction work began a few years ago.
 
 As per schedule, the work should have been completed in 2015, but it was still going on. In the meantime, the work was stopped by creating artificial sand shortage, which is against the norms of World Health Organization’s rules and regulation. 
 
 The main aim of the project is to ensure quality medial treatment to poor patients coming from southern districts including Sivaganga, Ramanathapuram, Theni, Dindigul, Tuticorin, Tirunelveli and Kanyakumari, the petitioner said.
 
 The speciality wing would consist of departments including neurology, nephrology, cardiology and urology. Due to non-completion of project work, providing quality treatment to poor people is getting delayed, the petitioner contended. 
 
 Following it, the bench ordered the central and state’s health and family welfare departments to complete the construction work in six months.
