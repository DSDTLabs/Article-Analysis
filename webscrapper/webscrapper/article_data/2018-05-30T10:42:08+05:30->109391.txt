COIMBATORE: The Directorate of Vigilance and Anti-Corruption ( ), Coimbatore, has registered a disproportionate assets case against  , 57, professor of surgery at the  , and his wife  , 56, who is working as junior engineer in Chennai. 

 
 According to the DVAC, the couple is in possession of pecuniary resources and properties disproportionate to their known source of income. 
 
 The inspector of police, DVAC, Coimbatore, M Kanagasabapathy, registered a case against the couple on May 25 under sections 13 (2) read with 12 (1) (e) of the Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988. 
 
 According to DVAC, Mallika was working as junior engineer at the office of the divisional engineer, National Highways, Coimbatore, since January 1, 2008. She hailed from Aarani in   district and she got married to Dr V Elango in 1984. The couple has two sons. 
 
 Mallika joined in the government service as an assistant draftsman at the office of divisional engineer, highways, Coimbatore division on November 21, 1981. She was promoted to junior drafting officer on January 30, 1997 and later she was promoted as junior engineer in 2010. 
 
 The couple was in possession of pecuniary resources to the tune of Rs 44.89 lakh in their name and in names of their family members as on January 1, 2008. It was the beginning of the check period.
 
 The couple was in possession of pecuniary resources and properties to the tune of Rs 1.97 crore on December 31, 2013, which was the end of the check period. 
 
 The couple had gained income from the lawful sources to the tune of Rs 1.97 crore and incurred expenses to the tune of Rs 1.35 crore during the check period. They could have saved Rs 61.79 lakh in the same period. However, the couple has acquired properties to the tune of Rs 1.52 crore during the check period. 
 
 But the couple is in possession and acquired pecuniary resources and properties to the tune of Rs 90.51 lakh, which is disproportionate to their known source of income during the check period. 
 
 This information disclosed the commission of the offenses of criminal misconduct by a public servant. 
 
 The FIR was submitted to the special court for the DVAC cases and the police started the investigation.
