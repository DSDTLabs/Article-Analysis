RAIPUR: Chief minister   ushered new year with a morning   and later had lunch with labourers in his constituency Rajnandgaon. 

 He also inaugurated the Deendayal Upadhyaya labour canteen scheme for the workers. 
 
 Chief minister Raman Singh along with his wife   on Monday morning took a stroll at Gandhi Udyan, close to his official residence. A large number of people who were present there extended New Year greetings to the chief minister and also clicked a selfie.
 
 Later, in the afternoon, Raman Singh launched a labour food scheme under the Deendayal Upadhyaya labour canteen scheme for the workers. As per the information, the government will provide food for the workers at the cost of Rs10 per plate. Initially, the scheme will be operational in 11 districts of the state. Around 30 low cost food centres would be opened. In the first phase of the scheme, the centres will be opened in Raipur, Durg, Bilaspur, Rajnandgaon and other seven cities of the state. At present, around 700 daily wage workers in the state capital are being served food by the state government.
