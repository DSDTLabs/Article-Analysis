Panaji: The Goa State   Commission on Wednesday issued notices directing the chief secretary, director of health services and dean of Goa Medical College and hospital to file a report within four weeks in connection with a complaint highlighting the poor emergency care in the state. 

 The complaint was filed on Tuesday stating that the government’s 108 ambulance service was not well equipped with life saving devices and arrives late when responding to emergencies.
 
 In his complaint advocate Aires Rodrigues stated that it took almost 45 minutes for the 108 ambulance to arrive at Patto on April 7 and went on to state that had it been fitted with the required life saving devices, advocate Satish Sonak’s life could have been saved. tnn
