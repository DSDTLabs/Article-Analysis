Vadodara: All eyes are set on Friday’s strike call given by parents association. Ahead of the strike call, the impasse between the agitated parents and managements of private schools continued in the city on Thursday.

 The Vadodara District Parents Association (VDPA) has declared its support to the statewide strike call given by the All Gujarat Parents Association to press for earliest implementation of the Gujarat Self-Financed Schools (Regulation of Fees) Act 2017.
 
 Interestingly, a couple of schools in the city have declared Friday as a joint holiday “on the occasion of Uttarayan” till January 15. 
 
 The Vadodara district education officer issued a cautionary circular asking the management of schools to give a patient hearing to parents .
 
 On Thursday, the Vadodara zonal fee regulation committee (FRC) also held a meeting in which it heard proposals of schools to collect their annual fees above the capped fees of Rs 15,000, Rs 25,000 and Rs 27,000 for primary, middle and high school levels as stated in the Act. 
 
 A total of 96 schools in seven districts that fall in Vadodara zone have submitted affidavits stating that they are ready to reduce fees in the range of Rs 2,000 to over Rs 10,000 before the FRC. 
 
 While there are 27 schools which are ready to reduce fees up to Rs 2,000, 24 other schools are ready to reduce fees between Rs 2,000 and Rs 5,000, 21 schools in the range of Rs 5,000 and Rs 10,000 and 24 other schools which will reduce more than Rs 10,000 as fees. 
 
 Of these, there are nine schools that fall in Vadodara city and district. FRC will now hold its second meeting on January 23 while it has decided to give a second chance to schools which have so far not submitted their proposals. 
 
 The committee will conduct hearing of such schools on January 30 and January 31 as well as on February 5 and February 6.
