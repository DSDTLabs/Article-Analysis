CHENNAI: Former chief minister J Jayalalithaa’s lawyer and designated senior advocate, B Kumar, has been named as an accused in the AIADMK ‘two leaves’ symbol case involving   and Sukesh Chandrasekhar.

 With a lookout circular (LOC) issued against the advocate, Kumar was stopped at Chennai airport on his way to the US.
 
 The Delhi police team probing the case has filed a chargesheet. Special judge Kiran Bhansal trying the Prevention of Corruption Act cases of the   ( ) at Tis Hazari Court in New Delhi mentioned that it was Kumar who put Sukeshin touch with Dhinakaran.
 
 The inter-state cell of the crime branch of the Delhi police has registered a case against the suspects Sukesh Chandrasekhar, Dhinakaran and seven others, including senior counsel Kumar, under Sections 8 of the Prevention of Corruption Act, and IPC Sections 419 (punishment for cheating and personation), 420 (cheating), 467 (forging document), 468 (forgery for the purpose of cheating), 471 (using a forged document as a genuine one), 474 (knowing a document is forged and intending touseit as genuine), 201(causing disappearance of evidence of offence), 170 (impersonating a public servant) and 120-B (criminal conspiracy).
 
 The Delhi police have named T T V Dhinakaran as the main accused, while the others named are T P Mallikarjun, Nathu Singh alias Naresh, Sukash alias Sukesh Chandrasekhar alias Kamlesh, Lalit Kumar K Shah alias Babu Bhai, Pulkit Kundra, B Kumar, Jai Vikram Haran alias Jai Bhai and Narendar Jain.
 
 Kumar has been booked under the IPC Sections 120-B (criminal conspiracy) and 201 (causing disappearance of evidence of offence) and Section 8 of PC Act.
 
 Policeofficialssaidseizure of Kumar’s mobile phone would help gather evidence to prove that the lawyer was in touch with Dhinakaran as well as Sukesh and had hatched a criminal conspiracy to get a favourable order from the ECI.
