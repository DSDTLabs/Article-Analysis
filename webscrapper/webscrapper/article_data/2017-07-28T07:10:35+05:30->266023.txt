CHENNAI: Seeing protagonists of the Tollywood epic drama `Baahubali 2: The Conclusion' shooting three arrows at a time from a single bow, one may have wondered if it was really possible to do so or it was just a technique that director S S Rajamouli invented for the film.

 The hero stones belonging to the Vijayanagara period discovered near Thally here on Thursday , however, seem to suggest it was a centuries-old technique of war and a sign of bravery. A team from   on Thursday found three hero stones at Gikur, a village near Thally close to   border in   district. Two showed fighters with three arrows in the right hand and a bow in the left hand, while the third was of a a fighter with sword and shield.
 
 According to Aram A Krishnan, president of the Aram Archaeological Research Centre, one stone depicted two fighters at war.
 
 “The fighter with sword and shield might have be longed to the Kangar community, the members of which are known for their bravery,“ Krishnan further said.
 
 Gikur is situated close to  's border with Karnataka. Krishnan said the kings who ruled these regions might have deployed thousands of fighters on the borders to save their kingdoms from enemies.
 
 According to the team of archaeologists which made the discovery, the hero stones belong to the 13th or 14th centuries.
