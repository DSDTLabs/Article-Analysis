CHANDIGARH: A district consumer disputes redressal forum here has directed an insurance company to pay Rs 50,000 as compensation for mental agony caused to a   resident when the company refused to pay the claim, which he had filed after losing his insured vehicle. The company was also directed to pay Rs 3.96 lakh with 9% per annum interest. 

 The complainant, Lalit  , got his car insured from the insurance company on June 5, 2013, and the validity of the agreement was till June 4, 2014.
 
 Singla told the court that on May 3, 2014, his vehicle was stolen. The matter was reported to police following which an FIR was registered. The cops on October 6, 2014 arrested   and   and filed a challan against them before a court in Panchkula, but failed to recover the vehicle from them. After failing to find the vehicle, the complainant applied for the claim. 
 
 "The company did not clear the claim and started giving excuses," Singla said.
 
 Thereafter, a consumer complaint was filed and the matter was taken to a lok adalat on June 3, 2016, which said that the complainant would submit the required documents and the key of the vehicle to the insurance company after which his claim would be processed within one month. "Compliance was done and the documents were supplied, but the claim was neither accepted nor repudiated," the complainant said.
 
 In its reply, the insurance company said that the complainant failed to produce documents. 
 
 The forum after hearing both the sides directed the company to "immediately indemnify the complainant by making payment of Rs 3.96 lakh along with 9% per annum interest from the date of presentation of the complaint till realisation, and pay Rs 50,000 to the complainant as compensation for deficiency in service, mental agony and harassment caused to him. It also directed the insurance company to pay Rs 10,000 as litigation expenses.
