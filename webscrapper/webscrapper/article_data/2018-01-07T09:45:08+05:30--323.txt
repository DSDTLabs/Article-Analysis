LUCKNOW: Bahujan Samaj Party (BSP) and Congress stayed away from an all-Opposition party meet called by the Samajwadi Party (SP) here on Saturday 

 Congress, however, wrote to SP expressing its support and promising to attend the next meeting likely to be held next week.
 
  The meeting was seeking to build a consensus over use of ballot paper for by-elections in Phulpur and Gorakhpur, seats vacated by deputy CM Keshav Maurya and CM Aditya Nath Yogi, when they became MLCs. However, as two prominent parties kept away, the first meeting yielded no result. 
 
 SP chief spokesperson Rajendra Chaudhary said that the meeting had been organized at the insistence of former CM and SP national president Akhilesh Yadav. "Political parties which attended have already expressed apprehension over the reliability of EVMs. All, with the exception of  , said that elections should be done using ballot papers instead of EVMs," he said.
 
 Those who attended the meeting included Akhilesh Yadav, Azam Khan, Ram Govind Chaudhary,  , Naresh Uttam Patel, SRS Yadav from SP,   Singh from RJD, Ramesh Dikshit from Rashtravadi Congress Party, SP Kashyap from CPIM, Gaurav   from AAP, Sanjay Chauhan from Janwadi Party, Suresh Niranan from JD (U), Pallavi Patel from Apna Dal and Mohammad Ayub from Peace Party.
 
 In its letter inviting political parties to join in Saturday's meeting, SP had said that serious questions had been raised over EVMs after the   elections and those in UP, Gujarat and other states.
