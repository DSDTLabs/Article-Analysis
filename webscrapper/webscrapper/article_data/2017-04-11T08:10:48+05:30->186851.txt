GUWAHATI: The BJP's partners in the Assam government -   (AGP) and   (BPF) - took part in   in New Delhi chaired by   on Monday. The meeting was attended by all allies of the saffron party from other states. 

 AGP president Atul Bora and BPF chief Hagrama Mohilary took part in the meeting convened to show the strength of BJP alliance ahead of the elections for the post of the President and Vice-President. Assam health minister Himanta Biswa Sarma is also in New Delhi and chief minister Sarbananda Sonowal is expected to leave for the national capital on Tuesday.
 
 Sources said AGP and BPF are expected to meet Amit Shah separately to discuss the issue of expansion of the Sarbananda Sonowal cabinet by inducting more MLAs from the two allies. AGP, of late, has been demanding more ministerial berths for its MLAs. At present, the regional party has two ministers - Bora and water resources minister Kesab Mahanta.
