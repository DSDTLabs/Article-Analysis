CHANDIGARH: Police here filed a chargesheet against eight persons on Monday who are accused of using Aadhaar and PAN card details of customers of a bank and a financial institution to avail loans for purchasing high-end mobile phones and then selling them for cheap.

 Running into 775 pages, the chargesheet has listed 37 witnesses against the accused — Chandigarh resident Ashish Kumar, Haryana native Vishal Sahgal, Panchkula resident Shakti Singh, Mohali resident Sanjeev Kumar, Himachal Pradesh native   (a former HDFC bank employee), Lalru resident Kapil Dhingra (a former employee of  ), and Balongi resident Baldev Singh. 
 
 In the chargesheet, police have sought framing of charges against the accused under sections 419 (cheating by personation (3)), 420 (cheating (7), 467 (forgery of valuable security, will (lifer or 10 years) etc), 468 (forgery for purpose of cheating (7), 471 (using as genuine a forged document or electronic record), and 120B (criminal conspiracy) of the Indian Penal Code (IPC).
 
 Of these, the charge of forgery of valuable security or will carries the harshest punishment — 10 years in jail or life imprisonment.
 
 The crime branch of Chandigarh Police busted the case on January 21 this year. Katyal and Dhingra were instrumental in providing documents of bank customers to their accomplices, and these were used to finance mobile phones.
 
 The chargesheet said Katyal and Dhingra would provide customers’ details to Baldev, who would then use their names and addresses to forge Aadhaar and PAN cards by changing the pictures of customers. The loan instalments would get deducted from the accounts of customers.
