HYDERABAD: Lashing out at the state government for presenting an ‘anti-poor’   and ‘neglecting’ the education sector, students on Tuesday took to the streets and burnt the effigies of the Telangana government and chief minister  . They alleged that ‘meagre’ Rs 12,705 crore for education will only be sufficient to pay salaries to teachers and will leave no money to improve infrastructure or for welfare programmes. 

 “The only thing we can conclude from this budget is that the CM wants to deprive underprivileged people of higher education and make sure that like our parents even we remain uneducated,” said Ayappa, state secretary,  . He added that scores of students participated in a rasta roko at   to   against the budget.
