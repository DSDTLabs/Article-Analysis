AURANGABAD: Snehal Dhande from   here has emerged the city champion of Classmate   Season 9 and will compete in the national finals of the competition.

 While Classmate and Radio Mirchi 98.3 FM launched season 9 of Classmate Spell Bee, country’s largest spelling competition, three months ago, it was conducted in Aurangabad recently.
 
 Aurangabad is one of the 30 cities in which such semi-finals were conducted. In the initial phase, an on-ground exercise was conducted across 20 schools, where students were given spelling tests. Top 15 scorers from each school then competed in the city finals round. The top performers from the city finals then competed in the semi-final stage of the competition, a release said.
 
 A city winner does not automatically qualify for the national finals. Only 16 students with the top 16 scores across India will qualify (purely on a score merit), the release added.
 
 The competition this year is powered by The Times of India, while Times NIE is the education partner,   is the television partner and Victor Tango is the event partner for the property.
 
 The culmination of Classmate Spell Bee Season 9 will see a gruelling competition between the top 16 scorers across the country to win the national championship.
 
 Shailendra Tyagi, chief executive of ITC’s education and stationery products business said Classmate has always endeavored to recognize, nurture and celebrate the uniqueness of every child.
 
 Sumit Aggarwal, senior vice-president and national head activations, Entertainment Network (India) Limited, said, “Spell Bee has grown beyond being just a competition for school children and today we are proud of celebrating its ninth season with its makers, the children themselves.
 
 The national champion will win a prize of Rs 2 lakh, besides the opportunity to witness the prestigious   with a parent in Washington, US, in an all-expenses-paid trip. The four semi-finalists will win cash prize of Rs 50,000 each.
