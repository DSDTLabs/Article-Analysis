PATNA: Passengers flying into and out of   increased 12 times over the past 12 years.

 According to the Bihar’s economic survey report for the fiscal 2017-18 released on Monday, the number of flyers using the   went up from 1.76 lakh in 2004-05 to 21.12 lakh in 2016-17, implying a growth from 483 to 5.787 passengers/day during the period.
 
 Airport officials also said around 7,000 passengers on an average use the airport daily these days.
 
 The ‘astronomical’ surge in the number of flyers has been attributed to rise in the number of aircraft flying into and out of Patna. The economic survey report says the number of aircraft movement from the city airport increased by almost four times during the 12 years: from 3,844 in 2004-05 to 15,508 in 2016-17.
 
 “The increase in the number of flights has led to competitive airfares. A Patna-Delhi ticket can be booked for as low as Rs 1,900 these days, which used to be above Rs 5,000 till a few years ago,” airport director R.S. Lahauria told TOI on Tuesday.
 
 The number of flights is set to further increase to 51 every day with commencement of 24x7 operations from the airport from March 25. Quite a few direct flights providing connectivity to cities like Chandigarh, Indore, Ahmedabad and Guwahati are proposed to operate then.
 
 Work on the development of the new terminal building utilising funds to the tune of Rs 800 crores will also start in April. According to the DPR prepared by Singapore-based Meinhardt (Singapore) Pte Ltd, the new terminal building will be a two-storey structure equipped with six aerobridges and other state-of-the-art facilities.
