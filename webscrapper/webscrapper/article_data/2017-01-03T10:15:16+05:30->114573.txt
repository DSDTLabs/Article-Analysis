DEHRADUN: Upper reaches of Chamoli,   have received snow on the first day of the year on Sunday.
 
  Besides Badrinath, snowfall was also reported in Rudranath, Hemkund Sahib and Bedni Bugyal in Chamoli district. Munsiyari and other high altitude places in Pithoragarh and Kedarnath in Rudraprayag also received snow and rain on Sunday afternoon.
  
  “A strong cloud development over the higher reaches of Pithoragarh, Chamoli, Rudraprayag and Bageshwar may have caused snowfall in the hills and rain in other places. The weather is expected to get normal after Monday afternoon,” said Bikram Singh, director, Met centre.
  
  The Met office in Dehradun had predicted light snowfall and rain on December 31 and January 1 in the higher reaches of hilly districts, especially in Kumaon region. According to meteorologists, inclement weather may prevail over some areas in Kumaon on Monday as well.
  
  The change in weather also led to dip in temperature across the state. Mukteshwar in Nainital district recorded maximum temperature at 12 degrees Celsius while minimum dropped to 3 degrees. The day’s temperature in Tehri remained at 14.4 degrees Celsius whereas mercury in night settled at 4.8 degrees. Owing to cold conditions, the minimum temperature in Pithoragarh plunged to 2.1 degrees Celsius.
