Panaji: Investigation into the   voyeurism case is almost complete with the crime branch of Goa police receiving the digital video recorder (DVR) that had been sent for analysis to the central forensic science laboratory (CFSL), Hyderabad. 

 The crime branch will file the chargesheet in the case after seeking legal opinion, a senior police official told TOI, and added that there was enough evidence in the video footage. 
 
 Goa police had made four arrests in the case. All four accused have been released on conditional bail.
 
 The crime branch also recorded the statements of managing director, Fabindia, William Bissell and chief executive officer, Fabindia, Subrata Dutta. So far, the crime branch has recorded statements of over 20 Fabindia officials in the case. 
 
 On April 3, 2015, Union HRD minister Irani visited the Fabindia, Candolim, outlet along with her husband. She was on a two-day visit to the state along with her husband. When Irani entered the trial room she noticed that the CCTV camera was pointing towards the trial room.

 He also called up the local BJP legislator, Calangute MLA Michael Lobo, who in turn called the police.
 
 Describing how the CCTV camera was positioned, the police had said that the camera was installed high on a wall just outside the trial room with the lens focused into the cubicle through a foot-high ventilation gap on its side.
 
 Police had registered the case under Sections 354 (assault or criminal force to woman with intent to outrage her modesty), 354 C (voyeurism), and 509 (intruding privacy) of the IPC and Section 66 E (punishment for violation of privacy) of the Information Technology Act, 2000.
