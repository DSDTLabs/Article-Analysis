GREATER NOIDA: The police on Wednesday asked local roadside restaurant owners and vendors in   not to prepare non-vegetarian food owing to the ongoing  . Police officers said the step has been taken because the number of pilgrims returning from Haridwar to   has increased.

 Ram Sen Singh, SHO, Dadri police station, told TOI, “We have also removed vendors serving non-vegetarian food. They will be off the road till the religious season of kanwar yatra is on,” he said. 
 
 Some local organisations, meanwhile, have made camps along GT Road to accommodate kanwariyas coming from Haridwar and also going for pilgrimage.
 
 On Wednesday evening, the police conducted an inspection of the dhabas along the roadside and asked the non-vegetarian dhabas to shut down for one week. 
 
 The shops and dhabas offering eggs, chicken, fish and mutton have been shut down. The police said that now only vegetarian dhabas were functional in the area.
 
 Ved Nagar, president of  , a local organisation, said kanwariyas preferred to eat vegetarian food during this season. “We welcome the move of the district administration and police which shows respect to the people’s sentiments,” he said.
