HYDERABAD: The ministry of health and family welfare has granted four medical colleges in the state permission to add 27 seats to their existing intake in two post-graduate courses, Doctor of Medicine (MD) and Master of Surgery (MS). Additional seats were given to  , Kakatiya Medical College, Nizam’s Institute of Medical Sciences and  .

 In a letter issued, the ministry informed the colleges that the one-time increase in seats was being done due to the revised student-teacher ratio, from 1:1 to 1:2 for all MD/MS disciplines and 1:1to 1:3 in subjects of Anesthesiology, Forensic Medicine, Radiotherapy, Medical Oncology, Surgical Oncology and Psychiatry in all medical colleges across the country.
 
 Gandhi Medical College, will now have two extra seats in MD (Anaesthesiology) and one seat in MD (Pulmonary medicine) while Kakatiya Medical College, Warangal, will add five seats to its MD (  and Gynaecology), three seats to MD (Radio Diagnosis) and one seat each to MD (Dermatology, ENT and Ophthalmology) courses. At  , six seats are added for MD (Anaesthesiology), while Osmania Medical College will get four additional seats for its MD (Obstetrics and Gynaecology) course and three seats for MS (ENT).
 
 “Permission for an increase, and admission of students will be till such time that the first batch of students admitted for the course(s) appear for the first final examination, unless permission is withdrawn earlier,” read the Letter of Permission issued by the ministry.
