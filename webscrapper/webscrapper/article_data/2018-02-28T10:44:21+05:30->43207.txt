NEW DELHI: Weak cues in the global markets and banking sector woes closer home meant that the Street opened with significant losses on Wednesday morning.

 30-share BSE   lost 190.76 points 34,155.63. The index extended losses to more than 200 points before paring to some extent. At 10.30 am, Sensex was down 185 points. The broader 50-share NSE   was also down more than half a per cent.
 
 In morning trade, the under pressure   stock lost further 5 per cent as the share price slipped below the Rs 100-mark. State-run bank scrips kept bleeding as the Nifty PSU bank sub-index was losing more than a per cent. IT and pharma were the only sectoral sub-indices trading in the green.
 
 The Indian markets saw some downturn due to global markets as well with the Dow Jones losing more than a per cent on back of federal rate hike concerns.
 
 Yes Bank, ICICI Bank, SBI, Hindalco and Larsen & Toubro were the major losers while Infosys, Dr. Reddy and Sun Pharma were gaining the most.
