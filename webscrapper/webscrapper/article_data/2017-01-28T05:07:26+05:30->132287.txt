THANE: Eight persons, including flex printers and mounters were arrested for displaying objectionable banners without permission. The 40x40-sized banner was put up near St Xaviers School, Manpada, said the police.

 The banners with black background had a white chair printed on them, which portrayed the Mayor’s chair and a saffron-coloured broken arrow cutting across it. Also placed on the chair was a lotus, said a cop. Printed on the banner was a sentence that read -- 25th incompetence of the 25-year rule in Marathi (25 varsha sattechi 25 ani nakartepanchi).
 
 It was found that the banners were put up by Gorakhnath Kale, a constable and his team, who had been assigned the task for the upcoming civic elections.
 
 The publisher’s name was found to be Prabhakar Sawant, who is a   (NCP) functionary, said a police officer.
 
 The arrested persons have been identified as Shiv Varma and Rajesh Shinde for printing the banners and Subhedar Bind, Guddu Bind, Jamvant Bind, Mukesh Sharma, among others, who had mounted the banners. 
 
 A case has been registered at the local police station under Sections 153 (a) (promoting enmity between different groups on grounds of religion, race, place of birth, residence, language), 501 (printing or engraving matter known to be defamatory) read with 34 of the Indian Penal Code and also Sections 3 and 4 of the Maharashtra Defacing of Property rules 1995 against the alleged accused in connection with the offence.
 
 The accused were produced in the magistrate court, which granted them bail, added the police officer.
