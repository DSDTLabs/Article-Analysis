MADURAI:   town police have booked six people, including three television reporters and a print journalist, for allegedly extorting money from a foreign national running an NGO on Kodaikanal hills. Based on a complaint from the woman, the police registered a case and are conducting an inquiry.

 Police sources said that the six-member group recently approached  , managing trustee of Aeon Centre of Cosmology run by   at   on Kodaikanal hills. The group which also included an employee of her trust, V Udayakumar, showed a photograph in which she was seen hunting a monkey in the estate using a gun. 
 
 They threatened to publish it so as to bring disrepute to her. In her complaint, Patti Anne Tower stated that they demanded Rs 10 lakh to refrain from publishing the photo, of which she handed over Rs 4.5 lakh on May 10. She claimed that the photograph was a fake one and she had not killed any wild animals. 
 
 Based on the complaint, a case was registered under IPC section 147- punishment for rioting, 471-using as genuine a forged document or electronic record, 385-putting a person in fear of injury in order to commit extortion, 388- extortion by threat of accusation of an offence punishable with death or imprisonment for life, and 506 (i)- criminal intimidation.
