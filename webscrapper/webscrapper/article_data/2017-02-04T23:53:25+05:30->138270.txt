Ahmedabad: The three-day international symposium on trends in healthcare ‘Advances in new drug discovery and development’, organized by Ramanbhai Foundation, concluded at the Gujarat University convention centre on Saturday. Over 700 delegates from India and abroad participated in the event.

 On the third day, speakers elaborated on topics such as physiology and therapeutic potential of FGF21 gene and protein, metabolism and metabolic ageing, melanocortins, peptide chemistry and chemical biotechnology for metabolic diseases.
 
 “The symposium aims to understand current trends in healthcare and how it will shape our future. Given Gujarat’s pharmaceutical and healthcare industry size, it was important to hear what international authorities in fields such as obesity, diabetes, cancer care and biotechnology think about the present scenario and future projections,” said an organizer.
