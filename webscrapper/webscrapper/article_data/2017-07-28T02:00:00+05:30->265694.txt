The discussion titled Heritage of Lucknowi/Awadhi Secular Culture, was introduced by activist and program executor  . Historian Amaresh Misra spoke at length on the various phases of the year long war. 
 Neelakshi Rai, a kathak dancer from Delhi, beautifully portrayed the sequence of events - from the taking away of the crown by the British from Wajid Ali Shah, his banishment from Awadh, the war of the Indian soldiers to take back the crown and proclaim   emperor-- through her dance performance.
 A 1928 Hollywood black and white film, The Relief of Awadh, was also screened on the occasion.
