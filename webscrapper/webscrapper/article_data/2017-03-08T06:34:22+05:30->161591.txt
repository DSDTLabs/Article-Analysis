PUNE: The   of   Education clarified on Tuesday that the board would not be conducting any re-exam for Higher Secondary Certificate ( , Class XII) exam papers whose questions went viral. 

 The clarification comes against the backdrop of many parents receiving messages on social media about the re-examination of the viral papers. One such message, circulated on cellphone messaging apps, stated that re-exam of Marathi and secretarial practice papers of commerce stream would be held in the coming days. The state board, however, cautioned the parents against falling for such messages. 
 
 “Only official communication on the board website should be considered as authentic,” the state board said.
 
 State board chairman Gangadhar Mhamane said such messages have not been circulated by the state board. “We have not thought about any re-exam for any subject of the HSC as yet. Messages on social media apps should not be believed,” he said.
 
 Marathi, physics, secretarial practice, political science and maths paper have gone viral so far. Two persons were arrested on Monday in connection with the maths paper. 
 
 Apart from the state board helpline, schools and junior colleges too have been receiving calls from frantic parents on re-examination. Sunita Parmar, principal of Ashok Mahavidyalaya said, “We have received many calls from students asking about the re-exam dates of viral papers. We have assured them that there will not be any re-exam because there was no notification from the board.”
 
 Confusion about timetable
 
 Mhamane also said every year there is confusion about the timetable and students often get confused with the one printed on hand outs by private tutors. “The board has already put up the timetable on its website and students should only follow this,” he added.
