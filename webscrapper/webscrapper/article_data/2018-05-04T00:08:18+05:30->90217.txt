Udhagamandalam: A man was trampled to death by an   late on Wednesday, near Gudalur in the Nilgiris. 

 Two other men accompanied by the deceased were injured in an attempt to escape from the attack of the elephant. The three friends were on their way back home after attending a marriage function. 
 
 Ramesh, 28, Jayaprakash 23, and Sivaprakash 24, all three residents of Padanthurai near Gudalur were returning home after attending a marriage function.
 
  “While crossing Mundankolli, a critical elephant crossing area, Ramesh was attacked by an elephant while the other two escaped from the spot”, P K Dilip, District Forest Officer (Gudalur Division) told TOI. 
 
 He further said, “While Ramesh died on the spot, the other two sustained injuries in an attempt to flee the situation”.
 
 Enquiries revealed that the incident took place at around 9 pm on Wednesday and it was very dark and misty. “The visibility was very poor when they encountered the elephant. The jumbo could not be traced”, said Dilip.
 
 According to the forest official, people in that area have been advised not to venture out late in the night. 
 
 “Despite having created awareness among the locals, people venture out after dusk and invite problems”, said the DFO.
 
 “Of late, behavior of elephants are not predictable. Also, with conservation activities, we expect reduction in conflicts. But people invite conflicts by venturing into critical areas”, the official added.
 
 A team of forest watchers has been pressed into service to keep a vigil on the wild elephant.
 
 An immediate relief of Rs 50,000 was handed over to the family of the deceased. The balance amount of compensation of Rs 3.50 lakh will be disbursed after the completion of procedural formalities, according to Dilip. 
 
 This is the second incident of elephant attack in the past one week in the Gudalur division of forests. 
 
 On April 26, a woman was trampled to death by two wild elephants near Nadugani in Gudalur taluk.
