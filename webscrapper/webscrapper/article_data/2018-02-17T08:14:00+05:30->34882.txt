CHANDIGARH: The special investigation team ( ), probing the violence in Panchkula following the conviction of Dera Sacha Sauda head Gurmeet Ram Rahim in rape cases in August 2017, returned empty handed from   as it failed to trace dera chairperson   on Friday.

 According to information, SIT members visited the police station where Vipassana had been quizzed and even went to the dera headquarters in Sirsa in search of her.
 
 Panchkula commissioner of police A S Chawla, who is monitoring the investigations, admitted that the team returned emptyhanded. He, however, maintained that since Vipassana had assured Sirsa police about joining the investigations, she could be served the notice again.
 
 Chawla said, “The SIT had obtained her arrest warrants from the court as she was not traceable and had not joined investigations in January. We have to complete a number of formalities before arresting her. Our investigations are still in progress for the verification of evidences which had cropped up against Vipassana.”
 
 Vipassana was the second-in-command after the dera head, who is serving a 20-year sentence in Sunaria jail of   for raping two sadhvis (female disciples). He was convicted by the special CBI court in Panchkula on August 25, 2017, which had triggered violence resulting into death of 41 persons, including five in Sirsa.
 
 Acting on the directions of Punjab and Haryana high court, Panchkula police had registered a case of criminal conspiracy at Sector 5 police station regarding the violence in which 30 persons have been arrested so far.
 
 Those arrested include Honeypreet Insan, alleged adopted daughter of Gurmeet, and other senior functionaries of the dera. Based on their interrogation, Vipassana too was named as one of the accused and the SIT had obtained her arrest warrants from the Panchkula court.
 
 On February 14, Vipassana had joined investigations in the matter at Sirsa after she had gone missing for a month.
