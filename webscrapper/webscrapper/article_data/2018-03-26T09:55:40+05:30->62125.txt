NEW DELHI: On Monday, private hotel group Lemon Tree Hotels will offer its shares for subscription through an Initial Public Offering ( ) worth Rs 1,040 crore. 

 The IPO will stay open for subscription till March 28 and the price band of the IPO has been kept at Rs 54-56 per share. Ahead of the IPO, anchor investors have raised Rs 311.60 crore. The company aims to raise 1,001.6-1,038.7 crore through the issue.
 
 The IPO is an offer-for sale (OFS) issue, so the company will not receive any proceeds. The proceeds will go to shareholders- private equity firm Warburg Pincus, and Dutch pension fund APG, who are selling off their stakes through the IPO.
