VISAKHAPATNAM: Actor-turned-politician Chiranjeevi’s much-awaited movie ‘Khaidi No 150’ is set to hit 1,404 film theatres in the two Telugu-speaking states — Andhra Pradesh and Telangana — on Wednesday.

 Posters of   and Ramcharan Tej, Chiranjeevi‘s son, donned the theatres after a nine-year break. Nearly 120 theatres in the three north coastal districts of Srikakulam, Vizianagaram and Visakhapatnam are set to screen Chiru’s 150th film four shows a day amid tight security measures.
 
 Additional deputy commissioner of police (special branch) PV Ravi Kumar told TOI that all the station house officers in the city police commissionerate limits have been instructed to keep a tab on the followers of two actors — Chiranjeevi and Balakrishna — to prevent any untoward incidents or clashes. Balaiah’s film ‘Gautamiputra Satakarni’ is slated for release on Thursday, December 12. “As it is Sankranti and the two actors are coming up with prestigious projects, it will definitely lead to mass gatherings at the theatres,” Ravi Kumar said.
 
 This apart, the theatre managements were also instructed to take safety measures to prevent stampedes keeping in view previous experiences.
 
 AP and Telangana   president L Chandrasekhar said, “Khaidi No 150 will definitionally be a blockbuster hit. We are sure that the movie will entertain masses of all categories.”
 
 Meanwhile,   Visakhapatnam district committee members including VV Srinivasa Rao demanded that the state government take action against theatre managements for increasing the price of tickets.
 
 “The managements of the theatres where ‘Khaidi No 150’ is set to be screened have increased the price of a balcony ticket from Rs 80 to Rs 150, and hiked by Rs 25 to Rs 50 for the other categories,”   said.
