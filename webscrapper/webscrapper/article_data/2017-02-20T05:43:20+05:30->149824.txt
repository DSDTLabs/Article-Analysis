VASCO: Problems continued for relegation battling Churchill Brothers as the former   champions let   FC off the hook in a 4-5 I-League defeat at the Tilak Maidan in Vasco on Sunday.
 
  The first half witnessed six goals with the teams being locked 3-3. The visitors had five different scorers in the form of  , Simranjeet Singh, Souvik Das, Loveday Okechukwu and Kareem Omolaja Nurain.
  
  Ghanefo   secured a brace while Adil Khan and Chesterpoul Lyngdoh scored a goal each for Churchill who are now winless after five matches and languish at the bottom of the table with six points from 10 matches. Minerva jumped to seventh spot with 10 points.
  
  With newly appointed head coach Derrick Pereira in their ranks, the Varca-based side were eager to click into gear on home turf where they last defeated Bengaluru FC.
  
  However, Churchill’s plans were dashed in the fourth minute. Kamalpreet Singh sent in a perfect cross between Adil Khan and   but it was Ngaihte who leaped highest to head in past Priyant Singh.
  
  Churchill were certainly taken by surprise but responded quickly within three minutes. Wolfe did well to find his striking partner Kromah and the Liberian fed winger Lyngdoh who did well to resist a challenge by Kamalpreet Singh and finish neatly.
  
  The home team then looked more confident and went ahead in the 18th minute with Adil heading off a Brandon Fernandes corner.
  
  But minutes later, the third consecutive headed goal arrived at the other end. Prayant could only get his fingers to the ball as Simranjeet’s effort hit the post and rolled in.
