CHANDRAPUR: Maharashtra government has announced Rs 25 lakh each cash award for the five tribal students from   who scaled Mount   under ‘Mission Shourya’. 

 States finance minister and guardian minister of Chandrapur, Sudhir Mungantiwar, announced the decision after a meeting with chief minister Devendra Fadnavis on Monday evening. He informed that the students whose attempt to scale the highest peak in the world could not succeed will also be given Rs 10 lakh each.
 
 Mungantiwar said these students have brought laurels to the state and hence he has requested the chief minister to provide government job to the five Everest   in the home department.
 
 Five students — Manisha Dhurve, Parmesh Aale, Umakant Madavi, Kavidas Kathmode and Vikas Soyam — had unfurled the flag of Maharashtra as well as the national tricolor on the highest peak of the world on May 16 and 17. They were part of the 10-student mountaineering expedition sent under the joint initiative of Chandrapur collectorate and tribal development department.
 
 Prime Minister Narendra Modi had lauded the feat of these students in his weekly programme Mann ki Baat. The students have reached Mumbai and will be meeting chief minister Devendra Fadnavis and governor C Vidyasagar Rao on June 1 before heading back home.
