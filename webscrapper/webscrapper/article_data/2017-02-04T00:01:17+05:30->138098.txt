The weekend set in with pollution levels breaching the ‘very poor’ mark in Mumbai on Friday. System of Air Quality Weather Forecasting and Research (SAFAR) recorded an air quality index of 305. AQI however improved to 300 by evening. 

 An AQI between 301 and 400 is considered ‘very poor’ which according to SAFAR means people with existing heart or lung diseases such as asthma, chronic obstructive pulmonary disease, congestive   or ischemic heart disease should avoid prolonged or heavy exertion. 
 
 Seven of the 10 locations monitored by SAFAR in Mumbai recorded AQI in the very poor category. Mazgaon with an AQI of 366 was most polluted followed by Malad, Andheri and Navi Mumbai. The least polluted was Worli with an AQI of 192.
