 An estimated 3 lakh employable youth population of Nashik has welcomed the I-T structures announced by finance minister   on Wednesday. For those earning anything between Rs 3 lakh and Rs 5 lakh, the income rate has been brought down from 10% to 5%.
 
 “I am not well versed with taxation. But if the government has reduced the rate, it will definitely benefit me as I need to pay less income tax, which means I shall have more money to spend,” said Yogesh Rane, an arts graduate from Indiranagar who works as an artist with an Ambad-based organisation.
 
 City-based tax consultant Manoj Kulkarni said a salaried person can save more by investing wisely in tax-free plans that attract rebates as well. “It is actually a win-win situation for the people as some tax concessions have been announced for income up to Rs 5 lakh. It’s a welcome move after demonetisation,” he said. 
 
 As per the 2011 Census, Nashik has a population of 10.14 lakh between the age group of 18 and 29. Considering the literacy levels (88%), 8.92 lakh are literate. Around three lakh are expected to have taken up higher studies or picked up jobs offering an annual package of more than Rs 2.5 lakh. According to some tax consultants the average salary in Nashik for youth at the entry level is from Rs 1.8 lakh to Rs 3 lakh a year.
