MUMBAI: Following the implementation of the pay-and-park scheme in commercial areas, A ward residents met BMC officials to voice their concerns.
The residents were led by   Raj  , who said he would continue to oppose the policy tooth and nail till the civic body puts to rest concerns of residents from his constituency.
 
 Some residents said they are okay with the policy but with certain conditions. “The policy needs to be properly implemented or it would create a law and order situation. The BMC needs to ensure the rates are fair and reasonable for all,“ said  , president of the   association.
 
 Assistant municipal commissioner (A ward) said, “Some citizens had doubts with regards to the scheme. We have clarified the same.“
