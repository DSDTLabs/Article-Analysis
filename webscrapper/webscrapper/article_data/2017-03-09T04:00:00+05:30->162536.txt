Rajkot: A 55-year-old woman died of swine flu in Rajkot city on Wednesday morning at private hospital. This is the second death due to the H1N1 virus in Rajkot in last two weeks.

 According to the health department of Rajkot Municipal Corporation (RMC), the deceased woman was identified as Chetnaben Trivedi, a resident of Shiv Kunj at Railnagar in Rajkot.
 
 “She was admitted to the hospital on Tuesday with severe cough and fever. She was also diabetic, suffering from arthritis and pneumonia,” RMC official said.
 
 Earlier, on February 27, 65-year-old Chaturben Hinshu, a resident of Krushnanagar in Rajkot had died of swine flu.
 
 After H1N1 deaths, RMC carried out a survey in 144 households and health check-ups were done to check whether any other members were infected. “We conducted health check-ups on 478 persons in the area (Krushnanagar) as preventive measures. Surveys are being carried out in areas nearby from where the swine flu positive patient was living. There are no other persons with the H1N1 symptom,” said an official.
 
 In the first week of February, a 52-year-old woman Kanta Bodar, a resident of Kothariya Road in Rajkot, succumbed to the deadly virus.
 
 According to RMC officials, in total, five persons tested positive this year of which three persons died. In 2016, in total, 21 swine flu cases were reported of which five persons had succumbed to the virus.
