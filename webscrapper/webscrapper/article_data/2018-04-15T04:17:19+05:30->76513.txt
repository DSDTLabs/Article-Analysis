NEW DELHI: CBI has filed a case against a Delhi-based diamond trading company for allegedly cheating a consortium of   of Rs 187 crore.

 
 The agency has named SSK Trading Pvt Ltd and its directors Surender Kumar Bansal and Shefali Bansal in its FIR. Six banks, with   as the lead bank, sanctioned credit facilities worth Rs 163 crore, with PNB having an exposure of Rs 55 crore, to the company in 2013. Other banks include Central Bank of India, Andhra Bank, Syndicate Bank, Bank of Baroda and Oriental Bank of Commerce.
 
 “The credit facilities sanctioned by PNB and other banks became non performing asset (NPA) on June 30, 2014 with balance of Rs 129.82 crore (excluding Bank of Baroda and Oriental Bank of Commerce’s accounts) and Rs 187.29 crore (including BoB and OBC) due to non-payment of instalments, interest and violation of terms and conditions governing the credit facilities as per the laid down procedure,” says CBI’s FIR.
 
 The assets hypothecated against the loan were removed and disposed by the accused persons. 
 
 “The accused persons were not entitled to dispose of the hypothecated goods/assets without prior permission of PNB,” says CBI.
