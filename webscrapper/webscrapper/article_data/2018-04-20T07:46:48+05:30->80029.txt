CHENNAI: The Tamil Nadu government has informed the Madras high court that it has so far ensured effective implementation of Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015 and has sanctioned over Rs 6.4 crore for construction and renovation of observation homes alone.

 This apart, Juvenile Justice Fund has been created with a corpus of Rs 25 lakh for welfare and rehabilitation of children in the state, said principal secretary to social welfare department. 
 
 When the plea came up for hearing before a division bench of Justice   and Justice R Dhandapani, the official informed the bench that so far 32 posts of district child protection officers have been created in all the districts.
 
 Steps have been initiated to impart vocational training to the children of the government child care institute at a cost of Rs 9.65 lakh and about 200 such children have been provided modern vocational training in 2017-18.
