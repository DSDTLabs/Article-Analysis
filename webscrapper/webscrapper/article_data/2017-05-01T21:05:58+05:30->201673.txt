NEW DELHI: Union minister for state for home   on Monday kick-started “Oorja –   talent hunt tournament’ for boys and girls in which eight teams of Delhi would play. 

 Bhaichung Bhutia, brand ambassador for Delhi state and Indian football legend, was also present on the occasion. 
 
 Director General of   ( ) –  , which is organising the tournament in Delhi, Telangana, Maharashtra, Goa, Andhra Pradesh, Dadra and Nagar Haveli, Daman and Diu, said that “Before FIFA U-17 world cup to be held in October 2017, the campaign for awareness amongst youths of the country is to be launched in a proper manner for popularity of the game”. 
 
 In his address, Rijiju said that “Prime minister Narendra Modi has desired that 11 million footballers from the country come out from this tournament”. 
 
 The matches in Delhi would be played from May 2 till May 9.
 
 The tournament is being organised in other states by other central paramilitary forces and union home ministry is supervising the whole mega event. 
 The CAPFs including BSF, SSB, ITBP, CRPF, CISF and Assam Rifles, under the aegis of All India Police Sports Control Board (AIPSCB) are organising the mega event in the country.
 
 Earlier, home minister Rajnath Singh had said, "Football is a very popular sports and it can be gauged from the fact that while United Nations has 193 members, FIFA has more members than that. I congratulate the team for conceiving the idea and hope football will reach to every village in the country". 
 
 "Even in disturbed areas of India, we need to use football to channelise their energy and bring peace to the regions," he had added.
