JAMSHEDPUR: The administrative work at the ground level to upgrade the   (MNAC) and Jugsalai municipality into a municipal corporation and municipal council respectively will begin from February 10, state food, consumer affairs and parliamentary affairs minister, Saryu Rai said on Thursday. 

 The state government has announced to elevate the MNAC and Jugsalai municipality under section 3 (1), (2), (4), (5), (6) and (8) of the   of 2011. According to officials in MNAC and Jugsalai municipality, nearly seven lakh people are likely to benefit from the government-sponsored schemes meant for the welfare and development works, once the corporation and council becomes operational. 
 
 "The requisite ground work of the administration will be completed within a month. Subsequently, wards will be constituted and an electoral roll will be published. The state government will thereafter approach the state election commission to hold elections (to the Mango municipal corporation and Jugsalai municipal council)," Saryu Rai said. 
 
 The minister, who represents the   west assembly constituency that also covers Mango, said the development funds meant for the speedy growth of Mango and Jugsalai will arrive once the civic units come into existence. 
 
 "The urban development funds, worth in multiple crores under JNNURM (Jawaharlal Nehru National Urban Renewable Mission, a scheme of the previous UPA government), did not reach Mango and Jugsalai in the absence of the elected urban bodies," Rai said. 
 
 Rai added that upon formation of the corporation and the council, the two respective bodies will be entitled to receive millions of rupees under AMRUT (Atal Mission for Rejuvenation and Urban Transformation) yojna of the central government. 
 
 The minister shared his views with the press soon after the state   issued a notification to begin the administrative work, earlier in the day.
