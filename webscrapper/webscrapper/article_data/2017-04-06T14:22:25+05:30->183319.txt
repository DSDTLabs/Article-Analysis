CHENNAI: In a bid to help newly enrolled   to learn the nuances of legal practice, the   will conduct a series of lectures for 15 days from April 10.

 Those who are interested in attending the series must send a mail to palkhivalafoundation 2002@gmail.com, said its trustee and senior advocate N L Rajah. 
 
 “We will send a user name and password. This will be delivered free of cost. Registrations are limited, and will be given on first come first served basis. The last date for registration is 5pm on April 7,” he said.
 
 The purpose of the   series, to be held at the Bar Council auditorium here from April 10 to April 25 (between 5pm and 6.30pm), is to familiarise new lawyers with nuances of legal practice and to serve as a bridge between theory and practice of the law.
 
 The lectures would be livestreamed on the internet so that law graduates from other parts f the state could log in and benefit from the lectures, , Rajah said.
 
 The series will be inaugurated in the presence of former advocates-general of Tamil Nadu and senior advocates R Krishnamoorthy and Habibullah Badsha, besides advocate general of Tamil Nadu R Muthukumaraswamy. The inaugural lecture will be delivered by former judge of   Justice K Chandru on professional ethics in the practice of law.
 
 D Selvam, former chairman of Bar Council of Tamil Nadu and Puducherry and incumbent member of special committee of the council, would also attend the function, said Rajah.
