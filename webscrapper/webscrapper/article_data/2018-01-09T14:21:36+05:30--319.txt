PATNA: Dense fog and poor visibility forced the railways to cancel six passenger trains either originating from Patna Junction or passing through Danapur division of East Central Railway (ECR) on Monday. The cancelled trains were Patna-Anand Vihar (Delhi)   (13257/13258) from both ends, Mahananda Express (15483/15484) from both ends, Anand Vihar-Malda Town Express (14004) and Sealdah-Anand Vihar Express (13119).

 Railway sources said four mail and express trains from Patna Junction were rescheduled due to abnormal late running in the Danapur division on Monday. While Patna-New Delhi Rajdhani Express was rescheduled at 11pm, Magadh Express was rescheduled at 10pm from Patna Junction. Similarly Patna-Hatia Express left Patna Junction at 3pm while Pataliputra-Lucknow Express was rescheduled at 8pm on Monday, sources said.
 
 Important mail and express trains which were reported running abnormally late on Monday were New Delhi-Patna Rajdhani Express (8 hours), New Delhi-Patna Sampoorna Kranti Express (6 hours),   (9 hours), Punjab Mail (10 hours),   (9 hours), New Delhi-Bhagalpur Vikramshila Express (7 hours),   (5 hours), Bagmati Express (3 hours),   (4 hours), Mumbai-Kamakhya Express (4 hours), Kosi Express (4 hours), Dibrugarh Rajdhani Express (13 hours),   (14 hours), sources said.
 
 Danapur DRM Ranjan Prakash Thakur made a surprise inspection of stations between Ara and Karisath to check alertness of the railway safety staff on Sunday midnight. Thakur, who was accompanied by a few senior railway officials of the division, talked to the patrol men about their difficulties in extreme cold and foggy weather conditions. 
 
 The DRM also checked safety equipment of the track patrol men, including protective clothings, boots, florescent jackets and lamps. The DRM directed safety officials concerned to supply tricolour torch to each track patrol man in the division. Supply of old kerosene lamps to patrol men should be done away with now, he said.
