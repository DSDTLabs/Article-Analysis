NEW DELHI:   solved a jewellery shop burglary case with the arrest of three men through fingerprint matching. 

 The accused had entered the jewellery shop at Dalai Mohalla, Bhola Nath Nagar and decamped with gold and silver bars worth a few lakhs. 
 
 Police recovered fingerprints from the spot and sent them to the   to establish the identity of the suspects. 
 
 The fingerprints were run through the Computer Remote Identification of Suspects system, which matched with criminal records of one of the accused, Subhan. 
 
 “Fingerprints collected from the door knobs and glass panels in the shop helped us in tracing the accused,” said Nupur Prasad, DCP, Shahdara.
