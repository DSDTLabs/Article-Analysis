 Muzaffarnagar: A dozen candidates in riot-torn Muzaffarnagar have cases pertaining to rioting and criminal intimidation against them. Kawal village itself -- it was from here that the riots began -- has candidates charged with criminal intimidation and breach of peace. Out of the 95 candidates in the six constituencies of Muzaffarnagar, as many as 12 have been booked for rioting and criminal intimidation. Five others have serious offences against them, including murder and kidnapping. All parties, including BJP, SP-Congress, BSP, Shiv Sena, RLD, independents and others, have fielded aspirants with chequered pasts.    Umesh Malik (BJP) and Kadir Rana (Independent), both from Budhana constituency, and Noor Saleem Rana (BSP) from Charthawal are accused in the 2013 Muzaffarnagar riots and have sections of rioting and criminal intimidation slapped against them.    Out of the total 17 candidates from the six constituencies of Muzaffarnagar who have criminal records against them, two each have been fielded by BJP, RLD, SP-Congress, and one each from BSP and Shiv Sena; five are independents and four are from small parties.    Talking to TOI, Noor Saleem Rana said, “The cases against me are politically motivated. I am sure I will get a clean chit in all.” He has as many as six police cases registered against him.    Prominent sections under which 17 candidates in Muzaffarnagar have been booked include IPC’s section 147 (rioting), 148 (rioting, armed with deadly weapon), 504 (breach of the peace), 506 (criminal intimidation), 302 (murder), 420 (fraud), 365 (kidnapping) and many more.    Umesh Malik, contesting from Budhana on a BJP ticket, who also has six criminal cases against him, said, “All the cases that I have are of provocative speech, rioting and others that were slapped by the then ruling SP government. I have been framed for no reason.”
