AHMEDABAD: A trial court in Kalol town of   district has exonerated 28 men who were accused of rioting, arson and desecrating a religious place in Paliyad village on February 28, 2002, a day after a violent mob set the S-6 coach of Sabarmati Express on fire near   station, killing 59 passengers.

 The court passed its order on January 31but it was made public only on Friday. The court noted in its order that there was no substantial evidence to nail any of the accused persons, defence counsel Bhavesh Raval said. All the accused have been out on bail since 2002. According to the police complaint, a mob of more than 200 people, mostly belonging to Paliyad, torched properties of the minority community. The mob even targeted a shrine in the village and damaged a portion of it.
 
 Some witnesses did not turn up for the trial, conducted by additional sessions judge B D Patel, as they had migrated from the village. Even those who deposed before the court did not identify any of the accused. During the trial, witnesses informed the court that they did not have any issue with the accused because members of both communities had reached a compromise.
 
 Raval submitted that the victims of the   were duly compensated and there was an atmosphere of peace and harmony in the village following the compromise.
