 Pareira, who stays in a room on the ground floor of Amarchand Mansion, said the guards were angry as he has complaining about security services in the building. “These watchmen don’t discharge their duties properly. The contractor who is providing the security too has not been paying attention,” said Pareira, who is unmarried. His mother stays in an orphanage and his older sister stays with her and takes care of senior citizens there. 
 
 “We have arrested Bhagwan Yadav (51), security supervisor in the building and two watchmen _   (53) and Praful Khaire (35) - and produced them before the court. They have been sent in one day police custody,” said  , senior inspector, Colaba police station. No society member has been named in the police case. 
 
 “After freeing myself, I went to the police station where they took down details and then I returned,” he said. Later, someone in the building who shot a video of the crime sent it to the police. 
 
 “At 10.30pm, cops came to my house and asked to accompany them to the police station. They asked for more details and then registered a case,” he added. 
 
 The police said that the watchmen were angry as Pareira used to instigate them and hence, they tied him up. “Nobody beat him up,” they told the police. 
 
 The accused have been booked under sections 341 (Punishment for wrongful restraint), 342 (Punishment for wrongful confinement)—both bailable offences— under the IPC and section 7(1)(a) of the Criminal Law Amendment Act.
