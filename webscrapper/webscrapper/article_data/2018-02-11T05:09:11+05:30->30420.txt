NEW DELHI: Indian   on Saturday launched the 'Voluntary Safety Reporting'   for its 13 lakh employees to report internal lapses in safety operations and hazards without disclosing their identity. These information will be used to fix lapses and prevent mishaps. 

 In an effort to make each staff alert to safety issues with the functioning of railways, the information received through the website will not be used as material evidence for taking disciplinary action against the person reporting the hazard. 
 
 The person reporting this is free to provide his contact   or not and his/ her identity will be confined up to chief safety officer. "These details will be utilised only for obtaining additional information if needed or to provide   on the action taken on the reported hazard," the website says.
 
 Persons reporting such lapses can also check the status or action taken on the hazard.
