Thiruvananthapuram: Tourism minister Kadakampally Surendran said there has been a drop of 15% to 20% in the arrival of the domestic tourists in the state following the Nipah outbreak. As such the Nipah scare has not affected the inflow of foreign tourists to the state, he said while speaking after the state tourism consultative committee meeting on Sunday.

 However, the inflow of tourists to Kozhikode district, worst-hit by  , has been affected. There has been a negative campaign against the state especially in certain north Indian states, he said.
 
 Surendran has urged all those working in the tourism sector to come forward to counter the campaigns against the state. tnn
