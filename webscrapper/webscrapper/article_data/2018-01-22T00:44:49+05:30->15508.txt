Coimbatore: Anti-poaching watchers (APW) from the state on Sunday sought to make their job permanent and give them priority in recruitment process. They also demanded the government to give seniority status based on their experience as some had worked for more than 10 years. The demands were made at the general body meeting of the Tamil Nadu Anti-Poaching Watchers Association at Palamalai in the city. 375 APWs participated in the meeting.  After passing a resolution on the demands, district-level general secretary of the association R G Sekar said the forest department should give preference to APWs while recruiting permanent employees.
