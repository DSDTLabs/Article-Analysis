MANGALURU:  , two-day meet of   organised by the  , resolved to urge the government for uniform rate for petrol and diesel. 

 Karnataka State Federation of Petroleum Dealers president HS Manjappa told reporters after the convention on Sunday that more people will buy petrol vehicles and thereby the level of pollution will be reduced drastically in cities. “People, at present, prefer diesel vehicles more as the price of petrol is comparatively high. This trend has led to an increase in the level pollution in most of the cities. If the government makes prices of diesel and petrol equal, then people start preferring petrol vehicles. Reduction in sale of diesel vehicles will ensure less pollution,” Manjappa said. 
 
 He suggested that the government may reduce the tax on petrol, which is 30% at present, to make prices of petrol and diesel uniform.
