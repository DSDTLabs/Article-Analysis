PATNA: Altogether 610   from 16 states and Nepal were arrested by   police last year on charges of running   smuggling racket in the state.

 ADG (headquarters) S K Singhal on Sunday said 185 arrested smugglers were from Jharkhand, 156 from Uttar Pradesh and 112 from Haryana. “Fifty-nine liquor smugglers from West Bengal, 29 from Punjab and 26 from Nepal were also arrested,” he said, adding the other states from where liquor smugglers entered the state included Rajasthan, Delhi, Assam, Madhya Pradesh, Maharashtra, Uttarakhand, Chhattisgarh, Karnataka, Odhisa and even Jammu and Kashmir.
 
 Singhal said eight police officers and 24 constables have been dismissed from service since enforcement of total prohibition in the state on April 5, 2016. “Altogether 169 officers and 132 constables have either been suspended or faced departmental proceedings for failing to implement prohibition properly under their respective jurisdictions,” Singhal said.
 
 He added more than 8.48 lakh litres of Indian-made foreign liquor (IMFL) and country liquor have been destroyed. “Police have si far seized 23.7 lakh litres liquor in the dry state,” he added.
 
 “Altogether 654 vehicles have been seized and 187 buildings attached across the state for liquor-related offences,” the ADG said.
