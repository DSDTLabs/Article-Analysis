 AHMEDABAD: The National Eligibility-cum-Entrance Test (NEET) â€“ the entrance examination to take up medical and dental courses, was held at 138 centres across the state on Sunday in which over 75,000 students from Gujarat appeared. The test is conducted by the Central Board of Secondary Education (CBSE). Most of the students found Biology section easier than Physics as this section was lengthy. According to some examinees, contrary to expectation, Chemistry section was not that lengthy. After making the NEET exam compulsory to take admission in medical and dental courses, the test was for the first time conducted in nine local languages including Gujarati.  Total 180 questions of 720 marks, including 60 each from the three sections, were asked in the exam. Each right questions brings four marks and for each incorrect response, 1 mark will be deducted. The exam duration was three hours. Experts said that most of the questions were asked from the central board and NCERT but questions of around 20 marks were out of the syllabus due to which some students faced difficulty. Bhumit Kanojiya, a student of Kameshwar Vidyalaya in Satellite area, appeared for the examination this year at the Calorx Public School in Ghatlodia. Kanojiya said that he struggled to complete the examination within the set time limit but fared well overall in the examination. Aniruddh Parmar, who came from Palanpur to appear in the examination this year, said he did not face any difficulties in understanding the questions as questions were in both English and Gujarati. According to him, Chemistry section was tougher than Biology. Overall, he was satisfied with his performance.
