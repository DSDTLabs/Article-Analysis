 Rains were also reported in parts of Noida, Gurgaon, Faridabad and Ghaziabad.
 
 The Safdarjung observatory received 22mm of rainfall between 8.30am and 5.30pm on Tuesday. Lodhi Road and Ayanagar weather stations, meanwhile, received 41mm and 18.4mm of rainfall in the same time period, while Ridge received a trace of rainfall.
 
 A regional meteorological department official said, “The south-west monsoon is bringing more rain to the region, including NCR. There are chances of overnight showers as well, though humidity will continue to stay high. The mercury, however, will drop to 3-4 degrees below normal.”
 
 Delhi’s maximum temperature on Tuesday was 35.8 degrees Celsius — which is around normal for the season — while the minimum was 28.5 degrees, one notch above normal. On Wednesday the maximum and minimum temperatures are expected to be around 32 and 27 degrees Celsius respectively.
 
 Meanwhile, waterlogging was reported at certain stretches like RTR flyover, Dhaula Kuan, Ashram, Chirag Dilli, Kashmere Gate, Pitampura and Lajpat Nagar. Instances of trees being uprooted was also reported.
