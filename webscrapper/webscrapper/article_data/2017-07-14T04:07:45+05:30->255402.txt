Mumbai: The   budget   on public works in the   of the financial year increased appreciably compared to the corresponding period in the last financial year. While it was around 5% of allotted expenditure   in the first quarter of the last financial year, it was 13% in the current financial year.

 In March,   Ajoy Mehta had told all departments to come up with a time-frame for utilisation of funds. He took a review at intervals to ensure timely implementation of proposals that had been decided upon.
 
 In the current financial year, the BMC allocated Rs 8,127 crore for capital expenditure, which is spending on development projects. In the first quarter of the last financial year, the BMC had managed to spend only Rs 582 crore of the total allocation of Rs 13,000 crore.
