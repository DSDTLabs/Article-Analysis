VELLORE: Supporters of chief minister Edappadi K Palaniswami and deputy CM O Panneerselvam celebrated the disqualification of 18 MLAs, who were supporting the sidelined party deputy general secretary T T V Dhinakaran, on Monday.

 MLAs Jayanthi Padmanaban (Gudiyattam), R Balasubramani (Ambur) and N G Parthiban (Sholinghur) were among the 18 MLAs disqualified under Schedule 10 of the Indian Constituency.
 
 The supporters of   and   in several parts including Pernambut, Gudiyattam, Ambur and   celebrated by bursting crackers and distributing sweets. They raised slogans against T T V and the MLAs who were staying in a resort in Coorg.
 
 Nearly 200 cadres of EPS and OPS factions led by party Gudiyattam Town secretary   demanded the supporters of Jayanthi Padmanaban to vacate the MLA office, which was occupied by the TTV supporters. 
 
 Gudiyattam DSP Prakashbabu and his team rushed to the spot and held talks with T T V supporter Nithyanandam and eight others in the MLA office. The police officials asked them to leave the MLA office. After they refused to leave the MLA office, the police arrested them.
 
 An official from public works department locked the MLA office in the presence of Tahsildar Nagammal. 
 
 Jayanthi Padmanaban told TOI that the speaker disqualified her and 17 other MLAs under the provision of schedule 10 of the constitution, which deals with anti-defecation law. “We did not shift our loyalty and joined any other party. We are sticking to the party and following the instruction of the party leaders V K Sasikala and T T V Dhinakaran. Hence, our disqualification is not valid. We have also approached the high court to face it legally,” she said. 
 
 In   district, the Arni police have arrested 11 supporters of TTV for staging road roko condemning the disqualification of the MLAs in their camp.
