BENGALURU: About 7,000 bank accounts in   and Goa are on the   as they saw an abnormal increase in deposits after demonetisation was announced on November 8, 2016. The money deposited in these accounts, which included Jan Dhan ones dormant before demonetisation, ran up to Rs 17,000 crore. 

 “We have identified over 7,000 bank accounts which received deposits of Rs 80 lakh and above. We have asked the banks concerned to furnish details about the accounts,” said R Ravichandran, director of income tax (I&CI), Bengaluru.
 
 The suspected accounts were found in both cooperative and commercial banks. Ravichandran said the I-T department has instructed the banks to alert them about suspect transactions on or before January 31. Of the 266 cooperative banks in Karnataka, 205 have reported high-value transactions.
 
 Post-demonetisation deposits to the tune of Rs 26,000 crore are currently being examined by the department across the country. 
 
 The department convened a workshop for nationalised/urban cooperative banks and the postal department on the procedures to be followed while reporting post-demonetisation transactions.
