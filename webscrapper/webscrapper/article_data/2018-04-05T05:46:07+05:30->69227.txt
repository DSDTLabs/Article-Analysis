MUMBAI: Tamil Nadu Congress leader P Chidambaram, who has been elected to the   from Maharashtra in June 2016, has allocated Rs 40 lakh for setting up dental cancer clinic in Nagpur from the MP local area development (MPLAD) scheme. Similarly, cricket legend Sachin Tendulkar has donated Rs 1 crore for the construction of a foot over-bridge at Elphistone Road station at a cost of Rs 10 crore. 

  Under MPLAD scheme, launched in 1993, each MP is entitled to spend Rs 5 crore per year for developmental work.
 
 Speaking about the allocation of funds by Chidambaram, a senior bureaucrat told TOI: "The entire amount has been released to the Nagpur-based Rashtrasant Tukadoji Cancer Hospital. We expect that the state-of-art clinic will come up soon." He told TOI on Wednesday that Chidambaram has, using his MP fund, adopted a village in Wardha district and allocated funds for development of basic infrastructure in Jalgaon, Dhule, Yavatmal and Nanded districts. 
 
 He has also earmarked around Rs 2.3 crore for Madani village in Wardha district, where construction of a community hall, fencing of a few buildings and construction of village roads have been proposed. In Nanded, home town of former CM Ashok Chavan, it has been proposed to construct a cultural centre and additional class rooms in a   school. In Jalgaon and Dhule districts, besides a state-of-the-art computer centre, it has been proposed to construct two crematoriums and a compound wall for a school.
 
  MP Arvind Sawant has spent Rs 80 lakh for purchasing two ambulances, one of which will be cardiac ambulance. In addition, Sawant has released funds for steel benches on a section of railway stations in South-Central Mumbai.
 
 Railway minister Piyush Goyal has earmarked Rs 52 lakh for various works, including laying of paver block and construction of roads near  , a state-of-the-art gymnasium in Tardeo and a 10,000 litre water tank in Colaba.
