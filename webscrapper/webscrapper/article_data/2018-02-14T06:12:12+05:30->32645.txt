KOLKATA: The Calcutta high court on Tuesday set aside the decision of the Union government regarding a strategic sale of surplus land owned by the iconic Bengal Chemicals & Pharmaceuticals (BCPL). The company was the first in India’s pharmaceutical space, set up by legendary scientist   ( ) Ray in 1901.

 On December 21 and 28, 2016, the Centre had taken a Cabinet decision to sell excess land of the company “to meet their outstanding liabilities and implement voluntary retirement (VRS) benefits to its employees”.
 
 The Bengal Chemicals Sramik Karmachari Union had filed a petition in the high court challenging the decision of the Centre in this regard.
