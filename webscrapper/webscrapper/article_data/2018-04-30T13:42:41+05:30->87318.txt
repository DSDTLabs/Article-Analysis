TRICHY: Prime Minister   has not responded to a request seeking his appointment for an all-party delegation from   to urge him to set up the Cauvery Management Board, said chief minister  .

 Talking to reporters in the city on Sunday, Palaniswami said the PMO has not granted appointment for a delegation of all-party representatives and farmers even after a reminder of the request was sent. Palaniswami was in Salem to lay a foundation stone for a memorial of AIADMK founder M G Ramachandran and late chief minister J Jayalalithaa.
 
 He said a resolution was passed at an all-party meeting convened by AIADMK in February that representatives of all the political parties as well as those from farmers’ associations would personally meet Prime Minister Narendra Modi and urge him to constitute the Cauvery Management Board. “The resolution was sent to the PM. We sought his appointment so that we could explain the need for constituting the CMB and the Cauvery Water Regulatory Authority. But there is no response yet,’’ he said. A reminder was also sent seeking PM’s appointment, he added. 
 
 However, Palaniswami expressed hope that the CMB would be constituted soon. “The   had directed the Centre to come up with a mechanism in six weeks’ time. But that was not done. So, we have filed a review petition in the apex court and the court had directed that the Centre should resolve the issue by May 3,’’ he said.
 
 Lok Sabha deputy speaker M Thambithurai said that the Tamil Nadu government had confidence in the Supreme Court and hence had knocked the doors of the court again.
