TIRUNELVELI: Two people were hacked to death in separate incidents that took place in Tuticorin district on Wednesday. 

 In the first incident, a 46-year-old man, who was accused in a murder case, was found dead in his home at   in Tuticorin city on Wednesday morning. Ambikapathy, a plumber by profession, might have been killed by the relatives of Shenbagavalli whom he murdered last year, police said.
 
 Police said Ambikapathy was unmarried and had been living alone in the house. Shenbagavalli, 36, was the wife of his neighbour  , a butcher. Ambikapathy and Shenbagavalli had a land dispute and used to fight often. They had a quarrel with each other on January 8 last year. The following day, Ambikapathy murdered Shenbagavalli by beating her with a log.
 
 He was arrested and remanded in judicial custody. Police said that he had got bail a few days ago. On Wednesday, neighbours found the door of his house open. Later, they found him dead and informed police.
 
 Police have picked up Shenbagavalli’s relatives Senthil Kumar, Pichaiah and Ganesan for questioning.
 
 The body was sent to the   for postmortem.
 
 In the other incident, P Arun Pandian, 25, was found murdered at   on Madurai - Tuticorin Road in the district.   police said that the victim hailed from Chinnamanur in Theni district. He used to work in a mill in Coimbatore and was out of work recently.
 
 Preliminary inquiry with his family revealed that he left home around 6pm on Tuesday. He had told his family members that he was going to Tuticorin to meet someone. He had called them last around 8.30pm and said that he was at the Arapalayam Bus Stand and the next call they received was from the police about his murder.
 
 Arun’s body was sent to the government hospital for postmortem and efforts are on to trace the murderers.
