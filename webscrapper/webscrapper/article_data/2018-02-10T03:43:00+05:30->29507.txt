Vijayawada/Delhi: TDP and YSRCP MPs continued their protest in both houses of  , as well as on the floor, for the fifth day, on Friday. The MPs protested near Mahatma Gandhi statue in Parliament, expressing dissatisfaction over the Union budget. 

 Speaker Sumitra Mahajan adjourned the Lok Sabha after TDP MPs shouted slogans and interrupted the proceedings.   was also was adjourned after protests from the MPs. Rajya Sabha chairman Venkaiah Naidu reprimanded the protesting MPs, ahead of finance minister Arun Jaitely’s speech. The MPs held banners saying “Save Andhra Pradesh”, “Save Democracy”.
 
 Interacting with the media outside the parliament, TDP MP Konakalla Narayana said they are ready to resign as MPs if justice is done to the state. YSRCP MP Vijayasai Reddy objected to the Union minister of state Sujana Chowdary’s protest against the Centre. He asked her, “How can you protest being part of NDA?”
 
 Earlier during the day, Sujana Chowdary held discussions with the finance minister Arun Jaitely before his statement. He reportedly sought a time frame for fulfilling promises as demanded by chief minister N Chandrababu Naidu.
 
 Meanwhile, BJP minister in the state cabinet for endowments, Manikyala Rao, said the ongoing differences between the two parties will be sorted out soon. “These differences are like between husband and wife, they will be resolved. The Centre has given funds to the state, but the chief minister is seeking more funds,” he said. 
 
 TDP leader KE Prabahkar said that TDP leaders ready to resign and fight for the state. YSRCP MP Mekapati Raja Mohan Reddy dubbed protests by TDP MPs as stunt, and said they were just an election strategy. “No one will believe the TDP even if it pulls out from coalition,” he said.
