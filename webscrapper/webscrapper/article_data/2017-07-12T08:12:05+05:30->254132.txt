 Those who have been granted bail are Iqbal Singh, Ram Shabad Maurya, Joginder Singh, and Pardeep, all former employees of Maruti Suzuki India Limited (MSIL). They were recently sentenced to five years in jail by a trial court in Gurugram.
 
 A division bench comprising Justice S S Saron and Justice Avneesh Jhingan granted relief to the accused as they had completed substantial period of their imprisonment.
 
 The counsel for the appellants told the court that the accused were sentence to five-year imprisonment and they had been behind bars for more than four years. The counsel for the Haryana government, however, had objected to their plea.
 
 On July 18, 2012, a group of MSIL workers had gone on a rampage at the company’s Manesar plant, setting afire a building in which general manager (HR) of MSIL Awanish Kumar Dev was burnt to death. Around 100 others were injured in the incident, including two Japanese officials. The plant was partially damaged and was shut down for some time. The carmaker company had sacked hundreds of workers after the incident.
 
 A total of 148 workers were arrested and charged with murder of the general manager along with other charges. After around five years of the incident, a trial court at Gurugram had acquitted 117 workers on March 10, 2017, and convicted 31. 
 
 On March 18 this year, the court gave life imprisonment to 13 employees, who were office bearers of the workers’ union, on charges of murder and attempt to murder besides rioting and other related offences. Other 18 were convicted on charges of rioting, trespassing, causing hurt and other related offences.
