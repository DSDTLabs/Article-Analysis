MOHALI: Mohali police have arrested a Ludhiana-based contractor accused in a construction   of school rooms and has been sent to judicial custody on Monday. The role of four other Punjab School Education Board ( ) employees have also come under scanner.

 Contractor   was arrested by Mohali Police following the complaint filed by Punjab School Education Board (PSEB) secretary J R Mehrok on the orders of the chairman Krishan Kumar.
 
 Recently Punjab government has ordered a fresh vigilance probe into the matter pertaining to siphoning of Rs 22.7 crore into the accounts of the contractor engaged for construction of 454 rooms for art and craft in schools across nine district of Punjab. PSEB employees including secretary Gurinderpal Singh Bath, sub-divisional engineer  , junior engineers Simar Sigh and   have also come under scanner.
 
 PSEB chairman, Krishan Kumar said, “Tainted contractor has been arrested by police and Punjab government under chairmanship of chief minister has ordered fresh probe into the matter. The vigilance team is yet to submit its report with the government.” The internal probe was conducted by the committee headed by retired IAS officer after which FIR was registered against the contractor, said Kumar.
 
 The work in the said districts are still pending, which is estimated to be worth Rs 4.4 crore approximately.
