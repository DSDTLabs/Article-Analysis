Gurgaon: Robbers targeted an unguarded   ATM in   village and decamped with cash after cutting open the machine on Tuesday.

 According to the complainant, advocate Manish Sood of Hitachi Payment Services Pvt Ltd — the ATM maintaining company, the accused entered the ATM booth, where no guard was present, around 3 am on Tuesday and damaged the CCTV camera before committing the crime. Bank officials did not confirm the amount stolen but the complainant claimed it to be over Rs 20 lakh. 
 
 The crime came to light at 8 am on Tuesday when a man reached the ATM to withdraw money. On seeing the machine cut open, he immediately made a PCR call. 
 
 “As Tuesday was a bank holiday on account of Maha Shivratri, we don’t have the exact figure from the bank. The amount was over Rs 20 lakh,” Sood told cops. 
 
 Following the complaint, an FIR was registered under sections 427 (causing damage) and 380 (theft) of the IPC against the accused at Manesar police station.
 
 Meanwhile, inspector Rahul Dev, SHO, Manesar police station, said that the stolen amount could be around Rs 1 or 2 lakh as the booth is located in the rural area. “We have registered the case and are trying to identify the accused with the help of the CCTV footage acquired from the nearby area. The exact stolen amount is not clear and it will be depend on bank’s report. We are on job and the accused will be arrested soon,” SHO Dev said. 
 
 It may be noted that ATM thieves have been active in the city for the last few months, rattling both police and banks. Police blamed security lapses on the part of banks and security agencies for these thefts. “We have held many meetings with banks on security measures to be taken, but here they did not even have a guard in the ATM booth,” said Sumit Kuhar, DCP (crime).
