NEW DELHI:   ( ), the annual gathering of Non-Resident Indians (NRIs) is all set for a grand opening.

 The 14th edition of the Pravasi Bharatiya Divas will be formally inaugurated by Prime Minister Narendra Modi on January 8 in the presence Portuguese Prime Minister Dr Antonio Costa, the Chief Guest of the convention.
 
  
  
 There are no fewer than 1,300 to 1,500   delegates attending the mega event in Bengaluru between January 7-9.
 
 Being organised in association with the government of Karnataka, the theme of this year's PBD is "Redefining engagement with  ".
 
 The Pravasi Bharatiya Divas was launched by the Atal Bihari Vajpayee-led NDA government in 2003 to step up India's engagement with its diaspora. Since then, the congregation of NRIs and persons of Indian origin has continued as an annual affair.
 
 January 9 is the main day of PBD as it was on this day in 1915 that Mahatma Gandhi, the "greatest Pravasi", returned to India from South Africa and led India's freedom struggle.
