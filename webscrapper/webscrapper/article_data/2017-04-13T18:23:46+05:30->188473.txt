BAREILLY: The Uttar Pradesh State Road Transport Corporation (UPSRTC) will soon be offering free Wi-fi facility for its passengers at old roadways and   bus stand in Bareilly. According to UPSRTC officials, the free Wi-Fi service is expected to come up at around 100 bus depots across the state. The bus stands, which come under the category of number 1, will be provided free Wi-fi service. Under phase 1, Ghaziabad,  , Lucknow, Kanpur and   are among the cities to get free Wi-fi at bus stations.
 
  Speaking with TOI, UPSRTC regional manager   said, “Thousands of passengers will benefit from this facility every day. They can connect with family, friends or surf internet once the service is available.” A telecom company will be setting up the facility of Wi-Fi at Satellite bus stand and old roadways bus station in Bareilly, he added.
  
  According to UPSRTC officials, 100 depots from major cities across the state will be provided with free Wi-fi, while Lucknow, Gorakhpur, Banaras, Bareilly, Allahabad, Meerut, Agra, Kanpur, Ghaziabad, Moradabad, Rampur are among the cities where the facility is to be launched in the first phase, said officials.
  
  “At a recent UPSRTC meeting held in Lucknow, we had put forward the proposal to provide free Wi-fi service to roadways passengers at the bus stands of A-category, following which it was decided to make free wireless internet facility available to passengers,” said Mishra. In Bareilly, around 25,000 to 30,000 people travel through roadways buses on a daily basis, while there are around 600 buses under Bareilly depot, which are operational from Satellite and old roadways bus stand, said sources.
