PANAJI: Special investigation team ( ) probing the Rs 35,000-crore illegal mining scam in the state, on Thursday, filed a chargesheet against former chief minister  , mine owner   and another for the alleged illegal condonation of the delayed renewal of Hede’s Collem mine’s lease in 2012.

 The 1,572-page chargesheet filed before the principal district and sessions court has listed 40 witnesses. Kamat, who was the chief minister between 2007 and 2012, had held the mines portfolio for that period.
 
 SIT officials had questioned Kamat and his son   for three hours each, in November 2017. The SIT had also insisted on custodial interrogation of Kamat, who, however, applied for and got anticipatory bail. 
 
 Then senior technical assistant A T D’Souza has also been named in the chargesheet as an accused.
 
 The chargesheet has been filed against the trio under various provisions of Mines and Minerals (development and regulation) Act, under Section 120 B (punishment of criminal conspiracy) and Section 420 (cheating) of the Indian Penal Code (IPC) and also under various Sections of the Preventions of Corruption Act.
 
 The probe into the Hede case commenced after a first information report (FIR) was registered on September 19, 2014, following a complaint by mines director   before the SIT.
