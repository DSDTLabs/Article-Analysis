CHENNAI: Weathermen have predicted a gradual rise in temperature as the city heads into peak summer. The daytime temperature, as per the summer forecast by  , is likely to be cooler by 0.14 degrees as compared to normal. 

 At Nungambakkam, the maximum temperature crossed the 36 degrees Celsius mark for the third time in a week on Wednesday. Meenambakkam so far has recorded a month-high of 38.5 degrees Celsius. 
 
 Meenambakkam records a higher daytime temperature than Nungambakkam. Weathermen attribute this to the proximity to the ocean. The observatory at Meenambakkam is 8-9 km from the coast while the Nungambakkam observatory is within 3-4km from the coast. 
 
 April experiences an average maximum temperature of 34.5 degrees Celsius at Nungambakkam. The maximum temperature on Wednesday was 36.1 degrees Celsius (one degree above normal). The last week of April could see the mercury rise to nearly 37 degrees Celsius. On average, Nungambakkam records a maximum temperature of 37.1 degrees Celsius during May and 37 degrees Celsius in June. 
 
 The night (minimum) temperature on Wednesday was 27.4 degrees Celsius (0.5 degrees above normal). In its summer forecast,   predicted nights to be 0.30 degrees warmer. 
 
 On Thursday, the sky is likely to be partly cloudy. The maximum and minimum temperatures are likely to be around 37 and 28 degrees Celsius respectively. 
 
 Interior districts of Tamil Nadu recorded light to moderate rainfall during the 24 hours between 8.30am on Tuesday to 8.30am on Wednesday. G Bazar (Nilgiris district) received 5cm rainfall, Gobichettipalayam (Erode district) and Hosur (Krishnagiri district) 4cm each, Mettur (Salem district) and Pennagaram (Dharmapuri district) 3cm each and Pollachi (Coimbatore Dist) 2cm.
