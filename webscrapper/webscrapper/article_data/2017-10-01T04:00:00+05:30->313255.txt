Vadodara: The Gujarat Gaurav Yatra that will kick off from Karamsad on Sunday morning is expected to reach the Vadodara district on its first day in the afternoon. The yatra will be led by deputy chief minister Nitin Patel.

 The yatra is expected to take the achievements of the state government and the party across the state. Chief minister Vijay Rupani and BJP state president Jitu Vaghani will address a public meeting at Padra on the occasion.
 
 BJP city unit president and Vadodara MP Ranjan Bhatt said that around 200 four-wheelers and 1,000 two-wheelers were expected to participate in the yatra. She added that all assembly constituencies in the Vadodara district will be covered by the yatra.
 
 After being welcomed at Sindhrot, the yatra will reach Padra and from there it will go to Karjan. The yatra will reach Dabhoi late in the evening. Public meetings will be held at Karjan and Dabhoi too.
