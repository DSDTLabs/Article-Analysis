AHMEDABAD: The Ahmedabad Fire and Emergency Services (AFES) deployed eight teams in city and district   from Dharoi dam into Sabarmati river on Tuesday morning. Five of the teams were deployed at Chandrabhaga, Shahibaug, Dadhichi Bridge, Sardar Bridge and Vallabh Sadan in the city and  ,   in Ahmedabad district.

 M F Dastoor, chief fire officer, AFES, said that fire vehicles have been kept at standby at each bridge, whereas divisional fire officers have been deployed to watch the situation on eastern and westernt city parts. “The team and Navagam village near Dholera rescued five persons from waterlogged areas,” said Dastoor.
 
 Rajesh Bhatt, additional chief fire officer, AFES, said that two teams were sent to Gangad village near Pirana after the water from Vasna catchment inundated the area. “Operations will continue till water levels recede,” Bhatt said.
 
 AFES attended to about five to six calls of trees falling on city roads on Tuesday. 
 
 Speaking about the flooding of Sabarmati Riverfront, officials said things will be back to norman by Wednesday morning. “It depends on rainfall in the city and north Gujarat. At the moment, the situation is under control as access to riverfront has been suspended,” said an official.
