NAVI MUMBAI: The Navi Mumbai Municipal   ( ) will be plying 10 mini buses that will ferry women  . 

 The transport body has initiated the process to acquire the buses that are part of the government initiative called Tejaswini. 
 
  "Last year, the state cabinet had launched the scheme wherein 300 odd buses are to be plied specially for women. The funds for the project were dispersed recently and therefore the process of procuring the buses has commenced,” said transport manager Shirish Aradhwad. 
 
 The state has released a fund of Rs 2.5 crore. 
 
 “There are a lot of measures being implemented to make NMMT efficient. Besides having buses for women, the transport department will also be increasing its fleet of buses. Sixty-five more buses are to be included to the existing fleet,” said civic chief Dr N Ramaswami. Besides Navi Mumbai, these buses will also be plying in other cities like Mumbai, Thane, Kalyan-Dombivli, and Nagpur.
