PUNE: The Pune Metropolitan Regional Development Authority ( ) has expressed willingness to take care of the to-be merged villages if their unification with the civic body falls through.

 PMRDA chief   told TOI recently that the authority was ready to shoulder the responsibility of providing basic infrastructure and amenities to such villages. “Many villages are part of the ring road project. We will carry out infrastructure projects in these areas, but providing basic amenities should be the local body’s responsibility. If some villages do not want to merge, we will continue to provide all the services to them,”   said, adding that the   had sought Rs 5,000 crore to take up the development of these villages, but PMRDA is developing them without any charge.
