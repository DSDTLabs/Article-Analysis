NEW DELHI: The Supreme Court on Wednesday refused to interfere with the decision of the Central Board of Secondary Education ( ) to conduct re-examination of Class XII economics paper after the question paper was leaked.

 A bench of justices S A Bobde and L Nageswara Rao said it was the discretion of CBSE to take a call and the court should not interfere in its functioning. It dismissed a batch of petitions filed by students and parents challenging the Board’s decision for a  .
 
 It also turned down the   to direct a CBI probe into the paper  .
 
 The petitioners contended that there was no justification to re-conduct the exams and the entire student community should not be punished by forcing them to sit for a fresh test. They said that only those students who benefited from the leaked question paper should be punished and action taken against them.
 
 “It is to be noted that this year 16.38 lakh students appeared for Class X and 11.8 lakh for Class XII CBSE board examinations. And therefore, to penalise the entire student community for an incident which is under investigation is arbitrary, illegal and unconstitutional. There is absolutely no justification whatsoever on the part of the CBSE in deciding to re-conduct the exam,” the petition said.
