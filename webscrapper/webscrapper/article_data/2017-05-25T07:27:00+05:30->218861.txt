LUDHIANA: A 45-year-old woman fell into   and drowned after a pick-up van rear-ended her bike near Dehlon on Tuesday. 

 In his police complaint, Jagdeep Singh, a factory worker and resident of   village, Sahnewal, said, “I was driving the bike and my mother,  , was riding pillion. We were on way home from Sankar village when a pick-up van ran into our bike on Dehlon-Sahnewal bridge of Sidhwan. I received minor injuries, but my mother fell into the canal. The pick-up van driver sped away as people started gathering at the spot.” 
 
 Commuters helped rescue  , who was rushed to a nearby hospital. She, however, succumbed during treatment. 
 
 Dehlon police have registered an FIR against the van driver under sections 279 (rash driving), 304-A (causing death by negligence), 337 (causing hurt by act endangering life or personal safety of others), 427 (mischief causing damage to the amount of fifty rupees) of the IPC.
