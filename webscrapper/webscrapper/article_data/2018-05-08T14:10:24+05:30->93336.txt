PANAJI: A 50-year-old man from   was duped by online fraudsters of Rs 1.2 crore after he was promised a job in Australia. Goa police’s cyber crime cell suspect a Nigerian gang is behind the  .

 The   began eight months ago, when the man replied to an email about a job option in Australia, crime branch superintendent of police Karthik Kashyap told TOI. A fraudster then contacted him and demanded money to start the recruitment process. The man had previously worked in a hotel abroad in a management position for 23 years, and quit to chase better job prospects. Thus, he uploaded his CV to several employment websites.
 
 After the initial reply, the first demand for money was to start processing the Australian visa. Then, the fraudster demanded money for a residential fee, then an anti-terrorist certificate, etc. Police said as and when money was demanded, the man deposited it in the bank accounts he was told to use.
 
 The man even got the certificates he paid for. “The victim got an anti-terrorist certificate, which certifies that he is not a terrorist,” said Kashyap, Kashyap, adding that he was even ready to sell his ancestral house to pay the fraudsters to get the job done.
 
 The cybercrime cell said in total, he transferred Rs 1.2 crore in 10 transactions, and that the fraudsters were in touch with him for even more money.
 
 Police said one day when he realised that he was duped of Rs 1.2 crore, he started crying and his daughter noticed. She then got the details from her father and approached police to file a complaint.
 
 “We have registered a case against unknown persons, and investigation is in progress,” Kashyap said. The FIR has been registered under Sections 419 and 420 r/w 34 of the Indian Penal Code, and Section 66D of the Information Technology Act.
