CHANDIGARH: Two unknown persons duped a 13-year old boy of Rs 30,000 in   on Monday. The boy went there to deposit the amount in his mother’s account.

 A complaint has been filed with the cops by the boy’s mother Hira Devi, 37, a resident of Burail village, Sector45. According to her complaint, she sent her son, Sachin, to the bank for depositing the amount. After reaching the bank, Sachin asked a for pen from two unknown persons, who later became friendly with him. Both of them then asked Sachin to give his money to them and they would deposit it. On hearing this, Sachin gave the money to them and left for home.
 
 Later, both the accused left the bank with the money without depositing it in the bank.
 
 Based on Hira Devi’s complaint, cops have registered a case under sections 420 (cheating and dishonestly inducing delivery of property) and 34 (act done by several persons with same intention) of the Indian Penal Code (IPC) in Sector 34 police station.
 
 Amrik Singh, the investigating officer, said, “We have registered a case on the basis of the complaint. The investigation is under progress.”
