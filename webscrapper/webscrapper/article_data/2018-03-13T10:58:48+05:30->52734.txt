LUDHIANA: For his contribution towards Punjabi language and culture, Punjabi writer   will be awarded a fellowship by the Punjabi Sahit Akademi. 

 The fellowship will be awarded at a function to be held at Punjabi Bhawan, Ludhiana, on Wednesday. Former Union minister   will be the chief guest. 
 
 Prof Gill was earlier awarded fellowship by  , Patiala, for a project named 'Kheti-Bari Lok Dhara', which is related to agriculture folklore. The Punjab government had awarded him the Shiromani Sahitkar Award too at that time. He has also served at Punjab Agricultural University, Ludhiana, for more than three decades and is former president of Punjabi Sahit Akademi, Ludhiana, and chairman of Maharaja Dalip Singh Memorial Trust, Bassian Kothi.
 
 Prof Gill has penned 16 books on Punjabi poetry. These include: Gulnaar, Sheesha Jhooth Bolda Hai, Har Dhukhda Pind Mera Hai, Bol Mitti Daya Baweya, Agan Katha, Khair Panja Paaniya Di, Dharti Naad, Phullan Di Jhanjhar, Paardarshi, Mor Pankh, Mann Tandoor, Camera Di Akh Boldi, Surkh Samundar, Do Harf Raseedi, Mann De Boohe Baariya and Tareya Nal Galla Kardeya.
 
 Thanking Punjabi Sahit Akademi for the honour, Prof Gill said he had always worked wholeheartedly for the promotion of Punjabi language and culture on all forums. "I am thankful to Punjabi Sahit Akademi for honouring me with such a prestigious honour," he said.
