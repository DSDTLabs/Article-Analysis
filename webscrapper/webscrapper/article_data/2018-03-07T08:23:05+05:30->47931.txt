 Girdhar Kachadiya of Devda village in Lodhika taluka of Rajkot lodged a complaint of cheating with University police station against officer bearers of International Human Rights Conference including its all India president Sandip Sharma, director Devendra Jain, Gujarat president Vivek Dave, Ahmedabad president Mahesh Bhatia and vice-president Aruna Nai, Mukund Parmar of Mehsana office and head of Rajkot office Maheshwari Agnihotri.
 
 According to case details, the organization floated a membership scheme wherein a farmer can become member of the organization by paying Rs 2,000 fees. The firm advertised in local newspapers that it would provide agriculture tools, tractor and cattle to those who become its member at half the market rate.
 
 Complainant Kachadiya became a member of the organization on January 6. He deposited Rs 2.91 lakh with the organization in two instalments to purchase a tractor. However, after two months when he did not get the tractor, Kachadiya lodged a complaint with the University police station on Monday evening against the eight accused.
 
 A similar cheating complaint was lodged against the accused by Prashant Sindhav of Veja village of Rajkot. He too had paid Rs 1 lakh for purchase of a tractor, but did not get anything in return.
 
 Police said that the organization had branches in other parts of the state like Mehsana and Ahmedabad also. None of the accused have been arrested so far.
