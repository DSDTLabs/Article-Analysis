AURANGABAD: The city police have launched a manhunt for a man who allegedly used papers of a sold property to bail out his minor kin who was detained on charges of repeatedly raping a minor. 

 Sources said that as the alleged cheating came to light, the bail of the minor suspect will stand cancelled. 
 
 Based on the complaint lodged by the   council staffer, the city police have booked the person on charges of cheating and dishonesty. The suspect used the documents of a sold property as a surety for bailing out 17-year-old youth accused of raping a minor girl on July 18, 2017. The case was registered with the Khultabad police station falling under the limits of   rural.
 
 As per the complaint lodged by Muniruddin Khwajamiya Shaikh, the suspect has already sold off his farmland situated in Sarai village, gat number 154 on March 29, 2017 to Sanjay Ambadas Padale. He used the 7/12 extract of the same farmland and filed an affidavit before the Juvenile Justice Court. 
 
 He used the extract and a personal bond of Rs 15, 000 as a surety for bailing out the minor rape suspect, who was slapped with the stringent sections of Protection of Children from Sexual Offences Act.
 
 City Chowk police stations inspector Hemant Kadam said, "We have booked the person on the charges of cheating and dishonesty as he allegedly submitted the 7/12 extract of a farmland he had already sold months back."
 
 The officer said that though the suspect man is yet to be arrested, but police would also seek a legal opinion in the case over the fate of the bail. Sources said that it was over a month back that the matter was brought to the notice of the Juvenile Justice council.
