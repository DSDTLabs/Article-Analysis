JAIPUR: There was no respite from scorching heat in the state on Monday when Barmer remained hottest with a maximum temperature of 44.4 degrees Celsius. Barmer has broken its all-time record as it was hottest day ever in March for denizens of western Rajasthan. 

 According to data available with   office, Barmer recorded a maximum temperature of 43.3 degrees Celsius on March, 30, 1946 which was all time record for the district. Since then, the city has been witnessing its maximum temperatures ranging from 37.0 degrees Celsius to 42.0 degrees Celsius. However, a maximum temperature of 44.4 degrees Celsius was recorded on Monday. If such is the scene in March, people in Barmer can expect more heat in upcoming months of May and June when summer would be in full swing. 
 
 Attributing the continued rise in mercury to active northerly winds, Jaipur Met office said ongoing conditions will prevail in next couple of days. Mercury will continue to take an upward swing across the state. 
 
 Jaipurities woke up to a warmer morning as a minimum temperature of 21.5 degrees Celsius was recorded. It was followed by hottest day of the season so far as a maximum temperature of 40.2 degrees Celsius, almost 6 degrees above normal notch. Roads wore a deserted look in the afternoon as people confined themselves to their offices and homes. Warm wind continued in the evening.
 
 Barmer was followed by Jaisalmer where a maximum temperature of 42.3 degrees Celsius was recorded, Jodhpur 41.5 degrees Celsius, Bikaner 41.5 degrees Celsius, Churu 41.2 degrees Celsius, Ajmer 40.5 degrees Celsius, Kota 40.2 degrees Celsius, Sriganganagar and Pilani 39.8 degrees Celsius each and a maximum temperature of 39.4 degrees Celsius respectively. 
 
 In its forecast, Jaipur Met office said heat wave conditions will prevail in the state in next 24 hours. Heat wave conditions will further aggravate in northern and eastern parts of Rajasthan.
