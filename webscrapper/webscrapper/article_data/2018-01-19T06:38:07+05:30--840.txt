CHENNAI: Revolt against authority in any manner is intolerable, not even in film songs, claims a public interest litigation ( ), moved in the Madras high court.

 The plea moved by an   cadre,P  , who claims to be the   secretary of AIADMK’s women’s wing, submitted that the lines– ‘veratti veratti velukka thonuthu, athigaara thimira, veratti veratti velukka thonuthu’ (to banish and bleed authoritative arrogance)– in the song ‘sodakku mela sodakku poduthu’ of the recently released Tamil movie ‘Thaanaa Serndha Koottam’ starring  , is defamatory as it portrays politicians and those who are in power in a bad light.
 
 This apart, such lines instigates violence, particularly among youngsters to revolt against people in power and politicians, the petitioner alleged. 
 
 He made a representation to the Chennai commissioner of police to take immediate steps to delete the line from the song. Since the commissioner failed to take action, the petitioner approached the court.The court adjourned the hearing to January 22.
