NEW DELHI: The Delhi government is launching a merit-cum-scholarship scheme for students getting admission in government institutions, including  , starting this academic session. Under the scheme, those from below poverty line families will get a 100% fee waiver, while those with annual salary up to Rs 6 lakh will be eligible for a 25-50% waiver.

 According to education minister  , students from a family with cards issued under the National Food Security Scheme will get a full fee waiver, while those with income up to Rs 2.5 lakh are eligible for the 50% fee waiver. Those coming from a family with annual salary between Rs 2.5 lakh and Rs 6 lakh will get a 25% fee waiver. 
 
 Applicants should have scored at least 60% in their Class XII board exams to be eligible for the fee waiver. “A lot of students in Delhi are not able to afford higher education. This scheme will help more students further their studies,” Sisodia said.
 
 The government has allocated Rs 10 crore towards this for 2017-18 academic year. An exemption of 5% has been made for students from SC/ST category, which means those scoring 55% in their Class XII boards will be eligible for the fee waiver. This scholarship will be renewed every year, if the student manages to remain meritorious throughout. The students will also be given Rs 5,000 apart from the fee exemption, for books and other materials. 
 
 These waivers will be applicable from this academic year to students who have or will gain admission to six Delhi state universities — GGS Indraprastha University, Delhi Technological University, Indraprastha Institute of Information Technology, Ambedkar University, Indira Gandhi Delhi Technological University for Women and Delhi Pharmaceutical Sciences and Research University.
