GUWAHATI: Thunder storms have damaged hundreds of houses in lower and central   districts since Friday night. Meanwhile, the   ( ) predicted heavy rainfall over West Assam on Sunday. 

 An Assam State Disaster Management Authority (ASDMA) spokesperson said in Barpeta and Nalbari districts of lower Assam, a total 14 villages were affected by thunderstorms, which damaged over 200 houses in the two districts since Friday night.
 
 In central Assam's Dabaka in Hojai district, over hundred families became homeless in the devastating storm which uprooted numerous residential houses as well as religious establishments and education institutions. Power supply was disrupted throughout the day in the affected areas of the district, where emergency services were also hampered.
 
 At Nagarbera in Kamrup district, several big trees were uprooted by the storm that swept the area, besides damaging quite a few rural houses.
 
 Meanwhile, the IMD has warned that thunderstorm accompanied with gusty wind is likely to occur at isolated places over Assam, Meghalaya, Mizoram and Tripura on Sunday.
 
 The IMD weather report on Saturday stated that light to moderate rain occurred at most places over Arunachal Pradesh, Assam, Meghalaya, Manipur and Tripura with isolated heavy falls over Assam in the last 24 hours. Light to moderate rain occurred at many places over Nagaland and Mizoram during the period. 
 
 "Day temperature rose over Meghalaya, fell over Mizoram but no large change was recorded over Arunachal Pradesh, Assam, Mizoram and Tripura during last 24 hours. Those were appreciably above normal over Arunachal Pradesh, appreciably below normal over Assam and Meghalaya and below normal over Manipur and normal over Tripura during the period," said an IMD official.
