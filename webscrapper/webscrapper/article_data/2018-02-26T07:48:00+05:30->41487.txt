LUDHIANA: Khanna police arrested three drug peddlers after 2kg   was recovered from them in   on Saturday. The accused were identified as Satpal Verma, Arshad (both of Jahan in Shahjahanpur, UP) and   of Jirohi in Hardoi, UP.

 Senior superintendent of police (SSP) Khanna, Navjot Singh Mahal, said as part of the drive against drug peddlers and rogue elements, a police team had set up a checkpost at T Point in Rorian village on Saturday. He added that at around 12.40pm, the police team saw three miscreants coming from Rorian village on foot.
 
 Police officials said on seeing the police party, the miscreants ran towards Kup Kalan, Maloud, and Rorian village. They added that one police party ran towards Rorian and caught Satpal, and on searching him, it recovered 1kg opium in the right pocket of his jacket, after which he was arrested and booked under Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances (NDPS) Act at Maloud police station.
 
 Cops said the second team ran towards Maloud and caught the second miscreant, who identified himself as Ram Lakhan. They added that he was searched, and the police recovered 500g of opium from the right pocket of his pant, after which he was arrested and booked under the NDPS Act.
 
 Police officials said the third team caught hold of Arshad, who ran towards Kup Kalan. On searching him, police recovered 500g of opium, after which he was booked under the NDPS Act at Maloud police station as well. They added that the trio was being questioned, and police is expecting more disclosures from them about the drugs trade.
