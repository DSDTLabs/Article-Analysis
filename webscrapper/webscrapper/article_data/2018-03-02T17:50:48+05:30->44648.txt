CHENNAI: The current storage levels in major   in the southern states is 27% of their total capacity. 

 A total of 31 reservoirs in  ,  ,  ,   and  , which are under the monitoring of the Central Water Commission (CWC), have a total live storage capacity of 51.59 BCM (billion cubic metre).
 
 The total live storage available in these reservoirs is 13.94 BCM, which is 27% of total live storage capacity of these reservoirs, as on March 1, according to an official statement from the Union water resources ministry.
 
  The storage during corresponding period of last year was 20%, and average storage during the corresponding period of last ten years was 34% of live storage capacity of these reservoirs, it said. "Thus, the storage during current year is better than that in the corresponding period of last year but is less than the average storage during the corresponding period of last ten years," it added.
