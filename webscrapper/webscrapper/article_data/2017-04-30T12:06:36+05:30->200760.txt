NAGPUR: Upcoming shuttlers   of Mumbai and   emerged champions in the   Junior Badminton Championship Season 3 which concluded at Andheri   Complex, Mumbai, on Friday.

 The five-day tournament witnessed dozens of young talented shuttlers engaging in exciting battles for the titles. In the boys singles U-17 category, Dhruv Patel from Maharashtra edged out Arjun Suresh from Maharashtra in a nerve-wrecking 3-game thriller which the former won with scores 22-20, 11-21, 21-14. It was a very close encounter and Dhruv managed to hold his ground after giving away the second game rather easily.
 
 In the girls singles U-17 section, Mumbai girl Pooja Kachare trounced Sejal Pathrabe in straight games 21-6, 21-14. Pooja was one of the 16 winners of the PNB MetLife National Badminton Scholarship in JBC Season 2 last year and has been undergoing rigorous training to prepare for JBC 3.
 
 Earlier in the semifinals, Pooja quelled the spirited challenge from Aditi Mahajan 15-21, 21-11, 21-19. The tall shuttler lost the first game but fought back gallantly to clinch the next two games and stormed into the final. In the other semis, Sejal Pathrabe also needed three games to oust Samidnya Machindar 21-19, 16-21, 21-18.
 
 Gracing the closing ceremony, chief guest Vimlesh Kumar, General Manager, Punjab National Bank, distributed the prizes. The tournament was being organized by Sara Foundation, an organization for the promotion of sports.
 
 The action will continue as PNB MetLife Junior Badminton Championship Season 3 starts in Bangalore from May 4. The finals will be held in New Delhi in June.
