NEW DELHI: A layer of moderate to   engulfed the national capital this morning, delaying 29 north-bound  , while forcing cancellations of two others.

 Flight operations, however, were normal, according to official website of  .
 
 According to a MeT department official, at 5:30 AM, the visibility was recorded at 500 metres, which improved to 600 metres three hours later.
 
 The minimum temperature settled at 11.9 degrees Celsius, three notches above season's average.
 
 At 8:30 AM, the relative humidity was recorded at 98 percent.
 
 "The fog layer caused delay in 29 train services by several hours, while forced cancellations of Delhi-Varanasi Kashi Vishwanath and Kota-Patna Express. 11 train services were rescheduled in view of the poor visibility," a senior railway official said.
 
 Meanwhile, the MeT official said that the skies are expected to remain generally cloudy during the day.
 
 The maximum temperature will hover around 25 degrees Celsius, he predicted.
 
 Yesterday, the maximum and minimum temperatures were recorded at 23.2 degrees Celsius and 9.5 degrees Celsius respectively.
