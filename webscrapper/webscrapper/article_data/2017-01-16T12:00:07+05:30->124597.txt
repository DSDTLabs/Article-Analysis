CHENNAI: The   on Friday has joined hands with the promoters of   'Pinakin' to supply information regarding places of tourist interest in the state. 

 The app, launched in mid 2016, has been well received by thousands who have downloaded it on both App Store on the iOS platform and Play Store on Android. In a statement, the tourism department said that information on Tamil Nadu's tourist destinations will be available in Tamil and English languages, the latter keeping in mind tourists from other parts of the country, as well as abroad.
 
 "This will largely facilitate tourists to know about various destinations without the assistance of guides," the statement said. Tourism Minister Vellamandi N Natarajan gave the green signal to the partnership.
 
 The app can be downloaded for free and will cover all major tourist destinations across Tamil Nadu in a phased manner, the statement added.
