NEW DELHI: Looking to shrug off a 2-day losing streak that saw   shedding more than 300 points, the BSE index gained 150 points in early trading on Thursday to regain the psychological mark of 35,000.

 
 At 9.30 am, Sensex was up almost half a per cent from previous closing mark while the the broader 50-share   was following suit to test 10,650-levels.
 
 In the last two sessions, markets have taken a beating, courtesy weak global cues and a weakening  .
 
 On Thursday morning, bank, pharma and auto stocks were bleeding while metal and   stocks were on the uptick.
 
 Coal India, HDFC Bank, Bharti Airtel and Hindustan Unilever were topping the charts in the morning trade.
