Bathinda: Two farmers of Mansa district died in separate accidents while returning from a protest held in Chandigarh on Tuesday. Three other farmers from the district were injured in another accident. 

 Seven farm organisations had held a one-day protest in Chandigarh on Tuesday, demanding complete debt waiver and compensation for suicide-hit farm families. They had demanded Rs 10 lakh each as ex gratia for families those killed in the accident and Rs 1 lakh each for the injured. The farm outfits also decided to start an indefinite protest outside the Mansa DC office on Thursday if the compensation was not provided to the families of accident victims. 
 
 Ajmer Singh (60) of Kahangarh village was standing on the roadside along with other farmers when a speeding vehicle hit him near Channo village in Sangrur. Ajmer owned 2.5 acres of land and leaves behind married son.
 
 In the other accident near Banur in Mohali district, Surjit Singh (55) of Kheeva Kalan village died whereas Sadhu Singh Alisher, Darshan Singh and Kanail Singh, all of the same village, sustained injuries. They were sitting by the roadside as their vehicle had broken down when another vehicle hit them. Surjit leaves behind a wife, two sons and two daughters. All his children are married.
 
 Bodies of both the victims are lying in the mortuary at Government Rajindra Hospital, Patiala. Farm organisation BKU (Ekta Ugrahan) general secretary Sukhdev Singh Kokri said the two dead farmers and the three injured belonged to their outfit. “Our sister farmer organisations, including BKU (Dakaunda), BKU (Krantikari), Krantikari Kisan Union, Kirti Kisan Union, Kisan Sangharsh Committee and Azad Kisan Sangharsh Committee, have asked the state government to stand by the farmers. If the demands are not met, our organisations will start a protest and the victims will not be cremated.”
