PIMPRI CHINCHWAD: With just six days to go, the BJP is the only party to have not yet released its manifesto for Pimpri Chinchwad. The NCP released its manifesto on Tuesday evening in Pune at the hands of party chief   

 The BJP has turned out to be the biggest opponent for the ruling NCP in Pimpri Chinchwad. During the launch of the   last week, chief minister Devendra Fadnavis had made a series of promises that there will be a separate police commissionerate for the twin township, a medical college attached to the municipal run Yashwantrao Chavan Memorial Hospital, Smart and modern city, and strengthening of public transport. 
 
 Speaking to TOI, Laxman Jagtap, Chinchwad BJP MLA and president, Pimpri Chinchwad unit of BJP said, “We are planning to release the election manifesto at the hands of chief minister Devendra Fadnavis and are waiting to get his appointment for it. It may be released on Wednesday or Thursday’’. He refused to divulge the details of the manifesto.
 
 From just three seats in the 2012 elections, an upbeat BJP is trying to takeover from the NCP which is trying for a hat-trick. In a way, it is a straight contest between chief minister Devendra Fadnavis, and NCP leader and former deputy chief minister Ajit Pawar. 
 
 The other two parties - the Shiv Sena and the Congress, have released their manifestos last week. In their election manifesto, the Shiv Sena has promised to waive off property tax for flats with an area of up to 500 sq ft and concessions to houses having an area of up to 700 sq ft. It has promised a medical college for the Yashwantrao Chavan Memorial (YCM) hospital, complete waiver on penalty tax on unauthorized constructions, Wi-Fi services, and modern toilets for women.
