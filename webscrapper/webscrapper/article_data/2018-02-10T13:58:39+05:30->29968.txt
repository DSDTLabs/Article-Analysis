BHUBANESWAR: Now passengers from western Odisha need not come all the way to Bhubaneswar or Cuttack to board the   as the Railways will run the train via   once a week with effect from Saturday. The return train will start from New Delhi on Sunday. 

 This has been a long-standing demand of the people of the western part of the state. Chief minister   and Union petroleum minister   too had requested railway minister Piyush   to run the train via the western Odisha city. Following this, Goyal had on September 27 last year taken the decision to run the sought-after train between Delhi and Bhubaneswar via Sambalpur. 
 
 According to its schedule, Rajdhani Express (train number 20817) will leave Bhubaneswar at 7.10am on every Saturday and reach New Delhi at 10.40am on Sunday. In the return direction, the express train (20818) will leave New Delhi at 5.05pm on every Sunday and arrive in Bhubaneswar at 8.35pm on Monday, said an East Coast Railway (ECoR) source.
 
 This train will halt in Cuttack, Angul, Sambalpur, Jharsuguda, Rourkela, Chakradharpur, Anara, Gomoh, Gaya, Mughalsarai and Kanpur Central between Bhubaneswar and New Delhi in both the directions. It will arrive at Sambalpur City station at 11.58am and leave the station after two minutes. The journey time between Bhubaneswar and Sambalpur is four hours and 48 minutes. The return train will reach the western Odisha station at 3.15pm on Monday, said the official source. 
 
 This train will halt in Cuttack, Angul, Sambalpur and Jharsuguda for two minutes only, but it will stop at Rourkela for 25 minutes. The train will be flagged off by Dharmendra and minister of state for railways Manoj Sinha through videoconferencing from Railway Board, New Delhi, on Saturday. After the introduction of Rajdhani Express via Sambalpur, the railways will cancel the Rajdhani services from Bhubaneswar via Bhadrak and Adra on every Saturday and from New Delhi on every Sunday, the source said. tnn
