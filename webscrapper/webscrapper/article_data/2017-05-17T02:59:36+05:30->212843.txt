GURUGRAM: A property dealer and a resident of Sector 57, Punit Sachdeva, was defrauded of over 10 lakh after a person pretending to be a bank executive called him and took OTPs 13 times on January 29.

 Punit Sachdeva said he received a call on his mother’s number on January 29 by a person pretending to be a bank executive who had called to “verify” his mother’s account. He was asked for his mother’s account, ATM card and CVV numbers, which Sachdeva gave, following which Rs 9,999 was deducted from the account. The accused called Sachdeva’s mother’s number again and told him that the deduction had taken place because of a fault in the server which will be rectified as soon as Sachdeva would tell him the OTP sent to the same number; and Rs 1,200 were deducted.
 
 Subsequently, Sachdeva divulged the OTPs eight more times, resulting in deductions of Rs 4,000, 19,000, 2,500, 2,000, 4,000, 19,990, 15,000 and 10,000.
 
 The accused then deducted money from the victim’s account three times, deductions of Rs 20,000, Rs 10,000 and Rs 20,000. 
 
 An FIR was registered nder sections 66 D (punishment for cheating by personation using computer resource), 419 (punishment for cheating by personation) and 420 (cheating) of the IPC.
