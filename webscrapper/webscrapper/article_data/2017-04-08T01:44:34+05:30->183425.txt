GURUGRAM:A 27-year-old man, who police said was a monk, was arrested for allegedly trying to   a nine-year-old   after abducting him on Thursday. He had brought the boy to  , where he was caught “red-handed” by three passersby. The accused was produced in a city court on Friday and sent to judicial custody, while the boy is being treated in a hospital. The accused monk, identified as Sushil Tiwari, is from Allahabad and was staying at a Hanuman temple in Sarai Alawardi village since Tuesday.

 According to police, around 5pm on Thursday evening, the accused abducted the child from the temple premises. Tiwari then brought him to Rajiv Chowk and took him to a park located behind an under-construction toilet.
 
 “The child raised cried for help when he started undressing him, which alerted passersby. Three of them caught the monk and called us. We rushed to the spot and arrested the monk. The boy was rushed to Civil Hospital,” said inspector Jai Pal Singh, the investigating officer.
 
  An FIR has been registered against Tiwari under Section 365 (kidnapping) of the IPC and Section 6 of the POCSO Act at the Civil Lines police station.
