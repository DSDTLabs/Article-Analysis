MYSURU: Barring stray incidents of violence in   and  , polling was, by and large peaceful, across the   region on Saturday. 

  Although there were no complaints of names missing from the electoral rolls, there were reports of malfunctioning Electronic Voting Machines (EVMs) in some locations. With security forces being deployed in large numbers across the district, the process was conducted peacefully. 
 
 Voter turnout across all districts comprising Old Mysuru region was more than 70% - Mysuru (70%), Mandya (77%),   and Hassan (78%), Chikkamagaluru (75%), Shivamogga (74%) and Kodagu (73%).
 
  Polling percentage was over 70% in Chamundeshwari, where chief minister Siddaramaiah is contesting from. Meanwhile, the CM himself exercised his franchise at Siddaramanahundi, which is part of Varuna segment, where his son Yathindra Siddaramaiah is contesting from. 
 
 At Shikaripur in Shivamogga, BJP state president BS Yeddyurappa was the first to cast the vote. Yeddyurappa is contesting the election from Shikaripur for the eighth time, and he offered pujas before he proceeded to the polling booth. Yeddyu8rappa later said that he was confident of an easy win.
