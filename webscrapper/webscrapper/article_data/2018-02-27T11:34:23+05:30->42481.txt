PANCHKULA: Three cases of theft of batteries from generator sets in government hospitals have emerged throughout the Tricity in the past five days. Police suspect the involvement of a single gang in all the cases. 

 In a recent incident in   on Saturday night, some unknown person stole batteries from the generator set of RO (Reverse Osmosis) water plant installed in the campus of civil hospital, Sector 6.
 
 The batteries were stolen during the night from the hospital premises which raises questions on the security arrangements. Despite the presence of a police post inside the hospital premises, nobody saw the thieves stealing the batteries from the generator set.
 
 A complaint was submitted with the cops by the office of principal medical officer (PMO) of civil hospital, Sector 6, Sanjiv  , stating that the three batteries worth Rs 10,000 were stolen from the hospital premises. A team of cops which came for investigation registered a case under sections 457 (lurking house-trespass or house-breaking by night in order to commit offence punishable with imprisonment) and 380 (theft in dwelling house, etc.) of the Indian Penal Code in Sector 5 police station.
