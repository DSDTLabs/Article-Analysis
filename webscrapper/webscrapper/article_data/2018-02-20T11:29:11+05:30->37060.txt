NAGPUR: Five months after wetland rules were notified by the Centre, state government has finally constituted a 21-member Maharashtra State   ( ) under environment minister.

 The move follows after the Union ministry of environment, forest, climate change (MoEFCC) notified the Wetland (Conservation & Management) Rules, 2017, in September. The new rules have replaced the rules formulated in 2010.
 
 Under the new rules, powers have been granted to the state government to protect and conserve wetlands. These rules call for setting up of state wetland authority in every state and Union Territory to be headed by environment minister.
 
 Now activities like conversion of wetland for non-wetland purposes including encroachments, disposal of waste, construction activities and discharge of effluents from units will be prohibited.
 
 The other members of the MSWA include additional chief secretary (environment) as vice-president and principal secretaries of forest, water resources, rural development, tourism, urban development, chief wildlife warden and other officials.
 
 Among the experts include various government officials including Dr Bharat Bhushan, HoD (environment), YASHADA, Pune, Dr Krishnan Narayanan, HoD of humanities and social science department, IIT Bombay, Dr Ritesh Kumar of Wetland International among others. The technical committee also includes members from ecology, hydrology, fisheries, landscape and other related fields.
 
 “The state wetland authority will now have to prepare in three months list of all wetlands to be notified. These wetlands will have to be notified in six months,” said forest officials.
 
 The experts committee will meet every three months and for wetland identification related works it will operate from   ( ) secretariat. The government has also directed to appoint office and clerical staff for MSWA.
