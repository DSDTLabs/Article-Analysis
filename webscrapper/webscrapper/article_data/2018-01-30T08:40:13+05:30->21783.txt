Surat, which stood fourth in last year’s ‘Swachh Survekshan’ hopes to break into first three this time as SMC spends nearly Rs 150 crore every year to keep the city clean.

 Dr Saurabh Chakraborty, senior assessor of Karvy Consultancy, which has been appointed by the government of India to conduct inspection in the city, and his colleague Jawahar Sharma inspected more than 10,000 pages of documentation prepared by   (SMC) for the same. 
 
 Cleanliness in residential, commercial, industrial and public areas; segregation of waste and garbage at source, daily cleaning and brushing, proper prescribed facilities of public pay and use toilets; solid waste management; plants to convert organic waste into manure; facilities to dispose of inorganic waste; proper sanitation and hygiene facilities are some of the cleanliness parameters that the two-member team is checking. 
 
 “Documentation and actual existence of these facilities have 1,400 marks out of a total of 4,000. Further, 1,200 marks are allotted after physical inspection under  ,” an SMC officer said. 
 
 An officer of ministry of urban development Aditi Sharma would arrive in the city on Tuesday to inspect the facilities along with different apps launched by SMC. 
 
 A third team would arrive in the city after a few days to look at citizen feedback and this carries 1,400 marks under Swachh Survekshan. 
 
 Municipal commissioner M Thennarasan told TOI, “We have upgraded all our facilities as required under the parameters of Swachh Survekshan and hope to do much better this time than last year.”
