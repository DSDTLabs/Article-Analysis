BHUBANESWAR:   (SECAC), an exhibition on wheel train, will enter into Odisha on Tuesday morning and halt at Bhadrak station for exhibition.

 The train will open to public at Bhadrak station from May 9 to 12 (except May 10 as holiday). Timings of the exhibition will be from 10 am to 6 pm on the nominated days, East Coast Railway (ECoR) sources said.
 
 The train, which was flagged off at Safdarjung station in New Delhi on February 17, will convey a message about climate change and create an opportunity to generate dialogue and discussion on the issue. 
  
 The SECAS is an innovative mobile science exhibition mounted on a 16 coach AC train. It has been custom-built for   (DST) by  , official source said. 
  
 Of the 16 coaches of the train, exhibition in eight coaches developed by Ministry of Environment and Forest and Climate Change are devoted to information, case studies and material related to various aspect of climate change, impacts, adaptation activities, mitigation solutions and policy approaches in a manner that is easy to understand and interesting for people including school students. In rest eight coaches, there are exhibition and activities put by the DST, said a release issued by the Railways.
 
 The train will also have stoppages at Puri and Chatrapur stations in Odisha. It will halt at Puri from May 13 to 16 and Chatrapur from May 17-19 for public exhibition.
 
 Three coaches of the train are fitted with solar panel on the rooftop. It will stop at 68 stations across 20 states in seven months. It will travel 19,000 km between February 17 and September 8, said the Railways sources. 
 
 This mobile exhibition was first launched on October 2007 by the DST.
