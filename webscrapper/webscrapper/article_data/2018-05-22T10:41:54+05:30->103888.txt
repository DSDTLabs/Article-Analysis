NASHIK: The   ( ) has imposed restrictions on Nashik-based Padmashri  .

 Accordingly, depositors will be allowed to withdraw a sum not exceeding Rs 1,000 at a time for six months. “Moreover, the bank will not be able to grant or renew any loans and advances, make any investment, incur any liability including borrowing of funds and acceptance of fresh deposits, disburse or agree to disburse any payment whether in discharge of its liabilities and obligations, without prior approval of the RBI,” a statement released by RBI read.
 
 The RBI said the bank will continue to undertake banking business with restrictions till its financial position improves.  , branch manager of the bank, said that the central bank has taken action as per the financial position during the financial year 2016-17.
 
 “But the fiscal condition of the bank has improved a lot in the last financial year,” he added.
 
 As per norms, the gross Non-performing Asset (NPA) should not go above 10%, but the NPA of the bank during the financial year 2016-17 had reached 30%. This forced the RBI to impose financial restrictions over the bank.
 
 “The gross NPA of the bank has now reduced from 30% in 2016-17 to 18% during the last financial year 2017-18,” said the bank official.
 
 According to Sonarikar, the RBI’s restrictions are for six months. “We have urged the RBI to give relief as the bank’s performance has improved during the last fiscal. We are expecting that the RBI will hold a meeting with us and we will inform them about the NPA reducing by 50%,” he said.
 
 The bank has only one branch with 6,000 account holders and depositors. With deposits worth Rs 6.5 crore, the bank has given advances amounting to Rs 3.5 crore.
