The new centre will be a dedicated facility for sports medicine and rehabilitation and will offer specialized services for management and prevention of sports injuries, improvement of physical fitness and performance levels of athletes and non-athletes.

 “Such centres are critical for not just sportsmen but for anyone leading active life. I think everyone should take up a sport and stay active,“ said Tendulkar after inaugurating the centre.
 
 Tendulkar also launched `6 Little Masters', an initiative by the hospital to promote fitness in schoolchildren and nurture sportsmen. To be implemented first at the Elamakkara Government Higher Secondary School, the Sports Medicine team will as sess school children aged from 10-14 on the basis of their physical fitness and work to improve their overall well-being, while emphasising on nutrition. A gymnasium will also be set up in the school to facilitate training.
 
 Further, the hospital will provide sports medicines and nutritional support for six students recommended by the school and help them compete at the state and national levels.
 
 “The initiative is our contribution to improving fitness standards of students in schools, while channelizing their energies into building character, self-discipline and teamwork,“ said Azad Moopan, chairman and managing director of Aster DM Healthcare.
 
 “Besides sportspersons, we aim to improve physical fitness of even those involved in physical activities like dance,“ added Dr Harish Pillai, CEO, Aster Medcity .
