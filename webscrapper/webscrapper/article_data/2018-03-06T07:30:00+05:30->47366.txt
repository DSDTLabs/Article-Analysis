PANCHKULA: The new excise policy of   released on Monday will increase the liquor rates in Panchkula and bring them at par with those in Mohali.

 Liquor is cheapest in Chandigarh in the Tricity.
 
 The policy increases excise duty to 15% per proof litre in country made liquor and 5% to 10% in Indian Made Foreign Liquor (IMFL). The new rates will come into effect from April 1.
 
 The prices of   will go up by Rs 20-50 per bottle, however, premium whisky will cost Rs 50-100 more.
 
 Similarly, the rate of   is expected to go up by Rs 15 to 20 per bottle.
 
 Liquor vend owners in Panchkula are worried about the increase in rate of liquor as people in Panchkula and Mohali tend to purchase booze from Chandigarh.
 
 Additional chief secretary, excise and taxation,  , who unveiled the excise policy, also announced an increase in the basic quota of country liquor from 950 lakh proof litres to 1,000 lakh proof litres and of Indian Made Foreign Liquor (IMFL) from 550 lakh proof litres to 600 lakh proof litres.
