ITANAGAR: Indian ambassador to Myanmar   on Wednesday called on governor   at   here and discussed the development of a stronger relationship between India and Myanmar through the northeast. 

 The governor said India and Myanmar could be 'natural friends' as they share traditions and religious practices. "With a clear vision, we can have a stronger relationship," he said.
 
 Saying he has proposed establishment of a medical college and hospital near the border area between Nagaland and Myanmar, the governor called for improved infrastructure, like roads and interpersonal communication, between the countries.
