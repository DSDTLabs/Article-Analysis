PATIALA: Acting on the information provided by one Inderjeet Singh Sandhu, who was nabbed by the Organised Crime Control Unit (OCCU) of   from   International Airport in New Delhi on December 20, 2017, the cops have arrested two more persons related to the Nabha jailbreak incident.

 The arrested persons, identified as Davinder Jeet Singh alias Rimpy Dabwali (a resident of Pathralla village in   district) and Gurpreet Singh alias Gopi Pandori (a resident of Pandori village in Amritsar district) are accused of harbouring Nabha jail escapees Harjinder Singh alias   and his accomplices who had escaped from the jail on November 27, 2016.
 
 While Rimpy has been sent to police remand till January 5, Gopi has been remanded in police custody till January 6.
 
 Patiala superintendent of police (detective) H S Virk said their names have been incorporated in an FIR registered on October 23, 2017, that was registered under Sections 392 (punishment for robbery), 382 (theft after preparation made for causing death, hurt or restraint in order to the committing of the theft), 384 (punishment for extortion) and 120B (criminal conspiracy) of the IPC and Section 22 of the Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substance (NDPS) Act and Section 25 of the Arms Act at Rajpura Sadar police station.
 
 With these arrests, OCCU has till date arrested 35 persons, who were either directly involved in the Nabha jailbreak or had harboured or provided shelter to the accused who had planned and executed the jailbreak.
 
 However, eight accused including Vicky Gonder, one of the gangsters who escaped from the jail; militant Baba Kashmir Singh; Prem Lahoria, a close aide of Gounder; Supreet Singh alias Harry Chatha; Gurpreet Singh aka Goppy Ghanshampria; Gurpreet Singh Aka Gopi Khora; Sukhmeet Singh; and Ramanjit Singh Romi, who had set up the control room in Hong Kong, are yet to be arrested.
