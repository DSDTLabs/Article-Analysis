 CBI raided several places in Vadodara, including the firm's office and residences of Bhatnagar, on Thursday.
 
 DPIL, engaged in manufacturing electric cables and other equipment, fraudulently availed credit since 2008. Its outstanding debt of as on June 29, 2016 was Rs 2,654.40 crore.
 
 At the time of formation of the consortium in 2008, Axis Bank (with an exposure of Rs 255.32 crore) was the lead bank for the term loan and Bank of India (with an exposure of Rs 670.51 crore) was the lead bank for cash credit limits. Other banks which provided loans to DPIL included Bank of Baroda (Rs 348.99 crore), ICICI Bank (Rs 279.46 crore), Allahabad Bank (Rs 227.96 crore), Dena Bank (Rs 177.19 crore), SBI (Rs 266.37 crore) and Corporation Bank (109.12 crore).
 
 CBI claimed that the company submitted false stock statements to the lead bank by treating receivables of more than 180 days (non-current asset) as those of less than 180 days (current asset) to get more drawing power in their cash credit accounts.
