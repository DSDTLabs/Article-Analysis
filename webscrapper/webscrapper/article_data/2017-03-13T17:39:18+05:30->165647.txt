BENGALURU: Inspired by the concentrated efforts of a core team which marketed prime minister  , prior to the 2014 elections and the recent Uttar Pradesh polls, wherein social media was successfully used by the BJP, JD(S) state president   on Monday launched a   by creating his personal accounts to interact with people online ahead of 2018 assembly elections.

 Under the name of " ", the JD(S) state president has opened his personal accounts on Facebook, Twitter, sound cloud, Google plus and You Tube to connect with voters.
 
 "The influence of social media on elections cannot be ignored anymore. In UP the BJP used 11,000 whatsapp groups to connect with the voters there. In an effort to remind the people of my achievements as CM and spell out my vision for the state, these social media accounts have been launched," said Kumaraswamy.
 
 As many as 35 to 40 qualified people will be handling the social media accounts for Kumaraswamy.
 
 The JD(S) state president said he will be dedicating at least one hour every month to have direct interaction, online, with voters to discuss the issues plaguing Karnataka.
 
 On being reminded that he had launched the same exercise prior to the 2013 assembly elections and renewed the same before the 2014 parliamentary elections, Kumaraswamy admitted that they had failed to take off.
 
 "We had used the same sort of platforms prior to the previous elections, but failed to sustain them after the polls. This time I will be personally using the space before and after the 2018 elections, until I retire from public life," said Kumaraswamy.
