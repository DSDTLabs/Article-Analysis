CHENNAI: The Madras high court is set to have an unprecedented number of  , with its collegium recommending 15 more names for appointment as judges.

 The 75 judge-chartered high court currently has 57 judges. Six names recommended early last year are still pending with the Centre. While its clearance is awaited, the high court collegium has now sent two separate lists -one containing four names of senior district judges, and a later one containing 11 members chosen from the Bar -to the Supreme Court.
 
 Names have, thus, been suggested for at least three vacancies anticipated to arise in the coming months.
