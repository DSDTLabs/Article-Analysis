LUCKNOW: Art of Living founder Sri Sri Ravi Shankar met former All India Muslim Personal Law Board ( ) member Maulana Salman Nadwi in Lucknow on Thursday to discuss an amicable and out-of-court settlement for the   further.

  It was decided that another round of meeting will be held in Lucknow on March 28. 
 
 Sri Sri and Nadwi had both met earlier on February 8 in Bengaluru after which Nadwi had proposed a formula pressing for construction of Ram Temple at the disputed site which had led to Nadwi's expulsion as executive committee member of the AIMPLB on February 10.
 
 On Thursday morning, a delegation of around 10 people headed by Maulana Nadwi met Sri Sri at the house of Munshi Nawal Kishore- of the famous Nawal Kishore Press in Lucknow's Hazratganj area. 
 
 The second round of meeting will be convened by Luv Bhargava(grandson of Munshi Nawal Kishore). 
 
 Nadwi is expected to bring all clerics and other religious heads in support of his Ayodhya formula on board in this meeting. 
 
 Apart from pressing for the construction of Ram temple, Nadwi had proposed that a bigger area of land than what was held by Babri Masjid should be given for the construction of a mosque and an Islamic university somewhere other than Ayodhya. 
 
 He had also demanded action against those who were involved in the demolition of the Babri Masjid and a guaranty that in future no Islamic seminary, mosque, shrine or graveyard will be unlawfully touched.
