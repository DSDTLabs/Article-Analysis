 In his inaugural address at the 10th India-Japan Annual Summit at Mahatma Mandir, Modi said that food services will be started for Japanese so that they can enjoy home-like food in Gujarat. In fact, he made an appeal for a chain of Japanese hotels to be started in Ahmedabad.
 
 Abe, in turn, wished that more Indians learn the Japanese language. Japan wishes to start 100 Japanese language centers across India. For  , a Japanese center will also be set up in Gujarat while a   will be set up in Varanasi.
 
 “We are going to introduce a Cool Box Service so that the Japanese people living in India can directly get their favorite food from Japan,” Modi said. “At the same time, I also request the Japanese business community to open more Japanese restaurants in India.” Indian PM was speaking at the India-Japan Business Leaders Forum at Mahatma Mandir in Gandhinagar at the annual summit.
 
 Addressing business leaders, legislators and state ministers, Abe batted for Japanese language teaching in India for wider and closer industrial cooperation.
 
 “We have decided to establish Japanese language certificate courses at 100 higher educational institutions in India as well as train 1,000 Japanese language teachers over the next five years,” Abe said.
 
 Talking about the convention centre in his Lok Sabha constituency, Varanasi, Modi said the city was is second home. 
 
 “The project of Varanasi convention centre is a symbol of cultural cooperation between Kyoto city of Japan and Varanasi,” the PM said. 
 
 “It was conceived by PM Abe and I when we visited Varanasi together in 2015. I have named it Rudraksh – the symbol of love, and a prasad of Lord Shiva to humanity. This Rudraksh will be a garland of love of Japan for Varanasi. It will also be a tribute to our shared Buddhist heritage at Sarnath,” Modi said.
