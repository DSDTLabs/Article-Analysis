CHENNAI: The collective storage of major reservoirs in South India continues to be one per cent less than the water storage levels of last year. While the current storage levels in 31 major reservoirs spread across the five southern states is 51 per cent, it was one per cent higher during the corresponding period of 2016.

"The storage during corresponding period of last year was 52% and average storage of last ten years during corresponding period was 72% of live storage capacity of these reservoirs.
