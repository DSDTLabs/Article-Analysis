 The cost of Nandini homogenized toned milk sold by northern district unions (Dharwad, Gadag, Haveri, Uttara Kannada, Vijayapura and Bagalkot) will increase from Rs 35 to Rs 36 while the figure for Kalaburagi, Bidar and Yadgir milk unions will go up from Rs 36 to Rs 37. A litre of curd will cost Rs 42.
 
 The rate revision has been effected by the Karnataka Co-operative Milk Producers Federation Limited to mitigate the distress faced by milk farmers due to severe drought conditions. The cost of milk production has gone up, the federation said, adding that despite the rate revision Nandini milk is still the cheapest in the state.
 
 Drought conditions have resulted in the scarcity of drinking water and fodder, making it difficult for milk producers and farmers to carry out dairy activities. The federation has requested customers to cooperate with the manufacturers till the new MRP printed packaging film is sourced.
