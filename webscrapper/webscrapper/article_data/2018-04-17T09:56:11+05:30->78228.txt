 As the sky was clear during the day, the maximum temperature rose to 40.7 degrees Celsius, two degrees above normal, while the minimum temperature was 22.4 degrees Celsius, a degree above normal. 
 
 Partly cloudy sky resulting in high humidity levels was witnessed from afternoon till the evening. 
 
 On Tuesday, maximum and minimum temperatures are expected to be around 40 and 22 degrees Celsius, respectively.
 
 State Met director JP Gupta said that dry hot northwesterly winds are lashing the city. As a result, hot conditions will continue. 
 
 On Monday, there was also mixing of easterly winds, which resulted in partly cloudy sky and humidity. However, later the weather was clear.
 
  The high temperatures are expected to continue for the next few days. In fact, both day and night temperatures may rise by a degree or two.
