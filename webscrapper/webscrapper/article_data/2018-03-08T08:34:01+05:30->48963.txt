 Razia Sultana, Punjab’s social security and women and child development minister, will receive the award from the President of India at   on Thursday on the occasion of International Women’s Day.
 BBBP scheme was launched on January 22, 2015, from Panipat in 100 districts across the country having adverse child sex ratio. Out of the 100 selected districts, 11were from Punjab. These districts were Amritsar, Barnala, Fatehgarh Sahib, Ferozepur, Gurdaspur, Mansa, Muktsar, Patiala, Sangrur, Mohali and Tarn Taran.
 
 The main goal of the scheme is to celebrate the girl child, prevent gender-biased sex selective elimination and facilitate her education.
 
 Tarn Taran district has now been selected for facilitation by the central government on the third anniversary of the BBBP scheme for remarkable outreach activities and new initiatives, said the minister.
