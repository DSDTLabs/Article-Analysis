Jaipur: In order to prevent drop out of girls during their puberty, the state government has roped in the industry department distribute sanitary napkins in government schools and dispose used ones by installing incinerators under corporate social responsibility (CSR).

 The corporates are being encouraged to enter uncharted territory under their CSR activity and promote menstrual   among girls. 
 
 The step is intended to prevent the drop out of girls, especially after attaining puberty. Due to ignorance and inability of girls in rural areas to buy sanitary napkins they were forced to absent themselves during their menstruation period, said an official. Officials said the disposal of used napkins is also a serious problem in the rural areas. In order to overcome this problem, the companies have been asked to install incinerators in school toilets.
