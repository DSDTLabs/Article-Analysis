GURUGRAM: A family in Ghata village, which is off   road, was allegedly attacked by alleged supporters of a newly elected   for not voting in his favour. Police said more than 15 people were booked.

 According to the complaint filed by Sohit Bokan, aresident of Ghata village, the accused were alleged supporters of  , the recently elected councillor from ward 30. The complainant claims that around 7.30am on Tuesday, he was sitting with his family members in the front yard of the house when more than a dozen men barged into the house carrying sticks, metal rods and a country-made pistol. They attacked the entire family and left them grievously injured. 
 
 “It was a neighbour’s intervention that saved us. My mother, Mukesh Devi, has also been seriously injured. Her   ornaments have been snatched. They targeted us because we did not vote for Mahesh Dayma,” Bokan alleged.
 
 An FIR was filed against Beer Singh, Arun, Monu, Abhishek, Kamal, Umesh, Ajit, Dharmbir, Tapesh, Chattarpal, Parmesh, Amit and others under sections 147 (rioting), 323 (causing hurt), 452 (house trespass), 379A (snatching), 506 (criminal intimidation) of IPC and 25-54-59 of Arms Act at Sector 56 police station. 
 
 “Acting on the complaint, we have registered the case and are verifying the facts first,” said ASI  , the investigating officer. When asked about the allegations, councillor Mahesh Dayma said they were all baseless. “The matter pertains to enmity between two families and my election has to do with it,” Dayma told TOI.
