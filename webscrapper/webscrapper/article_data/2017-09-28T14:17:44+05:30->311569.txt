VARANASI: For the first time in its history, the   administration on Thursday appointed Prof   as the   of the varsity.

 According to BHU spokesman Rajesh Singh, vice chancellor GC Tripathi appointed Royana Singh of the anatomy department of the Institute of Medical Science as the chief proctor 
 
 The move came two days after former BHU Chief Proctor O N Singh resigned from his post taking moral responsibility of the violence that took place at the varsity campus.
 
 On Wednesday, dean of students Prof M K Singh took the additional charge. Singh was asked to hold the chief proctor's office till the appointment of a regular chief proctor.
 
 Apart from ordering a separate probe, headed by a retired HC judge, the VC has already announced his decision to appoint senior girl students of the physical education and sports departments as women security personnel under the 'earn while learn' scheme of university.
 
 A high-level official report on the recent violence and arson in Banaras Hindu University (BHU) has reportedly noted that proper and timely communication with protesting girl students by the varsity administration could have nipped the problem in the bud and prevented it from escalating.
 
 According to sources, the VC was summoned by the MHRD department, though university officials claimed that he went to attend a quarterly meeting of the executive council of the university. 
 
 Trouble started at BHU campus on the night of September 21 when students of a girls' hostel started protesting 'sexual harassment' of a student of visual arts near Bharat Kala Bhawan.
 
 On September 23, protests against the alleged sexual harassment of a student took a violent turn on the campus when seven students, including girls, and two journalists were injured in police lathicharge.
