MADURAI: The Madurai bench of the Madras high court on Friday asked the city corporation to   commercial buildings in the city if their owners did not pay property tax dues in the next three weeks. 

 The order came on a day when corporation commissioner   appeared before the division bench of Justices N Kirubakaran and R Tharani as directed by the court on a petition filed by   Raj. 
 
 The court asked the commissioner, “The corporation revised the property tax in 2008 and by this time the corporation had to revise (it) twice, but it had not done it. What is the amount that has so far been paid and amount due to be  ?” The commissioner said the corporation had to collect Rs 168.29 crore in arrears. 
 
 The bench asked, “Have you issued any notice to the defaulters?” Additional advocate general   affirmed that notices were issued but that no response was received even after that.
