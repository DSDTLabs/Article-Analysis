Panaji: Property owners from Kerala and Goa who sold properties to leader of opposition Chandrakant ‘Babu’ Kavlekar have been summoned by anti-corruption branch (ACB) of the directorate of vigilance in the Rs 4.78 crore disproportionate assets case. ACB superintendent of police (SP) Priyanka Kashyap said ACB has started investigating the properties purchased by Kavlekar in the state. “We have summoned 15 property owners from Kerala and Goa to appear before the ACB from February 15 onwards,” she said. She also said that they want to verify whether the properties have been purchase from legal sources of income. “We want to verify the mode of purchase,” she told TOI. ACB has also summoned Kavlekar to appear at its office on Monday for questioning in the case. On September 15, 2017, ACB registered a first information report (FIR) in the disproportionate assets case under Section 13 (2) read with 13 (1) (e) of Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988. ACB had also booked Kavlekar’s wife Savitri in the case under Section 109 (abetment) of the Indian Penal Code (IPC) as she is a director of Kavlekar’s companies. ACB had conducted searches at Kavlekar’s residence at Quepem as well as his office and commercial offices at Quepem and Margao, respectively. In 2013, ACB had received information that Kavlekar, then chairman of Goa Industrial Development Corporation (GIDC), had acquired various properties in Kerala. Accordingly, ACB started preliminary investigations and conducted a verification drive into his entire tenure, from the time he was elected as MLA, till 2013. ACB found that Kavlekar purchased around 14 properties worth crores of rupees in Kerala. The ACB sleuths, who visited Mumbai to trace the companies that had lent several crores worth of loans to Kavlekar, found that the entities did not exist.
