BHOPAL: There was no respite from the sultry weather in   and parts of state on Saturday. Temperatures in the state are set to rise further. 

 The officials of meteorological department, Bhopal circle said there is a trough passing from Haryana, east Rajasthan and  .
 
 This would lead to convective activity in parts of state, including Bhopal, but heating during the day time would continue, which means there is no respite from the heat. 
 
 The day temperature in Bhopal on Saturday was recorded at 42.6 degrees Celsius, two degrees more than the normal mark while the minimum temperature in city was recorded at 29.0 degrees, three degrees more than normal mark. 
 
 The wind direction in the city was west northwest and average wind speed was recorded at 20 km per hour. The day temperature in Bhopal is expected to touch 45 degrees mark in a day or two, met officials said.
 
 A day earlier, the city recorded the maximum and minimum temperatures at 43.1 degrees and 20.8 degrees Celsius respectively.
 
 Meanwhile, the highest temperature in state was recorded at 46 degrees Celsius at Naogaon, Khargone and Hoshangabad.
 
 In the forecast for Bhopal on Sunday, the met officials said there would be partly cloudy sky and thundery development. The day and night temperature would be respectively at 42 degrees and 29 degrees Celsius and average wind speed will be 24 kmph.
 
 In the forecast for the state on Sunday, the met officials issued a warning of heat waves at places like Khargone, Khandwa, Burhanpur, Barwani, Damoh, Tikamgarh, Chhatarpur, Shajapur, Datia, Guna, Hoshangabad, Gwalior, Ashok Nagar and Sheopurkalan.
