FEROZEPUR: Police have booked 10 persons, including a  ,  ,   and stamp vendor, for allegedly usurping eight acres of land in   town by forging revenue documents. 

 In his statement to the police, Inder Mohan, a resident of Ferozepur, had accused influential persons and revenue officials of grabbing a piece of land measuring 64.5 kanals (32,250 sq yards) belonging to his brother   Nath. 
 
 He had earlier filed a complaint with the deputy commissioner in the matter, following which the police complaint was filed.
 
 Inder Mohan claimed that his brother Manmohan, who was mentally ill, had been missing for some time.
 
 Assistant sub-inspector Satnam Singh, who is the investigating officer in the case, said the Ferozepur DC had directed the sub-divisional magistrate (SDM) to the probe the matter. He said the SDM submitted his report to the DC who forwarded it to the superintendent of police (SP) for registration of a case after obtaining the legal opinion.
 
 A case of cheating, forgery and criminal conspiracy, under various sections of the Indian Penal Code, was registered against naib tehsildar Narinder Kumar, nambardar Paramjit Kaur Sodhi, registry clerk Manohar Lal, SRC Darshan Kaur, stamp vendor Gurnam Singh Thind, Jaswant Singh, Joginder Singh of Bamb Bandala, Vijay Kumar of Ferozepur, Gurjit Singh of Faridkot and Swarn Singh of Sudh Singh Wala. However, no arrests have been made so far, said a police official.
