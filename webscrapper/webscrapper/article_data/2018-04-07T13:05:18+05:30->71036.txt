MUSSOORIE: A 27-year-old forest beat officer,  , was allegedly brutally thrashed by a group of five people on Wednesday night for confronting a utility vehicle after he found it carrying stones illegally dug from the hillside near   village, about 27 kms from Mussoorie.
 
  Kumar is currently admitted in Doon Hospital, Dehradun. His family had registered a case with Mussoorie police in the matter against the five perpetrators late on Thursday. None of the men have been taken into custody as police is still probing the case.
  
  Speaking to TOI, Sub-inspector (SI) K N Purohit said, “A total of seven cases have been registered against five miscreants, including those under section 506 (Punishment for criminal intimidation), section 332 (Voluntarily causing hurt to deter public servant from his duty), section 147 (Punishment for rioting), section 353 (Assault or criminal force to deter public servant from discharge of his duty), section 323 (Punishment for voluntarily causing hurt) section 186 (Obstructing public servant in discharge of public functions) section 427 (Mischief causing damage to the amount of fifty rupees) and section 504 (Intentional insult with intent to provoke breach of the peace) of the IPC.”
  
  “A medical examination of the victim in Mussoorie has confirmed some abrasions and simple injuries. Some x-rays have also advised by doctors. We are investigating the matter.”
  
  Speaking to TOI from his hospital bed, Kumar said, “I was on a patrol when I saw a vehicle carrying stones illegally mined. As I challenged its driver and owner, they got into a scuffle with me and called the owner of the vehicle. A few minutes later, three people arrived in a car, one of them the owner, and beat me up badly. They then left me near some bushes. After gaining consciousness, I called up my office and police.”
