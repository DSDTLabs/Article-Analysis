BHUBANESWAR:   Aditya Padhi here on Tuesday asked district collectors and SPs to take steps to check rising road accidents in the state. While a total of 4,790 people died in road accidents in 2017, the death toll stood at 4,463 in 2016. 

 "We have asked police and   to suspend driving licences of rash bike riders and drivers. Strict enforcement is required to check accidents," read a statement from the chief secretary's office. An official source said the transport department suspended nearly 21,000 driving licences for rash driving last year.
 
 Wary of road accidents, especially involving a large number of helmet-less bike riders across the country, the   Committee on   led by Justice K S Radhakrishnan has asked all states to take action against people not wearing helmets.
