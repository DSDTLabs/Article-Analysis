TNN Panaji: Urban development minister Francis D’Souza on Tuesday directed Corporation of the city of Panaji (CCP) not to issue licenses to more eating outlets as also to be strict those operating illegalities. D’Souza told TOI that he Mayor Vithal Chopdekar and CCP commissioner Ajit Roy not to give any more permission for gaddas and hotels at a meeting called on Tuesday at the insistence of Panaji hotel and Traders association (PHATA). “So many hotels and gaddas should able to sustain themselves. The carrying capacity of the city has to be also considered,” said D’Souza adding that he addressed complaints by the Panaji traders and hoteliers that many small eateries operating without obtaining necessary permissions. Many do not have mandatory NOC from Food and Drugs Administration (FDA), the traders had complaint. Bernard Sapeco, who led the delegation said they want the corporation to crack down on illegal gaddas so many of them operating in the capital freely. “We will ensure that they are closed down,” he added. Other issues raised by traders during their last meeting with the minister, were discussed today. D’Souza said as to regards to the complaint by traders about repeated human interface in getting permissions from CCP, it would be attended to once permissions/licenses are issued online. “I agree that human interface has to be reduced,” the minister said. The traders also complaint to the minister about high rate of taxes charged by them. On taxes, D’Souza said he has asked mayor as well commissioner, submit him a report in two months.
