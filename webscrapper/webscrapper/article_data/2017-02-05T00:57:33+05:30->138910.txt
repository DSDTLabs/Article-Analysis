NEW DELHI: Nearly a dozen recommendations of groups of secretaries made it to the  . These include infrastructure status for affordable housing, transforming 1.5 lakh health centres as wellness centres, setting up wi-fi hotspots in panchayats to push digitisation, reducing maternity and infant deaths significantly by 2019 and putting special focus on logistics sector.

 The inter-ministerial groups of secretaries had held several rounds of brainstorming sessions to come out with ideas for the Budget and each ministry. Now, the Prime Ministerâ€™s Office has asked the groups to set a time-line for implementation of the recommendations.
 
 About half a dozen recommendations on health, urban development and sanitation from the group led by urban development secretary Rajiv Gauba have been incorporated.
 
 The group had recommended infrastructure status to incentivise affordable housing, reducing infant mortality rate to 28 per 1,000 births and maternal mortality rate to 100 by 2019, transforming 1.5 lakh health sub-centres to health and wellness centres and supplying safe drinking water in areas hit by arsenic contamination.
 
 Similarly, the group dealing with transport and communications had recommended creating wi-fi hotspots in every gram panchayat, providing additional funds to BharatNet and asset monetisation for civil aviation sector besides putting greater focus on logistics, which is going to be the big area for employment and growth.
 
 The Budget mentioned that an action plan has been prepared to reduce infant mortality rate from 39 in 2014 to 28 by 2019 and maternal mortality rate from 167 in 2011-13 to 100 by 2018-2020. It also mentioned the plan to provide safe drinking water to over 28,000 arsenic and fluoride-affected habitations in the next four years under a sub-mission.
 
 Similarly, allocation for BharatNet has been increased to Rs 10,000 crore.
