 Those retiring from the seats include SP chief Akhilesh Yadav, SP state president Naresh Uttam, SP chief spokesperson Rajendra Chaudhary, and minority affairs minister Mohsin Raza and rural development minister Mahendra Singh.
 
 The elections to the legislative council comes days after the BJP registered a win against the SP and   backed BSP candidate Bhimrao Ambedkar in the Rajya Sabha elections. 
 
 BSP is likely to field Bhimrao Ambedkar in the council elections. The party had fielded Ambedkar, backed by Congress and the SP, in the recent Rajya Sabha elections but he lost to BJP's candidate. 
 
 The Samajwadi Party, which enjoys a majority with 61 legislators in the council, will see its strength coming down by six. BSP's strength will also come down following the retirement of two members, including BSP leader in the council, Sunil Chittor. 
 
 BJP which has 13 legislators in the upper house, including CM Yogi Adityanath Yogi and deputy CMs Keshav Maurya and Dinesh Sharma, will see its tally increase from nine to 21.
