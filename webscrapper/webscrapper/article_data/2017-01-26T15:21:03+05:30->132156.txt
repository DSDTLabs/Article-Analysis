CHENNAI: Chief minister   unfurled the national flag at Marina on Thursday as Maharashtra Governor  , who holds the additional charge of Tamil Nadu, had to participate in the Republic Day celebrations at Mumbai. It is for the first time in many years that a chief minister of   hoisted the national flag on a Republic Day.
 
 However, the event on   was marked by the presence of few hundreds from the general public, in contrast to the previous years wherein people gathered in large numbers to witness the spectacular display of decorated floats and cultural programmes organised as part of the R-Day celebrations. 
 
 After unfurling the national flag near   statue, Panneerselvam exchanged salutes during the ceremonial march past of army, navy and the air force, besides the state police. It was followed by the procession of floats from 16 state government departments, which prominently featured the portraits of late chief minister   and the schemes launched during her tenure.
  
 Interestingly, the tableau of the agriculture department depicted the farmers festival of Pongal with the traditional earthen pot and bull. The same road, a few days ago, witnessed an unprecedented protest and violence to lift the ban on Jallikattu. It resulted in passing of a bill to amend the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals Act, 1960 to facilitate the conduct of the rural ancient bull taming sport in the state. Police was deployed across the stretch of Marina with spots like Vivekanandar House, the epicentre of pro-Jallikattu protests, recording significant presence of the cops.
  
 Earlier, the chief minister gave away various awards including the Anna Medal for Gallantry to a nursing student W Durgadevi. She (Durgadevi) jumped into Coovam River to rescue another student after a wooden bridge collapsed at Island Grounds during a marathon event in June last year (2016).
