LUDHIANA: Ludhiana   employees will observe a two-day strike on May 30 and 31 in the wake of the call given by United Forum of Bank Unions.

 The employees will also hold a massive demonstration at 9.30am on May 30 in front of Canara Bank, Bharat Nagar Chowk, and on May 31 in front of State Bank of India, Fountain Chowk. 
 
 Pawan Thakur, president of Punjab Bank Employees’ Federation, Ludhiana Unit, said, “The   and the government has failed to fulfil our long-pending demands. Besides, we were given a meagre hike in our salaries. This has forced us to go on 48-hour strike till 6am on June 1.”
 
 “All the employee associations under the banner of United Forum of Bank Unions and Punjab Bank Employees’ Federation will take part in the protest. We will ensure that the general public is not inconvenienced due to the agitation,” he added.
 
 Bank employees said they were offered only 2% hike in wages and their long-pending demands, like improvement in other service conditions, have remained unfulfilled.
