CHANDRAPUR: Industrialist Ratan Tata,  , a   which has a bulk of shareholding in the group firms, will be visiting Chandrapur on January 5.

 During Tata’s visit, an agreement between Tata Trust and Bamboo Research and Training Institute, coming up at Chichpalli in the district, will be signed. Tata Trust will shoulder the responsibility of modernizing the centre to make it an advanced institute in bamboo processing.
 
 Later in the day, another programme has been organized at Chanda Club ground where inauguration of incense sticks project of Tadoba Andhari Tiger Reserve (TATR), distribution of community forest rights and pattas to tribals along with launching of e-learning project in 571 zilla parishad schools will be held in Tata’s presence.
 
 Distribution of LPG cylinders under Dr Syama Prasad Mookerjee Jan-Van Scheme and essentials for newborn kids will also be held at the hands of Tata. Forest minister and guardian minister of district, Sudhir Mungantiwar, will accompany the industrialist during the programme.
 
 On December 28, Tata flew down to Nagpur to meet RSS chief Mohan Bhagwat. Described as a courtesy call, the meeting lasted 40 minutes in which Tata and Bhagwat discussed social work initiatives by both the organizations. In recent weeks, Tata has met several political personalities including Prime Minister Narendra Modi, finance minister Arun Jaitley, Maharashtra CM Devendra Fadnavis and UP CM Akhilesh Yadav.
