 Attributing the dip in the mercury to the active northerly winds Jaipur met office said that in the coming days mercury will further take a dip across the state especially in the northern and eastern parts of the state. 
 
 Jaipurities woke up to a chilly morning as a minimum temperature of 7.7 degrees Celsius was recorded in the city. It was followed by a shivering afternoon as a maximum temperature of 22.4 degrees Celsius was recorded in the city. During early morning and late evening hours the roads wore an empty look as people confined themselves to their respective homes. Fog and mist in the morning also affected the air, road and rail traffic across the state. At least 3 early morning flights had delayed take off while at least six early morning trains arrived late at the Jaipur railway station. The traffic on the highways connecting Jaipur to Agra, Sikar to Churu and Jaipur to Delhi was chaotic due to lesser visibility during early morning hours.
