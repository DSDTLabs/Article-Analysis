PATNA: As part of the Road Safety Week from April 23 to 30, chief Minister (CM)   will flag off 10 road safety awareness   from the Adhiveshan Bhawan on Friday.

 Being organized by the state transport department, the Road Safety Week is based on the theme ‘Sadak Suraksha, Jeevan Raksha’. The home department, the   (BSDMA), schools, colleges, universities and NGOs have been roped in to spread awareness about different traffic rules using placards and pamphlets. 
 
 “NCC cadets and schoolchildren will give roses to traffic violators on April 23 and a road safety seminar will be organized in schools, colleges and universities on April 24. Painting, debate and quiz competitions will be held in educational institutes on April 25 while on the remaining days, all vehicles will be inspected,” transport department deputy secretary Vinay Kumar Rai said on Wednesday and added local artists would perform street plays on major roads in Patna and other districts as well.
 
 While the Patna traffic police will conduct a seat belt and helmet drive, the BSDMA will organize a series of workshops on prevention of accidents.
 
 According to a   official, at least 50 trainers will cover 120 schools in 20 districts, including Araria, Arwal, Aurangabad, Bhojpur, Darbhanga, Gaya, Katihar, Madhepura, Muzaffarpur, Nalanda, Nawada, Patna, Purnia, Rohtas, Samastipur, Saran, Sitamarhi, Siwan, Supaul and   during the week. They will tell kids about road safety measures.
 
 Education department principal secretary R K Mahajan on Wednesday instructed district education officers to ask government school heads to conduct different activities from April 21 to 28.
