NEW DELHI: The capital saw   relief in terms of   on Thursday with   dropping by nearly 3 degrees Celsius to settle at a maximum of 36.1 degrees Celsius, just a day after it recorded the ‘hottest March day’ in nearly eight years. The maximum temperature on Wednesday crossed 40 degrees Celsius at Palam and a maximum of 38.6 degrees Celsius was recorded at Safdarjung.

 Met officials, however, forecast that temperature will continue to stay close to 36 degrees Celsius over the next two days, following which another spike is expected.
 
 “Delhi is likely to see a maximum of 36 degrees Celsius on Friday with a minimum of around 19 degrees Celsius,” an official said.
