 Chandigarh: A two-year-old child was seriously injured in an   on the road dividing sectors 16 and 17 on Tuesday morning. Police said a hatchback car with five occupants, including the child’s parents and two others rammed into an electricity pole, overturned and flipped on to the opposite road when the driver tried to avoid a speeding car. 
 
 Passersby straightened the car and found the toddler, Akom, who was seated next to the driver’s seat, bleeding. They rushed him to GMH-16, from where he was referred to PGIMER. Later, people informed the police. 
 
 The child’s father, Jitender Kumar, a resident of Kishangarh, said, “A speeding car overtook us, which scared me and I lost balance.” Hospital authorities said the child suffered head injuries and is in critical condition.
 
 SHO, Sector-17 police station, inspector Ranjit Singh said, “We have not registered a case as the incident occurred due to the driver’s fault.” Kumar’s wife was sitting on the back seat of the car, which had the ‘L’ sign on the rear window.
