MOHALI: Three people were killed in two hit-and-run cases in Mohali on Sunday.

 Vineet Dhir, 36, and his colleague Manish Kumar, 33, were killed when a speeding truck hit their car Hyundai Santro on the Rajpura-Airport road main crossing in the early hours of Sunday. The accident occurred around 2 am, police said. Vineet and Manish were residing in   in Zirakpur. Both hailed from Ludhiana. They were working in a textile company in Zirakpur, said assistant sub-inspector Ajit Singh, the investigating officer. Vineet and Manish are survived by their wives and two children each.
 
  
  
 
 The police official said, “We have recovered the registration number of the truck which sped away after hitting the car. The bodies of the two victims have been handed over to their family members after post-mortem. The last rites will be performed at Ludhiana.”
 
 The unknown truck driver was booked under Section 304 A (causing death due to negligent driving) of the Indian Penal Code (IPC) at the Zirakpur police station. The truck driver will be arrested soon, said Ajit Singh.
 
 In the second accident, a 55-year-old security guard working at a marriage palace in Zirakpur was killed after a car hit his bicycle on Landran-Banur road near Bhagomajra village in Mohali on Sunday morning. Dilbagh Singh, a resident of Morinda, was returning from his duty. When he reached near the village around 7am, the car rammed him. The driver sped away. Police officials said they have recovered a broken registration number plate of the vehicle from the spot. Police found out the car was a taxi registered in Punjab.
 
 Sohana police station house officer inspector Manjit Singh said police have booked the unknown car driver under Section 304 A (causing death due to negligent driving) of the Indian Penal Code (IPC) at the Sohana police station in Mohali.
