MOHALI: A private ambulance allegedly being driven on the wrong side hit a motorcycle at the light point in Phase VIII on Friday morning, killing an 18-year-old youth.

 Police said Ajay, who hailed from Uttar Pradesh, was waiting at the traffic signal for the light to turn green when the ambulance without any patient rammed into him around 6am. He was taken to Government Medical College and Hospital, Sector 32, Chandigarh, where doctor declared him brought dead. Police said   was going to his workplace, a factory, in Phase VIII. Police said the driver sped away, but he was later arrested and the vehicle seized.
 
 Ajay was living with his brother in   village in a rented accommodation
 
 Investigation officer Lakhveer Singh said, “The ambulance dragged Ajay a few metres. The driver was later caught. He was booked under Sections 279 (rash driving or riding on a public way), 337 (causing hurt by act endangering life or personal safety of others), 338 (causing grievous hurt by act endangering life or personal safety of others), 427 (mischief causing damage to the amount of fifty rupees) and 304 A (causing death by negligence) of the IPC at Phase I police station,” he said.
