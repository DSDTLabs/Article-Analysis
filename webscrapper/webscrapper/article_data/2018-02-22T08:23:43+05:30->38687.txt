PATIALA: Patiala police on Wednesday arrested one of the directors of   unit where four workers were killed and 11 had taken ill after inhaling ammonia gas that had spread after an explosion ripped apart a huge chemical container on Tuesday morning near Ghanaur. Police arrested  , a Patiala resident, who was booked along with his partner Satish Kumar after the incident.

 A case under sections 304 (culpable homicide not amounting to murder) and 336 (act endangering life or personal safety of others) of the IPC had been registered against the two directors following the incident.
 
 Meanwhile, the district authorities on Wednesday constituted of four-member panel that started the magisterial probe into the incident ordered by chief minister Captain Amarinder Singh. The committee comprises   sub-divisional magistrate (SDM), Ghanaur deputy superintendent of police (DSP), deputy director of factories and assistant labour commissioner, Patiala.
 
 The ammonia gas leakage occurred following a massive explosion in a huge gas cylinder installed outside the covered area of the unit located at Sandharsi village on the   road in Patiala district. 
 
 Patiala senior superintendent of police (SSP) S   said the arrested accused would be produced in court on Thursday to get his police custody for detailed investigation in the incident.
