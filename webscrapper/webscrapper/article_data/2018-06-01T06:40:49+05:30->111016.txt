HYDERABAD: After days of battling extreme heat, residents heaved a sigh of relief on Thursday, as sudden showers accompanied with thunderstorm and gusty winds lashed parts of the city. While meteorologists attributed the sudden downpour to formation of cumulonimbus clouds and not to the onset of southwest monsoon, the showers brought the temperature down to 40.1 degree Celsius by evening . 

 
 Over the last few days, city's average temperature has been hovering around 40-42 degree Celsius. According to meteorologists, this rise in temperature led to formation of rain bearing clouds over the city. "In the past few days, the maximum temperature touched 42.5 degree Celsius. The warm, moist air rises and cools, eventually condensing into rain bearing clouds," said Raja Rao, meteorologist at the   ( ), Hyderabad, that issued thunderstorm warning for some districts over next two days.
