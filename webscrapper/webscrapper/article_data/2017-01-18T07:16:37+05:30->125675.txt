HYDERABAD: The Telangana government on Tuesday admitted that cases on unnecessary caesarean deliveries of babies was a cause of concern in the state. Health minister Dr C   told the   during the question hour that all the district medical and health officers in Telangana have been instructed to crack down on such   deliveries.

 While not discussing any numbers, the minister said that all hospitals in the state have been instructed to send details of all surgeries performed by them every month along with reasons for the surgeries. “This will help us analyse and curb unwanted surgical operations,” the minister said.
 
 It was BJP floor leader G Kishen Reddy who demanded to know what the government was doing to curb “women being scared into undergoing C-sections” by private hospitals whose only interest was to charge huge bills from the hapless women.
