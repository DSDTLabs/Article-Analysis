The government has rolled out a Rs 1.4 lakh crore roadmap to turn farmers into generators of solar power, which they can use to run their pumpsets and sell the surplus to discoms.

 As an added benefit, barren land will be put to good use. The scheme, named KUSUM (Kisan Urja Suraksha Utthaan Maha Abhiyaan), will be spread over 10 years. 
 
 “The proposal has been appraised by different committees... Now it has been approved by the   (Expenditure   Committee) and the  . It will be launched next fiscal,” power minister   said on Friday. The refreshed scheme envisages spending Rs 48,000 crore over the next decade.
