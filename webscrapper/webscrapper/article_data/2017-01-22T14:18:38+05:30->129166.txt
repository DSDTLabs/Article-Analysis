GUWAHATI: The Archdiocese of Guwahati celebrated its silver jubilee at Jerome Institute on Saturday. 

 Chief minister   congratulated the Archdiocese for its services to the people of the city and the state. The president of the Catholic Bishops' Conference of India was also present on the occasion.
 
 The Diocese of Guwahati was set up by Pope John Paul II on August 16, 1992, with   as its first bishop. It was carved out of the dioceses of Shillong, Tura and Tezpur, the last contributing the largest portion. It was elevated to the status of an Archdiocese on August 1, 1995, and presently comprises Kamrup (Metro), Kamrup, Goalpara, Nagaon, Morigaon, Hojai and Tamulpur subdivision of Baksa district. Even east and south Kamrup come under the Archdiocese. 
 
 The Archdiocese runs 15 upper primary schools, 40 high schools, 12 higher secondary schools, one university, 31 boys' hostels, 41 girls' hostels, eight vocational development centres, five destitute homes, three hospitals and 12 healthcare centres and dispensaries in the state.
