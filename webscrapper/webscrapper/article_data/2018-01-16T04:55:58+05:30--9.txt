NEW DELHI: Two men have been arrested with 3 kg of “home-grown heroin” worth   in the international market. The drug was manufactured in UP using illegally sourced opium cultivated in fields around Bareilly for medicinal use. A hunt is on for the main supplier.

 The accused have been identified as Asiq Ali (36) from Bareilly and Savej (20) from Saharanpur in Uttar Pradesh. DCP (special cell) Sanjeev Yadav said that they received a tipoff about the accused coming to Anand Vihar after which a trap was laid.
 
 “Savez was spotted by an informer and held with two kilograms of drugs. He led the cops to Ali who was nabbed from Bareily with one kilogram of  . Several mobile handsets and SIM cards used in drug trafficking activities have been recovered from them,” Yadav said.
 
 Police said there has been massive cultivation of opium in Bareilly and Badaun till a few years ago. Licences were issued to some farmers for cultivating medicinal opium.
 
 “Several groups have cropped up who procure opium from farmers and process it to make heroin, and transport it to Delhi, UP, MP and Punjab through carriers. A key player in this racket has been identified as Riyaz who control a big chunk of the racket. The accused Ali is Riyaz’s Man Friday.” Yadav added.
