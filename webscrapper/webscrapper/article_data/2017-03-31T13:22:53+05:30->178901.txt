CHENNAI: The collective storage levels of major   in southern states further dropped by 2% within a week. 

 While the current live storage of 31 major reservoirs spread across  , Telangana,  ,   and   is 14% as on March 30, the collective storage was 16% on March 23.
  
 The Central Water Commission (CWC) monitors the storage levels of these 31 reservoirs in the five southern states, which have total live storage capacity of 51.59 BCM. 
 
 According to an official statement by the Union water resources ministry, the total live storage available in these reservoirs is 7.18 BCM, which is 14% of total live storage capacity of these reservoirs. 
 
 "The storage during corresponding period of last year was 17% and average storage of last ten years during corresponding period was 27% of live storage capacity of these reservoirs. Thus, storage during current year is less than the corresponding period of last year and is also less than the average storage of last ten years during the corresponding period," the statement said.
  
 Himachal Pradesh, Tripura, Andhra Pradesh, Karnataka, Kerala, and Tamil Nadu have lesser storage than last year for corresponding period, the statement said. 
 
 Punjab, Rajasthan, Jharkhand, Odisha, West Bengal, Gujarat, Maharashtra, Uttar Pradesh, Uttarakhand, Madhya Pradesh, Chhattisgarh and Telangana have better storage than last year for corresponding period.
