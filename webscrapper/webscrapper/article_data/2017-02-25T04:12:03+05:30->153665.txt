AHMEDABAD: Ramanujam Parthasarathy, a retired IAS officer of the   cadre, addressed the Faculty of Life Sciences and Biotechnology of the South Asian University, a new institution established by the   nations in New Delhi, on Gender Bias in Nobel awards. 

 Paying tribute to the legend   (1867-1934) whose 150th birth anniversary year falls this year, Parthasarathy concentrated on Science Nobel Prizes, specially the chemistry and biochemistry awards, and the few women who made it and many who did not despite outstanding contribution from the days of Madam Curie. 
 
 The lecture dealt with historical, biographical and scientific aspects of women chemists who fought for their rights for equality in the UK, USA, Europe and elsewhere.
