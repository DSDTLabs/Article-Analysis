MUMBAI: Heavy rainfall accompanied by hailstorm that started early on Sunday morning claimed two lives in Maharashtra. 

 Gusty winds and hailstorm have also destroyed standing crops of wheat, grapes and chickpea in Jaalna, Beed, Buldhana, Washim, Amravati and Akola districts of Vidarbha and Marathwada region. Heavy rainfall destroyed most of the winter harvest.
 
 
 
 
 According to an   (IMD) mid-day update on Sunday, a cyclonic circulation lies over south Maharashtra and neighbouring west Rajasthan and extends upto 0.9 km above mean sea level.
 
 
  
 
 The current Western Disturbance with associated Induced cyclonic circulation and its interaction with low level easterlies is likely to cause scattered to fairly widespread precipitation over north and central India during three days, the IMD said. 
 
 The IMD on Saturday had issued a warning of thunderstorms accompanied by hailstorms that are very likely to take place at isolated places in Maharashtra, Marathwada, Vidarbha, Madhya Pradesh, Jammu,  , Uttarakhand, Punjab, Haryana, Chandigarh and Delhi, west Uttar Pradesh on Sunday.
