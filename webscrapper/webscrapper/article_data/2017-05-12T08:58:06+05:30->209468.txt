 Briefing mediapersons on the arrest, Vipul Shukla, deputy inspector general (DIG) of police, Palamu range, said, "On Wednesday, a small team of police nabbed Ram who carried a reward of Rs 10 lakh on his head. The Maoist was wanted in connection with at least 20 cases."
 
 Shukla added, "Ram was a member of Bihar Jharkhand Special Area Committee but was most active in Palamu and Garhwa districts. He held the rank of area and zonal commander."
 
 The DIG further urged other rebels to surrender their arms and join the mainstream. "All Maoists who are still at large should immediately shun the path of violence and join mainstream," he said.
 
 According to the police, Ram was first imprisoned by Garhwa district police in 1999 and got bail in 2003. He later re-joined the Maoist group in 2005 but once against sent back to prison by Garhwa police in 2008. He got bail in 2011. 
 
 At least four permanent arrest warrants were issued against him by various courts.
