THIRUVANANTHAPURAM: Rains lashed most parts of the state on Friday bringing relief from the summer heat. The   (IMD) forecast heavy rains in parts of the state for the next two days and thundershowers for the next five days. Thunderstorms and gusty winds are also likely to affects parts of Kerala, mostly during the evening hours.

 “Heavy rains (7-11cm) are likely to continue till April 15. We have also issued alerts that thunderstorm accompanied with gusty winds are expected in Alappuzha, Ernakulum, Idukki, Kollam, Kottayam,  , Pathanamthitta, Thrissur and Thiruvananthapuram districts,” IMD director K Santosh said.
 
 Fishermen have been advised not to venture out into the sea. Squally winds, occasionally reaching 40-50kmph is expected over Kanyakumari, Maldives and Lakshadweep areas on April 14. The Indian National Centre for Ocean Information Services (INCOIS) has also issued high wind alerts for Kerala.
 
 Scientists at the Kerala State Disaster Management Authority (KSDMA) have also issued warnings across the state based on IMD forecast. They have also issued lightning alerts and a set of safety rules.
 
 In the past 24 hours, Manjeri in Malappuram district recorded the maximum rainfall of 6cm. Kozhikode and Idukki recorded rainfall of 4cm each, while parts of Thiruvananthapuram, Thodupuzha in Idukki, Konni in Pathanamthitta, Irikkur in Kannur and Kuppady in Wayanad received a rainfall of 3cm each.
