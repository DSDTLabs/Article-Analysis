CHANDIGARH: Intensifying his attack on the Manohar Lal Khattar-led BJP government in Haryana and the   at the Centre, former chief minister   on Saturday said the ruling party should apologise to farmers for the injustice meted out to them. Addressing a ‘Kisan Panchayat’ in Jind on Saturday, Hooda warned that his fight for farmers and labourers would continue till the government acknowledged their problems.

 “I will not sleep nor give up the fight against this government,” Hooda said, while addressing a huge turnout of farmers at the new grain market of Jind. He alleged that initially the BJP government hit the farmers below the belt and now it is attacking them with rifle butts and bullets. “This is what Prime Minister   and the Khattar government has given to farmers in the past three years. The Congress will not tolerate all this,” Hooda said.
 
 He also attacked the government for letting major projects shift out of Haryana and the deteriorating law and order situation in the state. “The government has only renamed projects announced by the Congress government and especially those on, which it had criticized us the most,” said the former CM.
 
 Prominent among those who spoke on the occasion included Hooda’s son and Rohtak MP Deepender Hooda and sitting and former Congress MLAs. Meanwhile, traffic snarls were witnessed on the roads connecting Kaithal to Jind, Rohtak to Jind and Hisar to Jind due to farmers’ turnout. 
 
 
 
 
 Congress chief spokesperson   took on the Centre over GST during a traders’ meeting held at Kaithal. Surjewala said the Union government had taken away ‘roti, kapda and makan’ from the common man. He assured the traders of Congress’s backing in their struggle till the NDA government rationalized the GST slabs.
