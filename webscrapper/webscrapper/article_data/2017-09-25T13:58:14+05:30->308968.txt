In Monday's intra day trade, the bearish sentiment loomed large as both   and   faced huge losses. This comes after the equity markets saw a major downturn on Friday as Sensex slipped almost 450 points.

 The 30-share BSE Sensex opened on 31,986.40, 63.96 points blow previous closing while the broader 50-share NSE Nifty lost just 4.30 points to open on 9,960.10. But, both the indices extended their losses and at 1.46 pm, Sensex and Nifty had lost 424.85 and 136.05 points respectively.
 
 Both the indices had lost more than 1.3 per cent as Sensex hit the day low of 31,493.02 points.
 
 About five shares declined for every share rising on the BSE. The BSE Midcap index was down 1.6 per cent and Smallcap declined 2.3 per cent.
 
 Among sectoral indices, metal and pharma stocks were bleeding the most with the Nifty Metal and Nifty Pharma index down 2.69 and 2.09 per cent respectively. All the sectors were trading in the red.
 
 Analysts believe that the stock markets which were already under pressure due to geopolitical tensions, cracked further after Finance Minister   hinted upon injecting funds in the economy, which may end up stretching the fiscal deficit target of the government.
 
 Tata Steel, Adani Ports, Lupin, Aurobindo Pharma and ACC were all losing above 3 per cent. PowerGrid, Tata Power, TCS and Hindustan Unilever were some of the few stocks performing better. Power stocks were on the rise in the anticipation of the announcement of 'Power for All' scheme by Prime Minister Narendra Modi later in the day.
