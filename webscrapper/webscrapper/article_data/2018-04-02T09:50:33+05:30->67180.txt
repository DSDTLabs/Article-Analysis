NEW DELHI: On Monday, the first trading session of financial year 2018-19, the benchmark equity indices opened on a flat note. Both   and   were in the green in early trade.

 
 30-share BSE Sensex gained 62 points to go past the 33,000-mark and open at 33,030.87 while the broader 50-share NSE Nifty started trading on 10,151.65, up 38 points. 
 
 The bank sectoral sub-index was in the red but only slightly. IT and   stocks were outperforming.
 
  Investors' wealth surged Rs 20.70 lakh crore during 2017-18 fiscal helped by robust broader market sentiment where the BSE benchmark index rose by over 11 per cent. Sensex soared 3,348.18 points, or 11.30 per cent, to 32,968.68 during 2017-18. The flagship index touched its all-time high of 36,443.98 on January 29, 2018. Small and mid-cap indices of the BSE took a bigger hit as compared to their bigger peers, with smaller stocks falling up to 12 per cent in FY18.
