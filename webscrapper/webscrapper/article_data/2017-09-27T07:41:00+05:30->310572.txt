Ludhiana: With a view to countering the heavy rush witnessed during the festival season, Northern   authorities have decided to run some special trains from the national capital to Mata Vaishno Devi Katra through Ludhiana railway station from September 27. As many as four trains would be run by Northern Railways between Delhi-Mata Vaishno Devi Katra route from Wednesday; one train will run for Katihar junction passing through city station to ease the rush on trains for Bihar, which the migrant labourers preferred to travel on, mostly during Diwali season.

 Giving information regarding special trains run by Northern Railways, station superintendent Ashok Salaria stated: “Train number 82401/82402 (Delhi-Shri Mata Vaishno Devi Katra) will run on September 27, 28, 30, and October 1, while the train from Katihar to the national capital will run on September 28, 30, and October 2, with almost 10 to 15 coaches. The trains would have stoppages at Panipat, Ambala, Ludhiana, and Jalandhar Cantt, Pathankot, Udhampur, and Jammu Tawi.”
 
 He added: “Another train which will help passengers reach Mata Vaishno Devi Katra is Anand Vihar-Shri Mata Vaishno Devi (04401/04402) from Anand Vihar to Katra on September 25 and 29, and it will come back from Katra to Anand Vihar on September 27 and October 1. The train will halt at Ghaziabad junction, Meerut City junction, Muzaffarnagar, Saharanpur, Yamuna Nagar, Ambala, Ludhiana, Jalandhar, and Pathankot.”
 
 Salaria further said: “For migrant labour, the Northern Railways will run Katihar-Jalandhar city special train (05717/05718), which will run from Katihar to Jalandhar City every Thursday from September 28 until October 26. On the other hand, it will run from Jalandhar to Katihar on every Saturday, from September 30 until October 28. The train would have stoppages at Naugachia, Khagaria, Begusarai, Chhapra, Gorakhpur, Gonda, Sitapur Cantt, Saharanpur, Ambala Cantt, and Ludhiana station.”
