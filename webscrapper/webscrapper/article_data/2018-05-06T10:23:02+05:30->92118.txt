Indore: An   was grievously injured after he was attacked by a pushcart owner with a sharp-edged weapon during a   in   on Saturday.

 IMC employees said that action was being taken against the encroachers, who had returned and set up their shops in the area. 
 
 The   worker Ashish, a resident of Kailashpuri at MR 10, allegedly entered into a verbal spat with owner of a coconut pushcart in front of a gym on the road. The argument escalated to an extent that the accused Anas, a resident of Khajrana, took out the knife used to cut coconuts and attacked Ashish with it. Locals claimed that Anas was not cooperating with the removal wing. 
 
 Ashish sustained severe injuries on his left ear and was rushed to a private hospital by the IMC staff. 
 
 Palasia police arrested the accused from the spot. “Anas was booked under Section 294 (obscene act in public place), 323 (punishment for voluntarily causing hurt), 353 (assault or criminal force to deter public servant from discharge of his duty), 332 (voluntarily causing hurt to deter public servant from his duty) and 506 (criminal intimidation) of the IPC,” said Palasia police station in-charge DS Yevale.
 
 Mayor Malini Gaud and IMC commissioner Ashish Singh also took note of the situation and asked Palasia police to initiate strict action against the vendor. Senior police officials were also on the scene.
