Kanyakumari: A flower market with 75 shops, built at a cost of Rs 3 crore, at Thovalai in Kanyakumari district, was inaugurated by chief minister Edappadi K Palanisami through video conference on Tuesday. The chief minister named the complex after Dr MGR. He also inaugurated an integrated agriculture extension centre building at Boothapandi in Thovalai that was built at Rs 1.5 crore. Agriculture department officials said that the flower complex was announced by former chief minister J Jayalalithaa in 2015-16 to facilitate sale of jasmine, rose and other flower varieties that are grown and sold by farmers and traders of the region. The complex will benefit stakeholders of Kanyakumari and Tirunelveli districts. Officials from the agriculture, horticulture, farmers and traders were present at the events. tnn
