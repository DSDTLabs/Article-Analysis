 “Today’s doodle, illustrated by Sudeepti Tucker, depicts Sarabhai with her signature parasol, standing proudly in the auditorium of that same academy (Darpana School of Performing Arts) where her students are dancing on stage,” mentioned the description of the doodle. Tucker is a Delhi-based illustrator.
 
 “Today’s doodle celebrates Indian classical dancer Mrinalini Sarabhai, who developed her own technique, spirit, and strength by training at a young age, studying both the South Indian classical dance form of Bharatanatyam and the classical dance-drama of Kathakali,” the description said.
 
 Born in Kerala on May 11, 1918, as one of the four children of lawyer S Swaminathan and freedom fighter Ammu Swaminathan, Mrinalini realized her passion for dancing at a very young age. She was an expert in Bharatanatyam as well as Kathakali. She did her studies in Kalakshetra and Shantiniketan.
 
 She married renowned physicist   in 1942. Seven years later, the couple started the Darpana Academy of Performing Arts in Ahmedabad.
 
 Their son Karthikeya Sarabhai and daughter Mallika Sarabhai too grew up to become famous artistes.
 
 Freedom fighter Captain Lakshmi was Mrinalini’s sister. Mrinalini was the first recipient of the state government’s Nishagandhi Puraskaram in 2013. She passed away at the age of 97 in 2016.
 
 Some of the Indian women Google has honoured with a doodle are the country’s first lady doctor Anandi Gopal Joshi, one of the first women physicians Rukhmabai, dancer Rukmini Devi Arundale, social reformer  , and actor Nutan, among others. A doodle was created on the 45th anniversary of Chipko movement led by a group of women.
