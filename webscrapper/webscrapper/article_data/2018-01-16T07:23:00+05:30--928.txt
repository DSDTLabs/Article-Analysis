Chandigarh: Human resource development (HRD) ministry has not released the grant of two city colleges under Rashtriya Uchchatar Shiksha Abhiyan (RUSA) scheme for not achieving the desired target of utilisation of the grant.

 In a communication received on Monday, it was stated that the grant has not been released by the MHRD to Sri Guru Gobind Singh, Sector 26 and Guru Gobind Singh College for Women, Sector 26, Chandigarh for not achieving the desired target of percentage utilisation of the amount received earlier for infrastructure.
 
 Principal Guru Gobind Singh College for Women, Sector-26 Charanjeet Sohi said the college will get the grant after they spend 75% of the grant received previously
 
 MHRD, under its RUSA scheme, has released the grant amounting to Rs 4 crore under infrastructure head as second installment. Seven colleges including Post Graduate Government College for Girls, Sector 42, Post Graduate Government College for Girls, Sector 11, Post Graduate Government College, Sector 46, Government Home Science College, Sector 10, MCM College for Women 36, Dev Samaj College for Women, Sector 45, DAV College, Sector 10 and Regional Institute of English, Sector 32, Chandigarh received rs 50 Lakh each.
 
 The grant has been released on the basis of percentage utilisation of earlier grant received from MHRD under the said head of RUSA. Earlier, the Project Approval Board (PAB) of Rashtriya Uchchatar Shiksha Abhiyan (RUSA) of MHRD, has accorded approval for the total amount of Rs 20 crore under infrastructure grant to city colleges.
 
 Rakesh Kumar Popli, state project director (RUSA) - cum- director higher education, UT said this grant would provide new dimension in strengthening the infrastructure resources in the existing colleges and helps in providing quality and branding in higher education in UT, Chandigarh.
 
 Dr Dalip Kumar, ASPD (RUSA), UT, Chandigarh, elaborated that the infrastructure grants will be utilised for new constructions like establishment of new laboratories for existing programmes and for new programmes, both at the level of PG and UG, new classrooms, toilets, computer labs, renovations like modernisation and strengthening of laboratories, sports facilities, improvement of supporting department, academic and administrative blocks, hostels, toilets, equipment like updation of software of learning resource, strengthening of libraries and access to knowledge resource and procurement of furniture and fixtures. He, further, mentioned that the action plan has already been prepared by the colleges in utilising the approved grant in time bound manner as well as following the norms of the MHRD and Chandigarh administration.
