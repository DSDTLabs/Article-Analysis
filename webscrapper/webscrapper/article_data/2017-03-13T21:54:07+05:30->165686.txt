The cops said that while the accused are locals, those whom the local lad De was trying to protect were people from outside the neighbourhood.

 According to police sources, around three men were conversing among themselves near the tea stall when another three men from the neighbourhood crossed them and tried to spray colour on them. When these men objected, an argument broke out. The police said that those at the receiving end called up their friends. Accordingly two more men arrived at the spot and the argument soon turned violent.
 
 De, whose relative runs the tea stall – decided to intervene. "It was then that someone suddenly picked up the kettle full of hot water and threw it towards De, leaving him severely wounded on his right leg. He also broke his leg as he fell down feeling immense burning sensation. He was admitted at the   and later to a local nursing home where his condition is stated to out of danger.
 
 Police said that they have so far identified two persons involved in the fracas. “We are trying to gather CCTV footage from the area to establish the exact chain of events. The statement of the tea stall owner, a relative of the victim, is being recorded. We have recorded a suo-motto case and have begun investigations. We will be carrying out raids in the area soon in this regard," said an investigating officer.
