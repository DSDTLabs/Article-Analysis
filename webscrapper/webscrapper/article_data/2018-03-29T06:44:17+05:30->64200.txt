 Here are some quick tips you need to know while filing the income tax returns ( ):
 
 
 
 IT department keeps reminding the taxpayers to not to wait for the last day to file their ITR. But in the worst case scenario, if you have missed the filing deadline of July 31, you can file a belated return within a year. For example, belated return for the financial year (FY) 2017-18 can be filed by March 31, 2019.
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 Income tax department never asks you for your OTP, Password or other such information for credit cards, banks or other financial accounts through e-mail or phone calls. Thus, taxpayers are cautioned much beforehand not to respond to such e-mails and phone calls and never to share their personal or financial information with anyone.
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 Interest earned on your saving bank account is taxable if the aggregate interest from all similar accounts is more than Rs 10,000. If the interest earned is less than this amount, you are required to report this in your ITR. In case, you are a senior citizen, interest up to Rs 50,000 is not taxable from the financial year 2018-19.
 
 
 
 
 
 
 People staying as tenants who pay rent of over Rs 50,000 a month must deduct TDS at 5 per cent. But your landlord will get credit for the TDS and you cannot set it off with your tax liability.
