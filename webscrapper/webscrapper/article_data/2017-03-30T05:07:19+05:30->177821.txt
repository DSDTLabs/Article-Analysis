NEW DELHI: A 46-year-old man, who worked as a   at an   in   was in for shock on Wednesday when he was attacked by someone inside the ATM booth. 

 According to police, the victim, identified as Rakesh, was working the night shift at the booth which would end in the morning. He told the cops that as he was feeling unwell, he thought of taking a small nap inside the booth around 2.30am.
 
 Rakesh told the cops that after around half an hour, a person came inside the booth and tried to fiddle with the machine. When he tried to stop the man, the accused hit him on the head with a stick and then fled from the spot. The victim fell unconscious after the blow and regained consciousness at 5am, and found himself was bleeding profusely from the wound on his head.
 
 The guard then called up his wife to inform her about the incident, who in turn called up the police. 
 
 "The ATM was intact when our team reached the spot, though the man had been attacked with sticks. We are now scanning the CCTV footage of the area to ascertain the identity of the accused," said Surender Kumar, deputy commissioner of police (south west). 
 
 A senior police official said that they have registered a case under section 323 (punishment for voluntarily causing hurt) of the IPC and are investigating into the matter.
