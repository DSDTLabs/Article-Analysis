Ghaziabad: An FIR was registered on Wednesday against unidentified persons for allegedly burning garbage in the open in Vasundhara Sector 7. The case was registered at Indirapuram police station on the basis of a complaint lodged by city-based environmental activist Vikrant Sharma.

 Sharma registered the complaint after he noticed smoke billowing from garbage set on fire on a plot of land just behind Indirapuram police station in Vasundhara. The plot belongs to UP Housing & Development Board and the fire was sighted at around 5 pm.
 
 “I noticed a huge cloud of smoke in the air while travelling to   from Delhi. I followed the smoke and its source was found in the garbage strewn over the vacant plot of land. Setting garbage on fire is also a violation of the orders of the  ,” Sharma told TOI.
 
 A Dial 100 police SUV reached the spot as soon as Sharma called up the control room. The fire was doused with the help of sprinklers and a fire brigade.
 
 The FIR has been registered under section 278 (making atmosphere noxious to health) of IPC. “We tried to ascertain the identity of the person who had set fire to the garbage but have failed so far. The area will be kept under vigilance in order to avoid future instances of fire,” said a police officer.
 
 According to Sharma, burning of municipal solid waste in open areas is widespread in several areas of the city notwithstanding the prohibitory orders issued by the green tribunal.
