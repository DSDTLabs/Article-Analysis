BIHARSHARIF: Nalanda district and police administration are geared up for President Pranab Mukherjee’s visit to Rajgir to participate in the closing ceremony of the three-day International Buddhist Conclave (IBC) on March 19. The conclave is to be inaugurated by Buddhist spiritual leader the Dalai Lama at Rajgir International Convention Centre (RICC) on March 17.   Bihar governor Ram Nath Kovind, chief minister Nitish Kumar and several central and state ministers are expected to attend the conclave.    President Mukherjee had last visited Rajgir on August 27, 2016 to lay the foundation stone of the proposed building of Nalanda University (NU) and give away degrees to the students of the first batch of NU who had joined the university in 2014.    The conclave is being organized jointly by Nav Nalanda Mahavihara (NNM), a deemed university located in the vicinity of the ruins of historic Nalanda University, and Union ministry of culture. Over 150 monks from southeast Asian countries and hundreds of delegates from across the world are likely to participate in the conclave, said NNM’s media in-charge Dr Srikant Singh.    Grammy Award winner guitarist Niladri Kumar, noted Kathak performer Sovana Narayan and over 100 folk artists from across the country are expected to perform during the first two days of the conclave.
