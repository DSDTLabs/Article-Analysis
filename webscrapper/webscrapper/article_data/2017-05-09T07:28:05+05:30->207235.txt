NEW DELHI: The labour ministry clarified on Monday that employees already on   will be eligible for enhanced paid leave under the amended Maternity Benefit Act which came into force on April 1. There was confusion if employees already on maternity leave would get the benefit.
 
 “Having received further queries and to remove doubts, it is further clarified that it is mandatory on the part of employers to extend the benefit of enhanced maternity leave to those women workers who were already on maternity leave on the date of enforcement of the Maternity Benefit (Amendment) Act, 2017 i.e. as on April 1, 2017,” a statement issued by the ministry said.
 
 The amended Act requires establishments with over 50 employees to provide creche facilities within a prescribed distance. The government notified the Maternity Benefit (Amendment) Act, 2017 on March 28 and it came into force with effect from April 1, 2017.
