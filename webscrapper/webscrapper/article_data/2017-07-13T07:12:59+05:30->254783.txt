In May 2014, police busted a child prostitution racket, rescued four minor girls and booked 18 people, including eight policemen and a retired police official.
 
 The modus operandi of the gang was to target runaway girls and entice them promising money and lavish life and force them into prostitution. The gang members mostly auto drivers spot minor girls loitering at the bus stand and offer them free ride to the beach where other members of the gang take control over the girls. The gang members initially offer them food and accommodation. Later, they force the girls into prostitution. The gang used guest houses in Puducherry and Cuddalore district for their criminal activities.
 
 Police said of the four girls rescued in the latest case, two hail from Puducherry, one each from Karaikal and Cuddalore district. All the four girls are runaway children. Three of them are orphans. One of the girls from Puducherry had eloped with her lover.
