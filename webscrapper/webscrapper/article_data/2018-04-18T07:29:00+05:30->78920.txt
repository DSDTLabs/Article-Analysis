SANGRUR: The police have busted an inter-state drug trafficking racket with the arrest of two people from whose possession four quintal of charas, 250 intoxicating tablets and Rs 17 lakh in cash was seized on late Monday evening.

 The accused identified as Pritpal Singh, alias Mani, of   in  , and Sham Lal, alias Kala of   were nabbed from the outskirts of Maanwala village near Dhuri.
 
 SSP Mandeep Singh Sidhu said the accused brought the contraband from their contacts in the neighbouring towns of Haryana and then distributed across   using their own network of peddlers. He said this was the second time that the accused were ferrying contraband into the state but were nabbed on the basis of a tip-off. A pick-up truck bearing a Haryana registration number has also been confiscated by the cops.
 
 Sidhu said investigators were now trying to break the supply chain between the drug peddlers. A case under Sections 21, 22, 61 and 85 of the Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances (NDPS) Act of 1985 has been registered against the accused at the Sadar police station in Dhuri, the SSP said.
 
 The SSP said police had also identified one Ashish Kumar of Barnala, who would further distribute the drugs amongst other peddlers. The police learnt that Rs 17 lakh seized from the accused had been collected by Pritpal from those he would sell drugs to.
 
 Sidhu said this was the biggest haul of charas recovered during the current year. He said the accused were trying to sell these drugs under the grab of some ayurvedic medicines.
