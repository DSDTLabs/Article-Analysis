Kolkata: Indian   here is holding a unique exhibition of rare sculptures of Jain Tirthankars from March 16-18. 

 This is the first time the museum’s rare treasures from its reserve collection will be put on display on the occasion of the 6th International Congress of Society of South Asian Archaeology at Indian Museum in association with All India Digambar Jain Heritage Preservation Organization, New Delhi.
 
 The 11 metal sculptures of Tirthankars include metal and stone carved statues made in the 9th, 10th, 11th and 12th centuries AD. “The three-day congress will see deliberations on historic Indian heritage and art by as many as 200 experts,” said Sayan Bhattacharya, education officer of Indian Museum and coordinator of the congress.
 
 The Jain Tirthankars relentlessly fought against passions and bodily senses to achieve enlightenment, said Nirmal Kumar Jain, president of Digambar Jain Samaj.
