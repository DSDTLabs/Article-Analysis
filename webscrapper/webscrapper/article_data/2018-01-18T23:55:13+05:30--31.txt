Hyderabad: Homegrown engineering, manufacturing, geospatial, network and operations management solutions provider   Limited has posted a 6.8% drop in net profit for the third quarter of FY18 at Rs 87.8 crore as against a net profit of Rs 94.2 crore in the corresponding quarter of FY17. During the third quarter of FY18, the company reported an exceptional operating loss of Rs 20.4 crore due to hurricanes in Puerto Rico and a loss of Rs 5 crore on divestment of an associate firm — IASI — it added.

 Despite a dip in profits, the company’s revenue grew 7.2% during the quarter ended December 31, 2017, to Rs 983.4 crore as compared to Rs 917.1 crore in the year-ago period. On a sequential basis, the company’s profit during the third quarter stood at Rs 87.8 crore, a 21.3% drop as compared to Rs 111.4 crore in the second quarter of FY18. However, QoQ revenues too increased to Rs 983.4 crore from Rs 965.4 crore. “Q3FY18 was in line with our expectations both in revenue and margin. We are coming off a strong quarter in Q2 and Q3 is a seasonally weak quarter due to lower working days. Despite this, we recorded the highest ever revenue of $152 million in the quarter, an increase of 11.9% YoY and 1.3% QoQ,” Cyient Limited MD & CEO Krishna Bodanapu said. 
 
 Geography wise, Americas contributed 55.1% to revenues, EMEA 27.7% and Asia Pacific 17.2%. The company also declared a second interim dividend of 80% (Rs 4 per share on face value of Rs 5) for FY18.
