BHOPAL: Chambal, Gwalior, Bhopal, Hoshangabad, Indore and Ujjain divisions are expected to witness a sharp rise in temperatures in coming days. Weatherman said dry winds from Rajasthan will blow in these places, resulting into an increase in day temperatures.

 Officials of meteorological department, Bhopal circle, said there is no system or moisture incursion in the state. Winters are almost over and temperatures are set to rise in most of the places in the state. The day temperature in Bhopal on Friday was recorded at 28.9 degrees Celsius, which was at the normal mark while the night temperature was recorded at 13.6 degrees Celsius, a degree above the normal mark. Wind direction in the city kept varying to northwest and northeast while the average wind speed was calm at 8 kilometres per hour.
 
 Meanwhile, the lowest temperature in the state was recorded in Rewa at 7 degrees Celsius while the maximum temperature was recorded in Ratlam at 33.6 degrees Celsius. Temperature dipping in Rewa was attributed to blowing of northerly cold winds due to western disturbance over Jammu and Kashmir. In the forecast for Bhopal on Saturday, the weatherman said there would be a clear sky and average wind speed will be around 12 to 14 kilometres per hour. The maximum and minimum temperatures will be respectively at 29 degrees and 13 degrees Celsius. In the forecast of state, the weatherman said   is likely to remain dry. Met department did not issue any weather warning for the next 24 hours.
