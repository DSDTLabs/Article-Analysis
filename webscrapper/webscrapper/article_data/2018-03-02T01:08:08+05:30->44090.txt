Tirunelveli: In a shocking incident a 9th standard student of a government school at Kalakkad in rural Tirunelveli stabbed a classmate in a fit of rage over the victim. The accused was booked under four sections of the Indian Penal Code (IPC) including attempt to murder, was arrested and lodged in the home for children in conflict with the law. The victim identified as P Kannan, 14, is undergoing treatment in the Tirunelveli Medical College and Hospital (TVMCH). The police said that his condition is stable and he is out of danger and will be under observation for a few days. According to the police, preliminary inquiry with the students revealed that Kannan from Kirubalini street in Kalakkad is the class representative. The boy accused of committing the crime was from Melapathai in a nearby locality. It is learnt that a few days ago Kannan complained about the activities of the accused and a few other boys to the class teacher. On Thursday morning while Kannan was entering the classroom the accused in a fit of rage stabbed Kannan with a knife and fled the place. Kannan was rushed to the Nanguneri Government Hospital, where he was given first aid and referred to the TVMCH. Meanwhile the Kalakkad Police registered a case against the accused under sections 307 (attempt to murder), 324 (voluntarily causing hurt by dangerous weapons on means), 506(ii) (criminal intimidation) and 294(B) (using abusive language) of the IPC and arrested him. He was produced before the Juvenile Justice Board and lodged him in the home for children in conflict with the law.
