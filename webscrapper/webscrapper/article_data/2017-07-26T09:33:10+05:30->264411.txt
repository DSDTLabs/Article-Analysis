 Prabuddha H R, the deputy conservator of forests, Ahmedabad, said that rescue calls came from waterlogged areas. “It is the season of snake-spotting because water enters the natural habitat of snakes and forces them out,” he said.
 
 Ahmedabad rural police has reported a snake-bite death. According to investigators, Raman Makwana, 40, was bitten by a snake at his residence in Kamod village on July 19. He remained at home for three days and then was shifted to a primary health centre. He died during treatment on July 24. Investigators said that snakes were being spotted in periphery of the Ahmedabad city.
