VIJAYAWADA: Due to the precarious state of Andhra Pradesh finances, the state government is looking to borrow 7000 crore. The state will avail of this facility under the Fiscal Responsibility and Budget Management Act ( ) Act of 2003, in the second half of the financial year 2017-18.

 Addressing a review meeting on Thursday, finance minister   said finances of state government are in distress with revenue not matching galloping expenditure. Echoing similar views, chief minister   also that Centre has to give about 13,000 crore to the state. If the funds are not coming, alternate sources have to be tapped, the chief minister added. Earlier this week, the finance minister reviewed the financial condition of the state.
