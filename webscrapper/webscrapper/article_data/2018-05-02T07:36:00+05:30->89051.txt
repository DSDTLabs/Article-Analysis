Amritsar: AirAsia X has announced the start of a direct flight to Kuala Lumpur from Amritsar. “It is a preferred destination not only for the Punjabi diaspora but also many foreign tourists. Connectivity is the heart of tourism in Punjab and the airline offers us its established wide network of connectivity that will link Amritsar to the cities it flies to,” said Punjab cabinet minister Navjot Singh Sidhu during the launch function of the airlines here on Tuesday.

 Starting from August 16, the four time-a-week service will have an annual capacity of 1,56,832 seats.
 
 On the occasion, Benyamin Ismail , CEO , AirAsia X, said that, “Many Sikhs and Punjabis have been asking us to fly direct to Amritsar, home to the world famous Golden Temple, and we are pleased to be able to offer this direct service to this holy city.” Among others who were present on the occasion included Amritsar MP Gurjit Singh Aujla and DC Kamaldeep Singh Sangha.
 
 Meanwhile, a team of Business Sweden, the trade and invest council that helps Swedish companies increase their global sales and also other international companies invest and expand in Sweden, headed by deputy trade commissioner Magnus Anderson interacted with Amritsar Smart City Limited CEO Deepti Uppal and other officials to get a better understanding on the latest technology trends. Anderson agreed to explore avenues on how Swedish companies could work on some pilot projects in Amritsar and take the collaboration to the next level.
