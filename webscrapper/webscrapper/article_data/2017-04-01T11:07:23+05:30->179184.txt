PANAJI: Around 19, 362 students will answer the Class X exams of the   and  , starting Saturday. The exams will begin with the first   and will be held across  .

 Students will get a day’s break on Sunday, following which they will be answering the mathematics paper on Monday.
 
 The exams will end on April 10 for regular students, on April 15 for pre-vocational course students and on April 21 for children with special needs.
 
 Last year, 20,814 students had appeared for the  . This year, the numbers have reduced by about 1,500. Board chairperson Jose Remedios Rebello said that the board is yet to determine the reason for this drop in numbers.
 
 While the examination will begin at 9.30am, the board has asked candidates to be present at the places of examination 30 minutes before the commencement of the paper. Late arrival beyond 30 minutes of commencement of the examination shall disqualify the candidate from appearing for the paper.
 
 The board has informed that students are not allowed to leave the examination hall in the first hour after commencement of the exam, to avoid any malpractices.
 
 “Under no circumstances, examinations shall be postponed, even if any day is declared a public holiday,” the board said.
 
 The board has launched a helpline number (2413118) to attend to students' queries on seating arrangements. Students will also receive details and cellphone numbers of conductors and observers of their centres to clarify any doubts.
 
 “The candidates are informed not to carry any electronic gadget and to strictly follow the instructions printed on the admission card and main answer book,” the board said.
