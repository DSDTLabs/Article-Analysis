THANJAVUR:   deputy chief minister   on Thursday said the state government would approach the Supreme Court to get its share of Cauvery water from  .

 While speaking to reporters at a marriage reception, Panneerselvam said that chief minister   had not received any reply from his Karnataka counterpart, Siddaramaiah, for a letter EPS had sent.
 
 As per the final verdict of the Cauvery water tribunal, Karnataka should release 192 tmc of Cauvery water. At least 10 tmc of water was the immediate need to save the standing crops in delta districts, he added.
 
 While alleging the BJP led central government for not constituting a Cauvery management board as per the order of the Supreme Court, Panneerselvam said the Tamil Nadu government would approach the Supreme Court to get justice.
