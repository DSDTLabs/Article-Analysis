LUCKNOW: As heat wave continued unabated, mercury rose further on Saturday, making it the hottest day of the season so far. No relief is likely on Sunday. The maximum temperature in the city was 41.8 degrees Celsius, six degrees above normal, and minimum temperature was 20.9 degrees Celsius, three degrees above normal. 

 It was also the `hottest start' of April in the past 15 years. Such high temperatures are normally witness in the second half of the month of April. On Sunday, maximum and minimum temperatures are expected to be around 40 and 20 degrees Celsius respectively. On Thursday, the city had recorded 41.1 degrees Celsius and on Friday 41 degrees Celsius. 
 
 State Met director JP Gupta said that the   (WD) responsible for high temperatures is expected to fizzle out by Sunday night. As a result, temperatures are expected to drop to normal levels on Monday-Tuesday. In the meantime, another WD would hit the hill region on Tuesday. This WD would have impact on plains as well. Cloudy sky and thundershowers are expected in   on Wednesday and Thursday. 
 
 Lucknow may also witness cloudy sky on Wednesday. There are 50:50 chances of light rains or thundershowers on Thursday.
 
  With the weather remaining largely dry throughout Uttar Pradesh, mercury crossed the 40 degree mark at number of places in the state. Allahabad was the hottest place in the state with maximum temperature touching 43.2 degrees Celsius, almost six degrees above the normal, while Varanasi recorded 42.5 degrees, Sultanpur 41.4 degrees and Rae Bareli 41 degrees Celsius. Jhansi recorded a high of 41.7 degrees Celsius. The maximum temperatures recorded in Basti and Kanpur were 40.2 and 40.6 degrees Celsius respectively.
