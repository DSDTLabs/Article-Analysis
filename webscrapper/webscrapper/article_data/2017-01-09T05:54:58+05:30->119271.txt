BENGALURU: Allaying fears about Bengaluru being unsafe for women, Karnataka chief minister Siddaramaiah on Sunday told the Indian diaspora that assault on women is taken seriously by the state government. 

 Speaking at the   (PBD) - 2017, Siddaramaiah said his government was committed to bringing to book those who attack women. His assurance comes in the backdrop of the shocking video of a girl being groped by some perverts at Kammanahalli in Bengaluru on the night of December 31. The incident, which was first reported by The Times of India, had sparked a nation-wide outrage with regard to safety of women. 
 
 Siddaramaiah said: "Women safety is top priority for us. We are also working on increasing the rate of conviction."
