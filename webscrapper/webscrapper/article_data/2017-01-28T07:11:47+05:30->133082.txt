LUCKNOW: Overnight   brought relief from the warm winter spell prevailing for the past five days in the city. The sky would mainly remain clear on Saturday with normal temperatures. 

 Strong winds along with drizzle lashed the city from early hours till afternoon on Friday. As a result, maximum temperature did not rise beyond 20.3 degrees Celsius, four degrees below normal. However, minimum temperature was 13.7 degrees Celsius, three degrees above normal, due to cloudy sky. Total 5mm rainfall was recorded during the day. 
 
 State Met director JP Gupta said the western disturbance responsible for thundershowers has fizzled out. Day temperatures will rise gradually during the day, but drop in the night. Normal   conditions are expected on Saturday.
