Ludhiana: Panic gripped Pritam Colony of Giaspura village, when the body of a woman — which had already started decomposing — was found from a field on Saturday morning. It is suspected that the woman’s face was burnt with acid. However, the police are yet to ascertain this.

 On Saturday around 10am, Rajiv Kumar, who works in the fields, saw the body of a woman and raised an alarm. The residents huddled there and the police was sounded. Assistant commissioner of police (ACP-South) Amandeep Singh Brar reached the spot with Focal Point police. Later, the forensic team of the police was also called on the spot. Rajiv Kumar told the police that in morning, he had gone to the fields for cutting the crops, when he smelled something foul. He checked around and saw a woman’s body. He got scared and fled from there to inform the owner of the fields, Balbir Singh. Balbir approached the village panchayat who called the police.
 
 ACP Brar said the body had started decomposing. However, it is yet to be ascertained if the face was burnt with acid or not. The police have started questioned Balbir and other villagers. Brar said the police would try to identify the woman from her clothes and accessories. She was wearing a suit of maroon colour, pink footwear, and a bracelet in her right hand. Her body has been sent to the Civil Hospital, where it would be preserved for 72 hours.
