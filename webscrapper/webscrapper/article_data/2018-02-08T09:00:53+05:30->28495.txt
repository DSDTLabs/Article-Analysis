NEW DELHI: Athletes from Maharashtra continued to dominate in various sports disciplines to swell their state's medals tally on the penultimate day of the   ( ). With 24 gold medals, Maharashtra held on to its slender lead over Haryana (23 gold) and hosts Delhi (22 gold) to sit atop the table with a total of 84 medals.

 
 
  
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
 With one more day left, the race for the honours in terms of gold is still wide open as more than 50 gold medals will be decided on a packed final day. Boxing itself will account for 26 gold on the concluding day of KISG competitions.
 
 
  
  
 
 
 Maharashtra picked up a rich haul of 16 medals, including five gold, on Wednesday - courtesy some fine performances by its gymnasts, judokas, weightlifters and kho-kho players - to inch ahead of Haryana in the medals tally. Karnataka (15 gold, 11 silver and 15 bronze) occupy the fourth spot, while Uttar Pradesh (9 gold, 18 silver and 20 bronze) are fifth.
