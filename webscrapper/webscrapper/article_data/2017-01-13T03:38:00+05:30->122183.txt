Panaji: Goa-based West Coast Shipyard and Portugal’s Estaleiros Navais de Peniche (ENP) signed an   on Thursday to build and develop vessels using composite materials for Indian and regional markets.

 “There is an investment from Portugal to accomplish the contract. There are projects we are looking at to build parts of the ships in Goa or at least assemble them here,” said ENP administrator Jorge Brandao.
 
 As part of the MoU, ENP and West Coast Shipyard intends to develop high-quality fibreglass or glass reinforced plastic (GRP) boats in India. The project will entail a transfer of design and technical know-how in GRP boat building.
