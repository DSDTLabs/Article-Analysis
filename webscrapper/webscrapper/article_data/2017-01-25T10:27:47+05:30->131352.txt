AHMEDABAD: Amdavadis who are still keeping their woollens handy were taken aback by Tuesday’s temperatures: by noon, many were sweating as the maximum temperature soared to 34.8° celsius, recording the hottest day of January in a decade. The maximum temperature was 6.5 degrees higher than normal. In fact, it was just 1.3 degrees below the all-time high of 36.1° celsius recorded in January 1912, according to meteorological records.

 Mercury soared above 33°celsius in over 12 towns of the state on Tuesday. Surat was the hottest in Gujarat, recording 36° celsius. The heat will pave the way for cloudy skies, and some light rains are expected in isolated places in Kutch on Wednesday and   on Thursday. Ahmedabad sky is likely to be partly cloudy with day temperature declining.
 
 “There is a western disturbance induced cyclonic circulation over central Pakistan and adjoining areas,” said IMD Gujarat director  . Weather officials said that the sudden rise in temperatures in most places in the state can be attributed to easterly-south easterly winds. These bring moisture and cause a spike in temperatures. Once the western disturbance clears, the night temperature is expected to record a fall on January 27 night. “There will be a perceptible reduction in the night temperature on Friday,” a weather official said. “It was unexpectedly hot on Tuesday and I switched on the AC in my car to beat the heat,” said  , a sales executive in Satellite.
