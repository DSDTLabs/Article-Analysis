NAGPUR: Antyodaya scheme, which is aimed at reaching the grassroots level, will cater to the interest of labourers and workers besides solving their problems, Union minister for public grievances and pensions Dr   has said.

 He was speaking after inaugurating Bharatiya Pratiraksha Mazdoor Sangh’s (BPMS) two-day national study camp, at Ordnance Factory, Ambajhari, on Friday. D V Gondnale, senior manager of Ordinance Factory; Narendra Tiwari, national president of BPMS; Shriram Batwe and MP Singh were also present on the occasion.
 
 Making its clear that the government led by Prime Minister Narendra Modi is for the poor, Singh said the regime is committed to provide justice to grassroots level of society during the birth centenary year of Pandit Deendayal Upadhyaya. The defence ministry’s personnel department has been working carefully on cadre review, he said.
 
 “Issues related to recruitment on the basis of compassion, beneficiaries under the new pension scheme and disparity in the   will be sorted out judiciously and satisfactorily,” the minister said.
 
 “The prime minister has been personally looking into the problems of labourers and small entrepreneurs. Soon, small entrepreneurs and hotel owners will be permitted to do business round the clock,” he said, adding that the central government gives top priority to the problems of workers.
 
 He invited attention of the audience to the facts that the government has already sorted out problems such as child labour and extendeing maternity leave of working women.
 
 A delegation of BPMS office-bearers submitted a memorandum to the minister on the occasion.
