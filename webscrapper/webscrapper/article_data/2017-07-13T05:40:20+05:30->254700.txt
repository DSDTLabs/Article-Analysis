CHENNAI: A   court in the Madras high court complex on Wednesday awarded seven years rigorous imprisonment to a senior manager of the   (IOB) for giving credit facility to a private company and thereby incurring a loss of Rs4.9 crore to the exchequer. A fine of Rs9.20 lakh was also imposed on him.

 The court also convicted five other people in connection with the case, while three others were acquitted. The court awarded a total fine amount of Rs19.2 lakh to all those who were convicted.
 
 XI Special Judge for CBI cases M P Ram convicted IOB senior manager, S Jagannathan. The Economic Offences Wing of CBI,  , investigated a case against   for sanctioning credit facilities to Joy Shipping Services Private Limited.
 
 Based on inflated collateral security and fabricated documents, Jagannathan had sanctioned the loan and caused a wrongful loss to the tune of Rs4.9 crores to Indian Overseas Bank in Parrys. 
 
 CBI registered a case against Jagannathan under the IPC Sections 120-B (criminal conspiracy) read with 419 (cheating by impersonation), 420 (cheating), 467 (submitting forgery document), 468 (forgery for the purpose of cheating) and 471 (using forged as a genuine document) and Sec 13(2) read with 3(1)(d) of Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988.
