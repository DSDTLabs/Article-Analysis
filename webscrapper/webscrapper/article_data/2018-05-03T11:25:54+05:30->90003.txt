THIRUVANANTHAPURAM: The Kerala Secondary School Leaving Certificate (SSLC) results 2018 was declared on Thursday, May 3. As per reports, 97.84 per cent students have qualified for higher studies. 

 The result was announced by Kerala education minister C Raveendranath at a press conference held in Thiruvananthapuram. Of the 4,41,103 students who appeared for the SSLC exam, about 4,31,162 have cleared the examination. Last year, the pass percentage was 95.98 per cent. 
 
 
 
 
  
  
 
 The result are now available on the official website of the Kerala Board of Public Examinations (KBPE) - http://kbpe.org
 
 As many as 34,313 students secured A+ for all subjects. While Ernakulam district come first with 99.12 per cent victory, Wayanad district come last with 93.87 per cent.
 
 The students can submit the application for revaluation and scrutiny of answer papers from May 5 to 10, 2018. 
 
 Speaking at the press conference, education minister Raveendranath said there will be enough plus one seats available for students seeking higher education opportunities.
 
 "There is significant increase in the number of schools where all students become eligible for higher studies," he added.
 
 The minister also mentioned that the government had been trying to streamline the continuing evaluation system in schools and expressed hope that the system would matured into a faultless one in the coming years.
