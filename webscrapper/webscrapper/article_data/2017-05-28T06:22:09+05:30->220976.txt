BENGALURU: BJP state president and chief ministerial candidate for the party, B S Yeddyurappa said he is willing to shoulder the responsibility of bringing Goa chief minister   and Maharashtra CM Devendra Fadnavis to the negotiating table on the Mahadayi river dispute.

 “I will get the BJP CMs to discuss the problem. Let CM Siddaramaiah call upon his Congress counterparts in the two states. Let there be a round table conference to resolve the issue,” he told mediapersons.
 
 The controversy over the sharing of Mahadayi water is pending with a river tribunal. The tribunal headed by Justice JM Panchal mooted the idea of the  , Goa and Maharashtra CMs coming together and resolving the issue through talks, in the ‘spirit of brotherhood in a federal system’.
