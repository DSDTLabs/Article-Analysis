 :   (WWICS) chairman Lieutenant Colonel Baljit Singh Sandhu (retd) has told police that he stayed in Kolkata, Haryana, and Uttar Pradesh over the past 51 days of being on the run. Police wanted to grill Sandhu since Chandigarh Transport Undertaking (CTU) employee Abhishek Guleria (28) was found dead at his resort called Forest Hill Golf and Country Club.

 Sandhu surrendered on May 16, after which he was arrested and charged under Section 304-A (causing death by negligence) and 201 (disappearance of evidence) of the Indian Penal Code (IPC). Mohali senior superintendent of police (SSP) Kuldeep Singh Chahal said: “We will file the charge sheet within a week. The SIT (special investigation team) has submitted its final report to the DGP (director general of police) and all accused carry the same charges as Lt Col Sandhu.”
 
 The SSP said the findings were based on forensic evidence and autopsy report, which ruled out murder. “It seems that Abhishek sneaked into the resort by either scaling the wall or using a gap in it near the sewage treatment plant. Security camera caught him roaming on the street outside. Asking where Sandhu has been hiding these 51 days, Chahal said: “He claims to have been switching his hideouts in Kolkata, Haryana, and Uttar Pradesh. He says he was also in a hospital somewhere in Haryana.”
