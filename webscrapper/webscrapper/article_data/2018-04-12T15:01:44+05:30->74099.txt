KOLHAPUR: Police have registered a case against seven people for allegedly cheating a   based trader to the tune of Rs 1 crore, on the pretext of offering   at low interest rate.

 Police has initiated an investigation in the case but have not made any arrests yet.
 
 The incident took place between May 2017 and November 2017. The accused had promised the trader to sanction a loan amounting to Rs 100 crore.
 
 Based on the complaint, police have registered a case against seven people under several sections of IPC including section 406 (punishment for criminal breach of trust), 467 (forgery of valuable security, will, etc), 466 (forgery of record of court or of public register, etc) and section 420 (cheating and dishonesty inducing delivery of property).
 
 A police officer attached with Shahupuri police station said, “As per the complainant, the accused contacted him in May 2017 and offered him loan at a cheap rate. The victim wanted a loan of Rs 100 crore to expand his existing business.”
 
 The officer added, “The accused said they need some money from the victim to process the loan proposal. The complainant agreed and paid them Rs 1.04 crore in instalments over the course of six months.”
 
 “After receiving the amount, the accused tried to avoid the complainant and started ignoring his calls. Realising that he was cheated, the victim lodged a complaint with Shahupuri police station,” the officer said.
 
 He added, “We are investigating the case and searching for the whereabouts of the accused. We are also gathering information about the amount that the victim paid the accused and how he came in contact with them.”
