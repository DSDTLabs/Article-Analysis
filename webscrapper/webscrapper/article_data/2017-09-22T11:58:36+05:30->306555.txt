 The accused are T H Vijaya Lakshmi, commissioner of IT (Audit) for Tamil Nadu and Puduchery and her husband Dr A Suresh, MLA who was a former Indian Railway Accounts Service (IRAS) officer.
 
 The CBI in the FIR said during the period April 2010 to February 2016, the accused have acquired assets/pecuniary advantage to the tune of Rs 5.95 crore against an income of Rs 4.84 crore. “Thereby they have amassed disproportionate assets to the tune of Rs 1.10 crore which is 22.86% of income earned by them, which they cannot satisfactorily account for,” the FIR charged.
 
 The assets of the couple detailed in the CBI FIR include plots in Hyderabad, Nalgonda, West Godavari district, Ranga Reddy district, gold jewellery and two cars.
 
 This has prima-facie constituted commission of offences by the accused under section 109 of IPC and section 13(2) read with section 13(1)(e) of the Prevention of Corruption Act, CBI said. These pertain to criminal misconduct of possessing assets and pecuniary resources disproportionate to known sources of income and abetment of the same.
 
 Lakshmi is a 1994 batch of Indian Revenue Service (IRS) and joined the IT department as an assistant commissioner. She worked in Bangalore, Raichur, Kurnool and Hyderabad in different roles before she was posted to Chennai.
 
 Suresh had held the position of deputy financial advisor and chief ccounts Officer (Dy FA and CAO) in Indian Railways. He subsequently resigned from government service and joined politics. He has been an MLA from 2009 to 2014 and from 2014 onwards. He was a member of committee on assurances, SC&ST welfare and on public accounts and hence has been labelled a public servant. He is a permanent resident of Markapur in Prakasam district, but currently lives in Jubilee hills in Hyderabad. Further investigation is underway in the process, CBI said.
