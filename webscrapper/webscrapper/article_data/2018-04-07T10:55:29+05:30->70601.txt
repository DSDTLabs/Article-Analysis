PANAJI: Additional sessions court at   granted conditional anticipatory bail to a couple, who apprehended arrest in a case of alleged cheating and transferring of a beach resort property at   by registering sale deeds.

 Police said that the accused couple, Chandrakant and  , had entered into a partnership agreement with a relative and purchased a hotel in the name of the partnership firm,  , in 2008. In 2015, the accused sold the hotel property to another registered firm—PCA Products—without taking authority from their relative. Incidentally, the accused are also partners in PCA Products.
 
 The applicants argued that the matter was basically a civil dispute. Police, in their reply, said that custody of the accused was required in order to recover the original sale deed and to obtain their signature and handwriting. Judge   held that custodial interrogation was not required for collection of documentary evidence. She directed that, in the event of arrest, the accused be released on furnishing a personal bond of Rs 50,000 and one surety in the same amount. Complainant Madhuri Dudhalkar alleged that she was cheated to the tune of Rs 1.5 crore.
