NEW DELHI:   is investigating the death of a 52-year-old party venue owner, who allegedly committed suicide in his office in outer Delhi’s Mianwali Nagar on Thursday. 

 Before taking his life, the victim accused a man of torturing and cheating him of crores of rupees.
 
 Deputy commissioner of police (outer) MN Tiwari said that a suicide note was recovered from his office, where the victim held the man responsible for his death. “We have initiated an investigation after registering the case under sections of abetment of suicide,” Tiwari added.
 
 Police sources said that the deceased was identified as Ramesh Kumar, a resident of New Model Town in northwest Delhi. Kumar was found hanging by a ceiling fan by his colleagues, who rushed him to a nearby hospital where he was declared brought dead. 
 
 The incident took place in the banquet hall, which he co-owned as a business partner.
 
 Police officers at the crime scene saw messages written on the wall, allegedly by Kumar, where he blamed a man for his death. The victim also wrote a suicide note, where he claimed that the man was mentally torturing him for the past two years and blamed him for his death.
 
 Kumar also wrote in the suicide note that he was cheated of crores of rupees by the man during their venture into banquet hall business.
 
 Police have taken the man named in the suicide note for questioning and are probing him for more information about events prior to Kumar’s suicide. It has been confirmed that there were some financial transactions between the deceased and the man.
 
 Cops said that they are examining the content of the suicide note and the messages written on the wall. Officers from the crime and forensic department were roped in to collect the fingerprints in the room. A handwriting expert was also called in to match the writing on the suicide note with that of Kumar.
 
 During his post-mortem, doctors found marks made by a rope on Kumar’s neck, which led to his death.
