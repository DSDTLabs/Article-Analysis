Surat: The Gems and Jewellery Export Promotion Council (GJEPC) has pledged contribution of $2 million or Rs 12.50 crore to Diamond Producers’ Association (DPA), a consortium of world’s leading diamond miners.

 The DPA, recently earmarked Rs 400 crore for marketing the generic promotion of diamonds and diamond jewellery in the world’s leading diamond consumer markets, including India, the US and China. 
 
 While a major portion of the funds will be used in promoting diamond jewellery in the US, a fair amount will be spent in India and China as well. 
 
 The marketing campaign ‘Real is Rare’ will feature in India starting September in order to boost the diamond jewellery consumer demand. 
 
 As per Diamond Insight report published by world’s largest diamond mining company De Beers, diamond jewellery consumers in India were severely affected due to demonetization, jewellers strike and fluctuating currency rates. The diamond jewellery consumer demand with a sharp decline of 8.8 per cent in the overall demand from Rs 21,000 crore in 2015 to Rs 19,000 crore in 2016. 
 
 The report further states that India is going to be the focus country to spur the diamond jewellery demand as nearly 60 per cent of its 1.2 billion population as per 2011 census is under the age of 30 years.
 
 Gems and Jewellery Export Promotion Council (GJEPC) regional chairman Dinesh Navadia said, “The GJEPC, DPA and De Beers are working in unison to work out marketing strategies to reach the millennials and the women.”
