SHIMLA: To provide livelihood to local youth by encouraging local entrepreneurship and promoting self-employment opportunities, the   cabinet in its meeting held on Thursday approved the draft notification of Mukhya Mantri Swavlamban Yojna-2018.

 Chief minister   presided over the meeting in which a decision was taken to launch   for women empowerment and environmental conservation in the state. Under this scheme, the state government will provide security for LPG connections and gas stoves to remaining households not covered under Ujjawala Scheme being run by the central government.
 
 While reviewing the departmental working of MPP and power department, it was decided to make electricity complaint system more effective, especially installation of new connections. Stress was also laid on bringing distribution losses to zero. It was also stressed to form a quick action team at divisional level so that action could be taken immediately in case of emergency. The cabinet also stressed that all projects nearing completion should be commissioned at the earliest and work on other projects should be speeded up. It also emphasized the need for making arrangements of solar cookers in mid-day meal programme and anganwadi centres.
 
 The cabinet gave approval to increase the honorarium of office-bearers of Panchayati Raj Institutions in the state, according to which honorarium of zila parishad chairman has been increased from Rs 8,000 to Rs 11,000, of vice-chairman from Rs 6,000 to Rs 7,500, of member from Rs 3,500 to Rs 4,500, of panchayat samiti chairman from Rs 5,000 to Rs 6,500, of panchayat samiti vice-chairman from Rs 3,500 to Rs 4,500, of panchayat samiti member from Rs 3,000 to Rs 4,000, of gram panchayat pradhan from Rs 3,000 to Rs 4,000 and of gram panchayat up-pradhan from Rs 2,200 to Rs 2,500. 
 
 Similarly, meeting allowance of gram panchayat member has been enhanced to Rs 240 per meeting from the existing Rs 225 with maximum two meetings a month. Nod was also given to enhance state share of honorarium of anganwadi workers, anganwadi helpers and mini-anganwadi workers from Rs 1,450 to Rs 1,750, from Rs 600 to Rs 900 and from Rs 750 to Rs 1,050 per month, respectively. With this enhancement, anganwadi workers will get honorarium of Rs 4,750, anganwadi helpers Rs 2,400 and mini-anganwadi workers Rs 3,300 per month.
 
 A decision was also taken to increase annual scholarship under Dr Ambedkar Medhavi Chhatravriti Yojna from Rs 10,000 to Rs 12,000 and increase the number of students eligible for this scholarship in 10+1+2 classes from 2,000 to 2,500.
 
 Approval was given to relax the condition prospectively for grant of full pension on completion of 20 years of qualifying service to all class-III and class-IV government employees who seek voluntary or premature retirement by modifying the notification issued by the finance department on November 11, 2014. It also gave approval to fill 333 posts of different categories on contract basis in National Health Mission to provide better healthcare facilities to the people of the state.
